> Elite endurance athlete featured as case study in Howon Noh's VO2 max video. Lab-tested VO2 max of 84 ml/kg/min with elite aerobic power but anaerobic-limited performance. Demonstrates advanced personalized training methodology.

## Athletic Profile

- **VO2 max (lab test)**: 84 ml/kg/min
- **Marathon time**: 2:25
- **Mile personal best**: 4:40 (sub-4:40)
- **Training specialty**: Distance running with elite aerobic capacity

## Training Case Study

Finton's testing revealed:
- **Aerobic Power**: Elite (VO2 max pace 4:48/mile, threshold pace 5:20/mile)
- **Anaerobic Battery**: Limiting factor (insufficient speed/intensity capacity despite elite aerobic output)
- **Programming adjustment**: Shifted to 60% easy / 30% anaerobic / 10% aerobic power split (vs. typical 80/20 rule) to maximize improvements within his actual limiting ceiling

## Personalized Training Protocol

- Days 1, 3, 5: 90 minutes-2 hours easy at 6:50/mile pace + 10 strides
- Day 2: Anaerobic focus — rotating 10x400m or 8x800m repeats at test-determined paces
- Day 4: Threshold run 30-40 min at 5:20/mile
- Week 2, Day 4: 3x1-mile repeats at 4:48/mile pace (VO2 max ceiling)
- Day 6: Long run alternating easy (6:50 pace, 2-2.5 hrs) and hard (5:06 pace) sessions

## Key Lesson

Elite aerobic power doesn't guarantee overall VO2 max ceiling improvement. Identifying and training the limiting factor (in Finton's case anaerobic battery) is essential for further gains.
