Heart Rate Variability—the variation in time intervals between successive heartbeats. Higher HRV generally indicates better recovery, parasympathetic nervous system activation, and stress resilience. Often tracked via wearables and used as a recovery/readiness metric.

## Key Distinction from VO2 Max

Optimizing HRV requires different training stimulus than maximizing [[VO2 max]]. An athlete can improve resting heart rate without moving VO2 max, or vice versa. Programming goals determine appropriate training emphasis.

## Seasonal Periodization

[[Howon Noh]] dedicates Fall training blocks specifically to improving HRV and stress resilience as part of his 90-day seasonal rotation (Winter: muscle, Spring: VO2 max, Summer: strength, Fall: HRV/resilience).

## Recovery Indicator

Higher HRV correlates with:
- Better sleep quality
- Reduced stress
- Improved readiness for hard training
- Lower infection/illness risk
- Better hormone balance

## Training Implications

HRV improvement focuses on recovery modalities, sleep quality, stress management, and lower-intensity movement rather than high-intensity training blocks.
