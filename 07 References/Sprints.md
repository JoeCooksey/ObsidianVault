Maximum-effort running at top speed for 20-30 seconds duration. Trains [[processing power]] and [[fast-twitch muscle fibers]]. Different from [[intervals]] in that you're going as fast as physically possible rather than targeting a specific pace.

## Protocol

- **Duration**: 20-30 seconds at absolute maximum speed
- **Effort**: 100% all-out; not paced, just go as fast as possible
- **Recovery**: Full recovery between sprints (walk or very easy run)
- **Volume**: 3-6 sprints per session
- **Frequency**: 1-2x per week (high neuromuscular demand)

## Training Benefits

- Develops top-end speed ceiling
- Trains neural power and muscle recruitment
- Maintains [[fast-twitch muscle fibers]]
- Prevents speed plateau in endurance athletes

## Distinction from Strides

- **Sprints**: 20-30 sec maximum effort, full recovery, 1-2x/week, high intensity
- **[[Strides]]**: 20 sec controlled fast, 30-40 sec walk recovery, end of easy runs, 2-3x/week, moderate intensity
