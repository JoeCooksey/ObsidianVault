The iron-containing protein in red blood cells responsible for oxygen transport throughout the body. Higher hemoglobin levels increase oxygen-carrying capacity, directly supporting [[VO2 max]] ceiling.

## Endurance Athlete Risk

High-volume cardio athletes face elevated hemoglobin and iron deficiency risk due to:
- **Repeated impact**: Breaks down red blood cells
- **Sweat loss**: Iron exits through perspiration
- **Inadequate replacement**: Most endurance athletes chronically low without knowing

## Micronutrient Requirements

Hemoglobin production requires three nutrients:
- **Iron** (structural component)
- **Vitamin B12** (red blood cell synthesis)
- **Vitamin B9 (Folate)** (red blood cell synthesis)

Deficiency in any one limits hemoglobin and VO2 max improvement regardless of training quality.

## Testing Protocol

Get blood work testing before supplementing, especially for iron (excessive supplementation contributes to oxidative stress and liver damage). Supplement only what tests show deficiency.
