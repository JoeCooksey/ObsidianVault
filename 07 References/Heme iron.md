Bioavailable form of iron found in animal products (red meat, poultry, fish). Heme iron is absorbed 2-3x more efficiently by the body than non-heme iron from plant sources. Preferred supplementation form when iron supplementation is indicated by blood testing.

## Safety Profile

Heme iron supplementation is safer than ferrous iron alternatives (cheaper non-heme forms), which carry oxidative stress and liver damage risk at higher doses.

## When to Supplement

Only supplement iron if blood work confirms deficiency. High-volume cardio athletes (especially runners) face elevated deficiency risk due to:
- Repeated impact destroying red blood cells
- Iron loss through sweat

## Dietary Sources

Red meat is the best natural heme iron source, along with salmon and other fatty fish. Combining with vitamin C increases absorption.
