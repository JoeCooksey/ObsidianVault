Muscle fibers specialized for rapid, powerful contractions. Critical for speed development and high-intensity effort capacity. Endurance athletes performing only easy-pace training gradually lose fast-twitch fiber recruitment capability.

## Training Stimulus

Fast-twitch fibers require regular fast stimulus to maintain and develop. Without specific speed work, the body "loses what you don't train"—neural pathways and muscle fiber patterns for speed degrade over months of high-volume easy running.

## Preservation Protocol

[[Strides]] (20-second fast bursts at run's end, 2-3x per week) maintain fast-twitch capacity without creating meaningful fatigue. This technique reminds legs what fast feels like and prevents gradual speed loss common in base-building phases.
