Sustained hard running effort at goal marathon pace, typically 20-40 minutes duration. Less intense than [[lactate threshold|threshold runs]] but significantly harder than [[Zone 2 training|easy running]]. Uncomfortable to maintain but not maximal effort.

## Protocol

- **Duration**: 20-40 minutes sustained hard effort
- **Intensity**: Marathon goal pace (uncomfortable but sustainable for the duration)
- **Heart rate**: Typically around 85-90% max HR
- **Frequency**: 1x per week in periodized training

## Training Benefits

- Develops lactate buffering capacity
- Increases running economy at hard paces
- Bridges gap between [[Zone 2 training]] and maximum intensity
- Teaches body to sustain discomfort without breaking down

## Distinction from Threshold Runs

- **Tempo**: Marathon pace, 20-40 min, slightly less intense
- **Threshold**: Lactate threshold pace, ~1 hour maximum, slightly more intense

Both are valuable; tempo is slightly easier and can be performed more frequently.
