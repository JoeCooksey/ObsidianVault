The maximum amount of oxygen your body can utilize during intense exercise, measured in milliliters of oxygen per kilogram of body weight per minute (ml/kg/min). VO2 max is the single best predictor of human longevity and cardiovascular health. Higher VO2 max enables greater sustained high-intensity effort and faster recovery between efforts.

## Key Distinctions

VO2 max improvement is fundamentally different from optimizing [[HRV]] (heart rate variability) or [[resting heart rate]]. Many runners improve resting heart rate without moving VO2 max, or vice versa. Programming requires understanding which metric you're targeting.

## Training Stimulus

VO2 max responds to specific training stimuli:
- **Aerobic Power** (heart's engine size): Trained via long runs, easy runs, [[threshold runs]], [[Norwegian 4x4]]
- **Anaerobic Battery** (speed/intensity capacity): Trained via 400m-800m repeats
- **Processing Power** (top-end speed): Trained via sprints and [[strides]]

The ideal training mix depends on your current limiting factor, identifiable through VO2 max lab testing.

## Typical Ceilings

- General population: 30-45 ml/kg/min
- Trained endurance athletes: 60-75 ml/kg/min
- Elite athletes: 80-90+ ml/kg/min (world-class skiers/marathoners)
- World record holders: 96 ml/kg/min (cross-country skiing)
