Top-end speed capacity—the fastest pace you can physically achieve. Analogous to iPhone processing speed: a newer phone runs multiple apps seamlessly, while an older device crashes under load. Higher processing power creates greater "headroom" at every pace below it, allowing faster sustainable speeds.

## Training Stimulus

Processing power improves through pure speed work:
- [[Sprints]] (20-30 sec at maximum effort)
- [[Strides]] (20 sec fast bursts)
- Frequency: 1-2x per week, always after aerobic base work

## Individual Variation

Athletes often neglect processing power, assuming "endurance" means ignoring speed. Result: speed plateaus completely despite improved aerobic fitness. Regular sprint work is non-negotiable for continued speed improvement.

## Training Ratio

Athletes whose [[VO2 max test]] identifies speed as limiting factor should allocate 10% of training to pure sprint work (75% aerobic power / 15% anaerobic / 10% processing power).
