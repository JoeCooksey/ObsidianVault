Your heart rate while at complete rest. A metric that often improves with training but is distinct from [[VO2 max]]. An athlete can improve resting heart rate significantly without moving their [[VO2 max]], and vice versa—they respond to different training stimuli.

## Key Distinction

Optimizing resting heart rate requires different programming than maximizing [[VO2 max]]. The two metrics don't necessarily move in tandem, so understanding which you're targeting is essential for training design.

## Typical Ranges

- **General population**: 60-100 bpm
- **Trained athletes**: 40-60 bpm
- **Elite endurance athletes**: 30-50 bpm

## Improvement Methods

Resting heart rate improves through:
- Consistent [[Zone 2 training]]
- Sleep quality
- Stress reduction
- [[HRV]] (heart rate variability) optimization
- Recovery-focused periodization
