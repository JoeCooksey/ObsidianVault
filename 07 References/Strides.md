Short 20-second fast-effort running bursts performed at the end of [[Zone 2 training|easy runs]]. Preserve [[fast-twitch muscle fibers]] and speed capacity without creating meaningful fatigue or impacting recovery.

## Protocol

- **Duration**: 20 seconds of fast effort per stride
- **Recovery**: Walk 30-40 seconds between each stride
- **Volume**: 6-8 strides per session (2-4 minutes total)
- **Frequency**: 2-3x per week integrated into easy runs
- **Effort**: "Fast" but controlled; not all-out sprint

## Benefits

- Maintains neuromuscular speed patterns
- Prevents gradual speed loss during base-building phases
- Teaches legs what fast feels like without taxing the system
- Can be skipped on low-energy or sore days (but don't skip the easy run)

## When to Perform

Always perform strides at the END of an easy run, never before or during, as the easy run provides warm-up and the strides serve as a finisher that doesn't impact the primary stimulus.
