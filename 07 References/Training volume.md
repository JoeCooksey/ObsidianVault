Total amount of training performed, typically measured in hours per week or miles per week. Training volume is the ultimate limiting variable for [[VO2 max]] ceiling—elite athletes achieving 80-90+ ml/kg/min VO2 max average 20-25 hours of training per week for 3+ years.

## Volume-VO2 Max Ceiling Relationship

- **10-20 hours/week**: 60-75 ml/kg/min typical ceiling
- **20-25+ hours/week**: 80-90+ ml/kg/min typical ceiling (elite/professional level)

However, extreme volume creates systemic trade-offs:
- Muscle mass loss
- Hormonal disruption
- Recovery impairment
- Opportunity cost for other health domains

## Sustainable Approach

[[Howon Noh]] recommends 7 hours/week maximum during [[VO2 max]] focus blocks as a sustainable upper limit that delivers 90% of benefits without systemic stress. This fits within seasonal periodization where different health domains (muscle, strength, HRV) receive emphasis in other quarters.

## Programming Principle

80% easy training volume provides disproportionate benefits; the last 20% yields diminishing returns while accumulating fatigue and injury risk.
