Moderate-intensity cardio performed at a conversational pace—intensity level where you can speak in complete sentences without heavy breathing. Zone 2 represents approximately 60-70% of maximum heart rate and forms the foundation of most endurance training programs.

## Characteristics

- **Conversational pace**: Can speak sentences; feels "almost too easy"
- **Heart rate zone**: Roughly 60-70% max HR
- **Energy system**: Primarily aerobic (fat and glycogen oxidation)
- **Recovery cost**: Minimal; can be performed frequently (even daily for some athletes)
- **Psychological**: Should not feel like "real training"

## Training Benefits

Zone 2 work builds:
- Aerobic base capacity
- Capillary density
- Mitochondrial density
- Fat oxidation efficiency
- Work capacity for harder efforts
- Recovery stimulus without creating fatigue

## Programming Guidelines

Most endurance programs allocate 80-85% of weekly volume to Zone 2 training. [[Howon Noh]] averages 20,000+ daily steps through frequent Zone 2 movement (walking 5 min every hour).

## Common Mistakes

- Running Zone 2 too hard (defeats purpose, increases injury risk, impairs recovery)
- Insufficient Zone 2 volume (limits ceiling for harder work)
- Mixing Zone 2 with other training types (should be distinct from hard days)
