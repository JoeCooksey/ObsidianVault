The running pace or effort level where lactate (a byproduct of muscular effort) accumulates in your blood faster than your body can clear it. Below your [[lactate threshold]], your body clears lactate as fast as it's produced. Above it, lactate buildup triggers leg burn, respiratory spike, and forced slowdown.

## Physiology

During running, muscles produce lactate continuously. At easy efforts, the body's lactate clearance rate exceeds production rate. As intensity increases, lactate production accelerates. The lactate threshold represents the effort level where these rates balance—the highest pace sustainable for approximately one hour.

## Training Adaptation

Training at or near your lactate threshold moves this breaking point higher over time:
- Lactate threshold increases
- Maximum sustainable pace increases
- [[VO2 max]] ceiling rises

## Threshold Run Protocol

- **Duration**: 20-40 minutes at sustained hard effort (or ~1 hour for threshold-specific training)
- **Intensity**: Fastest pace you can sustain for about an hour; very uncomfortable
- **Frequency**: Once per week in most training programs
- **Progression**: Pace increases as fitness improves
