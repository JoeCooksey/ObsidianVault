The body's capacity to sustain effort beyond what the aerobic system (oxygen delivery) can support. Analogous to a smartphone battery that drains faster the harder you push the device. The faster you run, the faster your anaerobic battery drains.

## Key Characteristics

- **Activation threshold**: Engaged whenever running pace exceeds what your aerobic system can sustain
- **Drain rate**: Proportional to intensity—higher speed = faster depletion
- **Limited capacity**: Determines how long you can sustain high intensity before forced slowdown
- **Trainable**: Improves through specific interval work

## Training Protocol

Anaerobic battery capacity increases via:
- **400m repeats**: 5-6 sets with full recovery; trains top-end speed and battery drain rate
- **800m repeats**: 4 sets with recovery; trains mid-range anaerobic capacity
- Frequency and volume depend on your [[VO2 max test]] results identifying anaerobic as your limiting factor

## Individual Variation

Athletes with elite [[aerobic power]] may still have moderate anaerobic capacity (as with [[Finton]], who had elite 4:48/mile VO2 max pace but anaerobic-limited performance). Testing identifies which component needs development.
