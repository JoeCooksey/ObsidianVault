Low-intensity movement performed between hard training sessions to enhance recovery, increase circulation, and maintain conditioning without creating fatigue. Circulation increases 2-3x when active versus sedentary, dramatically improving nutrient delivery and waste clearance.

## Implementation Strategies

- **Daily walking**: 20,000+ steps via frequent 5-minute walks throughout the day (e.g., every hour)
- **Post-meal walks**: [[Finton]]'s non-negotiable 10-minute walk after eating; improves glucose metabolism
- **Between hard sessions**: Easy pace or walking on recovery days rather than complete rest
- **Sport alternatives**: [[Howon Noh]] replaced some [[Zone 2 training|Zone 2 runs]] with pickleball for variety while maintaining heart rate zones

## Benefits

- Accelerates recovery between hard efforts
- Maintains aerobic capacity building
- Reduces stiffness and injury risk
- Improves blood glucose handling
- Supports mental health and stress resilience
- Zero negative impact on performance

## Key Principle

Active recovery is more effective than complete rest for most athletes. The intensity matters less than consistency—walking 20,000 steps provides greater stimulus than sitting all day followed by a single hard workout.
