The heart's maximum sustainable oxygen delivery capacity—essentially the "engine size" of your cardiovascular system. Measured during [[VO2 max]] testing via oxygen consumption at various running paces. Determines the maximum training volume and intensity your system can handle.

## Training Stimulus

Aerobic power increases through:
- Long runs (60-90 min extended effort)
- Easy [[Zone 2 training|Zone 2 runs]] (foundational volume)
- [[Tempo run|Tempo runs]] (20-40 min hard sustained effort)
- [[Lactate threshold|Threshold runs]] (~1 hour at breaking point)
- [[Norwegian 4x4]] (4x4 min hard repeats)

## Testing Context

VO2 max lab testing measures aerobic power by determining the highest oxygen uptake your body can achieve. Athletes with elite aerobic power but weak [[anaerobic battery]] or [[processing power]] won't see VO2 max ceiling improvements without addressing their limiting factor.

## Individual Profiles

- **Beginner/intermediate**: Usually aerobic power-limited; 80% easy / 20% hard training ratio optimal
- **Advanced runners**: Often have developed aerobic power; improvements require focus on anaerobic or speed work
