Repeated short hard efforts with recovery periods between sets. Different from [[tempo run|tempo runs]] in that you apply maximum effort, recover, then repeat. Effective for developing [[anaerobic battery]] and [[VO2 max]] capacity.

## Common Interval Formats

Rotation prevents adaptation:
- **5x400m**: Top-end speed and neuromuscular power (Week 1)
- **4x800m**: Anaerobic midpoint (Week 2)
- **3x1 mile**: Extended anaerobic effort (Week 3)
- **2x2 miles**: Long anaerobic capacity (Week 4, then cycle back)

## Protocol Guidelines

- **Full recovery**: Walk or very easy jog between sets (should feel ready to go hard again)
- **Volume**: Match format to session type (more reps for shorter distances, fewer for longer)
- **Frequency**: 1-2x per week depending on training level
- **Rotation**: Switch formats weekly to prevent adaptation and stagnation

## Training Effect

Intervals maximize cardiovascular stimulus in minimal time. One well-executed interval session activates both [[aerobic power]] and [[anaerobic battery]] systems simultaneously.
