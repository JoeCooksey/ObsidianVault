# 📘 [[Atomic Habits]] The "North Star" Quote
> "You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems."

---

## 🚀 The 1-Minute Summary
**Core Argument:** *In one sentence, what is the author trying to prove?*
The core argument of _Atomic Habits_ is that **drastic change doesn’t come from one-off "heroic" efforts or massive willpower, but from the compound interest of tiny, 1% improvements.**
**Top 3 "System Upgrades" (Action Items):**
- [ ] Never Miss Twice Rule (Never have two bad days in a row)
- [ ] Temptation Building for Studying (only doing something you like while doing something you don't like)
- [ ] The low barrier of Entry (Whenever its late and I'm tired try to make the barrier to entry of what I am trying to do very low)

### Chapter 3: [How To Build Better Habits in 4 Simple Steps]
> [!abstract] tldr; A habit is formed from a 4-step cycle, this cycle doesn't discriminate between beneficial and destructive habits.
> 

**🧩 New Mental Models (Concepts):**
*Don't just describe them here. Create a new link and define them there.*
- **[[Habit]]**: An action that your brain links with a problem solved.
- **[[Habit Loop]]**: 4-steps: Cue (A trigger to make you want something), Craving (Your brain wanting that feeling), Response (The action you take for the craving), Reward (Your brain giving you a dopamine hit completing the cycle) If any step is missing the habit will not be formed [[50]].
- [[Four Laws of Behavior Change]]: 1. How can I make it obvious, 2. How can I make it attractive, 3. How can I make it easy, 4. How can I make it satisfying. [[54]].

**🧪 Experiments & Evidence:**

**💬 Reflection:**

		- 2: [[Habit Tracking]] is attractive and gives you the dopamine hit when you mark a habit.
		- 3: [[Habit Tracking]] is also satisfying to mark each day keeping you coming back
	- [[Habit Tracking]] can be detrimental if used incorrectly [[Goodhart's Law]]
