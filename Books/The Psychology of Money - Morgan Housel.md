## 📖 Book Preview & Summary
**The Big Idea:** Doing well with money isn’t necessarily about what you know. It’s about how you behave. Genius is the man who can do the average thing when all those around him are going crazy.

**Key Themes:**
- **Soft Skills vs. Math:** Financial success is a soft skill, where how you behave is more important than how smart you are.
- **Lived Experience:** Our personal history with money shapes our rational (and irrational) decisions.
- **Compounding:** The most powerful force in finance is time, not necessarily high returns.
- **Enough:** The hardest financial skill is getting the goalpost to stop moving.

All these finance terms such as investing and roth IRA are only less than 100 years old and people think they have mastered these concepts.

## 📥 Chapter 2: Luck and Risk
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> Don't judge people based on how much money they have since a lot of it comes down to luck and how much you risk.

### 📝 Summary
Don't mistake a lucky win for being invincible, just like how a risk and pay off and you can win big it can also drain all your money.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Specific Person]]: this can be dangerous since a specific person's success can be credited a good amount of luck that is unpredictable.
- [[Case Studies]]: instead focus on these that show general trends of success which eliminates luck's factor.

### 💭 Reflection
- Be careful who you take advice from / look up to since their actions could have been greatly attributed to luck which is not applicable to your own life. Instead focus on trends and [[Case Studies]] to find out what works for populations instead of individuals.

---
## 📥 Chapter 3: Never Enough
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> People struggle to realize when they have reached the point of having enough money and to stop risking their money further when its unnecessary.

### 📝 Summary
Don't let the goalpost of money to keep increasing past a necessary point, don't compare your wealth to others, realize that enough is not a too little amount but a point where the risk outweighs the reward, and there are [[Untouchables]] that are more valuable than money.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Untouchables]]: reputation, freedom and independence, family and friends, being loved by those who you want to love you, and happiness are all invaluable and are never worth giving up for money.

### 💭 Reflection
- Although money is a key factor to living and what many people are motivated by, know when you have enough money, don't compare yourself to others, realize that enough money isn't too little money, and never sacrifice [[Untouchables]] for money as they are likely more valuable to you than money ever can be.

## 📥 Chapter 4: Confounding Compounding
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> Compounding forces are extremely powerful for growth and is the reason 99% of warren buffets wealth was built in the last 1/3 of his life.

### 📝 Summary
Good investing isn't just about making one superb 60% ROI trade, but being able to make millions of profitable investments over the span of your life that compound to huge amounts of wealth.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Compounding]]: the fact that your investments compound on top of each other to equal large sums of wealth.

### 💭 Reflection
- Instead of focusing on what you could have made if you invested into something at a particular time, instead think if I start now than this money will compound over time and when its time to retire be worth more than that single investment could have ever been worth.

## 📥 Chapter 5: Getting Wealthy vs Staying Wealthy
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> The good and bad times come and go, throughout history there are just more good times that increase the value of investments compared to bad times that decrease the value of investments.

### 📝 Summary
Making money and keeping money are too distinct skills and excelling in both requires surviving throughout the markets ups and downs. So instead of focusing on big returns you should rather invest in things that give consistent yet smaller returns that will also maximize your chance of surviving in the market.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Margin of safety]]: Raising the odds of success at a given level of risk by increasing your chances of surviving in the market.
- [[Barbelled Personality]]: Being optimistic about the future and not letting the present pessimism prevent you from getting to the future [[65]].

### 💭 Reflection
- Getting money and keeping money are two closely related things, and to maximize the odds of both one must prioritize surviving in the market and building up a [[Margin of safety]].

## 📥 Chapter 6: Tail's You Win
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> It isn't about how many times you win or lose, but how much you win and how much you lose.

### 📝 Summary
All famous and successful companies have tried many products that have failed, but hit big on one to become billion dollar companies. Such as Amazon who only really has Amazon Prime as their big win with many losses such as their fire phone, but their one win outweighs the magnitude of all their losses.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[1%]]: Most companies make 90% of their profits from 1% of their products.

### 💭 Reflection
- To make it in the investing world you don't have to profit off of each investment, instead try to make the sum of your profits much greater than the sum of your losses.

## 📥 Chapter 7: Freedom
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> Money doesn't directly buy happiness, but it buys freedom.

### 📝 Summary
Despite Americans being richer than ever, the rate of depression and anxiety are at an all time high. This is due to Americans increasing their means of living therefore still craving to make more money. Also with modernization long are the days where you go to a physical job and work with your hands and needing to go into the office. Now most people are in [[Decision-Making Jobs]] in which you can be just as productive at home as in the office making people lose their sense of control for time.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Reactance]]: People don't like being told what to do and find more joy in doing activities they decide on.
- [[Decision-Making Jobs]]: Jobs created due to modernization removed society's control of time therefore limiting happiness.

### 💭 Reflection
- Don't focus so much on making as much money as possible, instead make enough money to buy freedom and to have the ability to spend time on things that matter [[89]].

## 📥 Chapter 8: Man In The Car Paradox
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> Don't buy fancy materialistic items to impress others as they likely don't care.

### 📝 Summary
People seek respect and admiration from others, some believe that fancy cars and designer clothes will provide these, but most of society cease to care about these materialistic items.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Wealth Paradox]]: Most people looking at your fancy cars don't think "Wow that guy is so cool for having that car" but instead think "Wow I would look so cool driving that car". [[93]]

### 💭 Reflection
- Never buy expensive items to try to impress others as you are likely wasting money and people don't actually think any better of you.

## 📥 Chapter 10: Save Money
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> Wealth is just a accumulation of all your savings.

### 📝 Summary
Wealth is relative to how much you need so decreasing your means of living allows you to live a more comfortable life. Saving without having an end goal to save for allows you to create a safety blanket which provides flexibility.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[soft skills]]: These are becoming more and more important as technical skills become automated. Think flexibility, having this allows you to wait for better investments or job opportunities.

### 💭 Reflection
- I should save more money that goes towards no direct goal or anything to build up a bigger safety blanket and provide more flexibility in my opportunities.

## 📥 Chapter 11: Reasonable > Rational
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> Life isn't consistent so why should your investment strategy be.

### 📝 Summary
Humans are neither fully rational nor irrational, to make more consistent reasonable decisions we should invest into companies we believe strongly in since it makes making decisions on whether to hold or sale more reasonable.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Home Bias]]: the idea that people like to invest into companies from their hometown.

### 💭 Reflection
- Invest into companies that you believe in as when the stock starts performing poorly you are more likely to make a reasonable decision with the stock.

## 📥 Chapter 12: Surprise!
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> Historical data is only useful for general claims with the further back you go the more generic of a claim you can make.

### 📝 Summary
Wall Street investors always try to predict recessions and when the market will crash, these predictions are always just speculation as no one truly knows how the market will go.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Outlier Events]]: events that move the market the most [[125-126]] 

### 💭 Reflection
- Don't use historical data to try to predict the market instead use it as more general claims for the market with the further back in history you go the more general claims you can make.

## 📥 Chapter 13: Room For Error
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> Take risks with half of your money and be safe with the other half.

### 📝 Summary
Anything that can break will eventually break, need to have some room for error to account for this and be able to recover from the worst case scenario. This is why saving without a goal for the savings is so useful to do.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Margin of safety]]: try to keep waw 1/3 of your investment as a buffer which allows you to sleep at night if things go bad.

### 💭 Reflection
- Never put all your money into something, always keep some cash on hand to account for the worst case scenario. To do this save money without any end goal for the savings as this allows for extra flexibility.

## 📥 Chapter 14: You'll Change
> [!QUOTE] Core Idea
> We as humans are constantly changing our interests in careers, investments, etc... 

### 📝 Summary
In order to minimize the chances we are constantly changing interests we should try to avoid either ends of extreme planning instead finding a balance that is sustainable for a lifetime.

### 🔑 Key Terms
- [[Sunk Costs]]: Past decisions that can't be refunded and make us prisoners to our past self, don't be afraid to let these go if they are truly no longer beneficial.

### 💭 Reflection
- Interests are always changing, to minimize the odds of changing interests and maximize consistency we should avoid the extreme ends of financial planning and instead find something sustainable for life, also embrace the fact that goals and interests are always changing and don't be afraid to let go of [[Sunk Costs]].
