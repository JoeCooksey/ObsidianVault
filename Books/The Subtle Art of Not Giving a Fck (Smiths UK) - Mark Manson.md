## Chapter 1: Don't Try

---

### 🎯 Core Idea
> Many people care about shit that doesn't matter, choose sparingly what you care about.

---

### 📝 Chapter Summary
To not care about anything makes you a psychopath, but caring about everything also is not productive. You have to choose sparingly what really matters in your life to care about therefore allowing you to focus all your energy and time into those matters rather than shit that doesn't matter.

---

### 🔑 Key Terms
* [[Feedback Loop From Hell]]: The act of constantly questioning your actions then questioning your questions.
* [[The Backwards Law]]: The idea that the more you pursue feeling better the worse you end up feeling

---

### 💭 Summary Reflection
* **How does this apply to my life?**
    * Choose a couple of things that really matter to me: finances, family, myself and focus more energy into these and less into superficial stuff: what other people thing of me.
* **What challenged my current way of thinking?**
    * The fact that I shouldn't at all care about what people who don't matter in my life think about me.
* **Key takeaway for future action:**
    * When i'm out in public don't worry about what other think about me or whether someone is looking at me instead just focus on myself.

## Chapter 2: Happiness Is a Problem

---

### 🎯 Core Idea
> Don't choose goals based on the reward your seeking, but the struggles your willing to endure.

---

### 📝 Chapter Summary
Many people think that pain is fully avoidable in their life and the second they feel any they try to numb it with happiness. This is extremely destructive as people must choose their pleasure not by what sounds the best, but by what pain they are willing to endure to receive said pleasure.

---

### 🔑 Key Terms
* [[Victim Mentality]]: The act of blaming all your problems on anyone but yourself.
* [[Denial]]: People often deny their problems when they should accept them. 
* [[Hedonic Treadmill]]: Always working hard to change our life situation but still feel the same.

---

### 💭 Summary Reflection
* **How does this apply to my life?**
    * Don't ignore pain instead embrace it as pain is inevitable to go through life.
* **What challenged my current way of thinking?**
    * The fact that a pain free life is a fairytale scenario and your success is based on how and what pain your willing to go through.
* **Key takeaway for future action:**
    * Don't set goals based on the rewards I want to chase but the pain i'm willing to endure also don't expect a fairytale life without any pain.

### 💭 Summary Reflection

* **What challenged my current way of thinking?**
    * The fact that even these stories you hear of people accomplishing extreme accomplishments are just normal people at every other category.
* **Key takeaway for future action:**
    * Stop thinking that i'm special or better than anyone else instead accept that life is boring and everyone is average and its the basic experiences that matter the most to my life.

## Chapter 4: The Value Of Suffering

---

### 🎯 Core Idea
> Be careful what you choose your values to be as bad values force us to care about what doesn't matter while good values allow us to care about what does matter.

---

### 📝 Chapter Summary
Values are what we judge ourselves compared to other people on. Good values that are beneficial to us are based in reality, socially constructive, and immediate and controllable. Bad values on the other hand cause us to care about what doesn't matter and are superstitious, socially destructive, and not immediate or controllable. Good values happen internally while bad values are based on external factors.

---

### 🔑 Key Terms
* [[Self-Awareness Onion]]: Three Layers:
	* [[Emotion]]: Simple understanding of one's emotions, explaining why something makes you happy or sad.
	* [[Why?]]: Ask yourself why? Do you feel a certain emotion.
	* [[Success/Failure]]: The third and hardest ayer to reach of answering the question why a certain thing is a success or a failure.
* [[Values]]: Determines how we measure ourselves to others, [[Shitty Values]]: **82-85**.

---

### 💭 Summary Reflection
* **How does this apply to my life?**
    * Choose good values to compare myself to others based on.
* **What challenged my current way of thinking?**
    * The fact that bad values rely on what others think about us while good values are what we thing of ourselves.
* **Key takeaway for future action:**
    * Choose good values that don't rely on external factors these can be self-respect or vulnerability, and avoid bad values like feeling good all the time or being liked by everyone.

## Chapter 6: You're Wrong About Everything (But So Am I)

---

### 🎯 Core Idea
> Most of our personal growth stems from when we are wrong about things.

---

### 📝 Chapter Summary
99% of the time you are wrong about whatever it is, but you will always assume what you believe is actually correct. To counteract this we must first question why we feel a certain emotion to do this ask questions such as examples on pg. **136**. Second question we must ask is what does it mean if you were wrong for example question yourself on a belief you have and ask what if you were wrong about a belief **137**. Last question is asking yourself if you were wrong about a certain topic would it better or worse your current problem, most of the time if you are wrong it will create a better problem for you and the people around you.

---

### 🔑 Key Terms
* [[The Backwards Law]]: More certain you try to be about something the more uncertain you end up about said thing.
* [[Parkinson's law]]: Work expands so as to fill up the time available for completion.
* [[Murphy's Law]]: Whatever can go wrong will go wrong

---

### 💭 Summary Reflection
* **How does this apply to my life?**
    * Start questioning myself on my beliefs for example in an argument with someone ask myself why do I feel a certain way, what would happen if my thought was wrong, and if I was wrong would it be a net positive or net negative.
* **What challenged my current way of thinking?**
    * Most of the time what you originally believe is incorrect but humans hate to be incorrect therefore its hard to accept your wrong.
* **Key takeaway for future action:**
    * Realize that I am not special and that I need a simple identity to measure myself based off of such as a student, a partner, or a friend. These simple identities allow you to be more humble and more accepting to other ideas.

## Chapter 8: The Importance of Saying No

---

### 🎯 Core Idea
> Commitment to one thing allows you to really dig deep into that one thing.

---

### 📝 Chapter Summary
Toxic relationships are formed by two different types of people [[The Victim]] (people who take no responsibility for all their problems) and [[The Saver]] who takes responsibility for other people's problems. To be in a healthy relationship you need to have the power to say no as this keeps your partner's ego in check and keeps both of you grounded. The more options you have to choose for something the less satisfied you feel when you make your choice, this is why commitment to one thing is the best as it allows you to focus all your energy into that one thing and dig really deep into it.

---

### 🔑 Key Terms
* [[Tradeoff]]: To value X we must reject X
* [[Boundaries]]: 
	* Good: Responsible for own problems and can be there to help partner with problems.
	* Bad: Avoid own problems and take responsibility for partners problems.
* [[Yin and Yang of Any Toxic Relationship]]: 
	* [[The Victim]]: Starts problems to feel important, blames others for own problems.
	* [[The Saver]]: Solves others problems to feel important, takes responsibility for others problems.
* [[Paradox of Choice]]: The more options you are given the less satisfied you are when you choose one.

---

### 💭 Summary Reflection
* **How does this apply to my life?**
    * In any of my relationships don't always say no but learn when it is appropriate to.
* **What challenged my current way of thinking?**
    * The fact that saying no is a super important factor of any relationship and I shouldn't be offended when someone tells me no.
* **Key takeaway for future action:**
    * Never be afraid to say no if I think it is for the good and commit to a couple things for the long term as this allows for a really deep understanding.
