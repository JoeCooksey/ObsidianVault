## 🧠 Section 1: The Mindset

### Becoming a Connector
- Offering generosity allows for the building of relationships to be more authentic and has incredible virtues **12**.
### Don't Keep Score
- Most relationships you receive benefits based on how much you provide, shift from "what can I gain" to "what can I provide".
- Fundamental insights and observations : **21-23**.
### What's Your Mission
- [[Relationship Action Plan]]: list what you want to accomplish in 3 years than from there 1 year than 90 days.
- Making connections is best done when you have a goal that needs said connections. The best way to make said goal is to write down goals that are specific and believable **33** this allows for a predetermined plan which makes it much easier to connect with others.
### Build It Before You Need It
- You should build a network before you are in need of one (ways to begin **46**).
- Ask friends to help connect you to people

### The Genius of Audacity
- Failure is the only way forward, ways to get more comfortable **53-57**.

### The Networking Jerk
- Its too easy to leave people who you don't like, tips to avoid becoming unlikable **60-63**.

## 🛠️ Section 2: The Skill Set
### Do Your Homework
- People love to feel appreciated therefore do some research on the person you are trying to secure a deal with, some places to look at **70**.
- Common ground allows for deep connection so don't be afraid to bring up your research.

### Take Names
- Group your network into categories such as: **78-79**.
- [[Aspirational Contacts]]: Still keep these people's contacts, never know when they can be useful

### Warm the Cold Call
- Avoid cold calling instead swallow ego and ask a friend to introduce you to people. [[Warm calling]] 
- Establish credibility -> be specific with what you want -> don't be super detailed your just trying to secure a further meeting -> go for a compromise that is easy for the other person.
- E-mail guide: **90-91**.

### Gatekeepers and Artful Dodging
- Gatekeepers such as secretaries have immense influence on the manager/CEO. Try to make these people your allies since they are top tier.
- Try to guilt trip them if they are mean than offer a simple compromise that seems harmless.

### Never Eat Alone
- Don't see networking as a job instead see it as an enjoyable activity.
- Lincoln demonstrates the more you fail the more success there is available to you **104**.

### Share Your Passions
- Shared passions are the basic building blocks to top tier friendships. Schedule your day with your passions and try to invite like minded individuals.

### Follow Up or Fail
- The follow up is more important than the original interaction as this is when real connections are able to be made. What to include in your follow up: **114-115**.

### Be a Conference Commando
- Best way to influence a conference is to try and be one of the managers, call an event and ask if you can help out for free.
- Being a speaker allows you to be memorable for the audience.
- Connecting with the most popular person at an event allows you to get top tier connections.
- Try to have information, find who is attending, master [[Deep Bumps]]. What not to do **134-135**.
### Connecting with Connectors
- Try to connect with [[Super Connectors]] as these people make expanding your circle much easier.
### Expanding Your Circle
- Find other people that you can share you circle with each other to mutually benefit each other **151** rules for transaction.
### The Art of Small Talk
- Being vulnerable allows for your small talk to feel much more personal allowing for conversations to evolve into genuine friendships.
- Everyone has charm its called just being yourself, things to do before you utter a word **161**.
- Make sure the other person feels special, find a passion to talk about but don't just talk about said passion, match the other person's [[Johari Window]], mention something meaningful that you talked about when leaving and plan the next meeting, be a good listener and make the other person feel appreciated.
- ''You're wonderful. Tell me more" - unstoppable formula
- Fundamentals of small talk **168**.

## 🤝 Section 3: Turning Acquaintances into Friends
### Health, Wealth, and Children
- [[health, wealth, and children]]: These are the deepest desires if you help someone gain any of these your bonds will be strong.
- Since these issues are the most important treat them like your life depends on it.
- Change mindset to every human deserves to be helped in life. **183**.

### Social Arbitrage
- If someone has a problem instantly think about ways to solve their problem.
- Networking comes down to getting people important to you what they want.
- Easy way to be useful is come with some knowledge about various subjects.

### Pinging
- Guide to staying in contact **196**.
- Pinging people can be as simple as messaging them hello or scheduling some quick get together. Want to group people based on how close you are to them and the less close you are the more frequent pings you want to send. Remembering someone's birthday will always make them feel special.

### Find Anchor Tenants
- [[Anchor Tenants]]: invite a couple of these to a party to diversify the types of people there.
- Throwing a party is easy, main goal is to have fun, themes make them have a clear focus and more memorable.

## 🚀 Section 5: Trading Up and Giving Back
### Be Interesting
- Try to find a specific interest and build lots of knowledge around it to be more interesting.
- Teaching is the best way to learn a subjects
- [[Ten Tips To Become an Expert]] 
- Emotions makes your stories more compelling to viewers.

### Build Your Brand
- Your brand is defined by your expertise and humanity [[GVAC]].
- To build a brand you need to provide value: there is always a way to provide value **293**.
- Develop a [[PBM]] -> have a specific style you are known by -> put yourself out there so others can see your brand.
### Broadcast Your Brand 
- Embrace your accomplishments
- There is no need for PR when early on, you know you best.
- Find a local news article and see their style of articles they post and orient yourself in that style.
- Be careful what you say online as it is permanent.
### Getting Close to Power
- [[Power by Association]] 
- Places to find famous people: **322-324** tldr; a sports team is the most feasible for me right now.
- Accomplished people usually have to most you can learn from.
### Build It and They Will Comes
- [[Unique Selling Proposition]]: this allows for a compelling community to be created around a shared goal.
- When a group of people work together towards a shared interest great things are bound to happen.
### Never Give In to Hubris
- Don't let your growing network get to your head and make you think your better than others, always help out the people who started with you.
### Find Mentors, Find Mentees
- Studying people who have more knowledge than you expands our horizons.
- Everyone has some value to provide.
- Successful people were once ordinary too, don't feel needed to lie to try and impress them.
- A good mentor is more valuable than any salary, mentoring is one of the best learning methods
### Balance Is B.S.
- Money does not equal happiness, a sense of belonging does, should prioritize connections.
- Fill your life with people who care about you and you care about, then you will genuinely enjoy connecting with others.
### Welcome to the Connected Age
- No matter where you are in life, you have to start somewhere: go out there and make connections, gain knowledge, surround yourself with people you care about.
- A groups is needed in order to accomplish greatness.
