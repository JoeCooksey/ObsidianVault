## 📚 Lesson of the Day

**From: Atomic Habits** — *James Clear*
"You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems." Small 1% improvements compound over time — success isn't a single heroic effort but the result of daily systems that run even when motivation is gone. Build the system, and the results will follow. **Action:** Never miss twice — one bad day is a slip, two in a row is the start of a new (bad) habit.

---

**From: The Psychology of Money** — *Morgan Housel*
Doing well with money is a soft skill — how you *behave* matters more than how smart you are. There are [[Untouchables]] (reputation, freedom, family, happiness) that are infinitely more valuable than money and should never be sacrificed for it. Resist letting the goalpost keep moving; learn to recognize when you have *enough*, because "more" without a ceiling is a trap that has ruined people far wealthier than you.

---

**From: The Laws of Human Nature** — *Robert Greene*
The Law of Irrationality: Emotions cause us to act irrationally without us even realizing it. Rational people identify their emotion and *subtract* it from their decision-making; irrational people let emotions drive the wheel. The fix: when you feel a strong emotion, ask "will this matter in 30 days?" — then journal it, let it settle, and decide only after the heat passes.
