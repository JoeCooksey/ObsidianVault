# Daily Note: 2026-05-09

## 🙏 Daily Prayer

Lord God, I come before You today with a heart full of gratitude — not for what I've earned, but for what You have freely given. Thank You for the breath in my lungs, the clarity of a new morning, and the strength to face whatever this day holds. Thank You for a mind that can learn, grow, and reflect, and for the people in my life who love me without condition.

Thank You for being faithful in the seasons when I didn't deserve faithfulness, and patient when I chose distraction over devotion. You have provided for my needs before I even asked, and You have placed good things in my path that I too often walk past without noticing.

Today I choose gratitude before grievance. May I notice Your gifts before I notice my needs, and may this thankfulness carry into every ordinary moment that makes up this day. I don't need it to be a big day — just a faithful one. In Jesus' name, Amen.

## 📚 Lesson of the Day

**From: Atomic Habits** — *James Clear* — Any habit you want to build for life should start with the Two-Minute Rule: scale the habit down until it takes less than two minutes to begin. The goal isn't the two minutes — it's proving to yourself you can show up at all. Mastery always starts with showing up.

**From: The Laws of Human Nature** — *Robert Greene* — The Law of Aimlessness: unlike animals, humans have no hardwired instinct for direction — we must consciously cultivate purpose or we drift. Look back to what fascinated you in childhood *before* the world shaped your expectations; those Primal Inclinations are the clearest map to your life's true work.

**From: The Psychology of Money** — *Morgan Housel* — Money doesn't directly buy happiness, but it does buy freedom — the ability to wake up and decide how you spend your time without answering to anyone. The highest form of wealth is having control over your own hours, and Americans are richer than ever yet more anxious because they've let modernization strip away that control.
