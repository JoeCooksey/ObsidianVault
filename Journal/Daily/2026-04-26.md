## 📚 Lesson of the Day

**From: Atomic Habits** — *James Clear* — You do not rise to the level of your goals; you fall to the level of your systems. Drastic change doesn't come from willpower or one-off heroic effort — it comes from the compound interest of tiny 1% daily improvements stacked inside a system you can actually maintain. The Never Miss Twice Rule is the anchor: one bad day is an accident, two is the start of a new (bad) habit.

**From: The Psychology of Money** — *Morgan Housel* — Know your "enough." The hardest financial skill is getting the goalpost to stop moving — most people keep raising the bar until they risk things they can't afford to lose. There are Untouchables no amount of money is worth sacrificing: your reputation, your freedom, your family, your happiness. Define your enough before the market does it for you.

**From: Never Eat Alone** — *Keith Ferrazzi & Tahl Raz* — "The currency of real networking is not greed but generosity." Build your network before you ever need it, and build it by shifting your default question from *what can I gain?* to *what can I provide?* The people who get ahead fastest are not the most talented — they are the most generous with their time, connections, and knowledge long before they need anything in return.
