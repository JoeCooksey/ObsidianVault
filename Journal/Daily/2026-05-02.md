# Daily Note: 2026-05-02

## 🙏 Daily Prayer
*Category: Thanksgiving*

Lord, thank You for this morning — for breath in my lungs and a mind that is still capable of growing. Thank You for the opportunities You've placed in front of me that I haven't fully appreciated: the professors who share knowledge freely, the books that carry wisdom from those who came before me, the people in my life who believe in me even when I don't believe in myself. Thank You for the hard days too — the ones that felt like failure but were secretly building character. You have been more faithful to me than I have been to You, and still You are here. I don't want to take that for granted. Help me to live today with eyes open to Your goodness, and let my gratitude show up in how I treat the people around me. Amen.

## 📚 Lesson of the Day

**From: Atomic Habits** — *James Clear*
Lasting change doesn't start with your behavior — it starts with your identity. The two-step process: decide the kind of person you want to become, then cast small daily votes for that identity through your actions. Every habit is a vote for or against who you are becoming.

**From: How to Win Friends and Influence People** — *Dale Carnegie*
The most powerful thing you can do in almost any conversation is ask a genuine question, then actually listen to the answer. People rarely feel truly heard — when you give them that, they remember how you made them feel long after they forget what you said.

**From: The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People** — *Stephen R. Covey*
High-effectiveness people live primarily in Quadrant 2 — Not Urgent but Important (planning, learning, relationship-building). They prevent crises instead of reacting to them, which means saying "no" to distractions so they can say "yes" to what actually moves the needle. Organize your week around roles and values, not just your daily fire-drill list.
