## 📚 Lesson of the Day

**From: Atomic Habits** — *James Clear*
"You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems." Your habits aren't failures of willpower — they're failures of design. Build the system first (environment, cues, low barriers to entry), and the behavior follows automatically. The Never Miss Twice rule is your safety net: one bad day is an accident, two in a row is the start of a new habit.

**From: The Psychology of Money** — *Morgan Housel*
Know your Untouchables — reputation, freedom, family, happiness — and never risk them for money. The hardest financial skill isn't picking stocks; it's recognizing when "enough" is enough and refusing to let the goalpost keep moving. Most financial ruin comes not from bad math but from chasing more past the point where the risk stops making sense.

**From: The Laws of Human Nature** — *Robert Greene*
The Law of Irrationality: emotions aren't the enemy, but acting on them unexamined is. When you feel a strong emotional pull, ask one question — *will this actually matter in 30 days?* Rational people identify the emotion and subtract it from their decision; irrational people let it drive. Journaling the emotion is the fastest way to diffuse it and see it clearly.
