## 📚 Lesson of the Day

**From: Never Eat Alone** — *Keith Ferrazzi* — "The currency of real networking is not greed but generosity." The core insight of the book is that you should build your network *before* you need it — reach out to people when you have nothing to ask, share value freely, and help others connect. When the moment comes that you genuinely need a door opened, you won't be starting from zero; you'll be calling a friend.

**From: The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People** — *Stephen R. Covey* — Most people live in Quadrant 1 (urgent + important crises) and wonder why they are always burned out. Covey's insight is that the highest-leverage time is Quadrant 2: things that are *important but not urgent* — planning, skill-building, relationship investment, health. Q2 work prevents Q1 crises before they happen. The discipline is saying no to the urgent-but-unimportant so you can say yes to what actually matters long-term.

**From: How To Win Friends and Influence People** — *Dale Carnegie* — The deepest human craving is the desire to feel genuinely important and appreciated. Flattery is cheap and everyone can detect it — what people remember is *specific, sincere appreciation*. Before every meaningful interaction, ask yourself: "What is there about this person that I can honestly admire?" Find it, name it directly, and you will fulfill a craving that most people rarely experience — and they will remember how you made them feel long after they forget everything else.
