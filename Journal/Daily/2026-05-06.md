## 📚 Lesson of the Day

**From: The Psychology of Money** — *Morgan Housel* — Doing well with money isn't about how smart you are — it's about how you behave. Behavior is hard to teach even to brilliant people, because financial decisions are made at the dinner table, not on a spreadsheet, shaped by personal history, ego, and fear.

**From: Never Eat Alone** — *Keith Ferrazzi & Tahl Raz* — Success is not a solo act; it is built on a web of genuine relationships. The difference between people who get ahead and those who don't is not talent or luck — it is how freely and generously they invest in others *before* they ever need anything back.

**From: Steve Jobs** — *Walter Isaacson* — Jobs's defining belief was that the best innovations live at the crossroads of technology and the liberal arts. His one audited calligraphy class at Reed College — taken with no practical goal in mind — directly produced the Mac's beautiful typography, the first personal computer with multiple fonts and proportional spacing.
