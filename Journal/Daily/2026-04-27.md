## 📚 Lesson of the Day

**From: Atomic Habits** — *James Clear*
"You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems." Drastic change doesn't come from one-off heroic efforts or massive willpower — it comes from the compound interest of tiny 1% improvements, and from building systems that make good behavior automatic. The Never Miss Twice rule is one practical anchor: one bad day is a slip, two in a row is the start of a new (bad) habit.

**From: The Psychology of Money** — *Morgan Housel*
Doing well with money is not about how smart you are — it's about how you behave. Never sacrifice your Untouchables (reputation, freedom, family, happiness) for money; they are almost certainly worth more to you than any dollar amount. The hardest financial skill is knowing when you have *enough* — and getting the goalpost to stop moving.

**From: Never Eat Alone** — *Keith Ferrazzi & Tahl Raz*
"The currency of real networking is not greed but generosity." Success is not a solo act — it is built on a web of genuine relationships. Shift your default question from "what can I gain?" to "what can I provide?", and build your network before you ever need it. Ping people constantly, not just when you need something.
