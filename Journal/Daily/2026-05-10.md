# Daily Note: 2026-05-10

## 🙏 Daily Prayer

Lord, You are holy — unchanging, faithful, and beyond anything I can fully comprehend. Before I ask You for anything today, I simply want to declare who You are: the Creator of every good thing, the God who holds time in His hands, the One who knows my name and still calls me worthy. There is no one like You.

I confess that I too often come to You with a shopping list rather than a surrendered heart. I rush past the presence of God in search of the provision of God, and in doing so I miss the best part. Forgive me for the days I've treated You as a last resort rather than a first love.

Today is Sunday — a day made for rest and remembrance. Help me slow down enough to actually worship, not just go through the motions. Give me a mind focused on You throughout the day. And where I need guidance, courage, or patience today, provide it in Your timing and by Your grace. I trust You with this day entirely. In Jesus' name, Amen.

## 📚 Lesson of the Day

**From: How to Win Friends and Influence People** — *Dale Carnegie* — The single most effective thing you can do in almost any conversation is ask a genuine question and then actually listen to the answer. People who feel truly heard become loyal friends and advocates — not because of what you said, but because of the space you made for them to say what mattered to them.

**From: Steve Jobs** — *Walter Isaacson* — The most transformative innovations live at the intersection of technology and the humanities: Jobs's calligraphy class at Reed College directly became the Mac's beautiful typography — proof that caring about art, design, and liberal arts makes technology meaningful in a way that pure engineering never can. The people who build the future are fluent in both worlds.

**From: The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People** — *Stephen R. Covey* — True effectiveness isn't a technique — it's a character. Most people live in Quadrant 1 (urgent and important, constant fires) or Quadrant 3 (urgent but unimportant, other people's priorities) and never invest in Quadrant 2: the non-urgent but deeply important work of planning, relationships, health, and learning. Q2 is where crises are prevented before they happen, and the only path there is saying no to everything else first.
