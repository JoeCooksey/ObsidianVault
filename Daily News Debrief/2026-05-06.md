## Key Themes & Synthesis

Today's stories converge on two major risk pivots: the U.S.-Iran standoff and the AI infrastructure boom. The fragile Iran ceasefire — plausible deal but "impossible" demands — is the single biggest macro wildcard; oil below $100 is doing real work for inflation and equities, but that can reverse fast. Meanwhile, the AMD and Nvidia stories are not isolated wins — they confirm that agentic AI is the new demand driver, not just raw LLM inference, and that optical interconnects (Corning deal) are becoming a strategic bottleneck. The Samsung trillion-dollar milestone and KOSPI +6% in a single day underscore how concentrated this semiconductor cycle has become globally. The domestic political story — Trump consolidating control via primaries and immigration spending riders — suggests the legislative environment will remain chaotic regardless of geopolitical outcomes.
