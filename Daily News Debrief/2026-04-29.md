## Key Themes & Synthesis
The Iran war is no longer a regional story — it's the operating system of today's news cycle. Oil at $112, UAE leaving OPEC, gas at a 4-year high, UK facing a £35B economic threat, Booking Holdings cutting guidance, markets jittery: the conflict is now visibly repricing risk across energy, travel, and finance simultaneously. Meanwhile, the AI infrastructure buildout continues under its own momentum (SpaceX, Google-DoD, $600B Big Tech capex race) even as markets punish companies like Arm that can't sustain the hype. The King Charles Congressional address and EU's Meta charges are quieter signals worth watching: both point to allies recalibrating their posture toward the U.S. — one through ceremony and flattery, one through legal enforcement. The Fed's rate decision today, likely a hold, will land into a market already nervous about energy inflation making rate cuts harder to justify.
