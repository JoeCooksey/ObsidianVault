# Daily News Debrief — April 13, 2026

## Top Headlines
- **US Military Blockade on Iran Takes Effect** — The blockade on Iranian ports began at 10 a.m. ET after weekend peace talks in Islamabad collapsed after 16 hours. Trump warned the US will sink any Iranian "fast attack ships" that approach the blockade zone. Source: CNN
- **Over 5,000 Killed in Iran-Lebanon Conflict** — At least 3,375 killed in Iran since US-Israel strikes began in February; Lebanon's health ministry reports 2,089 dead since March 2. The six-week conflict is escalating sharply. Source: GMA News / CNN
- **Pope Leo XIV Defies Trump** — Pope Leo said he has "no fear of the Trump administration" after Trump called his peace appeal "terrible for foreign policy." The Pope pledged to continue advocating for dialogue and multilateral solutions. Source: Al Jazeera
- **Hungary Votes Out Orbán** — Hungarian voters turned out in record numbers since the 1990s, ending Viktor Orbán's 16-year grip on power and his Fidesz party's dominance. Source: Various
- **UK Will Not Join US Blockade** — Prime Minister Keir Starmer stated Britain will not participate in the US blockade of Iranian ports, distancing the UK from direct involvement in the conflict. Source: Just Security / CNN

## Key Themes & Synthesis
The dominant thread today is escalation with no off-ramp: the US-Iran conflict has moved from airstrikes to a naval blockade, oil has crossed $100, and weekend diplomacy produced nothing. Markets are pricing in a prolonged disruption, not a quick resolution. The UK's refusal to join the blockade signals fractures in the Western coalition, while Pope Leo's defiance of Trump adds an unusual moral-authority dimension to the geopolitical story. On the tech side, the quantum threat to encryption is quietly becoming urgent — Google's new findings suggest the post-quantum migration window is shorter than most organizations have planned for, and the Marimo exploit shows adversaries are moving faster than ever from disclosure to weaponization.
