# Daily News Debrief — April 22, 2026

## Top Headlines

- **Iran attacks ships in Strait of Hormuz hours after ceasefire extension** — Iran's Revolutionary Guard seized two vessels and disabled a third in the Strait of Hormuz; a container ship was also struck by Iranian fire, all within hours of Trump announcing an indefinite ceasefire extension. Source: CNN, Al Jazeera
- **Trump extends ceasefire but keeps naval blockade** — President Trump extended the US-Iran ceasefire indefinitely, giving Tehran time to submit a unified peace proposal, but kept the naval blockade on Iranian ports in place. Iran says it won't negotiate "under the shadow of threats" while the blockade stands. Source: NBC News, KSAT
- **West Bank violence continues** — Israeli settlers killed two people, including a child, in the occupied West Bank; Israeli strikes in southern Lebanon wounded civilians despite a 10-day ceasefire there. Source: Al Jazeera
- **Trump's economic approval drops to 30%** — A new AP-NORC poll finds Trump's approval on the economy fell from 38% in March to 30% in April, as the Iran war drives fuel and consumer prices higher. Source: AP-NORC via multiple outlets

## Key Themes & Synthesis

The Iran conflict is the single thread connecting almost every story today: it's driving the ceasefire-extension diplomacy, the Strait of Hormuz shipping attacks, the market volatility (and today's relief rally), the European airline capacity cuts, and Trump's cratering economic approval. The ceasefire extension bought a diplomatic pause, but Iran's simultaneous ship seizures signal that Tehran is playing both tracks at once — negotiating while maintaining pressure — which makes any stable resolution look distant. On the tech side, the Amazon-Anthropic mega-commitment ($33B+) continues the pattern of hyperscalers locking in exclusive AI infrastructure deals, effectively turning cloud compute into geopolitical leverage of a different kind. Watch Tesla earnings tonight for a read on whether consumer spending is holding up despite the inflation the war is feeding into energy and goods prices.
