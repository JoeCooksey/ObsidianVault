## Key Themes & Synthesis

Today's stories reveal a sharp split between financial markets and political reality. Wall Street is euphoric — AI capex is surging, Apple is thriving, and April delivered its best month in five years — while Main Street is agitated enough to stage nationwide May Day walkouts and teacher strikes against Trump administration policies. The Iran nuclear thread runs through both the geopolitics and the market: stalled talks are keeping a lid on any deal, yet even the rumor of a proposal is enough to move oil prices, showing how tightly markets are tracking diplomacy. On the tech front, the space infrastructure race is quietly accelerating — Amazon's Ariane 6 launch signals that Starlink's dominance in satellite internet is no longer uncontested, and the parallel dismantling of US science advisory committees raises longer-term questions about American research competitiveness precisely when that competition is intensifying globally.
