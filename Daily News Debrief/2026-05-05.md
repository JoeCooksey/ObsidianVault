## Key Themes & Synthesis

The dominant thread today is **energy and conflict risk converging**: the US-Iran standoff in the Strait of Hormuz directly threatens ~20% of global oil supply, which explains why crude spiked before easing on ceasefire optimism — markets are one escalation away from a major supply shock. The AI story echoes this: just as fossil fuel infrastructure is being stressed by geopolitical instability, electrical infrastructure is being stressed by AI's power demands, suggesting "energy scarcity" may become the defining constraint of the mid-2020s. On the political front, the simultaneous ceasefire proposals from both Russia-Ukraine sides and the US-Iran pause suggest war-weary actors testing off-ramps, but none of these halts are durable yet. Anthropic's Wall Street partnership is the quieter signal worth watching: when AI companies start embedding directly into financial infrastructure, the systemic risk surface expands significantly.
