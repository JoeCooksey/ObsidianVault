1. Observational Skills:
	- When we were children we were great observers of others, this was lost as we grew up. Developing this skill is just rediscovering what was already known as children.
	- Must practice, good goals would be to observe a couple facial expressions that go against what they are saying, must be engaged.
	- Pay great attention to mixed signals as these usually leak negative emotions.
	- Subtract personal bias or opinions when trying to observe these emotions.
	- Apply this to your own emotions, observing how often you give off fake smiles doing this will make you more aware of others fake smiles.
2. Decoding Keys:
	1. Dislike/Like cues:
		- Hostile actions never come out of the blue
		- Sudden squinting of the eyes, overall tenseness in the body, usually not seeable until their dislike towards you has reached a boiling point.
		- Micro expressions: when someone is aware of a negative feeling and tries to suppress it, but it leaks out for a bit. When someone is unaware of their hostility and it shows itself in quick flashes of the body.
		- Compare people's body language towards you and towards other people. 
		- Finding out dislike cues allows you to act first.
		- Like cues have less need to be hidden, genuine smile, standing closer to people.
		- You can apply like cues to other people, but don't be overly aggressive early on.
	2. Dominance/submission cues:
		- People with power tend to look around more at others, speak faster, interrupt and control.
		- Be careful with dominant leaders.
	3. Deception cues:
		- One part of face is more expressive than the other part.
		- When someone is trying to deceive you don't instantly call them out, show interest in what they are doing than ask an uncomfortable question and pay extra attention to their body language.
3. Impression Management:
	- Master the nonverbal cues: be aware of your nonverbal style, mimic other person.
	- Be a Method Actor: learn how to put yourself in the mood fit for the situation, just as important is coming back to a baseline.
	- Adapt to Your Audience: Know who you are talking to and shift your cues accordingly.
	- Create the Proper First Impression: tone down your cues, relaxed smiles and looking in their eyes.
	- Dramatic Effects: Have some mystery to yourself, withhold some information, be less predictable.
	- Project Saintly Qualities: Project sincerity and honesty
