# Robert Greene

**Type**: Author, strategist, popular psychology writer
**Born**: 1959, Los Angeles, CA
**Known for**: *The 48 Laws of Power*, *The Laws of Human Nature*, *Mastery*, *The Art of Seduction*

## Overview

Robert Greene is an American author specializing in strategy, power, human behavior, and historical psychology. His books synthesize lessons from history, biography, and psychology into practical frameworks for navigating human dynamics. His work is heavily influenced by Machiavelli, Nietzsche, and Jungian psychology.

His style: dense historical examples (Caesar, Napoleon, Cleopatra, MLK Jr., Stalin) used to illustrate universal psychological patterns. Each book is structured as a series of "laws" with named principles, examples, and practical guidance.

## Key Books

| Book | Year | Core Theme |
|---|---|---|

## Core Philosophy

Greene's consistent thesis across books: **most people are driven by unconscious forces they do not understand and cannot control**. The person who understands these forces — in themselves and in others — gains an enormous advantage: not a cynical advantage for exploitation, but the clarity needed for genuine effectiveness, strategy, and self-awareness.

The goal is not to become manipulative. It is to stop being naive. The laws describe reality, not ideals.

## Intellectual Influences Visible in His Work

- **Carl Jung**: Shadow Self, Anima/Animus, archetypes
- **Machiavelli**: clear-eyed realism about human motivation
- **Nietzsche**: will to power, life-affirmation, authenticity
- **Heraclitus / Stoics**: acceptance of change, Memento Mori
- **Deep historical biography**: Greene treats historical figures as psychological case studies

## Notes

Greene had a stroke in 2018 — the same year *The Laws of Human Nature* was published. He has spoken publicly about how confronting his own mortality sharpened the themes of Law 18 in the book. The irony is not lost: his most psychological and Stoic book came out as he was living the Law of Death Denial in real time.
