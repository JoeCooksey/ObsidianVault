# Intel

Semiconductor company and inventor of the x86 CPU architecture. Dominant CPU manufacturer for personal computers and servers from the 1980s through the 2010s. Founded 1968.

## Key CPU Milestones

| Year | Product | Significance |
|------|---------|--------------|
| 1971 | Intel 4004 | World's first microprocessor; 2,300 transistors; 4-bit |
| 1978 | Intel 8086 | Founded x86 instruction set architecture (ISA) |
| 1985 | Intel 80386 | First 32-bit x86 CPU; enabled modern operating systems |
| 1993 | Pentium | Superscalar execution; 3.1M transistors |
| 2006 | Core 2 Duo | First Intel multi-core mainstream CPU |
| 2015 | Skylake | Tock in Intel's tick-tock cadence; 14nm; last major perf-per-clock leap before stagnation |

## AMD 64-bit Challenge

In 2003, AMD (not Intel) introduced the x86-64 extension enabling 64-bit computing. Intel adopted AMD's extension as "Intel 64," ceding the 64-bit architectural lead.

## Current Status

Intel faces competitive pressure from AMD (Zen architecture) and ARM (Apple Silicon, Qualcomm). Intel's manufacturing fell behind TSMC's process node leadership during the 2015–2022 period.
