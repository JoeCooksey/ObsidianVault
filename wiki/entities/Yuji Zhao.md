# Yuji Zhao

## Role
Associate Professor, Rice University Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering.
Lab: **WIDE Lab** — Wide-bandgap semiconductor devices; Houston, TX.

## Research Focus
GaN and ultra-wide-bandgap (UWBG) semiconductor device physics, with emphasis on:
- Interface engineering and trap reduction (BN interlayer work)
- Gate-recess process optimization for E-mode GaN MISHEMTs
- β-Ga₂O₃ device fabrication and reliability
- Extreme-environment electronics
- CHIMES / JUMP 2.0 semiconductor center participation

## Core Research Philosophy
**Trap-limited performance improvement via deliberate interface/process engineering.**
If a device's reliability is limited by traps or interface defects, carefully designed process steps (or thin dielectric interlayers) yield disproportionately large electrical gains — without geometry scaling.

## Key Recent Papers

### Two-Step Gate Recess in GaN E-Mode MISHEMTs (2025)
- High-power ICP etch + low-power etch → Dit halved (1.2–2.2 × 10¹² cm⁻²eV⁻¹)
- Ron: 71.4 → 31.7 Ω·mm; TDDB gate lifetime: 4.5 V → 5 V (10-year criterion)
- Lesson: single process modification → measurable trap density + reliability improvement

### β-Ga₂O₃ MIS Diodes with BN Interlayer (2023)
- 2.8 nm BN interlayer; breakdown 732 V → 1035 V (+41%)
- Mechanism: passivated surface defects + reduced reverse leakage
- Demonstrates BN as versatile UWBG interface material

### Low-T CVD BN for AlGaN/GaN MISHEMTs (2024 APL)
- 3 orders lower gate leakage; on/off ratio 10⁸; Ron 5.40 Ω·mm; Dit ~ 5–6 × 10¹¹ cm⁻²eV⁻¹
- Confirms BN interface engineering is a group-wide design direction
