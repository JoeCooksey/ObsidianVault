# Dale Carnegie

**Born**: November 24, 1888, Maryville, Missouri  
**Died**: November 1, 1955  
**Known for**: *How to Win Friends and Influence People* (1936); founder of the Dale Carnegie Course in effective speaking and human relations

---

## Background

Carnegie grew up in rural poverty in Missouri and worked his way through the Missouri State Teachers College. After a stint as a salesman and actor, he began teaching public speaking at the YMCA in New York City in 1912 — charging on commission since the YMCA would not risk a salary. His courses became wildly popular because he focused on practical psychology, not theory.

His flagship book, *How to Win Friends and Influence People*, was published in 1936 and has sold over 30 million copies, making it one of the best-selling self-help books of all time.

---

## Core Contributions

- Pioneered the field of **human relations training** as a systematic, learnable discipline
- Identified the **desire to feel important** as the central motivating force in human behavior (independently of Maslow's later hierarchy)
- Developed a **case-study pedagogy**: principles illustrated with historical examples (Lincoln, Roosevelt, Rockefeller) rather than abstract theory
- Founded **Dale Carnegie & Associates**, which still runs courses worldwide

## Intellectual Context

Carnegie was writing against a backdrop of the Great Depression — a time when social mobility depended more on interpersonal skill than on capital. His framework is behaviorist in spirit (focus on actions and their effects, not inner states), though he intuited many ideas that cognitive psychology and neuroscience would later formalize: the backfire effect when criticism arouses defensiveness, operant reinforcement via praise, identity-consistency as a motivational lever.
