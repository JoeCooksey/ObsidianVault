# Susan David

Psychologist, author, and co-founder of the Institute of Coaching at Harvard Medical School affiliate McLean Hospital. Recognized as one of the world's most influential management thinkers.

## Key Work

- **Book**: *Emotional Agility* (2016) — #1 Wall Street Journal bestseller
- **TED Talk**: "The Gift and Power of Emotional Courage" (2017) — 10M+ views
- **Framework**: Emotional agility — practical tools for navigating thoughts, emotions, and stories in ways that are compassionate, curious, and values-connected

## Origin of Framework

Lost her father to cancer at age 15. Grew up in apartheid South Africa under a culture of emotional denial. A high school teacher's invitation to journal ("write like no one is reading") was the turning point — the experience of turning *toward* difficult emotions rather than away became the foundation of her life's work.

## Core Claims

- Suppressing difficult emotions (toxic positivity) causes lower mental health, burnout, and relationship damage over time
- Emotions are functional signals (Darwin) — they signpost values and needs, not weaknesses
- Precise emotion labeling ([[Emotion Granularity]]) activates the brain's readiness potential, moving from paralysis to action
- Language matters: "I am angry" fuses self with emotion; "I notice I'm feeling angry" creates space for choice
- Self-compassion functions as a secure base (Bowlby) — enabling growth, risk, and resilience

## Affiliation
