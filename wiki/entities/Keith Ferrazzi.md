# Keith Ferrazzi

American author, entrepreneur, and relationship strategist. Best known for *Never Eat Alone* (2005; updated 2014), widely regarded as the definitive modern guide to relationship-building and networking.

---

## Background

Ferrazzi grew up in a working-class family in Pennsylvania. His path into elite institutions — Yale, Harvard Business School — and eventually to roles as CMO at Deloitte and CEO of Yum! Brands marketing was built almost entirely on relationships he cultivated through genuine generosity. His background is the proof-of-concept for his own philosophy: he did not have money or connections when he started, but he became known for being the most helpful person in the room.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "The currency of real networking is not greed but generosity."

Ferrazzi argues that the traditional model of networking — attending events, collecting cards, making asks — is both unpleasant and ineffective. The better model is radical generosity: give first, give consistently, give without keeping score. The result is not a contact list but a genuine web of mutual support that compounds over time.

Key claims:
- Build your network before you need it — waiting until you need help is too late
- Follow-up matters more than the initial meeting; execute within 24 hours
- Help someone with their health, wealth, or children and you forge an unbreakable bond
- Super connectors multiply your reach; invest in those relationships disproportionately
- Brand = expertise + humanity; always provide value before asking for anything

## Relationship to Other Thinkers in This Wiki

- **Andrew Huberman / [[Limbic Friction]]**: The "Genius of Audacity" principle (act despite fear of rejection) is precisely the practice of acting against high limbic friction. Ferrazzi arrived at this behaviorally; Huberman provides the neuroscience.
