# Morgan Housel

## Profile

**Morgan Housel** is an American author and partner at the Collaborative Fund, a venture capital firm focused on companies working toward a better world. He is best known for *The Psychology of Money* (2020), one of the best-selling personal finance books of the decade.

Prior to the Collaborative Fund, Housel was a financial columnist at *The Motley Fool* and *The Wall Street Journal*.

## Key Works

- **The Psychology of Money** (2020) — Harpers Collins; 20 short chapters on the behavioral and psychological dimensions of financial decision-making; Joe started reading ~3/1/2026
- *Same as Ever* (2023) — follow-up; focuses on what doesn't change across history and how to think about an uncertain future

## Core Philosophical Positions

1. **Behavior > knowledge in finance** — "Doing well with money isn't about what you know. It's about how you behave." Financial math is easy; behaving well over 40+ years is hard.
2. **Time is the most underrated variable** — compounding over long durations is more powerful than high short-term returns.
3. **Define "enough"** — failure to define a sufficiency threshold leads to endless goalpost movement and the sacrifice of things money can't replace.
4. **Freedom > wealth** — the highest-value output of money is autonomy over your time; raw net worth is a proxy, not the goal.
5. **No one's crazy** — everyone's financial behavior is rational given their personal history and experience; disagreement often comes from different lived contexts, not different intelligence.

## Writing Style

Housel writes in short, punchy vignettes with historical anecdotes rather than formulas or prescriptions. Each chapter of *Psychology of Money* is a self-contained argument, building to a cumulative view of how psychology shapes financial outcomes. He avoids pretending to predict markets, instead focusing on timeless behavioral principles.
