# Mark Manson

American author, blogger, and entrepreneur. Known for combining philosophy, psychology, and personal development into direct, profanity-accented writing aimed at the self-help genre's avoidance of hard truths.

## Key Facts

- **Full name**: Mark Manson
- **Born**: 1984
- **Nationality**: American
- **Role**: Author, blogger, entrepreneur

## Major Works

| Title | Year | Core Argument |
|---|---|---|
| *The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F\*ck* | 2016 (published 2017) | Selectivity over positivity; choose better values; embrace necessary pain |
| *Everything Is F\*cked: A Book About Hope* | 2019 | Hope as the source of human motivation; the tension between feeling brain and thinking brain |
| *Models: Attract Women Through Honesty* | 2011 | Vulnerability and authenticity as the foundation of attraction |

*The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F\*ck* spent over 100 weeks on the *New York Times* bestseller list and has sold more than 15 million copies worldwide.

## Intellectual Influences

Manson draws heavily from:
- **Alan Watts** — the [[Backwards Law]] is directly attributed to Watts (*The Wisdom of Insecurity*, 1951)
- **Ernest Becker** — [[Immortality Projects]] concept originates in Becker's *The Denial of Death* (1973)
- **Stoic philosophy** — acceptance of the uncontrollable; death awareness; internal vs external values
- **Existentialism** — Sartre, Camus; responsibility, choice, and meaning

## Core Philosophical Position

Manson's position is a secularized, practical existentialism:
1. Life is inherently difficult and suffering is unavoidable
2. The only meaningful question is *which* suffering is worth it
3. That is determined entirely by your values
4. Most people hold bad values (externally dependent, pleasure-seeking, socially performative)
5. Choosing better values is the whole game

This overlaps substantially with Susan David's [[Emotional Agility]] framework — both argue that running from difficult emotions and external validation is the core psychological error, and that values-connected action is the solution.
