# Andrew Huberman

Professor of Neurobiology and Ophthalmology at Stanford School of Medicine. Host of the **Huberman Lab** podcast, which translates peer-reviewed neuroscience and biology into practical tools for mental health, physical health, and performance.

## Contributions to Wiki

- Coined the term **[[Limbic Friction]]** to describe the activation energy required to override anxious or lethargic states when attempting to form or execute habits
- Developed the **[[Three-Phase Day Framework]]** for aligning habit execution with circadian neurochemistry
- Popularized the **[[Task Bracketing]]** concept from basal ganglia research for practical habit formation
- Author of the **[[21-Day Habit Formation System]]**

## Key Claims

- Up to 70% of waking behavior is habitual
- Habit strength is determined by context independence and limbic friction required
- Phase 1 (0–8h post-waking) is neurochemically optimal for hard habits due to elevated norepinephrine, epinephrine, and dopamine
- Linchpin habits — habits one already enjoys — act as force multipliers for other habits
