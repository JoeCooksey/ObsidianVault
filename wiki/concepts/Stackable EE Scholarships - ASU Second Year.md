# Stackable EE Scholarships — ASU Second Year

Additional scholarships to stack on top of NAU Scholarship + FAFSA (already secured) for a second-year Electrical Engineering student at ASU. Ranked by dollar value × effort ratio.

**Already locked in:**
- NAU Scholarship (auto-renews: maintain 3.0 GPA + 30 credits/yr)
- Pell Grant via FAFSA (if income-eligible, up to $7,395/yr)
- Federal subsidized/unsubsidized loans (FAFSA)

---

## Tier 1 — ASU Internal (Highest ROI, Apply Every Year)

### Fulton Schools Scholarship Universe
- **Amount**: $1,000–$5,000+ per award (multiple scholarships available)
- **Total pool**: $1.7M+ awarded to undergrads annually
- **Available to**: Continuing students — not just freshmen
- **Portal**: [scholarships.asu.edu](https://scholarships.asu.edu) — requires ASURITE login
- **Opens**: November 1 each year
- **Deadline**: February 1 (most scholarships)
- **Selection**: Academic merit AND talent, story, creativity — not GPA-only
- **Action**: Set a calendar reminder for November 1; log in and filter for Electrical Engineering; apply to every scholarship you meet the criteria for

### ASU ONSA (Office of National Scholarships Advisement)
- **What it is**: ASU's advising office for competitive national fellowships (Goldwater, SMART, Fulbright, etc.)
- **Why it matters**: Goldwater and DoD SMART require institutional nomination — you must go through ONSA first
- **Website**: [onsa.asu.edu](https://onsa.asu.edu/scholarships)
- **Action**: Schedule a meeting with an ONSA advisor in Fall 2026 semester to start Goldwater/SMART pipeline

## Tier 2 — Professional Society Scholarships (Apply Each Spring)

### IEEE PES Scholarship Plus Initiative
- **Amount**: Up to **$10,000/year**
- **Focus**: Power and energy engineering (directly aligned with WBG/power electronics track)
- **Requirements**: Min 3.0 GPA; U.S. citizen or PR; full-time bachelor's student; at least 3 power/energy courses completed
- **Deadline**: April 30 annually
- **Stackable**: Yes — can combine with ASU scholarships
- **Website**: [ee-scholarship.org](https://ee-scholarship.org)

### IEEE AESS Engineering Scholarship
- **Amount**: **$10,000** (2 awarded annually — low competition)
- **Focus**: Electrical Engineering and Systems Engineering
- **Requirements**: Min 2.8 GPA (lower bar than PES); AESS Student Membership required; essay on EE importance and your impact; 3 letters of recommendation (2 from professors)
- **Selection**: Essay quality + financial need
- **Website**: [ieee-aess.org/awards/education-awards/engineering-scholarship](https://ieee-aess.org/awards/education-awards/engineering-scholarship)
- **Note**: Only 2 scholarships awarded total — small applicant pool relative to dollar value

### AFCEA STEM Majors Scholarship
- **Amount**: $2,500–$5,000
- **Focus**: STEM majors including Electrical Engineering
- **Requirements**: Sophomore or junior enrollment (perfect timing for second year); min 3.0 GPA; U.S. citizen or PR; full-time at accredited 4-year school
- **Deadline**: Check annually at afcea.org (typically spring)
- **Website**: [afcea.org/stem-majors-scholarships](https://www.afcea.org/stem-majors-scholarships)

## Tier 3 — Prestigious Competitive Programs (High Effort, Life-Changing Value)

### DoD SMART Scholarship-for-Service
- **Amount**: **Full tuition + $30,000–$46,000 annual stipend** + summer internships at DoD facilities + civilian job offer after graduation
- **Commitment**: Work at a DoD facility for a period equal to your scholarship duration after graduation
- **Requirements**: U.S. citizen; pursuing EE (one of 24 approved STEM disciplines); enrolled at accredited U.S. university; commit to at least 1 year of remaining degree funding
- **Application window**: Opens **August 1**, closes first Friday of December
- **Website**: [smartscholarship.org](https://www.smartscholarship.org/smart/en)
- **Note**: The single highest-value scholarship available to an EE undergraduate. If you are open to DoD/national security work, this is worth all-in effort.

### Barry Goldwater Scholarship
- **Amount**: Up to **$7,500/year** (up to 2 years for sophomores)
- **Focus**: Students pursuing research careers in natural sciences, mathematics, and engineering
- **Requirements**: Sophomore or junior status; min 3.0 GPA; U.S. citizen or PR; research experience strongly expected; institutional nomination required (go through ASU ONSA)
- **Deadline**: January/February — ASU internal deadline earlier
- **Website**: [goldwaterscholarship.gov](https://goldwaterscholarship.gov)
- **Note**: FURI research experience is the key differentiator for Goldwater. Start the FURI pipeline now (see [[90 Day Project Tier List]]) to qualify by Fall 2026 nomination.

## Tier 4 — Discovery Platforms & Easy-Apply (Do These Now)

### Bold.org "Be Bold" No-Essay Scholarship
- **Amount**: **$25,000** (awarded periodically)
- **Requirements**: None — any education level, any major, any GPA, any state
- **How to apply**: Complete your Bold.org profile (takes 20 min); auto-matched to scholarships
- **Website**: [bold.org/scholarships/the-be-bold-no-essay-scholarship](https://bold.org/scholarships/the-be-bold-no-essay-scholarship/)
- **Note**: Low effort, high upside; create profile once and get matched to dozens of additional scholarships automatically

### Scholarships360 EE List
- **What it is**: Aggregated list of 100+ EE-specific scholarships with deadlines, amounts, and requirements
- **Website**: [scholarships360.org/scholarships/electrical-engineering-scholarships](https://scholarships360.org/scholarships/electrical-engineering-scholarships/)
- **Action**: Review quarterly; filter by current eligibility level

### Scholarship Universe (External Scholarships via ASU)
- The same ASU portal (scholarships.asu.edu) also surfaces external scholarships matched to your profile — not just internal Fulton awards
- Filter for EE + undergraduate + continuing student each fall
