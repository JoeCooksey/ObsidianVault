# ASU Scholarships for California EE Students

Complete scholarship guide for a California student attending ASU Fulton Schools of Engineering (EE program), ranked by total dollar value and accessibility.

---

## Tier 1 — ASU Institutional (Auto-Awarded at Admission)

### New American University (NAU) Scholarship
- **Amount**: $5,500–$17,500/year (out-of-state)
- **How awarded**: Automatically at admission — no application required
- **Based on**: High school GPA in core competencies; optionally ACT/SAT (submit by May 1 to be considered)
- **Tiers**: University Award → Dean's → Provost's → President's → National Scholar
- **Renewal**: Up to 8 semesters (4 years); maintain 3.0 cumulative GPA + full-time enrollment
- **Action**: Use the [ASU Scholarship Estimator](https://financialaid.asu.edu/estimator) to see your specific award

### ASU Commitment Scholarship *(NEW — replaces WUE for Fall 2026)*
- **Amount**: $5,500–$7,500/year
- **Eligibility**: First-time, first-year out-of-state U.S. citizen; enroll directly from high school (no gap year); family income ≤ $200,000; full-time on-campus enrollment
- **Stackable**: Yes — can combine with NAU Scholarship
- **Campus/major**: No restrictions
- **Renewal**: Same criteria as NAU Scholarship (3.0 GPA, full-time)
- **Key**: This automatically fills the WUE gap for Fall 2026 admits

### WUE (Western Undergraduate Exchange) — ⚠️ DISCONTINUED
- WUE (150% of in-state tuition ≈ $15k/yr savings) is being **phased out for Fall 2026 admits**
- If you are admitted Fall 2026 or later, WUE is not available to you
- The Commitment Scholarship is ASU's replacement

**Combined NAU + Commitment potential**: $11,000–$25,000/year stacked

## Tier 2 — Fulton Schools of Engineering Scholarships

- **Total pool**: $1.7M+ awarded to undergrads annually (2024–25)
- **Portal**: [Scholarship Universe](https://scholarships.asu.edu) — requires ASURITE login
- **Opens**: November 1 each year
- **Deadline**: February 1 (most scholarships); some open year-round
- **Selection basis**: Academic merit AND talent, personal story, creativity — not just GPA
- **Named scholarships**: Via the [ASU Foundation Fulton page](https://www.asufoundation.org/colleges-and-programs/schools-and-colleges/ira-a.-fulton-schools-of-engineering/ira-a.-fulton-schools-of-engineering-scholarship-CA107808.html)
- **Action**: Log into Scholarship Universe with your ASURITE ID and filter for Electrical Engineering each November

## Tier 3 — External Professional Scholarships

### IEEE PES Scholarship Plus Initiative
- **Amount**: Up to **$10,000/year**
- **Focus**: Power and energy engineering (perfect for EE)
- **Requirements**: Min 3.0 GPA; U.S. citizen or permanent resident; full-time bachelor's student
- **Deadline**: April 30 annually
- **Website**: [ee-scholarship.org](https://ee-scholarship.org)
- **Note**: One of the best EE-specific external scholarships available; power electronics track aligns perfectly with WBG research path

### Society of Women Engineers (SWE) Scholarships *(if applicable)*
- **Amount**: Varies; SWE awarded $1.5M+ across 328+ scholarships in 2024
- **Eligibility**: Women pursuing engineering bachelor's degrees
- **Website**: swe.org

### NSPE (National Society of Professional Engineers)
- Academic merit-based; varying amounts
- Website: nspe.org

---

## Tier 4 — Federal Aid (Income-Based)

### Federal Pell Grant
- **Portable to any accredited school** including ASU
- **Amount**: Up to ~$7,395/year (2025–26 amounts)
- **Eligibility**: Based on Expected Family Contribution (EFC) from FAFSA
- **Action**: File FAFSA as early as possible (October 1 opens; priority deadline March 2 in California)

### Federal Student Loans (Subsidized/Unsubsidized)
- Subsidized (no interest while enrolled): up to $3,500/yr freshman
- Available to all FAFSA filers regardless of income

## What Does NOT Work for ASU (California Students)

| Program | Why it doesn't apply |
|---|---|
| Cal Grant (A, B, C) | Restricted to California institutions only — not portable out of state |
| ACEC California Scholarship | Requires enrollment in a California-based engineering program |
| WUE (for Fall 2026+) | Phased out — no longer available for new admits |

| Source | Low estimate | High estimate |

| NAU Scholarship (auto) | $5,500 | $17,500 |
| Commitment Scholarship (auto) | $5,500 | $7,500 |
| Fulton Schools scholarship | $1,000 | $5,000+ |
| IEEE PES (apply each year) | — | $10,000 |
| Federal Pell Grant (if eligible) | — | $7,395 |
| **Total potential** | **$12,000** | **$47,000+/yr** |

ASU out-of-state tuition (2025–26): ~$32,000/yr base tuition + fees

**Action sequence:**
1. Use ASU estimator to confirm NAU + Commitment amounts at admission
2. File FAFSA by March 2 (California priority deadline)
3. Apply for Fulton Schools scholarships on Scholarship Universe by Feb. 1
4. Apply for IEEE PES Scholarship Plus by April 30 each year
5. Search Scholarship Universe and Bold.org annually for additional EE-specific opportunities
