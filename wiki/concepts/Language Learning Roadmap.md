# Language Learning Roadmap

A complete self-study roadmap from zero to conversational fluency. Structured for a busy student with 20–30 minutes per day.

## Phase 0 — Decide and Set Up (Week 1)

**Pick your language — and commit.**
Do not "dabble" across three languages. Single-threading to conversational fluency in one language (2–3 years) beats spending 10 years starting and stopping five.

For recommendation on which language to pick, see [[Language ROI Rankings]].

**Set up your environment:**
- [ ] Install Anki (free, cross-platform)
- [ ] Download the top-2000-words frequency deck for your target language (Anki shared decks)
- [ ] Get one grammar reference book — read it like a map, not a textbook (don't drill, just orient)
- [ ] Set a daily Anki reminder (same time every day — habit anchor)

**Baseline your starting point:**
- Can you read the alphabet/script? If no, that's Week 1 focus.
- What's your current vocabulary? Zero is fine.

---

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 1–3)

**Goal**: 500–1,000 words internalized; understand ~20% of simple content.

**Daily routine (25 min):**
| Task | Time |
|------|------|
| Anki reviews | 10 min |
| New Anki cards (10–15/day) | 5 min |
| Passive immersion (podcast/TV background) | 10 min |

**Week-by-week milestones:**
- Week 2: Can read the phonetic system and sound out words
- Week 4: 100 words in Anki; can say basic greetings and numbers
- Month 2: 400 words; can understand ~10% of children's TV
- Month 3: 700 words; can follow simple YouTube content with subtitles

**Resources:**
- Grammar: *Assimil* series, *Complete [Language]* by Teach Yourself, or *Pimsleur* (audio-first)
- Vocab: Anki frequency deck
- Immersion: children's YouTube channels, Dreaming Spanish (for Spanish), Easy German/Easy French YouTube

---

## Phase 2 — Intermediate (Months 3–12)

**Goal**: 2,000–3,000 words; conversational in everyday topics; understand 60–70% of native content.

**Daily routine (30–40 min):**
| Task | Time |
|------|------|
| Anki reviews | 10 min |
| New cards / sentence mining | 5 min |
| Active immersion (native TV/YouTube with target subs) | 15–20 min |

**Key unlocks this phase:**
- Switch from premade deck to **sentence mining** — pull real sentences from your immersion content
- Install Language Reactor on Netflix — click any word to get instant definition + example
- Join a language community (Reddit: r/Spanish, r/German, etc.)
- Begin 1 italki lesson/week at month 6 — native speaker feedback is irreplaceable

**Month milestones:**
- Month 6: Hold a basic 5-minute conversation on scripted topics (introduce yourself, hobbies, school)
- Month 9: Understand ~70% of a TV show you've watched before
- Month 12: Read simple news articles; text with a native speaker

---

## Phase 3 — Advanced (Months 12–36)

**Goal**: C1-level comprehension; can discuss complex/unfamiliar topics; near-native listening.

**Daily routine (20–30 min — now sustainable for life):**
| Task | Time |
|------|------|
| Anki reviews (declining) | 5 min |
| Native media (entertainment, not study) | 15–20 min |
| Weekly: 1 italki session or language exchange | 1 hr/week |

**Key unlocks:**
- Stop using learner content — watch actual TV, YouTube, podcasts made for native speakers
- Write in target language: journal entries, language exchange messages, Reddit posts
- Shadow audio: pick a 30-second clip of native speech, transcribe it, then record yourself saying it identically
- Find a recurring social context — language exchange partner you enjoy talking to

**Milestone: ~300 total hours = basic fluency**
Most learners reach comfortable conversation around 300 hours of real exposure (not clock time — actual engaged hours with the language).

## The One-Year Sprint (Spanish Example)

If you start today and do **30 min/day**, here's the year:

- **May 2026** (now): Install Anki, start frequency deck, learn alphabet/sounds
- **Aug 2026**: 300 words, understand basic greetings and scripted phrases, first Pimsleur unit done
- **Nov 2026**: 700 words, watching Dreaming Spanish comprehensible input daily
- **Feb 2027**: 1,200 words, first italki lesson, can hold a 3-minute conversation
- **May 2027** (Year 1): ~2,000 words, ~180 hours accumulated, solid A2/B1 border — **basic conversational Spanish**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Duolingo as primary method | Use it only for gamification; not enough input volume |
| Translating in your head | Train yourself to think directly in target language |
| Waiting to feel "ready" before speaking | Speak at month 3–4 even if terrible; errors are data |
| Grammar drilling without input | Grammar is a map; immersion is the actual territory |
| Learning multiple languages simultaneously | Serial beats parallel for beginners |
| Too many new Anki cards | 10–15/day max; review pile will overwhelm you otherwise |
| Quitting during the "desert" (months 2–4) | This is normal; motivation dips before first comprehension breakthrough |

## Weekly Template (30 min/day)

```
Mon: Anki (15) + podcast/audio commute (15)
Tue: Anki (15) + YouTube native content (15)
Wed: Anki (15) + sentence mining from content (15)
Thu: Anki (15) + speaking practice / shadowing (15)
Fri: Anki (15) + reading (15)
Sat: Long session — italki lesson or 30-min immersion movie
Sun: Review week; update Anki deck; light reading
```
