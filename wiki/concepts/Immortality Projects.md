# Immortality Projects

A concept from existential psychologist **Ernest Becker** (*The Denial of Death*, 1973 — Pulitzer Prize). Popularized in the self-help domain by Mark Manson in *The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F\*ck* (Ch9). An immortality project is any effort to create a **conceptual self** — an identity or legacy — that will persist beyond physical death.

## Becker's Original Framework

Becker argued that the awareness of mortality is the central anxiety of human life. To manage death terror, humans create symbolic selves: narratives, achievements, communities, and legacies that "live on" after the body dies.

> The individual is not just a biological organism but a self-aware being who knows they will die. The response to this knowledge is the creation of "immortality projects" — heroic attempts to transcend death through meaning.

These projects take many forms:
- **Religious**: the soul lives on; God remembers you; you will be resurrected
- **Cultural**: your nation, tribe, or group carries on after you
- **Creative**: your art, writing, or music outlives you
- **Reproductive**: your children and descendants carry your genes and memory
- **Occupational**: your work, your company, your discovery persists
- **Social**: the lives you changed remember you

## Manson's Application

Manson uses Becker's framework to reframe the final question of life values:

> "In some ways, our immortality projects are our values in action — what we spend our time building reveals what we believe is actually worth our time."

The question death forces you to ask: *What are you actually building? And does it deserve the finite time you have?*

This is Manson's conclusion to the book: most people drift into immortality projects by default — social approval, consumer accumulation, status performance — without consciously choosing them. The awareness of death is an invitation to audit those projects and replace them with something genuinely worth building.

## Death as Clarifier

The Stoic practice of **[[Memento Mori Practice]]** ("remember that you will die") is the same tool Manson recommends from a different philosophical tradition. Both use death awareness to:
1. Cut through trivial anxieties that feel urgent in the moment
2. Reveal what actually matters when time is visibly limited
3. Force commitment to values over comfort

> A fear of death is a fear of living fully. Avoiding death-awareness means avoiding the clarity that death provides.

## Healthy vs Unhealthy Immortality Projects

Not all immortality projects are equal. Using the [[Values Framework (Manson)]]:

| Type | Project Example | Value Quality | Problem |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unhealthy** | Social media follower count | External, unstable | Adapts away; platform-dependent |
| **Unhealthy** | Accumulating status symbols | External, material | [[Hedonic Treadmill]]; no real legacy |
| **Unhealthy** | Being liked by everyone | External, uncontrollable | Requires managing others' opinions indefinitely |
| **Healthy** | Raising children with integrity | Internal, relational | Enduring; within your control |
| **Healthy** | Building real expertise | Internal, constructive | Compounds; creates genuine value |
| **Healthy** | Deep relationships | Internal, relational | Harvard data: strongest predictor of life satisfaction |
| **Healthy** | Honest creative work | Internal, constructive | Outlasts the body; contributes beyond the self |

## Terror Management Theory

Becker's framework spawned an empirical research program called **Terror Management Theory** (TMT), developed by Greenberg, Pyszczynski, and Solomon. Key TMT findings:
- Reminders of death (mortality salience) increase in-group identification and out-group hostility
- Mortality salience increases investment in the individual's immortality project (religious belief, national pride, creative output)
- People protect their worldviews more aggressively when reminded of death, because worldviews are part of their immortality structure

This dark side of immortality projects — tribalism, aggression, fanaticism — is what happens when the project is built on external, zero-sum values.
