# PHY 131 University Physics II E&M — Topics and Prep

**Lecture Instructor**: Quan Qing (office hrs Tue 1:30–2:30 PM, PSB 147)
**Lab Instructor**: Reaves (PHY 132)
**Textbook**: Young & Freedman, *University Physics with Modern Physics*, 15th ed. (available via Canvas)

## Topic Map (in order)

| # | Unit | Core Concepts |
|---|------|--------------|
| 1 | **Electric Charge & Coulomb's Law** | Charge quantization, force between charges, superposition |
| 2 | **Electric Field** | E-field from point charges, field lines, continuous distributions |
| 3 | **Gauss's Law** | Electric flux Φ_E, Gauss's Law ∮E·dA = Q_enc/ε₀, symmetric geometries |
| 4 | **Electric Potential** | V, ΔV, relation E = -∇V, energy of charge configurations |
| 5 | **Capacitance & Dielectrics** | C = Q/V, parallel-plate, energy stored, dielectric constant |
| 6 | **Current & Resistance** | Current density J, drift velocity, resistivity ρ, Ohm's Law |
| 7 | **DC Circuits** | KVL, KCL, series/parallel R, Kirchhoff analysis (overlaps EEE 202) |
| 8 | **Magnetic Field** | Force on moving charge F = qv×B, circular motion in B |
| 9 | **Biot-Savart Law** | B from current element, infinite wire, circular coil |
| 10 | **Ampere's Law** | ∮B·dl = μ₀I_enc, solenoid, toroid |
| 11 | **Faraday's Law** | Induced EMF ε = -dΦ_B/dt, Lenz's Law, motional EMF |
| 12 | **Self & Mutual Inductance** | L = NΦ/I, energy in inductor, transformers |
| 13 | **RL, LC, RLC Circuits** | Transient response (overlaps EEE 202 Units 4–5 exactly) |
| 14 | **AC Circuits** | Phasors, impedance, resonance (overlaps EEE 202 Unit 6) |
| 15 | **Maxwell's Equations** | Unification of E and B; displacement current |
| 16 | **EM Waves** | Speed c = 1/√(ε₀μ₀), wave energy, intro only |

## Critical Math Prerequisites

- Vector calculus: dot product (A·B), cross product (A×B)
- Surface integrals (∮ notation) — needed for Gauss's and Ampere's Law
- Derivatives and integrals (Calculus II level)
- Complex numbers (for AC circuit units)

> [!gap] Gauss's Law and Ampere's Law require surface/line integrals. If multivariable calc hasn't been taken yet, rely on the symmetry-argument approach: long straight wire, spherical shell, infinite plane.

## Hardest Topics — Where to Spend Extra Time

1. **Gauss's Law** — vector flux integral feels abstract; practice the 3 canonical geometries (sphere, cylinder, plane)
2. **Faraday's Law** — sign convention (Lenz's Law) confuses most students; do 10+ problems
3. **RL/RLC transients** — requires solving ODEs; same math as EEE 202 Unit 5 (study together)

## Summer Prep Priority

1. **Electrostatics first** (Units 1–4): Coulomb, E-field, Gauss's Law, potential — this is the first 4 weeks of class
2. **Gauss's Law specifically** — do 10 practice problems on sphere, cylinder, and infinite plane
3. **Leave magnetism for later** — Faraday/Ampere appear in weeks 9–12 of class; focus on E first
