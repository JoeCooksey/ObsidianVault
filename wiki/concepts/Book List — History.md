# Book List — History

> History is compressed decision-making data. The people who built great things, made catastrophic errors, and navigated systemic collapse are the best case studies available.

## S-Tier — Reshape Your Mental Model of the World

- **Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind** — Yuval Noah Harari — cognitive revolution through modernity; agriculture, money, empire, and science as intersubjective myths; the macro view of the human story; best first history book for someone who hasn't read history seriously; ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **The Guns of August** — Barbara Tuchman — how WWI started in 30 days; cascading decisions, misread signals, plans that couldn't adapt; Pulitzer Prize; required reading for understanding how catastrophes happen
- **The Rise and Fall of the Third Reich** — William L. Shirer — the definitive narrative history of Nazi Germany; journalist turned historian who was there; 1,400 pages but comprehensive; no ideological softening

## A-Tier — High Value

- **Genghis Khan and the Making of the Modern World** — Jack Weatherford — the Mongol Empire's real contribution to world history (trade, religious tolerance, free exchange of ideas); overturns almost everything the Western canon teaches
- **1776** — David McCullough — the most decisive year of the American founding; Washington's army nearly collapsed; shows how close failure was
- **A People's History of the United States** — Howard Zinn — history from the bottom; labor, indigenous peoples, women, and dissidents; read alongside a traditional account for full picture
- **The Silk Roads** — Peter Frankopan — recenters world history on Central Asia and trade routes; the "Eastern" history Western education ignores; major revisionist scope
- **SPQR: A History of Ancient Rome** — Mary Beard — Rome from the ground up; what Roman life actually was vs. the Hollywood version; rigorous, accessible, modern scholarship

## B-Tier — Solid

- **The Warmth of Other Suns** — Isabel Wilkerson — the Great Migration; 60 years of Black Americans moving from South to North; narrative history at its best; reads like a novel
- **Lies My Teacher Told Me** — James Loewen — what American history textbooks systematically get wrong; critical media literacy for anyone educated in American schools
- **The Crusades Through Arab Eyes** — Amin Maalouf — the medieval invasions told from the perspective of the peoples being invaded; empathy as historical method

## Long-Form Investments (High Reward, High Time Cost)

- **The Story of Civilization** — Will Durant (11 volumes) — the supreme work of humanistic history; any single volume standalone; *Caesar and Christ*, *The Age of Reason Begins*, and *The Age of Napoleon* are the highest-yield starting points
- **The Years of Lyndon Johnson** — Robert Caro (4 volumes) — the definitive study of how American political power actually works; 3,500 pages; the greatest biographical achievement in American letters

## Reading Strategy

- Start with *Sapiens* for the macro frame, then pick an era or region to go deep
- Ancient Rome: *SPQR* → Cicero's letters → Suetonius's *Twelve Caesars*
- WWII: *Guns of August* (context) → *Rise and Fall of the Third Reich* → Victor Frankl (*Man's Search for Meaning*)
- American history: *1776* → *Alexander Hamilton* (Chernow) → *A People's History*
