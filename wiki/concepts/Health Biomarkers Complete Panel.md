# Health Biomarkers — Complete Blood Panel & Key Markers

> [!important] "Normal" lab ranges ≠ "optimal" ranges. Reference ranges are statistical (based on population averages including unhealthy individuals). Optimal ranges for longevity are often stricter. This page lists both.

---

## Priority 1: Non-Negotiable — Test These Every 6–12 Months

### Metabolic / Glucose

| Biomarker | Normal Lab Range | Optimal Longevity | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **HbA1c** | <5.7% (pre-diabetes: 5.7–6.4%) | **4.8–5.2%** | 2–3 month glucose average; early T2D risk predictor |
| **Fasting Glucose** | 70–99 mg/dL | **70–85 mg/dL** | Immediate insulin/metabolic picture |
| **Fasting Insulin** | <25 μIU/mL | **<5 μIU/mL** | Most sensitive early insulin resistance marker |
| **HOMA-IR** | <2.5 (calculated) | **<1.0** | Fasting glucose × insulin ÷ 405; best IR proxy |

### Cardiovascular / Lipid

| Biomarker | Normal Lab Range | Optimal Longevity | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **ApoB** | <100 mg/dL | **<70 mg/dL** (<60 in high-risk) | Best LDL particle count proxy; stronger CVD predictor than LDL-C |
| **LDL-C** | <100 mg/dL | **<70 mg/dL** | Less accurate than ApoB; can be misleading in lean-mass hyper-responders |
| **HDL-C** | >40 (men), >50 (women) mg/dL | **>60 mg/dL** | Reverse cholesterol transport; protective |
| **Triglycerides** | <150 mg/dL | **<80 mg/dL** | Metabolic health marker; sugar and carb intake |
| **Lp(a)** | <30 mg/dL | **<14 mg/dL** | Genetic; independent CVD risk; measure once in adulthood |
| **hs-CRP** | <3.0 mg/L | **<0.8 mg/L** | Systemic inflammation; metabolic + cardiovascular risk |
| **Homocysteine** | 5–15 μmol/L | **<9 μmol/L** | Methylation; B12/folate status; CVD and cognitive risk |

### Organ Function

| Biomarker | Normal Range | Optimal | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-------------|---------|----------------|
| **eGFR** | >60 mL/min/1.73m² | **>90** | Kidney filtration; declines gradually with age |
| **Creatinine** | 0.7–1.3 mg/dL (men) | Low-normal | Kidney + muscle mass; rises with dehydration |
| **ALT** | <40 U/L (men) | **<25 U/L** | Liver health; NAFLD; alcohol; drug metabolism |
| **AST** | <40 U/L | **<20 U/L** | Liver + cardiac; both needed to differentiate source |
| **GGT** | <60 U/L | **<16 U/L** | Oxidative stress + liver; very sensitive metabolic marker |
| **Albumin** | 3.5–5.0 g/dL | **>4.3 g/dL** | Protein status + liver function; falls with poor nutrition or disease |
| **Bilirubin (total)** | 0.2–1.2 mg/dL | Low-normal | Liver + hemolysis; elevated in Gilbert's syndrome (benign) |

### Complete Blood Count (CBC)

| Biomarker | Normal Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Hemoglobin** | 13.5–17.5 g/dL (men) | Oxygen-carrying capacity; low = anemia risk |
| **Hematocrit** | 39–50% (men) | % red blood cells; tracks hydration + anemia |
| **MCV** | 80–100 fL | Low = iron deficiency; high = B12/folate deficiency |
| **RDW** | 11–14% | Red cell distribution width; rising RDW predicts all-cause mortality |
| **WBC** | 4.5–11k cells/μL | Immune status; very high = infection; very low = suppression |
| **Platelets** | 150–400k | Clotting; high = inflammation/CVD risk |
| **Lymphocytes** | 20–40% of WBC | Immune function indicator |

## Priority 2: Hormonal Panel — Test Annually

### Thyroid

| Biomarker | Normal Range | Optimal | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-------------|---------|----------------|
| **TSH** | 0.4–4.0 mIU/L | **0.5–2.0 mIU/L** | Best first-line screen; high = hypothyroid |
| **Free T4** | 0.8–1.8 ng/dL | Mid-range | Thyroid hormone storage form |
| **Free T3** | 2.3–4.2 pg/mL | **Upper-normal** | Active thyroid form; low despite normal TSH = conversion issue |
| **Reverse T3 (rT3)** | 9–25 ng/dL | Low | Blocks T3; elevated in chronic stress, low-carb extremes |
| **TPO Antibodies** | <35 IU/mL | <9 | Hashimoto's autoimmune thyroiditis screen |

### Sex Hormones (Male)

| Biomarker | Normal Range | Optimal (Young Male) | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Total Testosterone** | 300–1,000 ng/dL | **600–900 ng/dL** | Energy, muscle, libido, mood, cognitive function |
| **Free Testosterone** | 50–200 pg/mL | **Upper quartile** | Bioavailable T; critical if total T is mid-range |
| **SHBG** | 17–56 nmol/L | **25–40 nmol/L** | Binds T; very high SHBG = low free T even with normal total |
| **LH** | 1.7–8.6 mIU/mL | Mid-range | Pituitary signal to testes; low = central hypogonadism |
| **FSH** | 1.5–12.4 mIU/mL | Mid-range | Sperm production; high = testicular failure |
| **Estradiol (E2)** | 10–40 pg/mL | **20–30 pg/mL** | Too high = gynecomastia, fat gain; too low = bone loss, low libido |
| **DHEA-S** | 280–640 μg/dL (young males) | Upper range | Adrenal health; precursor to androgens; declines with age |
| **Prolactin** | 2–18 ng/mL | <15 | High prolactin suppresses testosterone |

### Other Hormones

| Biomarker | Normal Range | Optimal | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|---------|-------|
| **Cortisol (AM)** | 7–20 μg/dL | **12–18 μg/dL** | Peak at 8am; low = adrenal fatigue; high = chronic stress |
| **IGF-1** | 100–300 ng/mL (age-dependent) | **120–200 ng/mL** | GH proxy; too high = cancer risk; too low = poor recovery |
| **Insulin** | <25 μIU/mL | **<5 μIU/mL** | See metabolic section; most skip this |

## Priority 3: Nutritional Deficiencies — Test Annually

| Biomarker | Deficiency Cutoff | Optimal | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| **25-OH Vitamin D** | <20 ng/mL (deficient) | **50–70 ng/mL** | Most common deficiency in young adults (36%); bone + immune + mood |
| **Ferritin** | <12 ng/mL (depleted) | **Men: 75–150; Women: 30–75** | Iron storage; low = fatigue, poor cognition, hair loss |
| **Serum Iron** | 60–170 μg/dL | Mid-range | Context with ferritin and TIBC |
| **TIBC** | 250–370 μg/dL | — | Total iron binding capacity; high = iron deficiency |
| **Vitamin B12** | >200 pg/mL (normal) | **>600 pg/mL** | Neurological function; high MMA more accurate if borderline |
| **Folate (RBC)** | >140 ng/mL | Upper range | DNA synthesis; neural tube; better than serum folate |
| **Zinc (serum)** | 60–120 μg/dL | 80–110 μg/dL | Immune, testosterone, taste; not great marker but useful |
| **Magnesium (RBC)** | >4.2 mg/dL | >5.0 mg/dL | Serum Mg almost useless; use RBC Mg instead |
| **Omega-3 Index** | >4% (minimal) | **>8%** | EPA+DHA as % of RBC fatty acids; cardiovascular risk predictor |

## Priority 4: Inflammation & Aging Markers

| Biomarker | Optimal Target | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| **hs-CRP** | **<0.8 mg/L** | Systemic inflammation; below 1 is excellent; above 3 = high risk |
| **Homocysteine** | **<9 μmol/L** | Methylation, B vitamins; CVD + cognitive risk independent of LDL |
| **Fibrinogen** | <300 mg/dL | Clotting protein; elevated = chronic inflammation |
| **Uric Acid** | <6 mg/dL (men) | Gout, CVD risk, metabolic syndrome; above 5.5 is worth acting on |
| **GDF-15** | Low (lab-specific) | 2025 expert consensus aging biomarker; rises with age + morbidity |
| **Klotho** | High (emerging) | Anti-aging protein; declines with age; kidney + brain health |
| **IL-6** | <5 pg/mL | Pro-inflammatory cytokine; predicts metabolic disease |
| **TNF-alpha** | <8.1 pg/mL | Inflammatory signaling; elevated in obesity, autoimmune |

## Priority 5: Advanced / Longevity-Focused

| Biomarker | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| **Epigenetic Age (TruAge, Levine Clock)** | Methylation-based biological age; ~$300–500; repeat every 1–2 years |
| **Telomere Length** | Relative telomere length; correlation with aging; $200–400 |
| **Microbiome Sequencing (Viome, Zoe)** | Functional microbiome analysis; personalized nutrition guidance |
| **VO2 Max (formal test)** | Strongest longevity predictor; test in lab or estimate via fitness tracker |
| **DEXA Scan** | Body composition gold standard; visceral fat, lean mass, bone density |
| **Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM)** | 2-week wear → full metabolic picture; Levels, Dexcom, Nutrisense |
| **CAC Score (Coronary Artery Calcium)** | X-ray; measures calcium in coronary arteries; best CVD risk for 40+ |
| **ApoE Genotype** | One-time genetic test; APOE4 = 3–4× Alzheimer's risk; stratifies omega-3 need |
| **MTHFR Genotype** | Methylation enzyme variant; determines active folate need |

## Recommended Testing Cadence

| Frequency | What to Test |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Every 6 months** | HbA1c, fasting glucose, fasting insulin, lipid panel, ApoB, hs-CRP, CBC, CMP |
| **Annually** | Full hormonal panel (testosterone, thyroid, cortisol, DHEA), Vitamin D, ferritin, B12, omega-3 index, homocysteine |
| **Every 2–3 years** | Lp(a) (once is enough unless treating), ApoE genotype, MTHFR, DEXA scan |
| **One-time baseline** | Epigenetic age clock, CAC score (>40), whole-body MRI (optional) |
