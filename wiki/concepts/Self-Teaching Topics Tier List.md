# Self-Teaching Topics Tier List

A ranked guide to subjects you can master independently with textbooks or free resources. Ranked by **long-term life ROI** × **self-teachability** × **compounding effect on other domains**. Joe-specific context applied throughout.

---

## S-Tier — Master These First (Highest Compounding ROI)

### 1. Mathematics
**Why S-tier:** Every S-tier career (EE, ML, finance, physics, computer science) has math as a direct prerequisite. An hour of math compounds into every adjacent domain. Most self-teachable with free resources. No other subject comes close for foundational leverage.

| Sub-topic | Best Resource | Level |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Calculus (single + multi) | Stewart *Calculus* (applied) or Spivak *Calculus* (rigorous) | Beginner → Intermediate |
| Linear Algebra | Strang *Introduction to Linear Algebra* + MIT 18.06 OCW (free) | Beginner → Intermediate |
| Probability & Statistics | Ross *Intro to Probability and Statistics for Engineers* | Intermediate |
| Proof-writing | Hammack *Book of Proof* (free PDF online) | Intermediate |
| Discrete Math | Rosen *Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications* | Intermediate |

**Joe note:** Linear algebra is the biggest unlock — directly feeds EEE 202 (circuit matrix methods), MAT 343 (the course itself), ML, and signal processing. Start here after Calc 2.

---

### 2. Personal Finance
**Why S-tier:** A Roth IRA opened at 19 vs 35 compounds to a ~$500k+ difference by retirement (at 7% avg return). Credit, emergency funds, index investing — the mechanics are simple and the payoff is mathematical and certain.

| Sub-topic | Best Resource | Level |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Personal finance foundations | Sethi *I Will Teach You to Be Rich* | Beginner |
| Investing & index funds | Collins *The Simple Path to Wealth* (free blog) | Beginner |
| Mental models of money | Housel *The Psychology of Money* | Beginner |
| Compound interest math | Khan Academy + spreadsheet practice | Beginner |

**Joe note:** [[Financial Literacy Roadmap]] and [[Financial Order of Operations]] already in vault. Priority action: open Roth IRA at Fidelity/Vanguard now.

---

### 3. Programming (Python + C++)
**Why S-tier:** The universal tool of the 21st century. Python is the scripting language of science, ML, and automation. C++ is the embedded/firmware language of power electronics. Already partially covered in vault — confirm and deepen.

| Sub-topic | Best Resource | Level |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Python fundamentals | Automate the Boring Stuff (free online) | Beginner |
| Python for EE | [[Python EE Project Ladder]] (vault) | Intermediate |
| C++ fundamentals | K&R *The C Programming Language*, then Stroustrup | Beginner → Intermediate |
| Algorithms & data structures | Sedgewick *Algorithms* or CLRS | Intermediate |

**Joe note:** [[Python Self-Teaching Roadmap for EE]] and [[C++ Self-Teaching Roadmap for EE]] in vault. Python first, C++ by Year 2.

---

### 4. Logic & Critical Thinking
**Why S-tier:** The *meta-skill*. Logic improves how you reason about every other subject — math, physics, arguments, code, decisions. Most people never study it explicitly yet it's the infrastructure of all rigorous thought.

| Sub-topic | Best Resource | Level |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Informal logic & argumentation | Weston *A Rulebook for Arguments* ($5) | Beginner |
| Cognitive biases | Kahneman *Thinking Fast and Slow* | Beginner |
| Formal logic intro | *Introduction to Logic and Critical Thinking* (open textbook, free) | Intermediate |
| Fallacy identification | Pirie *How to Win Every Argument* | Beginner |

## A-Tier — Very High ROI, Build After Foundations

### 5. Writing & Communication
**Why A-tier:** The skill that multiplies all others. Engineers who can write and speak clearly earn more, advance faster, and get funded. A textbook + daily practice is sufficient.

| Sub-topic | Best Resource | Level |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Non-fiction prose | Zinsser *On Writing Well* | Beginner |
| Style fundamentals | Strunk & White *The Elements of Style* | Beginner |
| Email/professional writing | Williams *Style: Lessons in Clarity and Grace* | Intermediate |
| Public speaking | Toastmasters + Carnegie *How to Win Friends...* | Beginner |

---

### 6. Economics
**Why A-tier:** Supply/demand, incentives, game theory, and market structures explain the majority of human decisions at scale. Makes you a more effective agent in the world.

| Sub-topic | Best Resource | Level |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Microeconomics foundations | Hazlitt *Economics in One Lesson* (free online) | Beginner |
| Comprehensive overview | Sowell *Basic Economics* | Beginner → Intermediate |
| Game theory | Dixit & Nalebuff *Thinking Strategically* | Intermediate |
| Macro/financial systems | Dalio *Principles for Navigating Big Debt Crises* (free PDF) | Intermediate |

---

### 7. Philosophy (Stoicism → Ethics → Epistemology)
**Why A-tier:** Philosophy is the operating system under all other thinking. Stoicism specifically is a high-ROI starting point: handles adversity, frustration, and clarity about what's in/out of your control.

| Sub-topic | Best Resource | Level |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Stoicism — practice | Aurelius *Meditations* (free, Gutenberg) | Beginner |
| Stoicism — letters | Seneca *Letters from a Stoic* (free, Gutenberg) | Beginner |
| Philosophy survey | Gaarder *Sophie's World* | Beginner |
| Epistemology | Russell *The Problems of Philosophy* (free, Gutenberg) | Intermediate |
| Ethics | Singer *Practical Ethics* | Intermediate |

---

### 8. Statistics & Probability
**Why A-tier:** The language of scientific literacy. Required to read research papers critically, design experiments, evaluate claims, work in data science or ML, and understand medical/nutritional research.

| Sub-topic | Best Resource | Level |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Conceptual entry | Wheelan *Naked Statistics* | Beginner |
| Engineering-focused | Ross *Intro to Probability & Stats for Engineers* | Intermediate |
| Free comprehensive | Pishro-Nik *Intro to Probability, Statistics, and Random Processes* (free online) | Intermediate |
| Bayesian thinking | Downey *Think Bayes* (free online) | Intermediate |

**Joe note:** Directly feeds ML/AI work, research design, and reading scientific literature critically.

## C-Tier — Good Investments With Narrower Returns

### 13. Spanish (or a Second Language)
500M+ native speakers. Opens career doors in medicine, law, business, and government. Best self-teachable with *Language Transfer* (free audio course) + Anki + Duolingo combo. Slow to reach fluency but high ceiling.

### 14. Biology & Health Science
Understanding physiology, nutrition, and basic biology gives you agency over your own health. Vault already covers applied nutrition/supplements heavily. *Campbell Biology* or *Netter's Anatomy* if going deeper.

### 15. Constitutional Law & Legal Literacy
*The Constitution of the United States: A Primer* + *Law School in a Box* (Henke). Understanding contracts, rights, and legal structures prevents expensive mistakes.

### 16. Computer Science Theory
CLRS *Introduction to Algorithms* + Sedgewick *Algorithms*. High ROI if going software/ML track. Lower priority if staying power electronics/hardware-focused.

### 17. Formal Logic / Symbolic Logic
Nick Smith *Logic: The Laws of Truth*. Deep formal training in propositional/predicate logic. Excellent for mathematical rigor but narrow compared to informal logic (covered in S-tier).

### 18. Music Theory
*Fundamentals of Music Theory* (free, Coursera Edinburgh). Supports guitar/piano self-teaching, cognitive benefits, creative outlet. [[Music as a Life Skill]] in vault.
