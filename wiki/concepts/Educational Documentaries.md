# Educational Documentaries

Ranked tier list of the best documentaries for learning. Coverage: science, mathematics, physics, technology, business, economics, history, and philosophy. Tiers: S (essential, transformative) → A (excellent, worth watching) → B (good, narrower audience).

---

## Science & Physics

### S-Tier

**Cosmos: A Personal Voyage (1980)** — Carl Sagan
- 13 episodes | IMDb 9.3/10 | PBS
- The greatest science documentary ever made. Covers origin of life, stellar evolution, history of science, ancient civilizations, and humanity's place in the universe. Sagan's narration is uniquely philosophical and moving. Still unmatched 45+ years later.
- *Watch this first if you watch nothing else.*

**Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey (2014)** — Neil deGrasse Tyson
- 13 episodes | IMDb 9.3/10 | National Geographic / Fox
- Modern remake with stunning visual effects. Covers the same scope with updated science (gravitational waves, exoplanets, etc.). Best watched as a companion to the original, not a replacement.

**Particle Fever (2013)**
- 99 min | IMDb 7.9/10 | Documentary
- Follows six scientists during the 2012 Large Hadron Collider launch and Higgs boson discovery. The best "science as human drama" documentary ever made. Shows what theoretical vs. experimental physicists actually think about each other — and what's really at stake in fundamental physics.

### A-Tier

**The Elegant Universe (2003)** — Brian Greene
- 3-part PBS series
- Covers string theory, 11 dimensions, parallel universes, and the search for a theory of everything. Brian Greene is an exceptional communicator. Best entry point to theoretical physics beyond standard model.

**Secrets of Quantum Physics (2014)** — Jim Al-Khalili
- 2-part BBC series
- Al-Khalili (University of Surrey) shows where quantum mechanics appears in everyday life — photosynthesis, bird navigation, human smell. Bridges the gap between "quantum is weird" and "quantum is real."

**The Universe in a Nutshell (2018)** — Brian Cox
- Features particle physicist Brian Cox discussing time, stars, gravity, and the creation of the universe.

## Mathematics

### S-Tier

**The Story of Math (2008)** — Marcus du Sautoy
- 4-part BBC documentary
- Oxford mathematics professor traces the history of mathematics from ancient Egypt and Babylon through Newton and Euler to the unsolved problems of today. The best single documentary for understanding mathematics as a human discipline across civilizations.

### A-Tier

**A Trip to Infinity (2022)** — Netflix
- Documentary exploring infinity through interviews with leading mathematicians and physicists. Beautiful production. Covers Cantor's diagonal argument, infinitely large infinities, and what infinity means for physics.

**Fermat's Last Theorem (1996)** — Simon Singh / BBC Horizon
- 45 min documentary following Andrew Wiles' seven-year secret proof of Fermat's Last Theorem. Best mathematics-as-drama film ever made. Simon Singh's writing is exceptional. Shows what mathematical obsession actually looks like.

**The Man Who Knew Infinity (Biographical Film, 2015)**
- Feature film about Srinivasa Ramanujan's collaboration with G.H. Hardy. Bridges documentary and drama; included here because it is accurate and educational.

## Business, Technology & Economics

### S-Tier

**Inside Bill's Brain: Decoding Bill Gates (2019)** — Netflix
- 3-part series | Directed by Davis Guggenheim
- Reveals how Bill Gates approaches global problems (nuclear energy, sanitation, polio). Shows his mental models, reading habits, and thinking frameworks. Highest intellectual density of any tech documentary.

**The Social Dilemma (2020)** — Netflix
- 1h 34min | Directed by Jeff Orlowski
- Former engineers and designers from Google, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and Pinterest explain the precise design mechanics behind behavioral manipulation. The best documentary for understanding the attention economy. Must-watch for anyone trying to reduce phone use or doom-scroll patterns.

**Becoming Warren Buffett (2017)** — HBO
- 1h 27min
- Rare access to Buffett's home, routine, and philosophy. Covers value investing, patience, frugality, and compound interest as a life philosophy. Best watched alongside *The Intelligent Investor* (Graham) or *The Psychology of Money* (Housel).

### A-Tier

**Dirty Money (2018–2020)** — Netflix
- Multi-episode series examining financial scandals: Volkswagen emissions fraud, Wells Fargo fake accounts, payday loan industry, Scott Tucker. Shows how financial fraud actually operates at scale.

**Saving Capitalism (2017)** — Netflix
- Former US Labor Secretary Robert Reich examines the roots of economic inequality and the structure of modern capitalism. Best viewed alongside Piketty's *Capital* or Reich's own *Aftershock*.

**The Inventor: Out for Blood in Silicon Valley (2019)** — HBO
- 2h 0min | About Elizabeth Holmes and Theranos
- Best documentary on startup fraud, Silicon Valley hype culture, and the danger of "fake it till you make it" in a regulated industry. Pairs perfectly with *Bad Blood* (John Carreyrou's book).

**Banking on Bitcoin (2016)** — Netflix / Various
- Historical origin of Bitcoin, cryptography, and how decentralized finance challenges sovereign money. Covers Satoshi Nakamoto's whitepaper and early cypherpunk community.

## History & Society

### S-Tier

**13th (2016)** — Ava DuVernay | Netflix
- 1h 40min | Oscar-nominated
- Traces the 13th Amendment's "involuntary servitude except as punishment for crime" clause from post-Civil War era through mass incarceration. One of the most intellectually rigorous historical documentaries on American history.

**Free Solo (2018)** — National Geographic
- Alex Honnold free-soloing El Capitan. Not purely intellectual — but the study of elite human performance, risk tolerance, and psychological preparation makes it one of the most compelling human-achievement documentaries ever made.

### A-Tier

**The Last Dance (2020)** — Netflix
- 10-episode series on Michael Jordan's career and the 1997–98 Chicago Bulls. Exceptional case study in obsession, leadership, competitive excellence, and the psychology of peak performance.

**Won't You Be My Neighbor? (2018)** — Fred Rogers documentary
- About compassion, intentionality, and human connection as a professional and life philosophy. Unexpectedly profound.
