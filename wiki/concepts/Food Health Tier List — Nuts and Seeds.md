# Food Health Tier List — Nuts and Seeds

Ranking criteria: **pure healthiness** — omega-3 to omega-6 ratio, micronutrient density (vitamin E, magnesium, zinc, calcium), fiber, protein quality, anti-inflammatory evidence, and cardiovascular outcomes. Sources: Harvard Health, PMC umbrella review (28g/day nuts → 21% CVD reduction, 11% cancer mortality reduction, 22% all-cause mortality reduction), Center for Science in the Public Interest.

Serving size: 28g (1 oz) for nuts; 2 tbsp for seeds.

---

## S Tier — Nutritional All-Stars

| Nut / Seed | Per Serving | Why |
|------------|-------------|-----|
| **Walnuts** | 4g protein, 2.5g ALA omega-3, 2g fiber | Highest ALA omega-3 of any nut (2.5g/oz — more than most fish per gram); folate, vitamin E, manganese; linked to improved memory and reduced depression; brain-shaped for a reason |
| **Chia Seeds** | 5g protein, 10g fiber, 5g ALA omega-3 per 2 tbsp | 2–3× the ALA of walnuts per gram; 10g fiber per 2 tbsp (37% DV); calcium (18% DV — same as a glass of milk); all 9 essential amino acids; anti-inflammatory phenolic compounds; gels in water → slower digestion |
| **Flaxseeds (ground)** | 3g protein, 4g fiber, 3.5g ALA omega-3 | ALA omega-3; lignans (highest of any food — 800× more than other plant foods) → hormone balance, cancer-protective; **must grind** — whole seeds pass undigested; also contains cholesterol-lowering fiber |

---

## A Tier — Excellent Daily Choices

| Nut / Seed | Per Serving | Why |
|------------|-------------|-----|
| **Almonds** | 6g protein, 3.5g fiber, 7.4mg vitamin E | #1 ranked nut by many analyses; highest vitamin E of any nut (50% DV/oz); calcium (8% DV — unusual for a nut); magnesium; phytosterols reduce LDL cholesterol; most studied nut for metabolic health |
| **Pumpkin Seeds (Pepitas)** | 7g protein, 2.5g fiber, 156mg magnesium | Highest magnesium of any seed (37% DV/oz); zinc (20% DV) for immune function and testosterone; tryptophan → serotonin → melatonin for sleep; complete protein |
| **Hemp Seeds** | 10g protein per 3 tbsp (complete), 1g omega-3 | Most bioavailable plant protein (edestin protein closely resembles human blood plasma); ideal omega-6:omega-3 ratio of 3:1 (most nuts are 30:1+); magnesium, iron, zinc |
| **Pistachios** | 6g protein, 3g fiber, 3g fiber | Lowest calorie nut (159 cal/oz vs. macadamia at 204); complete protein when eaten in combination; B6 (highest of any nut — 25% DV); potassium; lutein + zeaxanthin (eye health); shown to reduce LDL |

## B Tier — Solid, Worth Regular Rotation

| Nut / Seed | Per Serving | Why |
|------------|-------------|-----|
| **Hazelnuts** | 4g protein, 2.7g fiber, 4.2mg vitamin E | Clinical study: hazelnut-enriched diet reduced total cholesterol 8%, triglycerides 7%, increased HDL 6%; high vitamin E; folate; oleic acid (same as olive oil) |
| **Sunflower Seeds** | 5g protein, 3.1g fiber, 7.4mg vitamin E | Equal to almonds in vitamin E; selenium; magnesium; but high in omega-6 linoleic acid — fine in moderation, problematic in excess |
| **Sesame Seeds** | 5g protein, 3.3g fiber, 14% DV calcium | Highest calcium of all seeds; copper (bone/connective tissue); lignans (sesamin, sesamolin) → anti-inflammatory; tahini is a convenient delivery format |
| **Cashews** | 5g protein, 1g fiber, 83mg magnesium | Iron (11% DV), zinc (15% DV), copper; lower fiber than most nuts; higher carbohydrate; still health-positive but less impressive than top nuts |
| **Peanuts** | 7g protein, 2.4g fiber | Technically a legume; high protein, niacin, folate, resveratrol (same as red grapes); but high in omega-6 and often roasted in inflammatory oils; choose dry-roasted or natural |

## C Tier — Good But With Limitations

| Nut / Seed | Per Serving | Why |
|------------|-------------|-----|
| **Brazil Nuts** | 4g protein, very high selenium | **1 Brazil nut = 175% DV selenium** — a pro and a con; selenium toxicity risk from eating more than 2/day; eat as a precision supplement (2/day), not as a snack; keep them in C for the binary risk |
| **Macadamia Nuts** | 2g protein, 1g fiber, 21g fat | 80% of fat is monounsaturated (oleic acid) — same as olive oil; but lowest protein/fiber of any tree nut; mainly a fat source; delicious, occasionally fine |
| **Pecans** | 3g protein, 2.7g fiber | Antioxidant score is actually high (highest among common nuts); but lower protein and fiber than almonds/walnuts; high in oleic acid; calorie-dense for the nutrition returned |
| **Pine Nuts** | 4g protein, 1g fiber | Vitamin K, zinc, magnesium; unique pinolenic acid may reduce appetite; but expensive, short shelf life, and not particularly outstanding in any category |

## D Tier — Processed Nut Products

| Product | Why |
|---------|-----|
| **Honey-Roasted Nuts** | Added sugar, often coated in seed oils; destroys the health value of the underlying nut |
| **Flavored Seed Mixes (sodium coated)** | High sodium negates cardiovascular benefit; often with artificial flavors/dyes |
| **Commercial Peanut Butter with hydrogenated oils** | Trans fats + added sugar cancel out peanut benefits; choose natural (ingredients: peanuts, salt only) |

## Key Principles for Nuts and Seeds

1. **Omega-3 is the differentiator**: Walnuts, chia, flax dominate because ALA omega-3 is rare in the food supply and most people are deficient
2. **Omega-6:omega-3 ratio matters**: Most nuts are 10:1 to 30:1 omega-6:omega-3 — this is not a problem in moderation, but adding more omega-6 without omega-3 is pro-inflammatory at excess
3. **28g/day = maximum observed benefit**: Meta-analyses show 28g (1 oz) nuts/day reduces CVD by 21%, cancer mortality by 11%, all-cause mortality by 22% — benefits don't obviously increase beyond this
4. **Grinding flaxseed is essential**: Whole flaxseed passes through undigested; buy whole and grind fresh (or buy pre-ground and refrigerate, as ALA oxidizes quickly)
5. **Brazil nuts are a precision supplement**: 2/day = perfect selenium dose; more = risk selenium toxicity (nausea, hair loss, nerve damage)
6. **Roasting degrades some nutrients** but not significantly; avoid nuts roasted in vegetable oils — oil-free dry roasting is fine
