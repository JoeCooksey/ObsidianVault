# Creatine for Cognitive Performance

Creatine monohydrate is best known as a muscle performance supplement, but it also plays a direct role in brain energy metabolism. The brain uses creatine-phosphocreatine to rapidly regenerate ATP during intense cognitive work — the same mechanism that benefits muscle during exercise.

## Evidence Summary (Meta-analysis, 2024)

From a systematic review and meta-analysis of 24 RCTs with ~1,000 participants (PMC11275561):

| Cognitive Domain | Effect Size (SMD) | Significance | Evidence Grade |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------|----------------|
| Memory | 0.31 (95% CI: 0.18–0.44) | p < 0.00001 | **Moderate** |
| Processing Speed | −0.51 (faster) | p = 0.04 | Low |
| Attention Speed | −0.31 (faster) | p = 0.03 | Low |
| Executive Function | Non-significant | — | Low |

**Negative SMD = faster completion time** (i.e., improvement).

## Who Benefits Most

- **Adults 18–60**: significant attention improvements (SMD = −0.55)
- **Over 60**: no significant benefit detected
- **Female participants**: significant processing speed improvement (SMD = −0.87)
- **Male participants**: no significant processing speed benefit in this meta-analysis
- **People under cognitive stress** (sleep deprivation, intense mental load): larger effect sizes than resting baseline
- **Vegetarians / vegans**: largest benefit — dietary creatine intake is near-zero; supplementation raises brain creatine stores most dramatically

## Practical Details

**Dosage:**
- 3–5 g/day creatine monohydrate — standard maintenance dose
- Loading phase (20 g/day for 5–7 days) is optional — achieves saturation faster but not required
- No meaningful benefit over 5 g/day for most people

**Form:** Creatine monohydrate is the most studied, cheapest, and equally effective vs fancy alternatives (creatine HCl, buffered creatine). Don't pay more for other forms.

**Timing:** Any time of day. Timing does not significantly impact efficacy.

**Safety:** One of the most extensively studied supplements. No credible evidence of kidney or liver harm in healthy individuals at 3–5 g/day. Long-term safety well-established.

**Cost:** Roughly $0.05–0.10 per daily dose. One of the best value supplements.

## Realistic Expectation for a Healthy 19-Year-Old Male

The cognitive effect in healthy young males is modest compared to elderly or cognitively stressed populations. The clearest benefit is likely during exam periods, all-nighters, or periods of high cognitive load — not at baseline. It is also unambiguously beneficial for physical performance, making it high-value regardless of the cognitive debate.

> [!gap] The meta-analysis found female participants showed larger processing speed gains than males. The cognitive mechanism in young healthy males specifically has lower certainty (low evidence grade). The physical performance benefit is high-certainty.
