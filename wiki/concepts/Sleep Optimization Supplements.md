# Sleep Optimization Supplements

Sleep is the most powerful cognitive enhancer. Memory consolidation, synaptic pruning, and waste clearance (glymphatic system) all occur during sleep. A 19-year-old averaging 6 hours of sleep is cognitively impaired regardless of what supplements they take. Fix sleep first.

> [!key-insight] Sleep > any supplement
> No supplement stack compensates for chronic sleep deprivation. Prioritize sleep hygiene before spending money on nootropics.

---

## Magnesium Glycinate (Primary Recommendation)

Already covered in [[Foundational Health Supplements]] — also the first-line sleep supplement.

**Why it helps sleep:**
- Magnesium activates GABA receptors (the brain's primary inhibitory neurotransmitter)
- Regulates cortisol and body temperature — both affect sleep onset
- Reduces muscle tension and restlessness

**Evidence:** RCT (n=46): 500 mg magnesium × 8 weeks → significantly improved sleep time, sleep efficiency, reduced sleep onset latency, reduced cortisol (medium evidence quality).

**Dose for sleep:** 200–400 mg elemental magnesium as glycinate, 30–60 minutes before bed.

**Form matters:** Glycinate binds well and is gentle on digestion. Oxide is poorly absorbed. L-threonate (Magtein) crosses the BBB better for cognitive effects but costs more.

## Melatonin

Melatonin is a hormone, not a sedative. It signals the brain that it is dark → nighttime → sleep time. It shifts circadian phase rather than directly inducing sleep.

**Best use cases:**
- Delayed sleep phase (natural night owl tendencies common in college students)
- Jet lag and schedule shifts
- Re-setting sleep schedule before a demanding semester

**Evidence:** Works well for circadian disruption; less clear for primary insomnia in otherwise healthy young adults.

**Dose:** **0.1–0.5 mg** is physiologically active. The 5–10 mg doses sold at pharmacies are pharmacological overdoses that cause daytime grogginess and may suppress natural melatonin production with chronic use.

**Timing:** 30–60 minutes before intended sleep time. Earlier (2 hours before) for phase-shifting purposes.

**Safety:** Short-term generally safe. Long-term daily use at high doses not recommended — long-term data lacking.

---

## L-Theanine (for Sleep)

200–400 mg L-theanine alone (without caffeine) before bed promotes relaxation and may improve sleep quality — particularly sleep satisfaction and reduction of nighttime awakenings. Does not cause sedation.

Safe to use nightly. Low cost. Well-tolerated.

---

## What Not to Take for Sleep

- **High-dose melatonin (5–10 mg)**: pharmacological overdose; causes grogginess, may reduce natural production
- **Diphenhydramine (Benadryl / ZzzQuil)**: anticholinergic; degrades sleep quality despite causing sedation; associated with cognitive impairment with regular use
- **Alcohol**: reduces REM sleep and sleep quality despite aiding sleep onset

---

## Sleep Hygiene (More Important Than Any Supplement)

1. Consistent wake time — the anchor of circadian rhythm
2. Dark, cool room (65–68°F / 18–20°C)
3. No screens 60 minutes before bed (or blue light blocking glasses)
4. Caffeine cutoff: no later than 2 PM (caffeine half-life 5–7 hours)
5. Exercise during the day — one of the most reliable sleep quality improvers
