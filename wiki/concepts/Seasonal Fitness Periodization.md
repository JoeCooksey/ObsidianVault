# Seasonal Fitness Periodization

An annual training plan aligned with the four seasons. Each season has a primary fitness pillar in focus, but all pillars receive maintenance attention year-round. The goal is to avoid the plateau of year-round generic training by cycling emphasis — peaking each quality when the season favors it, then maintaining it while building another.

---

## The Core Framework

### Three-Level Cycle Structure
| Level | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Macrocycle** | Full year | Annual arc across all 4 seasons |
| **Mesocycle** | 4–6 weeks | Phase-specific adaptation block |
| **Microcycle** | 1 week | Day-by-day load arrangement |

**Microcycle rhythm (universal)**: 3 hard weeks → 1 deload week (reduce volume ~40%, maintain some intensity).

---

## Full Annual Seasonal Map

### Fall — General Preparation (September–November)

**Theme**: Reset, rebuild the foundation. Transition out of summer laziness or in-season fatigue.

| Pillar | Fall Focus | Approach |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Cardiovascular Endurance** | Aerobic base building | Zone 1–2 only; 3–4×/week, 45–60 min; no intervals yet |
| **Muscular Strength** | Anatomical adaptation / endurance | High reps (15–25), light load (50–60% 1RM), 3×/week |
| **Muscular Endurance** | **Primary focus** | 20–25 rep sets; circuit-style; build joint tolerance |
| **Flexibility/Mobility** | **Elevated focus** | 20–30 min sessions 3×/week; assess movement deficits |
| **Body Composition** | Recomposition | Maintenance calories (+/- 100 kcal); shift muscle:fat ratio |

**Fall Guiding Principle**: protect tissues and build capacity before you load them. This is the most injury-preventing phase — skip it at your peril.

---

### Winter — Specific Preparation (December–February)

**Theme**: Maximum productive overload. Build the engine.

| Pillar | Winter Focus | Approach |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Cardiovascular Endurance** | Aerobic volume peak | Zone 2 at highest annual volume; 4×45–60 min/week |
| **Muscular Strength** | **Primary focus** | Hypertrophy → Strength progression: 8–12 reps → 3–6 reps → 1–5 reps over 3 mesocycles |
| **Muscular Endurance** | Maintained in warm-up | High-rep finishers or circuits after main lifts |
| **Flexibility/Mobility** | Dedicated sessions | 2–3×/week mobility work; exploit indoor training time |
| **Body Composition** | Lean bulk | +200–300 kcal/day surplus; protein 0.7–1.0 g/lb BW; build muscle |

**Winter Guiding Principle**: this is when you get stronger. Everything else is maintenance. The gym is your primary environment — weather removes outdoor constraints and creates opportunity.

**Strength Mesocycle Progression (12 weeks)**:
1. Weeks 1–4 (Hypertrophy): 65–75% 1RM, 8–12 reps, 3–4 sets
2. Weeks 5–8 (Strength): 80–90% 1RM, 3–6 reps, 4–5 sets
3. Weeks 9–12 (Peak Strength): 85–95% 1RM, 1–5 reps, 5–6 sets

---

### Spring — Build / Pre-Competition (March–May)

**Theme**: Convert gains. Sharpen tools. Reveal the physique.

| Pillar | Spring Focus | Approach |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Cardiovascular Endurance** | **VO2 max blocks** | 2–3 week concentrated VO2 max mesocycles; 4×4 min intervals; pull back Zone 2 volume slightly |
| **Muscular Strength** | Power conversion | Reduce reps (1–3); add explosive/speed work; maintain heavy loading |
| **Muscular Endurance** | Maintained | Circuit finishers, Tabata, sport-specific conditioning |
| **Flexibility/Mobility** | Daily minimum | 10–15 min dynamic before sessions; static post; re-test ROM |
| **Body Composition** | **Cut** | −300–500 kcal/day; high protein (preserve muscle); 8–12 week cut |

**Spring Guiding Principle**: stop building, start sharpening. You should enter summer with your highest power output and lowest body fat of the year.

**VO2 Max Interval Protocol** (insert 2–3 week blocks):
- 4–6 × 4-min intervals at 95–100% max effort
- 4 min full recovery between reps
- 1 session/week is minimum; 2 sessions/week maximum (requires recovery)

---

### Summer — Competition / Peak (June–August)

**Theme**: Perform, display, and enjoy. Don't build — maintain and show.

| Pillar | Summer Focus | Approach |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Cardiovascular Endurance** | Maintain | 2–3 sessions/week; mixed Zone 2 + sport activity |
| **Muscular Strength** | Maintain | 1–2×/week full-body; reduce volume 30–40%; keep intensity |
| **Muscular Endurance** | Active lifestyle | Hiking, swimming, sports; keep the body moving |
| **Flexibility/Mobility** | Daily minimum | 10 min/day; essential for injury prevention in active summer |
| **Body Composition** | Maintain / slight cut | Maintenance calories; protein-first eating; summer physique |

**Summer Guiding Principle**: preserve all winter/spring gains with minimal training volume. This is not the season to set PRs or make gains. More recovery = better performance when it counts.

## Key Periodization Principles

- **Progressive overload**: each mesocycle must increase the stimulus before the deload week
- **Specificity**: adaptations match the stimulus — train what you want to improve
- **Reversibility**: gains decay in 2–4 weeks without maintenance stimulus; never fully skip a pillar
- **Individuality**: adjust volume/intensity for your recovery capacity — 3:1 is not universal
- **Never cut during a strength peak**: caloric deficit crushes strength gains; align cut with Spring only
- **Stability year-round**: 5–10 min of foundational movement work before every session, every season

## Sample Weekly Structure by Season

### Fall Week (6 days)
- Mon: Full-body endurance strength (15–25 reps)
- Tue: Zone 2 cardio 60 min
- Wed: Mobility/flexibility 30 min + active recovery
- Thu: Full-body endurance strength
- Fri: Zone 2 cardio 45 min
- Sat: Full-body endurance strength OR outdoor activity
- Sun: Full rest

### Winter Week (6 days)
- Mon: Heavy strength (lower body focus)
- Tue: Zone 2 cardio 60 min + mobility 20 min
- Wed: Heavy strength (upper body focus)
- Thu: Zone 2 cardio 60 min
- Fri: Heavy strength (full body)
- Sat: Zone 2 cardio 45–60 min
- Sun: Full rest

### Spring Week (6 days)
- Mon: Power strength (lower body)
- Tue: VO2 max intervals (4×4 min)
- Wed: Power strength (upper body) + mobility
- Thu: Moderate Zone 2 (recovery cardio)
- Fri: Power strength (full body)
- Sat: VO2 max intervals OR sport activity
- Sun: Full rest

### Summer Week (4–5 days)
- Mon: Full-body maintenance strength (moderate)
- Tue: Zone 2 / outdoor activity (bike, swim, hike)
- Wed: Rest or mobility
- Thu: Full-body maintenance strength
- Fri: Zone 2 or sport
- Sat–Sun: Active lifestyle, rest

---

## Transition Periods

Between major seasons, insert a **1–2 week transition** (especially Fall ↔ Summer):
- Reduce all training volume 50%
- Unstructured movement only (walks, casual sports)
- Mental reset — motivation matters for the long-term arc
- No structured strength or intervals
