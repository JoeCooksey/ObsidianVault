# Implementation Intention

A pre-decided commitment that specifies *exactly* when and where a behavior will occur. The single most evidence-backed strategy for closing the gap between intention and action.

## Formula

> **"I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]."**

Examples:
- "I will study organic chemistry at 9:00 AM in the campus library."
- "I will do 5 push-ups at 7:00 AM in my bedroom before showering."
- "I will review my habit tracker at 10:00 PM at my desk."

## Why It Works

Most habit failures happen not from lack of desire but from lack of a triggering cue. The mind defaults to the path of least resistance — which is usually the existing habit.

An implementation intention:
1. **Binds the cue** — time + location becomes an automatic trigger (no decision needed in the moment)
2. **Reduces friction** — no mental energy spent deciding when/where to start
3. **Crowds out competing behaviors** — the slot is taken; other activities must fit around it

Research by Peter Gollwitzer (1999) showed that people who specified when and where they would exercise were 2–3× more likely to follow through vs. those who only set intentions.

## Relationship to [[Habit Loop]]

Implementation Intention is a **Cue-design tool** — it operates at the first stage of the [[Habit Loop]] by making the cue explicit, predictable, and unambiguous.

Without implementation intention:
> "I want to study more" → no specific cue → habit does not fire reliably

With implementation intention:
> "I will study at 9 AM in the library" → time + location = reliable cue → habit fires automatically

## Paired Technique: Habit Stacking

[[Habit Stacking]] is a variant of Implementation Intention where the "time" component is replaced with an existing habit:

> **"After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]."**

This is often *more powerful* than a clock-based intention because:
- The existing habit already has a neural trigger
- No reliance on remembering a clock time
- The chain creates a sequential behavioral flow

See [[Habit Stacking]] for full treatment.

## Design Notes

- **Specificity matters**: "I will study sometime tomorrow morning" is NOT an implementation intention. Time and place must be concrete.
- **One at a time**: stacking too many new implementation intentions dilutes all of them
- **Keep it achievable**: combine with the [[Two-Minute Rule]] — make the first version of the habit trivially easy so the implementation intention triggers action even on low-motivation days
