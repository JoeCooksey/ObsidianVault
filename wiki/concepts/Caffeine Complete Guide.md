# Caffeine Complete Guide

Caffeine is the most widely consumed psychoactive substance on Earth. It is an adenosine receptor antagonist — it blocks the brain's fatigue signals rather than adding energy. This page covers mechanism, evidence-graded health effects, dosing, safety limits, and tolerance.

---

## Mechanism of Action

1. **Adenosine antagonism**: Adenosine accumulates throughout the day causing drowsiness. Caffeine binds adenosine A1 and A2a receptors without activating them — blocking the drowsiness signal.
2. **Secondary effects**: Caffeine blockade increases dopaminergic and noradrenergic activity → improved mood, focus, reaction time.
3. **Tolerance mechanism**: Chronic use causes the brain to upregulate (add) adenosine receptors to compensate. Higher receptor density = more caffeine needed to achieve the same block.

**Half-life**: 5–6 hours on average (range: 1.5–9.5 hours due to CYP1A2 genetic variation). This means 100 mg at noon leaves ~50 mg active at 6 PM.

## Health Effects by Confidence Level

### High Confidence (multiple independent RCTs or large meta-analyses)

| Effect | Direction | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Cognitive performance | ↑ | Memory, attention, reaction time, verbal fluency improved acutely |
| Physical performance | ↑ | Endurance, strength, power output — 3–6 mg/kg consistently ergogenic |
| Parkinson's disease risk | ↓ | Dose-dependent reduction; later age of onset in users |
| Alzheimer's disease risk | ↓ | Protective association across multiple cohort studies |
| Type 2 Diabetes risk | ↓ | 1–3 cups/day associated with reduced incidence |
| Liver disease risk | ↓ | Including cirrhosis and liver cancer |
| Sleep disruption | ↑ | Even 6 hours pre-sleep disrupts sleep architecture (see [[Caffeine Timing Protocol]]) |

### Medium Confidence (single large study or partial agreement across sources)

| Effect | Direction | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Cardiovascular protection | ↓ risk | 3–4 cups/day (≈450–600 mL coffee) shows protective association |
| Cancer prevention (skin) | ↓ risk | UV-exposed skin; not well-replicated |
| Muscle soreness post-exercise | ↓ | Reduces inflammatory markers |
| Cortisol increase | ↑ | Acute effect; tolerance develops within 5 days of regular use |
| Blood pressure | ↑ | Acute rise; habitual users develop tolerance; concern for cardiac patients |

### High Confidence — Risks

| Risk | Threshold | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Anxiety / jitteriness | Variable; starts for sensitives at ~200 mg | Genetic; ADOA2A gene variants increase sensitivity |
| Dependency | Develops within 1–2 weeks daily use | 64.6% of regular users experience withdrawal symptoms |
| Sleep disruption | Any dose within 6–12 hrs of bed | Decreases REM sleep; impairs emotional recovery |
| Arrhythmia risk | High doses (>600 mg) | Rare in healthy adults at moderate intake |

## Dosing Guidelines

| Goal | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Cognitive enhancement | 75–200 mg | Lower end = less anxiety; combine with L-theanine |
| Physical performance | 3–6 mg/kg body weight | ~200–400 mg for 70 kg person; take 30–60 min pre-exercise |
| Daily general use | ≤400 mg/day | FDA and USDA ceiling for healthy adults |
| Pregnant / breastfeeding | ≤200–300 mg/day | Current guideline; even this is under scientific review |

> [!gap] No single "optimal" daily dose has been established in controlled trials — 400 mg/day is a safety ceiling, not a target.

---

## Tolerance and Dependency

- **Onset of tolerance**: Begins within 4–7 days of daily use
- **Peak performance benefits**: Days 1–4 (largest ergogenic effect)
- **Substantially diminished**: By day 15–20, benefit reduced to small-to-moderate
- **Withdrawal**: Affects ~65% of users; peak symptoms at 20–51 hrs after last dose; resolves by day 7–9
- **Common withdrawal symptoms**: Headache (most common), fatigue, irritability, brain fog, depressed mood, flu-like symptoms

## Safety Ceiling

- **Healthy adults**: ≤400 mg/day (FDA)
- **Adolescents**: ≤100 mg/day (American Academy of Pediatrics)
- **Pregnancy**: ≤200 mg/day (contested — some studies show risk below this)
- **Lethal dose**: ~10 g (equivalent to ~100 cups of coffee); energy drinks cannot reach this in a single sitting but supplements/powder can — pure caffeine powder is extremely dangerous

> [!warning] Pure caffeine powder is measured in milligrams, not teaspoons. Teaspoon-level doses of powder can be lethal. Never use.
