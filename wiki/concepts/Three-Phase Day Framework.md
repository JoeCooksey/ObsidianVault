# Three-Phase Day Framework

A habit scheduling framework developed by [[Andrew Huberman]] that aligns habit execution with the natural neurochemical phases of the circadian cycle. The key insight is that **state, not clock time, determines habit execution probability**.

## Phase 1: 0–8 Hours After Waking

**Neurochemistry**: Elevated norepinephrine, epinephrine (adrenaline), and dopamine  
**State**: High alertness, action-oriented, focus-ready

The nervous system is naturally primed for overcoming resistance in Phase 1. This window is optimal for **high-limbic-friction habits** — the hardest behaviors to initiate.

**What to place here**:
- Exercise (especially if it requires motivation to start)
- Deep focused work
- Any habit with historically high resistance

**Why it works**: Elevated catecholamines lower the cost of overriding internal resistance ([[Limbic Friction]]). Placing hard habits here leverages neurochemistry rather than fighting it.

---

## Phase 2: 9–15 Hours After Waking

**Neurochemistry**: Dopamine and norepinephrine tapering; serotonin rising  
**State**: Alert but calming; socially present; less suited for intense effortful override

Phase 2 is optimal for **low-to-moderate limbic friction habits** — behaviors that are somewhat challenging but don't require intense activation energy to initiate.

**What to place here**:
- Journaling
- Language learning
- Music practice
- Socializing
- Light creative work
- Heat exposure (sauna, hot bath/shower) — supports the serotonergic state

**What to avoid**: Bright artificial light (taper toward evening), high-stress activities, stimulants like caffeine

---

## Phase 3: 16–24 Hours After Waking

**Neurochemistry**: Melatonin rising; cortisol at minimum  
**State**: Sleep — neural consolidation window

Phase 3 is not for habit *execution* — it is when the **neuroplasticity triggered in Phases 1 and 2 is consolidated**. The neural rewiring that makes habits stick happens here during deep sleep.

**Protect Phase 3 by**:
- Minimizing light (especially bright or blue-spectrum)
- Keeping room temperature cool (body must drop 1–3°C to enter deep sleep)
- Avoiding caffeine (blocks melatonin)
- Avoiding large meals close to sleep
- Using minimum light if waking at night

> [!note] Sleep is non-negotiable
> Disrupting Phase 3 undoes the habit-formation work of Phases 1 and 2. The neural rewiring requires deep sleep to consolidate. A single night of ~6 hours impairs cognition more than most supplements can compensate. See [[Sleep Optimization Supplements]].

---

## Key Principle: State Over Schedule

The nervous system generates behavior based on **neurochemical state**, not clock time. Rigid clock-based schedules are useful short-term but do not build the deep [[Task Bracketing]] that produces context-independent habits.

Scheduling habits within phase windows (rather than "at 7:00am exactly") is more neurobiologically durable.

---

## Once a Habit Is Consolidated

After a habit achieves context independence (see [[Habit Strength]]), it no longer needs to be anchored to a specific phase. Deliberately moving it across phases (e.g., exercising in the afternoon some days) **confirms and deepens** context independence. This is the test that a habit has truly been wired in.
