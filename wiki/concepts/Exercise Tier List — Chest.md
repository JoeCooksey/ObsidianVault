# Exercise Tier List — Chest

Based on Jeff Nippard's evidence-based chest exercise rankings (March 2024). Scoring criteria: (1) stretch and tension in the lengthened position, (2) mind-muscle connection without pain, (3) ease of progressive overload.

---

## Ranking Criteria

The most important factor for chest hypertrophy is **tension at the lengthened/stretched position** (bottom of the range of motion). Exercises that maintain load at full stretch outperform those that go slack at the bottom.

---

## Tier List

### S-Tier — Gold Standard

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| **Machine Chest Press** | Best of all worlds: deep stretch, constant tension, safe, easy progressive overload |
| **Seated Cable Pec Flye / Cable Crossover** | Constant cable tension across full ROM; excellent stretch; high mind-muscle connection |

### A-Tier — Primary Lifts

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Barbell Bench Press** | Strength king — not S because barbell limits depth of stretch |
| **Incline Barbell Bench Press** | Upper chest emphasis; limited ROM at bottom |
| **Flat Dumbbell Press** | Better stretch than barbell — dumbbells can move past rib cage |
| **Incline Dumbbell Press** | Upper chest + good stretch; best free-weight upper chest |
| **Dips** (chest version) | Deep stretch; body forward; aggressive pec stretch at bottom |
| **Pec Deck / Chest Flye Machine** | Constant tension; safe; great pump |
| **Smith Machine Bench** | Progressive overload easy; reduced stabilizer demand |

### B-Tier — Good Secondary Options

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Decline Barbell Bench Press | Less ROM; lower chest has less need for emphasis |
| Decline Dumbbell Press | Same issue as decline barbell |
| Banded Pushups | Adds resistance at top (ascending strength curve match) |

### C-Tier — Acceptable

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Bodyweight Pushups | Good for beginners; progressive overload is difficult |
| Barbell Floor Press | Cuts ROM — no benefit over bench at less risk |

### D-Tier — Suboptimal

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Plyometric Pushups | Builds explosive power, not hypertrophy |
| Dumbbell Pullover | Primarily lats, not chest |

### F-Tier — Avoid

| Exercise | Why to Avoid |
|---|---|
| **Guillotine Press** | Bar to throat — high injury risk; no extra chest activation |
| Hex Press | Tricep-dominant at top; loses chest activation |
| Single-Arm DB Press | Imbalanced loading; no advantage |
| Plate Press | Redundant; no tension at stretch |

---

## Programming Notes

- **Best chest program structure**: Machine press or incline DB press (S/A tier) + cable flye (S tier) + optional pec deck finisher
- **Barbell bench press**: Include for strength, not strictly required for hypertrophy
- Chest responds well to the **stretched position** — cables and machines excel here
- Upper chest (incline exercises) is often underdeveloped — prioritize
