# Exercise Tier List — Arms

Based on Jeff Nippard's biceps and triceps exercise rankings. Key principle for arms: **stretch-based exercises** (long muscle length under tension) produce more hypertrophy than short-ROM peak contraction exercises.

---

## BICEPS Tier List

The brachii has two heads (long and short) and the brachialis underneath. Best bicep exercises provide tension at the **stretched (fully extended)** position.

### S+-Tier

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| **Face-Away Bayesian Curl (cable)** | Arm extended behind body = maximum bicep stretch under load; Nippard's #1 bicep exercise |

### S-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Dumbbell Preacher Curl** | Deep stretch at bottom over pad; excellent isolation; high tension at long length |
| **Machine Preacher Curl** | Same as DB preacher but more stable; easier to load progressively |
| **Preacher Hammer Curl** | Brachialis + brachii; neutral grip; great thickness builder |

### A-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| EZ-Bar Curl | Reduces wrist supination stress; excellent volume exercise |
| Standing DB Curl | Classic; supination at top adds peak contraction |
| Incline DB Curl | Arm extends behind torso — extra stretch; excellent |
| Lying DB Curl (on bench) | Similar to incline; stretch at bottom |
| Standing Cable Curl | Constant tension; add D-handle for single arm |
| Hammer Curl | Brachialis emphasis; neutral grip; adds arm thickness |

### B-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Barbell Curl | Classic; good strength exercise; slightly less ROM than DB |
| Flat Bench Curl | DB curl on flat bench; OK stretch |
| Chin-Up | More bicep-dominant than pull-up; compound option |

### C-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Scott Curl (EZ bar preacher) | Good but less effective than DB preacher at stretch |
| Drag Curl | Limits shoulder flexion; unusual motor pattern |
| Spider Curl | Face-down incline bench; OK but awkward progressive overload |
| 21s | Pump technique; not optimal for hypertrophy |

## TRICEPS Tier List

Triceps = 3 heads (long, lateral, medial). The **long head** (largest) is only fully stretched in overhead position. Most gym-goers overtrain the lateral head (pushdowns) and neglect the long head.

### S+-Tier

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| **Overhead Cable Triceps Extension (bar)** | Fully stretches the long head in overhead position; Nippard's #1 tricep exercise |

### S-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Barbell Skullcrusher** | High tension at stretch; heavy loading; long head dominant |

### A-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Tricep Pressdown (straight bar) | Best pressdown; lateral head; easy progressive overload |
| Overhead Cable Triceps Extension (rope) | Long head stretch; slightly less effective than bar |
| 1-Arm DB Overhead Extension | Long head isolation; unilateral; great for imbalances |
| DB Skullcrusher | Like barbell skull but unilateral and more ROM |
| Close-Grip Bench Press | Compound; heavy loading; all 3 heads; strength staple |
| Cable Triceps Kickback | Constant tension; peak contraction at top |

### B-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Tricep Pressdown (rope) | Less stable than bar; still effective |
| DB French Press | Overhead extension with DB; OK |
| JM Press | Hybrid between skullcrusher and close-grip bench |
| Close-Grip Dips | Body upright, elbows close; tricep-dominant version |
| Machine Dips | Safe, easy to load; decent for beginners |
| Diamond Pushups | Good bodyweight option; limited loading |

### C-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Reverse Grip Pressdown | Medial head; not worth prioritizing |
| Bench Dips | Shoulder impingement risk; limited ROM |
| Close-Grip Pushups | Beginner only |

### C-Tier (Bottom)

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| **DB Triceps Kickback** | Light weights only; momentum kills tension; replace with cable version |

---

## Programming Notes

**Optimal bicep session**: Bayesian curl or preacher curl (S+ or S tier) + incline DB curl or EZ curl (A tier)

**Optimal tricep session**: Overhead extension (S+ tier, long head) + pressdown or close-grip bench (A tier, lateral head)

**Key insight**: Most people overtrain the **lateral tricep head** (pressdowns) and undertrain the **long head** (overhead extensions). The long head is the largest — fix this imbalance.

**Volume targets**:
- Biceps: 10–15 sets/week (already partly trained by back work)
- Triceps: 10–15 sets/week (already partly trained by chest/shoulder pressing)
