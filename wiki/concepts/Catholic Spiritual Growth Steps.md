# Catholic Spiritual Growth Steps — Complete Guide

**What:** Master reference for all actions a Catholic can take to deepen their relationship with Jesus Christ, ranked by priority and evidence from the Catholic tradition.

---

## S-Tier — Absolute Foundation (Non-Negotiable)

These are not optional or "extra credit" — they are the objective channels Christ instituted.

| Practice | Frequency | Time | Why It's S-Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Holy Mass** | Every Sunday + Holy Days | ~60 min | Source and summit of Christian life (CCC 1324); receiving Christ in the Eucharist is the highest act of worship |
| **Confession (Reconciliation)** | Monthly (min: quarterly) | 15–30 min | Restores sanctifying grace; removes the weight of mortal sin; sacramental grace specifically heals the soul |
| **Daily Personal Prayer** | Every day | 15–30 min | Relationship requires communication; consistency > length; morning is ideal |

**Why start here:** These three practices are the objective backbone. Everything else amplifies them. A Catholic who does only these three will grow substantially. A Catholic who does everything *except* these will plateau.

---

## A-Tier — High-Leverage Daily Practices

Add one at a time until habitual before adding the next.

| Practice | Frequency | Time | How to Start |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lectio Divina** | Daily | 15–20 min | Open a Gospel. Read slowly. Pause on a word that strikes you (meditatio). Let it become a prayer (oratio). Sit silently with God (contemplatio). |
| **Daily Examen (Ignatian)** | Daily, before bed | 10 min | 5 steps: gratitude → review the day → where did I fall short → choose one moment to pray from → ask for tomorrow's grace |
| **The Rosary** | Daily or 3–4×/week | 20 min | Start with one decade (10 Hail Marys + 1 mystery). Work up to a full five-decade Rosary. |
| **Spiritual Reading** | Daily | 15 min | Begin with *The Imitation of Christ* (Book 1 first). Read slowly, not for information but for formation. |

**Stacking note:** Lectio Divina in the morning + Examen at night is the most powerful daily pair. Add the Rosary to a commute, walk, or chores.

## B-Tier — Weekly Practices That Accelerate Growth

| Practice | Frequency | Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Eucharistic Adoration** | Weekly | 30–60 min | Sit before the Blessed Sacrament (tabernacle or exposed Host). Silent listening. Many churches have perpetual adoration. |
| **Daily Mass** (beyond Sunday) | 1–2× per week | 30 min | Available most mornings at Catholic parishes; deepens the Eucharistic relationship |
| **Liturgy of the Hours** | Daily | 10–15 min | Morning Prayer (Lauds) and Evening Prayer (Vespers) are the most accessible; use the iBreviary or Universalis app |
| **Scripture Study** | Weekly | 30–60 min | Study the Sunday readings in advance (lectio + commentary); use a Catholic study Bible (RSV-2CE or NABRE) |

## C-Tier — Periodic Practices (Monthly/Annually)

| Practice | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Spiritual Direction** | Monthly | One-on-one with a trained director (priest, religious, or lay person); start by asking your parish priest |
| **Day of Recollection** | Quarterly | Half-day or full-day of silence, prayer, and Mass; often offered by parishes or religious houses |
| **Silent Retreat** | Annually | 3–8 day Ignatian retreat or other retreat format; the single most powerful periodic intervention |
| **Novenas** | As needed | 9-day prayer devotions; excellent for specific intentions; deepen intercessory prayer habit |

## D-Tier — Always-On Practices (Ascetical Life)

These run in the background of normal life and do not require dedicated time blocks.

| Practice | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Friday Fasting / Abstinence** | The Church requires abstaining from meat on Fridays (or another penance); full fasting on Ash Wednesday and Good Friday |
| **Works of Mercy** | Corporal: feed the hungry, clothe the naked, visit the sick. Spiritual: counsel the doubtful, instruct the ignorant, pray for the living and dead. Schedule at least one regular act monthly. |
| **Morning Offering** | 30-second prayer on waking offering the day to God; sets the intention for the day |
| **Grace Before Meals** | Brief prayer acknowledging dependence on God; resets attention throughout the day |
| **Parish/Small Group Community** | Join one Catholic small group, Bible study, or ministry; faith is not private |

## The Three Ways — Know Your Stage

The Doctors of the Church identify three stages of spiritual growth (non-linear, cyclical):

| Stage | Who | Main Practice | Key Challenge |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Purgative Way** | Beginners; anyone in serious sin | Breaking sinful habits; frequent Confession; basic prayer | Pride, discouragement, inconsistency |
| **Illuminative Way** | Progressing souls; stable in prayer | Lectio Divina, spiritual reading, virtue formation | Periods of aridity and dryness in prayer (normal!) |
| **Unitive Way** | Advanced; habitual union with God | Contemplative prayer; becoming an instrument of grace | Dark Night of the Soul (St. John of the Cross) |

Most people are in the Purgative or early Illuminative Way. Aridity (feeling nothing in prayer) is a sign of progress, not failure.

## Recommended Spiritual Reading Stack

| Priority | Book | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | *The Imitation of Christ* | Thomas à Kempis (c.1420) | Most-read Christian book after the Bible; radical Christ-centredness |
| 2nd | *Introduction to the Devout Life* | St. Francis de Sales (1609) | Specifically for laypeople in the world; gentle and comprehensive |
| 3rd | *Story of a Soul* (autobiography) | St. Thérèse of Lisieux (1898) | The "Little Way" — holiness through small daily acts of love |
| 4th | *The Interior Castle* | St. Teresa of Ávila (1577) | Map of the soul's journey to union with God; advanced |
| 5th | *The Dark Night of the Soul* | St. John of the Cross (1584) | Explains the painful stages of spiritual purification; for when prayer feels dead |

## Catholic Prayer Methods — Quick Reference

See [[Catholic Prayer Methods]] for full guides on each.

- **Lectio Divina** — Sacred reading; 4 movements: read → meditate → pray → contemplate
- **Ignatian Examen** — 5-step daily review of God's activity
- **Rosary** — 20 mysteries of Christ's life in 4 sets; ~20 min
- **Liturgy of the Hours** — Church's official daily prayer; Morning/Evening Prayer most accessible
- **Centering Prayer** — silent receptive prayer toward union with God (advanced)
- **Chaplet of Divine Mercy** — short chaplet on mercy; powerful for Confession prep

## Joe's Recommended Starting Stack (Catholic Beginner-to-Intermediate)

**Week 1–4 (Foundation):**
- Sunday Mass (already doing if Catholic)
- Go to Confession once
- Add 15 min morning prayer (start with one Psalm + spontaneous conversation with Jesus)

**Month 2–3 (Build):**
- Start Lectio Divina 3× per week using Sunday's Gospel readings
- Add the Examen before bed (use Hallow app)
- Begin *Imitation of Christ* — 1 chapter per day (they're short)

**Month 4–6 (Deepen):**
- Add the Rosary 3× per week
- Find an adoration chapel and go once a week
- Ask your parish priest about spiritual direction

**Month 6+ (Sustain and Grow):**
- Go on a silent day of recollection
- Join a parish small group or Bible study
- Begin *Introduction to the Devout Life*
