# Pinging System

[[Keith Ferrazzi]]'s framework for staying top-of-mind and maintaining relationships over time through low-effort, high-frequency contact.

> A ping is any small gesture that says: "I'm thinking of you." It does not require a meeting, a long email, or a favor. It only requires attention.

---

## What Is a Ping?

A ping is a lightweight touchpoint — intentionally low-effort so it is actually sustainable:
- A shared article relevant to their work or interests
- A birthday message (remembered or calendar-prompted)
- A brief "thinking of you" note after seeing something that reminded you of them
- An invitation to grab coffee or attend an event
- Congratulating them on a public achievement (promotion, publication, project launch)
- A question about something they mentioned in your last conversation

The ping does not need to be elaborate. The point is continuity — the relationship stays warm rather than going cold and requiring a "cold call" to restart.

---

## Contact Categorization

Ferrazzi organizes contacts into tiers that determine ping frequency:

| Tier | Category | Ping Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Close friends / key relationships | Weekly or ad hoc | These ping you back naturally |
| B | Warm professional contacts | Monthly | People you want to develop deeper ties with |
| C | Passive contacts | 2–4× per year | Keep the door open without over-investing |
| Aspirational | People you want to reach | Build up to; give first | Research them; find a reason to reach out |

The categories are fluid — people move between them based on relevance to current goals (see [[Relationship Action Plan]]).

## The Birthday Effect

Ferrazzi specifically calls out birthdays as the highest-leverage single ping. Why:
- Nearly everyone values being remembered on their birthday
- Very few people in a broad contact list actually remember or acknowledge it
- A birthday message stands out precisely because most people don't send one
- It requires zero context — the reason for contact is self-evident

**Implementation**: import contacts' birthdays into calendar. Send a personal note — not just a Facebook "HBD" — that references something specific about the person.

---

## Why Frequency Beats Intensity

A relationship maintained through 12 small pings per year is stronger than a relationship based on 1 intensive annual meeting. The cognitive mechanism:
- Repeated exposure increases familiarity and trust (the mere-exposure effect)
- Small gestures signal that you think of someone outside of when you need them
- People who ping consistently are perceived as reliable and thoughtful — not just active when they need something

This also applies to the [[Generosity-First Networking]] philosophy: pinging without an ask is pure giving. The person receiving it knows you're not after anything — you just remembered them.

---

## Implementation as a Habit

The Pinging System works best as a scheduled habit rather than an intention:

1. **Weekly scan**: 5 minutes on Sunday — is there anyone I haven't contacted in a while who I should reach out to?
2. **Birthday calendar**: set reminders 1 day in advance so you can send a personal note on the day
3. **Article/content pipeline**: when you read something interesting, immediately think "who in my network would find this useful?" and send it
4. **After any significant life event** of a contact: congratulations or check-in note within 48 hours

This converts pinging from an occasional good intention into a reliable system — which is what makes it effective. The identity frame: "I am someone who stays in touch." See [[Identity-Based vs Goal-Based Habits]].

---

## Relationship to Follow-Up

The [[Follow-Up Formula]] covers the critical first ping after an initial meeting (within 24 hours). The Pinging System covers ongoing maintenance after that baseline relationship is established. Both together form the full lifecycle:

1. **Meet** → 2. **Follow-Up Formula** (within 24h) → 3. **Pinging System** (ongoing)

Without the Pinging System, the follow-up creates a relationship that immediately begins to cool. Without the follow-up, pings never have a foundation to maintain.
