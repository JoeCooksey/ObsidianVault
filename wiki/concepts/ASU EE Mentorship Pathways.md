# ASU EE Mentorship Pathways

Quick-reference tier list for finding an electrical engineering mentor at Arizona State University. Joe-specific (Year 1, power electronics track, Livermore home base).

## Formal ASU Programs

| Program | Type | When | Access |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engineering Peer Mentor | Student → student | Day 1 (auto-assigned) | students.engineering.asu.edu/peer-mentors/ |
| Engineering Futures | Faculty/staff + financial aid | First-year, fall application | Pell Grant eligible only |
| FURI | Faculty research mentor | Sophomore+ | Apply per semester; $1,500 stipend |
| Barrett Alumni Mentorship | Alumni → student | Oct–March | Barrett students; barrettmentors@asu.edu |

## IEEE Pathways

- **IEEE ASU Branch** — 600+ members; weekly events; project sub-teams = organic mentor pathway; join at students.engineering.asu.edu/ieee/
- **IEEE Collabratec Mentor Program** — global industry mentors; self-select by criteria; requires IEEE student membership (~$32/yr); activate at sites.google.com/ieee.org/ieee-collabratec-info-pages/mentoring-program
- **IEEE PELS** — Power Electronics Society; 6-month mentor pairings; mentorship roundtables at APEC conference annually

## Cold Email Formula (Faculty)

1. Read 1–2 papers from their group first
2. Write 7–12 sentences: who you are → what you liked specifically → what you offer → request 15-min meeting or office hours visit
3. One email at a time; wait 7 business days before following up
4. No generic emails — personalization is everything

## Year 1 Action Stack

1. Week 1 — attend one IEEE ASU event, introduce yourself
2. Week 1 — contact assigned peer mentor
3. Month 1 — activate IEEE membership + Collabratec Mentee profile
4. Month 2–3 — visit Ranjram or Ayyanar office hours with a prepared question
5. Year 2 (Spring) — FURI application targeting MAPEL or Ayyanar lab
