# Chess Rating Roadmap

Phase-by-phase breakdown of what to study, what to expect, and which resources to use at each chess rating level. Ratings are approximately Chess.com/Lichess Rapid equivalents.

---

## Phase 0 — Raw Beginner (Unrated → 400)

**Goal:** Learn the rules completely, avoid instantly losing pieces.

**What to focus on:**
- Piece movements, values, special moves (castling, en passant, promotion)
- The concept of checkmate — recognize it, don't fall into it
- Never leave pieces hanging undefended

**Resources:**
- Chess.com Learn section (free interactive lessons)
- Lichess "Learn from your mistakes" tool
- *Chess Fundamentals* by Capablanca (free online — timeless beginner text)

**Signs you're ready for Phase 1:** You understand why you lost most games (hung a piece, missed a one-move threat)

---

## Phase 1 — Beginner (400–800)

**Goal:** Stop losing material for free; see one-move threats.

**Primary focus: Tactics (70%)**
- One-move threats and responses
- Forks, pins, skewers, simple back-rank mates
- Hanging pieces (yours and opponent's)

**Secondary: Opening Principles (20%)**
- Memorize 0 opening lines; learn the 5 principles
- Control center, develop pieces, castle early

**Endgames (10%):** K+Q vs K (can you mate?), K+R vs K (can you mate?)

**Study plan:** 10–15 tactics puzzles/day + play 2–3 games per week (15+ min time control)

**Key resources:**
- Chess.com "Learn" basics + Puzzle Trainer (free)
- Lichess Puzzles (free)
- YouTube: Daniel Naroditsky's "Speed Run" series (2.7M views — best beginner resource)

---

## Phase 2 — Novice (800–1200)

**Goal:** Recognize tactical patterns consistently; build a real opening system.

**Primary focus: Tactics (65%)**
- Multi-move tactics: discovered attacks, double check, overloading, deflection
- Pattern library: at least 10 core motifs recognized on sight
- Spaced repetition: review puzzles you've gotten wrong

**Opening (20%):**
- Choose your three openings (one as White, two as Black)
- Learn 4–6 moves + the *ideas* behind them
- Recommended: Italian Game/London (White), Caro-Kann or French (Black vs e4), QGD or KID (Black vs d4)

**Endgames (15%):**
- King and Pawn vs King — opposition, key squares, the square rule
- Basic K+R vs K checkmate

**Study plan:** 15–20 puzzles/day + analyze 1–2 games/week + play 3 long games/week

**Key resource:** Yasser Seirawan's *Winning Chess Tactics* + *Winning Chess Openings* (both ~$15, extremely clear)

---

## Phase 3 — Club Player (1200–1600)

**Goal:** Develop positional understanding; stop tactical blunders reliably.

**Primary focus: Tactics (50%)** — deeper puzzles, 2–4 move combinations

**Strategy/Middlegame (20%):**
- Silman's concept of "imbalances" — what's different between the two positions?
- Pawn structure analysis: isolated pawns, doubled pawns, passed pawns
- Piece activity: good vs. bad bishops, outpost squares for knights
- Plan creation: "what am I trying to achieve in the next 5 moves?"

**Opening (20%):** Deepen your repertoire — learn the main plans in your systems

**Endgames (10%):**
- Lucena position (K+R+P vs K+R — winning technique)
- Philidor position (K+R vs K+R+P — drawing technique)
- Rook behind passed pawn (Tarrasch Rule)

**Study plan:** 20 puzzles/day + analyze every slow game + 30-min weekly endgame study session

**Key books:**
- *How to Reassess Your Chess* — Jeremy Silman (imbalance framework, transformative for this level)
- *Silman's Complete Endgame Course* — read chapters for 1200–1600 players
- Yusupov 9-book series, Volume 1 ("Build Up Your Chess")

---

## Phase 4 — Intermediate (1600–2000)

**Goal:** Win clearly won endgames; exploit positional advantages.

**Positional Play (25%):**
- Prophylaxis — prevent opponent's plans before they happen
- Weak squares, outpost creation, piece coordination
- Conversion technique — how to convert a small advantage into a win

**Tactics (35%):** Complex multi-move problems; defensive tactics

**Endgames (15%):**
- K+P endgame theory deeply (corresponding squares, triangulation, breakthrough)
- Rook endgame theory (Philidor/Lucena from both sides, rook activity)

**Opening (25%):** Build secondary lines; understand transpositions

**Study plan:** 20 min tactics + 30 min positional/strategy + analyze all serious games

**Key books:**
- Yusupov series Volumes 4–6 ("Boost Your Chess")
- *My System* — Nimzowitsch (positional chess bible)
- *Dvoretsky's Endgame Manual* (start with pawn and rook chapters)

---

## Phase 5 — Advanced (2000+)

**Goal:** Deep preparation, calculation training, opening repertoire with original ideas.

**Endgames (25%):** All theoretical positions; conversion technique

**Calculation (25%):** Solve positions in head without moving pieces; Kotov tree-of-analysis method

**Openings (20%):** Deep repertoire with understanding of pawn structure novelties

**Tactics/Combinations (20%):** Complex sacrificial attacks; defensive combinations

**Study plan:** Structured coach sessions or Chess Dojo program; work through Dvoretsky systematically

**Key books:**
- *Dvoretsky's Endgame Manual* (complete)
- *Zurich 1953* — Bronstein (best tournament book for positional ideas)
- *Chess Training Pocket Book* — Alburt (300 positions essential at master level)

## Platform Comparison

| Platform | Cost | Best For |
|---------|------|----------|
| **Chess.com** | Free / ~$8/mo premium | Puzzles, lessons, playing, analysis |
| **Lichess** | 100% free | Puzzles, playing, analysis, no ads |
| **Chessable** | Free basics / $7–15/course | Opening memorization (spaced repetition) |
| **ChessMood** | ~$29/mo | Structured improvement courses (GM-level) |
| **Chess Dojo** | Free / $10/mo | Systematic training programs by rating |

**Recommendation for Joe:** Chess.com free + Lichess free + Chessable free basics covers 95% of needs up to 1800.
