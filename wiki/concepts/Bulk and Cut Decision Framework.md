# Bulk and Cut Decision Framework

The structured approach to cycling between caloric surplus (bulk) and caloric deficit (cut) to maximize muscle gain while controlling body fat.

---

## Core Concepts

**Bulking** — sustained caloric surplus to maximize muscle protein synthesis and muscle gain. Inevitably gains some fat alongside muscle.

**Cutting** — sustained caloric deficit to lose body fat while preserving lean mass. Requires high protein + continued strength training.

**Body Recomposition** — eating at or near maintenance while simultaneously gaining muscle and losing fat. Most effective for beginners and those with elevated BF%.

---

## Decision Rule: Use Body Fat %, Not BMI or Scale Weight

| BF% (Males) | Action |
|---|---|
| <10% | Lean bulk |
| 10–15% | Lean bulk or recomp |
| 15–20% | Recomp (beginners) or cut → then bulk |
| >20% | Cut to 12–15% first |

**Why BF% matters:** High body fat impairs insulin sensitivity and shifts the P-ratio (the ratio of muscle-to-fat gained during a surplus) unfavorably. Above ~20% BF, bulking generates disproportionately more fat than muscle.

---

## Lean Bulk Protocol
- **Surplus:** +200–300 cal/day above TDEE
- **Weight gain rate:** 0.25–0.5 lb/week (1–2 lbs/month max for beginners)
- **Protein:** 1.6–2.2 g/kg bodyweight
- **Duration:** 3–6 months, then reassess BF%
- **Stop when:** BF% reaches ~18–20% — transition to cut

**The math on bigger surpluses:** At an intermediate level, a 1,000 cal/day surplus generates 7.5 lbs of fat for every 1.5 lbs of muscle — 5:1 ratio. A 300 cal surplus at beginner level achieves closer to 1:1.

## Cut Protocol
- **Deficit:** 300–500 cal/day below TDEE
- **Weight loss rate:** 0.5–1% bodyweight/week (for 156 lb male: 0.75–1.5 lbs/week)
- **Protein:** 1.8–2.2 g/kg — HIGHER than during a bulk to prevent muscle catabolism
- **Maintain strength training:** mandatory to preserve lean mass during deficit
- **Stop when:** BF% reaches ~12–15% — transition to lean bulk

## Body Recomposition Protocol
- **Calories:** Maintenance (TDEE ± 100–200 cal)
- **Protein:** 1.6–2.2 g/kg
- **Progressive overload required:** muscle gain at maintenance requires training stimulus
- **Best for:** beginners (<2 years training), those returning after a break, anyone 15–25% BF
- **Track:** waist circumference + progress photos + strength — NOT scale weight

---

## Typical Bulk/Cut Cycle
1. **Lean bulk** from ~12% → ~18% BF (3–6 months)
2. **Cut** from ~18% → ~12% BF (2–4 months)
3. Repeat — each cycle adds net lean mass at higher baseline

Net muscle gain per cycle varies by training age:
- Beginner: 8–15 lbs/cycle
- Intermediate: 3–8 lbs/cycle
- Advanced: 1–3 lbs/cycle

## Key Insight
**Beginners should not obsess over bulk/cut cycles.** The most important lever is: (1) consistent training with progressive overload, and (2) adequate protein. Dietary phase cycling matters more as training age increases and newbie gains plateau.
