# Exercise Tier List — Shoulders

Based on Jeff Nippard's shoulder exercise rankings (September 2024). Shoulders = three distinct heads requiring separate exercise selection.

---

## Shoulder Anatomy for Programming

| Head | Function | Best Exercises |
|---|---|---|
| **Anterior (front) delts** | Shoulder flexion / horizontal press | Already trained by pressing movements |
| **Lateral (side) delts** | Shoulder abduction — VISUAL WIDTH | Lateral raises (cable > dumbbell) |
| **Rear (posterior) delts** | Horizontal abduction / health | Reverse flyes, face pulls |

> Anterior delts are overtrained by most people (every pressing movement). Focus on lateral and rear delts.

---

## OVERALL SHOULDERS Tier List

### S-Tier — Gold Standard

| Exercise | Head | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Cable Lateral Raise** | Side delts | Nippard's #1 for side delts — constant tension, easy progressive overload, no momentum |
| **Reverse Cable Crossover** | Rear delts | Nippard's #1 for rear delts — cross-body line of pull maximizes rear delt activation |
| **Cable Y-Raise** | Lower trap + rear delt | Corrects most posture issues; extremely effective |
| **Behind-the-Back Cable Lateral Raise** | Side delts | Best stretch position for lateral delts |

### A+-Tier — Excellent Primary Lifts

| Exercise | Head | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Machine Shoulder Press** | Front + side delts | Best press for hypertrophy: fixed path, easy progressive overload, no balance tax |
| **Atlantis Standing Machine Lat Raise** | Side delts | Best machine lateral raise |

### A-Tier — Primary Movements

| Exercise | Head | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lean-Away DB Lateral Raise | Side delts | Body angle increases stretch at bottom |
| Rope Face Pulls | Rear delt + external rotation | Essential for shoulder health; pairs with all pressing |
| Seated DB Overhead Press | Front + side | Good strength base; balance requirement |
| Arnold Press | Front + side | Full ROM rotation; moderate side delt emphasis |

### B+-Tier

| Exercise | Head | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Standing Barbell Overhead Press (OHP)** | Front + side | Excellent for overall strength; lower pure hypertrophy efficiency vs machine |

### B-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Standing DB Lateral Raise | Classic; momentum makes it less effective than cable version |
| Bent-Over Reverse DB Flye | Good rear delt but harder to load progressively than cable |
| Seated Machine Lateral Raise | Good option; less range than cable |
| Upright Row | Side + trap; wrist and shoulder joint stress for some people |

### C-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Banded Lateral Raise | Ascending resistance curve — OK if cables unavailable |

### D-Tier — Avoid

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| **Front Raise** | Anterior delts are already overtrained by pressing — front raises are redundant for almost everyone |
| Behind-the-Neck Press | High impingement risk; no advantage over standard OHP |

---

## Programming Notes

- **Standard shoulder session structure**: Machine press + cable lateral raise + reverse cable crossover/face pull
- **Front delts**: Do NOT need direct training if you bench press and overhead press
- **Lateral delts** are the key to visual shoulder width — prioritize cables over dumbbells
- **Rear delts**: Train 2× per week minimum for posture health and balanced development
- Face pulls are one of the most important injury prevention exercises in existence

---

## Lateral Raise Cable Technique

1. Stand sideways to cable, anchor at hip height
2. Slight body lean away from cable (increases stretch)
3. Raise to shoulder height — lead with elbow, not hand
4. Pause briefly at top
5. Controlled eccentric (2–3 sec down)
