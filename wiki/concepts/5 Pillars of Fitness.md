# 5 Pillars of Fitness

The ACSM (American College of Sports Medicine) defines five components that together constitute complete physical fitness. No single pillar is sufficient alone — ignoring any one creates imbalances, elevates injury risk, and caps performance.

> Note: Peter Attia's *Outlive* framework uses 4 pillars (Stability, Strength, Zone 2, VO2 Max) with Stability as the foundation beneath all others. The synthesis below incorporates his contribution as a 6th lens for longevity.

---

## The 5 Pillars

### 1. Cardiovascular Endurance
The heart and lungs' ability to deliver oxygen to working muscles sustained over time.

- **Primary metric**: VO2 max (mL/kg/min) — the single strongest longevity predictor
- **Secondary metric**: Lactate threshold (the pace you can sustain without accumulating lactate)
- **Training tools**: Zone 2 steady-state (3+ hrs/week), VO2 max intervals (4×4 min @ max)
- **ACSM minimum**: 150 min moderate or 75 min vigorous exercise/week
- **Two layers to develop**: aerobic base (Zone 1–2) first, VO2 max (Zone 4–5) second
- **Seasonal priority**: Fall/Winter for base; Spring for VO2 max blocks

---

### 2. Muscular Strength
Maximum force a muscle group can produce in a single effort (1RM).

- **Primary metric**: 1-rep max on compound lifts (squat, deadlift, bench, overhead press)
- **Training zone**: 80–95% 1RM, 1–5 reps, 3–6 sets, long rest (3–5 min)
- **Why it matters**: directly correlated with all-cause mortality, metabolic health, bone density
- **Builds on**: muscular endurance phase must precede heavy strength loading
- **Seasonal priority**: Winter (peak strength phase after hypertrophy buildup)

---

### 3. Muscular Endurance
A muscle group's ability to sustain repeated contractions against resistance over time.

- **Primary metric**: max reps at a given % of 1RM (e.g., reps at 60% 1RM)
- **Training zone**: 50–70% 1RM, 15–25 reps, 3–6 sets, moderate rest (60–90 sec)
- **Why it matters**: joint tolerance, lactate buffering, foundational for strength and cardio crossover
- **Often neglected**: most programs skip straight to hypertrophy — this is a periodization mistake
- **Seasonal priority**: Fall (early preparatory phase, before heavier loading)

---

### 4. Flexibility and Mobility
**Flexibility** = passive range of motion at a joint (tissue property, slow to change — months).
**Mobility** = active range of motion under load (neuromuscular, faster to change — weeks).

- **Primary metric**: functional movement screen (FMS), specific joint ROM assessments
- **Training tools**: dynamic warm-up, static stretching post-workout, dedicated mobility sessions
- **Protocol**: 10–20 min daily; dynamic before training, static after
- **Not a seasonal pillar** — needs year-round attention; emphasis increases in Winter when training volume is lower
- **Seasonal priority**: consistent year-round; dedicated sessions in Winter/Transition

---

### 5. Body Composition
Ratio of lean mass (muscle, bone, organs) to fat mass. Body fat % is the practical metric.

- **Primary metric**: body fat % (DEXA is gold standard; body tape + visual = practical)
- **Healthy ranges** (men): 10–20% general; <15% performance; <10% competitive
- **Drives**: hormonal health (testosterone, insulin sensitivity), performance, longevity
- **Not trainable in isolation** — emerges from the interaction of the other 4 pillars + nutrition
- **Seasonal rhythm**: lean bulk fall/winter → cut spring → maintain/display summer

---

## Attia's Missing Pillar: Stability

Peter Attia argues the ACSM framework omits the most underrated component:

**Stability** = the ability to create and dissipate force safely across joints; balance, single-leg control, rotational control, spinal loading patterns.

- It is the foundation beneath strength and power, not a separate goal
- Without it, heavy strength work leads to injury, not gains
- Train it **in every phase** with 5–10 min blocks before other workouts (e.g., McGill Big 3, single-leg work, Copenhagen planks)

## Pillar Priority by Goal

| Goal | Priority Stack |
|------|----------------|
| Longevity / healthspan | Stability → Zone 2 → VO2 Max → Strength → Flexibility |
| Athletic performance | Cardio Endurance → Muscular Strength → Muscular Endurance → Stability → Flexibility |
| Aesthetics | Body Composition → Muscular Strength → Muscular Endurance → Cardio → Flexibility |
| General fitness | All 5 equally — see [[Seasonal Fitness Periodization]] for annual balance |

---

## Weekly Minimum to Hit All 5 (General Fitness, Non-Athlete)

| Pillar | Minimum Weekly Dose |
|--------|-------------------|
| Cardiovascular endurance | 3 hrs Zone 2 + 1 VO2 max session |
| Muscular strength | 2–3 full-body sessions (compound lifts) |
| Muscular endurance | Built into strength sessions via reps or dedicated circuits |
| Flexibility/mobility | 10–20 min daily (dynamic AM, static PM) |
| Body composition | Nutrition (cannot be outrun by training alone) |
| Stability | 5–10 min before each session |
