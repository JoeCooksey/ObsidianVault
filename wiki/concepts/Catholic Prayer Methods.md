# Catholic Prayer Methods

**What:** The main structured prayer methods in the Catholic tradition for deepening personal relationship with Jesus Christ.

---

## Lectio Divina (Divine Reading)

Latin for "divine reading." Sacred reading of Scripture as a form of prayer and dialogue with God.

**The 4 Movements:**
1. **Lectio (Read)** — Read a short passage slowly, aloud if possible. Pause when a word or phrase strikes you.
2. **Meditatio (Meditate)** — Ruminate on that word or phrase. Let it interact with your life, memories, and desires.
3. **Oratio (Pray)** — Let the meditation become a personal prayer. Speak to God from what arose.
4. **Contemplatio (Contemplate)** — Rest silently in God's presence. Receive rather than speak.

**Best source:** Sunday Gospel readings are an excellent starting point (available at usccb.org/bible/readings).

**Time:** 15–20 min. Shorter is fine — one verse done well > whole chapter skimmed.

---

## The Ignatian Daily Examen

Developed by St. Ignatius of Loyola (1491–1556). A 10–15 minute evening prayer review of the day to detect God's presence and discern His direction.

**The 5 Steps:**
1. **Become Aware of God's Presence** — Settle into the awareness that you are in God's presence.
2. **Review the Day with Gratitude** — Walk through your day looking for gifts, joy, moments of grace.
3. **Examine Areas of Concern** — Where did you fall short? Not with guilt but with honest compassion.
4. **Choose One Feature to Pray From** — The Holy Spirit highlights one moment; bring it to prayer.
5. **Look Toward Tomorrow** — Ask God for light and grace for the next day's challenges.

**App:** Hallow app has excellent guided Examen sessions.

## The Rosary

A meditative prayer of 20 mysteries (scenes from Christ's life) prayed through 5 decades of Hail Marys.

**The 4 Sets of Mysteries:**
- **Joyful Mysteries** (Monday, Saturday) — Annunciation, Visitation, Nativity, Presentation, Finding in the Temple
- **Sorrowful Mysteries** (Tuesday, Friday) — Agony, Scourging, Crowning with Thorns, Carrying the Cross, Crucifixion
- **Glorious Mysteries** (Wednesday, Sunday) — Resurrection, Ascension, Pentecost, Assumption, Coronation
- **Luminous Mysteries** (Thursday) — Baptism, Wedding at Cana, Proclamation of Kingdom, Transfiguration, Institution of Eucharist

**How to pray it:** Hold each mystery in mind while saying the prayers. The prayers are the vehicle; the mysteries are the meditation. Distraction is normal — gently return to the mystery.

**Time:** ~20 min for a full 5-decade Rosary. One decade = 2–3 min.

## Liturgy of the Hours (Divine Office)

The Church's official daily prayer, structured around 7 "hours" throughout the day. Not just for priests and religious — Vatican II encouraged all laypeople to pray it.

**Most Accessible Hours:**
- **Morning Prayer (Lauds)** — ~10–15 min; Psalms + canticle + intercessions
- **Evening Prayer (Vespers)** — ~10–15 min; Psalms + Magnificat + intercessions

**Apps:** iBreviary, Universalis, or Magnificat magazine.

---

## Eucharistic Adoration

Sitting in silent prayer before the Blessed Sacrament (the Eucharist), either reserved in the tabernacle or exposed in a monstrance.

**Why it works:** The Eucharist is the Real Presence of Christ. Adoration is not "doing" but "being with." Bring your thoughts, worries, and joys. Read Scripture. Journal. Sit in silence. No agenda needed.

**How to find it:** Most Catholic parishes have regular adoration times; many have perpetual adoration chapels. Search "perpetual adoration [your city]."

---

## Centering Prayer

A contemplative method of resting silently in God's presence, developed from the Desert Fathers and popularized by Fr. Thomas Keating in the 20th century.

**Method:** Choose a sacred word (e.g., "Jesus," "Love," "Come"). Sit silently for 20 min. When thoughts arise, return gently to the sacred word. The goal is receptivity, not thought.

**Note:** This is an advanced practice best used after a foundation in Lectio Divina is established. It builds toward the Unitive Way.

---

## Morning Offering

A 30-second prayer on waking that consecrates the entire day to God. One of the most powerful daily habits because it reframes every subsequent action as an act of worship.

**Simple form:** "O Jesus, I offer You all my prayers, works, joys, and sufferings of this day in union with the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass throughout the world."
