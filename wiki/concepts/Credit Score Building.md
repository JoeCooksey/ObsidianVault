# Credit Score Building

> How credit scores work, why they matter, and the fastest path from 0 to 750+ as a young adult.

## Why Credit Score Matters

A good credit score (700+) unlocks:
- **Apartment rentals** — most landlords require 650+ and may deny or require large deposits otherwise
- **Car loans** — the difference between 4.5% (750+ score) and 15%+ (no/poor credit) on a $20k car = ~$5,000 extra paid in interest
- **Future mortgage** — on a $350k mortgage, 760+ vs. 620 score can mean 1–2% lower rate = $75,000+ saved over 30 years
- **Employer background checks** — some employers in finance/government check credit as part of hiring
- **Insurance rates** — in most states, credit-based insurance scores affect car and home insurance premiums

**At 19 with no credit history:** you likely have no FICO score at all (not a bad score — just no score). It takes about 6 months of credit activity to generate your first FICO score.

---

## How FICO Score Is Calculated

| Factor | Weight | What Moves It |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Payment History | **35%** | On-time vs. late payments; collections; bankruptcies |
| Credit Utilization | **30%** | Balance / credit limit ratio; lower is better |
| Length of Credit History | **15%** | Average age of all accounts; age of oldest account |
| Credit Mix | **10%** | Having both revolving (cards) and installment (loans) credit |
| New Credit | **10%** | Recent hard inquiries; new accounts opened |

**Implication:** Payment history + utilization = 65% of your score. Pay on time and keep utilization below 10% and you'll have a 750+ score within 2–3 years regardless of everything else.

## Building Credit from Zero: The 4-Step Path

### Step 1 — Get a Secured Credit Card
A secured card requires a cash deposit (usually $200–$500) that becomes your credit limit. This eliminates risk to the lender, making approval near-universal even with no credit history.

**Best options for beginners:**
- **Discover it Secured** — refundable deposit, cash back rewards, automatic review to upgrade to unsecured after 7 months; no annual fee
- **Capital One Secured Mastercard** — $49/$99/$200 deposit, no annual fee; widely accepted
- **Citi Secured Mastercard** — no annual fee; reports to all 3 bureaus

**How to use it:**
- Assign one small recurring subscription (Spotify $11/month, YouTube Premium)
- Pay the full statement balance every month before the due date
- Never carry a balance; never pay interest
- Keep utilization below 10% of limit (on $200 limit: keep balance under $20)

---

### Step 2 — Become an Authorized User
Ask a parent or trusted family member with good credit history (7+ years, clean payment record) to add you as an authorized user on one of their oldest, best-managed credit cards.

- Their account history appears on your credit report immediately
- You do not need to use the card or even receive a physical card
- Single highest-leverage action for quickly generating a score
- No credit risk to the primary cardholder

---

### Step 3 — Get a Student Credit Card (at 6–12 months)
After 6+ months with the secured card and a clean record, apply for one student card or regular cash-back card.

**Good options:**
- **Discover it Student Cash Back** — 5% rotating categories + 2% restaurants; no annual fee; first year cash back matched
- **Chase Freedom Student** — 1% cash back; $20 credit for on-time payments; no annual fee
- **Capital One Savor Student** — 3% dining, entertainment; no annual fee

**Do not open more than 1–2 cards in a year** — each application is a hard inquiry that temporarily drops score 5–10 points.

---

### Step 4 — Automate and Ignore
- Set all credit cards to autopay **full statement balance** every month
- Never miss a payment — a single 30-day late payment can drop score 100+ points and stays on report for 7 years
- Leave old cards open even if unused — closing them reduces average account age and total available credit (both hurt score)
- Check credit for free at AnnualCreditReport.com (once/year free from each bureau) or Credit Karma (free, ongoing)

## Credit Score Milestones by Age

| Age | Realistic Target | What It Unlocks |
|-----|-----------------|-----------------|
| 19 | First FICO score generated (580–650) | Basic credit access |
| 21 | 680–720 | Most apartment rentals; good auto loan rates |
| 23 | 720–750 | Premium credit cards; best auto loan rates |
| 25 | 750–800 | Excellent mortgage rates; no deposits required |

## Utilization Strategy (The Biggest Lever)

Credit utilization = (total balances ÷ total credit limits) × 100

- **Under 30%** = generally good
- **Under 10%** = excellent; this is the score-maximizing target
- **0%** (never use card) = CAN actually slightly hurt score; cards may be closed for inactivity

**Hack for ultra-low utilization:** Pay the balance before the statement closes (not just before due date). Utilization is calculated on the statement balance, not the payment. Paying before close date shows 0% on report.

---

## What to Avoid

| Action | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Missing a single payment | −50 to −100 points; stays on report 7 years |
| Maxing out a credit card | High utilization crushes score; can drop 100+ points |
| Applying for 5 cards at once | Multiple hard inquiries = red flag to lenders; temporary 30–50 point drop |
| Closing old accounts | Reduces available credit + average account age |
| Co-signing loans for others | Their late payments become your problem |
| Payday loans | Extremely high rates; do not touch |

---

## Credit vs. Debt: The Key Distinction

**Good use of credit:** Use a credit card for normal purchases you already budgeted for; pay in full every month. This earns cash back or points, builds credit history, and costs you zero in interest.

**Bad use of credit:** Spend money you don't have, carry a balance, pay 20–27% interest. This is wealth destruction.

The goal of building credit is not to go into debt — it is to demonstrate to future lenders that you are a trustworthy borrower, unlocking better rates and terms when you actually need them (mortgage, car, business loan).
