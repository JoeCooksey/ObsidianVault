# Biohacking Health Products Protocol — Skin, Hair, Oral & Body

Evidence-based product guide organized into four domains: **health/body supplements**, **skincare**, **haircare**, and **oral care**. Each section includes a ranked product tier, specific brands, doses, and a step-by-step daily/weekly protocol. Products are rated by evidence quality × effect size × cost-benefit.

> [!warning] This is educational, not medical advice. Consult a physician before starting finasteride, prescription retinoids, or any pharmaceutical-grade intervention.

## Part 2 — Optimal Skincare Protocol

### The Foundation (Non-Negotiable)

UV exposure causes **up to 80% of visible facial aging**. The biggest skincare lever is not a serum — it's daily SPF 50 and a consistent sleep schedule. Dermatologist consensus: **do less, consistently**.

### Evidence-Based Active Ingredients (Ranked)

| Rank | Ingredient | What It Does | Evidence |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **1** | Retinoids (retinol / tretinoin) | Accelerates cell turnover, stimulates collagen, reduces fine lines, fades hyperpigmentation | Gold standard; >40 years of RCT data |
| **2** | Broad-spectrum SPF 50 sunscreen | Prevents UV-induced aging, photocarcinogenesis | Causally proven; non-negotiable |
| **3** | Niacinamide (Vitamin B3) | Regulates oil, calms inflammation, evens tone, strengthens skin barrier | Multiple RCTs; well-tolerated |
| **4** | Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid 10–20%) | Antioxidant; brightens, fades dark spots, boosts collagen when used with SPF | Strong clinical evidence |
| **5** | Copper Peptides | Tissue regeneration, firming; top-tier 2026 collagen stimulator | Emerging, strong mechanism |
| **6** | Ceramides + Hyaluronic Acid | Barrier repair, hydration | Multiple RCTs; excellent tolerability |

### Recommended Products (Specific Brands)

**Cleansers:**
- **CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser** — ceramide-based, SLS-free, excellent for daily use (~$15)
- **La Roche-Posay Toleriane Purifying Foaming Wash** — oily/combo skin; gentle; dermatologist #1 pharmacy pick

**Sunscreens (SPF 50, daily):**
- **EltaMD UV Clear SPF 46** — niacinamide + zinc; no white cast; acne-safe; dermatologist top pick
- **La Roche-Posay Anthelios Melt-in Milk SPF 60** — chemical broad-spectrum; elegant texture
- **Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun SPF 50+** — best for minimal cast; excellent daily wear

**Retinoids (apply PM only, start 2×/week):**
- **Differin Gel (adapalene 0.1%)** — OTC retinoid; FDA-approved; less irritating than tretinoin; $13; best starting point
- **Tretinoin 0.025–0.05%** — prescription only; most potent; 40+ years of data; ramp up slowly over 8–12 weeks
- **The Ordinary Retinol 0.2–1% in Squalane** — accessible entry-level OTC retinol progression

**Vitamin C Serums (apply AM before SPF):**
- **TruSkin Vitamin C Serum** — 15% L-ascorbic acid; budget-friendly; ~$20
- **SkinCeuticals C E Ferulic** — gold-standard formula; 15% vitamin C + vitamin E + ferulic acid; $180 (high-end benchmark)
- **Timeless 20% Vitamin C + E + Ferulic** — near-identical formula to SkinCeuticals at $25; best value pick

**Moisturizers:**
- **CeraVe Moisturizing Cream** — ceramides + hyaluronic acid; barrier repair; ~$18
- **La Roche-Posay Cicaplast Baume B5** — sensitive skin, post-procedure; panthenol + shea butter

**Copper Peptides (advanced):**
- **NIOD Copper Amino Isolate Serum 2:1** — top formulation; alternates with retinol on separate nights

### Daily Skincare Protocol

**Morning:**
1. Gentle cleanser (or water rinse only if no sweat)
2. Vitamin C serum (5–10 drops; let absorb 30 sec)
3. Moisturizer (CeraVe or equivalent)
4. SPF 50 sunscreen — **never skip this step**
5. Reapply SPF every 2 hours if outdoors

**Evening:**
1. Double cleanse if sunscreen/heavy product used (oil cleanser → water cleanser)
2. Niacinamide serum OR copper peptide serum (not same night as tretinoin)
3. Retinoid (2× per week to start; ramp to nightly over 8–12 weeks; apply to dry skin)
4. Moisturizer (layer over retinoid to reduce irritation)

**Weekly:**
- Gentle chemical exfoliant 1×/week (e.g., The Ordinary AHA 30% + BHA 2% Peeling Solution or Paula's Choice 2% BHA) — only when not using tretinoin on same days

**Protocol Notes:**
- Do NOT layer vitamin C and retinol on the same application — vitamin C AM, retinol PM
- SPF is required every morning regardless of weather or being indoors near windows
- Retinoid purging (breakouts weeks 2–6) is normal — do not stop; it clears by week 10–12
- Skin barrier repair takes 4 weeks minimum; results from retinoids take 12 weeks minimum

## Part 3 — Optimal Hair Care Protocol

### The Evidence Hierarchy

Hair loss (androgenetic alopecia) is DHT-driven in genetically susceptible follicles. The most effective interventions block DHT either systemically or at the follicle, or directly stimulate follicle blood supply. **The earlier you start, the better the result** — these treatments preserve hair, not always regrow it.

### Ranked Interventions by Evidence

| Tier | Intervention | Mechanism | Effect Size |
|------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **S** | **Finasteride 1 mg/day** (oral) | Inhibits 5α-reductase type II; reduces scalp DHT by ~70%; FDA-approved | Stops loss in >80% of men; regrowth in ~65% at 2 years |
| **S** | **Minoxidil 5% topical** (1 mL 2×/day) or **2.5 mg oral** | Vasodilator; prolongs anagen phase; stimulates follicle blood supply; FDA-approved | Effective alone; superior when combined with finasteride |
| **A** | **Finasteride + Minoxidil combined** | Complementary mechanisms; additive and possibly synergistic | Highest efficacy in most comparative studies |
| **A** | **Ketoconazole 2% shampoo** (2–3×/week) | Anti-androgenic + antifungal; reduces scalp inflammation; adjuvant | 40-week study: hair density ↑ comparable to minoxidil 2% alone |
| **B** | **Pumpkin Seed Oil** (400 mg/day oral) | Inhibits 5α-reductase; 40% hair count increase vs. 10% placebo (24-week RCT) | Moderate; good for those who cannot use finasteride |
| **B** | **Saw Palmetto** (320 mg/day standardized) | Inhibits type II 5α-reductase; 25% increased hair density at 24 weeks | Weaker than finasteride but OTC and side-effect profile is mild |
| **B** | **Low-Level Laser Therapy (LLLT)** (e.g., iRestore, Capillus) | Photobiomodulation; stimulates follicle activity; FDA-cleared for hair growth | Multiple RCTs showing 20–40% hair count increase; requires consistency |
| **B** | **Caffeine Topical Shampoo** (e.g., Alpecin) | Penetrates follicle; inhibits testosterone-induced suppression of hair root keratinocytes | In-vitro strong; human RCT data limited but positive |
| **C** | **Biotin** (>2,500 mcg/day) | Only beneficial if biotin-deficient (rare); does not benefit those with adequate biotin | No benefit in normal individuals; popular but over-marketed |
| **D** | **Most DHT-blocking shampoos** (non-ketoconazole) | Inadequate penetration; DHT effect not replicated in robust RCTs | Weak/no evidence for most; Nizoral (ketoconazole) is the exception |

### Recommended Products (Specific Brands)

**Prescription (consult physician):**
- **Finasteride 1 mg** (Propecia or generic) — generic is ~$10–20/month; Hims or Keeps telehealth for convenience
- **Oral Minoxidil 2.5 mg** — off-label but increasingly prescribed; high compliance vs. topical; requires physician
- **Compound topical** (some compounding pharmacies offer Minoxidil 5%/Finasteride 0.25%/Ketoconazole 2%) — highest convenience; single application

**OTC Topical:**
- **Rogaine Men's 5% Foam** (minoxidil) — gold standard OTC; $25–35/month; apply 1 mL 2×/day to dry scalp
- **Keeps 5% Minoxidil Solution** — identical active ingredient, lower cost

**Shampoo:**
- **Nizoral 2% Ketoconazole Shampoo** — 2–3×/week; leave on 2–3 min before rinsing; reduces scalp DHT and inflammation
- **Alpecin Caffeine Shampoo** — daily use; leave on 2 min; adjunct to primary treatments

**Laser Devices:**
- **iRestore Professional** — FDA-cleared; 282 lasers; 25-min 3×/week sessions; ~$700
- **Capillus 202** — wearable cap; 6 min/day; FDA-cleared; ~$700

**Supplements (OTC support):**
- **Pumpkin Seed Oil** (e.g., NOW Foods) — 400 mg 2×/day with food
- **Saw Palmetto** (e.g., Nature's Way) — 320 mg standardized extract/day

### Daily Hair Protocol

**Daily:**
1. If using topical minoxidil — apply 1 mL to dry scalp, part hair to access skin, do NOT rinse immediately; apply AM + PM (or oral minoxidil 2.5 mg once daily)
2. Finasteride 1 mg — take any time of day, same time daily; results take 3–6 months minimum, evaluate at 12 months

**3× per Week (non-consecutive days):**
3. Wash with Nizoral 2% shampoo; apply to scalp, lather, leave 2–3 minutes, rinse
4. On non-Nizoral days: mild sulfate-free shampoo (e.g., Free & Clear, Vanicream) — avoid harsh surfactants that strip scalp

**Monthly:**
- Photograph hairline in consistent lighting to track changes — hair loss tracking requires objective data, not subjective perception

**Protocol Notes:**
- Finasteride must be taken daily without interruption; stopping causes rapid return of hair loss within months
- Side effects of finasteride (sexual dysfunction) occur in ~2% of men; reversible upon stopping; discuss with physician
- Minoxidil shedding (increased fall-out weeks 2–6) is normal — follicles cycling; push through it
- Results from any protocol take 12–18 months to fully assess; do not abandon after 3 months

## Part 4 — Optimal Oral Care Protocol

### The Evidence Foundation

Oral health is systemic health. Periodontal disease is independently associated with cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and Alzheimer's (inflammation pathway). The most important oral care interventions are also the cheapest. A 2025 systematic review of clinical practice guidelines found brushing 2×/day and interdental cleaning are the interventions with the strongest evidence base.

### Evidence-Based Active Ingredients

| Ingredient | Benefit | Evidence |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Fluoride (sodium fluoride 0.24%)** | Remineralization, cavity prevention | Decades of robust RCT data; gold standard |
| **Nano-Hydroxyapatite (nHA 5–10%)** | Remineralization matching or exceeding fluoride; sensitivity reduction; biocompatible | Multiple clinical trials; strong 2025–2026 data; comparable to fluoride in enamel repair |
| **Stannous Fluoride** | Cavity prevention + antimicrobial + sensitivity | Stronger than sodium fluoride for gum protection; Sensodyne Pronamel uses this |
| **Cetylpyridinium Chloride (CPC)** | Antimicrobial mouthwash that does not disrupt good oral bacteria the way alcohol-based rinses do | Better long-term microbiome impact vs. alcohol mouthwash |
| **Xylitol** | Reduces Streptococcus mutans colonization; adds no fermentable sugars | Well-replicated evidence; best as additive in gum or toothpaste |

### Recommended Products (Specific Brands)

**Toothpaste:**
- **Boka Ela Mint nHA Toothpaste** — 7% nano-hydroxyapatite; SLS-free; antioxidant-rich (green tea/cardamom); market leader in 2026; ~$12
- **RiseWell Daily Defense** — 7% nHA + probiotics; SLS-free; vegan; supports oral microbiome
- **Davids Nano-Hydroxyapatite Toothpaste** — naturally sourced; whitening; sensitive teeth; ~$10
- **Sensodyne Pronamel** — stannous fluoride; best option for those preferring traditional fluoride with sensitivity protection; ~$8
- **Dr. Brite Nano-HA + Fluoride** — 5% nHA + 0.24% fluoride; cavity resistance + remineralization

**Electric Toothbrushes:**
- **Oral-B iO Series 9** — AI-powered pressure sensor + round oscillating head; best clinical plaque removal; ~$150–200
- **Philips Sonicare DiamondClean 9500** — sonic technology; excellent gum care mode; ~$150–180
- **Oral-B iO Series 7** — step-down from Series 9; similar performance, lower cost; ~$100

**Water Flossers:**
- **Waterpik Aquarius (WP-660)** — gold standard; 10 pressure settings; countertop; ~$50–70; most studied device in RCTs
- **Oral-B Water Flosser Advanced** — quieter than Waterpik; compact; ~$50
- **SNOW AquaJet Pro** — ranked #1 ease-of-use Q1 2026; USB-C charging; portable; ~$60

**Tongue Scrapers:**
- **Stainless steel U-shaped tongue scraper** — reduces volatile sulfur compounds (bad breath) by ~75% vs. brushing alone; $5–10; use daily AM

**Mouthwash (use selectively — not default daily):**
- **TheraBreath Fresh Breath Oral Rinse** — CPC-based; alcohol-free; doesn't strip microbiome; best for bad breath management
- **Act Anticavity Fluoride Rinse** — sodium fluoride rinse; useful for high-caries-risk individuals
- **Avoid**: Listerine and most alcohol-based mouthwashes for daily use — strip beneficial oral bacteria

**Floss:**
- **Cocofloss** — textured coconut-oil-coated; high patient compliance (important: the best floss is the one you actually use)
- **Oral-B Glide Pro-Health** — smooth PTFE tape; best for tight contacts
- Water flosser alone is insufficient for subgingival plaque — combine with string floss OR floss picks

### Daily Oral Care Protocol

**Morning:**
1. Tongue scrape (U-scraper, 5–10 strokes from back to front, rinse)
2. Brush 2 minutes with electric toothbrush — use nHA or fluoride toothpaste — gentle pressure (let the brush work; do NOT scrub)
3. Spit, do NOT rinse — let active ingredients dwell on enamel
4. If using mouthwash, wait 30 min after brushing (rinsing immediately washes out fluoride/nHA)

**Evening (most important session):**
1. Floss before brushing — dislodge interdental food/plaque before brushing sweeps it away
2. Water flosser if available — 60–90 seconds; trace gumline around each tooth
3. Brush 2 minutes — electric toothbrush; target gumline at 45° angle
4. Spit, do NOT rinse with water — leave toothpaste residue on teeth overnight
5. Optional: CPC mouthwash (TheraBreath) if bad breath concern — use last, after brushing

**Weekly:**
- Check for gum bleeding during flossing — a bleed-free floss session is the target; bleeding indicates gum inflammation, not "fragile gums"

**Professional Cadence:**
- **Low risk** (no active disease, excellent home care): cleaning every 12 months
- **Moderate risk**: cleaning every 6 months
- **High risk** (active gum disease, high caries rate, dry mouth): every 3 months
- Annual bitewing X-rays to catch interproximal decay invisible to clinical exam

**Protocol Notes:**
- Electric toothbrush removes 11% more plaque and significantly reduces gum bleeding vs. manual; upgrade is worth it
- nHA toothpaste performs comparably to fluoride for remineralization in 2025 trials; combining low-dose fluoride + nHA is the cutting edge
- Mouthwash is NOT a replacement for brushing or flossing; use only as adjunct
- Oil pulling (coconut oil): modest evidence for reducing Streptococcus mutans; fine to add but not transformative
