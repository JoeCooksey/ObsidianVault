# Muscle Building Complete Guide

Master reference for building muscle through evidence-based resistance training. See also: [[Workout Splits Tier List]], [[Muscle Building Nutrition Guide]], [[Muscle Group Priority Guide]], [[Overall Exercise Tier List]].

---

## The 3 Mechanisms of Hypertrophy

| Mechanism | Description | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Mechanical Tension** | Force applied to muscle fibers under load — primary driver | S-tier |
| **Metabolic Stress** | Cellular fatigue, pump, lactate accumulation | A-tier |
| **Muscle Damage** | Micro-tears from eccentric loading | B-tier (secondary) |

> Modern consensus: mechanical tension at long muscle lengths (stretched position) is the dominant mechanism. Train through a full range of motion, feel the stretch.

---

## Volume — Sets Per Muscle Per Week

| Weekly Sets | Result |
|---|---|
| <5 sets | Maintenance / beginner gains |
| 5–9 sets | Good hypertrophy |
| 10–20 sets | Optimal hypertrophy (most people) |
| 20+ sets | Advanced athletes only; high fatigue |

- **Per session maximum**: ~8 sets per muscle group before fatigue exceeds stimulus
- **Start low** (10 sets/week), add sets when gains plateau
- (Source: Schoenfeld et al., dose-response meta-analysis, PMC)

---

## Frequency — How Often to Train Each Muscle

- **Minimum**: 2× per week per muscle group
- **Optimal**: 2× per week for most; 3× for lagging muscles
- Training frequency 2× per week produces superior hypertrophy vs 1× per week (high confidence, multiple RCTs)
- Distribute volume across sessions rather than concentrating in one

---

## Intensity — Rep Ranges

All rep ranges from 5–30 reps build muscle similarly when taken close to failure. Sweet spot for hypertrophy:

| Rep Range | Load | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| 4–6 reps | 80–90% 1RM | Strength + some size |
| **6–12 reps** | 65–80% 1RM | **Hypertrophy sweet spot** |
| 12–20 reps | 50–65% 1RM | Size + endurance |
| 20–30 reps | 40–50% 1RM | Metabolic stress, feeder sets |

Use the full spectrum across a training week; don't restrict yourself to one range.

---

## Proximity to Failure — RIR Guidelines

| RIR (Reps in Reserve) | Effect | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| 0 (failure) | 100% stimulus, high fatigue | Last set of exercise only |
| 1–2 RIR | 90–95% stimulus, manageable fatigue | **Optimal for most sets** |
| 3–4 RIR | ~75% stimulus | Warm-up sets, first sets |
| 5+ RIR | Suboptimal | Too easy — waste of time |

- 2024 meta-analysis: RIR 1–2 produces comparable hypertrophy to training to failure with significantly less systemic fatigue
- Training every set to failure impairs recovery and total volume capacity

---

## Progressive Overload — The Master Principle

Without progressive overload, the body has no reason to continue adapting. Apply in this order of preference:

1. **Add reps** within your target range (e.g., go from 8 to 12 reps)
2. **Add weight** (drop back to lower end of range, repeat)
3. **Add sets** (increase total weekly volume)
4. **Improve ROM** or control
5. **Decrease rest** (only if goal is endurance)

- Track every session. If you're not adding reps or weight over months, something is wrong.

---

## Rest Periods

| Goal | Rest |
|---|---|
| Hypertrophy | 60–90 seconds (metabolic stress) |
| Strength + size | 2–3 minutes |
| Strength | 3–5 minutes |

Longer rest between sets allows more total volume. 2–3 min is the evidence-based compromise.

---

## Training to Build Muscle — Program Structure

A complete hypertrophy session structure:
1. **Compound movement** (primary mover, heavy — 6–10 reps)
2. **Second compound or machine compound** (8–12 reps)
3. **Isolation / cable / machine** (12–15+ reps, stretch emphasis)
4. **Optional finisher** (15–20+ reps, pump/metabolic)

Put priority muscles first. Performance and growth are highest at the start of a session.

## Recovery — Non-Negotiables

| Factor | Minimum Target |
|---|---|
| Sleep | 7–9 hours (non-negotiable — GH released in deep sleep) |
| Protein | 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day |
| Caloric surplus | +200–500 kcal/day for lean bulk |
| Deload | Every 6–8 weeks (reduce volume 40–50% for 1 week) |
