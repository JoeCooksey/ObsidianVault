# First Job Roadmap — Livermore / Tri-Valley

**Context**: First-year EE student at ASU, living in Livermore, CA. No engineering work experience yet. Self-study assets: Python, LTspice, breadboard, Git (already initiated per [[EE Freshman Self-Study Stack]]).

---

## The Strategic Frame

You have two parallel tracks running simultaneously:

| Track | What it is | Payoff window |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| **Local Income Track** | Part-time job now for immediate income + work history | Weeks |
| **Engineering Career Track** | Build portfolio → internship at LLNL/Sandia/Lam | Summer 2027 |

Do NOT wait for the "perfect" engineering job. Get *any* job now while engineering track builds in background.

---

## Phase 0 — Right Now (April–June 2026)

### 1. Get a Local Job Immediately
You have a world-class advantage: you live 10 minutes from LLNL and Sandia. But *those* internships require applications in October. In the meantime, get any job:

**Best student-friendly options in Livermore:**
- Grocery / retail (Safeway, Trader Joe's, Costco in Livermore — reliable hours)
- KidStrong coaching ($22/hr, 24–30 hrs/week, explicitly student-friendly)
- Tutoring math/physics (Mathnasium, Kumon, or private — your MAT 265/266 = immediate credential)
- School bus driver (City of Livermore — college student explicit target)
- Amazon fulfillment (Tracy) — higher pay, flexible scheduling

**Where to apply:**
- [Indeed Livermore](https://www.indeed.com/l-livermore,-ca-jobs.html)
- [Tri-Valley Career Center Job Board](https://jobs.trivalleycareercenter.org/)
- [ZipRecruiter Entry Level Livermore](https://www.ziprecruiter.com/Jobs/Entry-Level/-in-Livermore,CA)

### 2. Set Up Your LinkedIn (1 hour)
This is your most leveraged free action.
- Headline: `EE Student @ ASU | Python + LTspice + Embedded Systems | Seeking 2027 Summer Internship`
- Add ASU, graduation year, Livermore location
- Connect with 5 ASU EE alumni working at LLNL/Sandia/Lam Research — search LinkedIn: "ASU electrical engineering Livermore"
- Photo, summary, skills section (Python, LTspice, MATLAB, Git, Breadboarding)

### 3. Set Up GitHub Portfolio (2 hours)
Even tiny projects count. Start with what you have:
- Repo 1: Python scripts from coursework / MAT 265 numerical practice
- Repo 2: LTspice simulation exports with documentation
- Repo 3: Any Arduino or breadboard project (LED, RC filter, transistor switch)
- Pin top 3 repos with descriptive README files
- Profile tagline: "EE freshman at ASU — building portfolio in embedded systems + power electronics"

### 4. Do Forage Virtual Simulations (Free)
[Forage](https://www.theforage.com/) offers free virtual job simulations from real companies. These appear on your resume as legitimate experience:
- Look for: Electrical Engineering, Systems Engineering, Hardware, Data Analysis tracks
- Complete 1–2 in the next 30 days
- These replace "no experience" with "completed [Company] virtual simulation in [skill]"

---

## Phase 1 — Summer 2026 (June–August)

### Goal: First Real Experience on Resume

**Option A: ASU Global Virtual Internship**
- Apply through ASU Career Services / Global Education Office
- Fully remote, earns academic credit, 13,000+ companies
- Apply 2–3 months before desired start — apply NOW for summer
- URL: goglobal.asu.edu/global-virtual-internship

**Option B: Local Part-Time Tech-Adjacent Work**
- ASU Makerspace (Tempe) has remote project support roles
- LLNL Livermore Valley Open Campus (LVOC) hosts open events — volunteer, tour, network
- Local electronics labs, repair shops, or IT support roles

**Option C: Personal Project → GitHub**
Your Year 1 self-study projects (LTspice RC filters, Python data analysis, Arduino) become your portfolio by summer. Aim for 3 pinned repos by August.

### Summer Project Goal
Build one project you're genuinely proud of that you can demo:
- Suggested: LTspice RLC circuit simulation + Python analysis of frequency response (bridging MAT 265 + EEE 202 preview)
- Post it on GitHub with a README that explains the physics
- This becomes your "tell me about a project" answer in every interview

---

## Phase 2 — Fall 2026 (October–December)

**This is the most critical window of the entire roadmap.**

### Apply to LLNL and Sandia Summer 2027 Internships
Applications open October–January. You are now a second-semester freshman / rising sophomore. This is when to strike.

| Program | Deadline | Key Req |
|---------|----------|---------|
| LLNL Engineering Summer Internship | ~January 2027 | US citizen/LPR, enrolled |
| Sandia DOE CCI Program | ~January 7, 2027 | GPA ≥ 3.0, full-time enrolled |
| LLNL Computing Internship | ~January 2027 | US citizen/LPR |

**How to stand out at LLNL/Sandia as a freshman:**
1. GitHub with 3–5 repos (even breadboard + LTspice projects count)
2. LinkedIn presence (labs actively search for local students)
3. GPA ≥ 3.0 — maintain this, it's a hard filter for Sandia CCI
4. Student section IEEE membership (signal of engagement with the field)
5. One Forage virtual simulation listed as experience

### Expand the Target List
Don't only apply to national labs. Broaden to:
- **Lam Research** (Fremont, 25 min): semiconductor equipment — directly relevant to EE
- **10X Genomics** (Pleasanton): instrumentation engineering internships
- **Chevron** (San Ramon): power/electrical engineering roles
- **PG&E** (Oakland): electrical/grid engineering rotational programs
- **Bay Area tech** via ASU Handshake: filter by "Livermore," "Pleasanton," "Dublin," "San Ramon," "Fremont"

### Attend Tri-Valley Career Center Job Fairs
Free events; direct employer access. Check: [trivalleycareercenter.org/tri-valley-job-fair](https://www.trivalleycareercenter.org/tri-valley-job-fair/)

---

## Phase 3 — Spring/Summer 2027

By now you have:
- 1 year of part-time work experience (any job counts)
- 3–5 GitHub projects
- Virtual internship or Forage simulation listed
- LLNL/Sandia/Lam applications submitted

**If LLNL/Sandia offer**: Accept. These are exceptional resume builders and pay well ($25–35/hr for undergrad summer interns).

**If not**: Target Tri-Valley/Bay Area companies with your now-real portfolio. Apply to FURI at ASU (apply sophomore year) for research experience credit that companies value.
