# Polarized Training

A training intensity distribution model developed from Dr. Stephen Seiler's observation that elite endurance athletes naturally cluster training at the extremes: mostly easy, some very hard, almost nothing in between.

## The 80/20 Rule

| Zone | Intensity | % of Training Sessions |
|---|---|---|
| Zone 1 (Low) | <75% max HR / <2 mmol lactate | ~80% |
| Zone 2 (Moderate) | "Comfortably hard" / threshold | ~5% |
| Zone 3 (High) | >90% VO2 max / >4 mmol lactate | ~15–20% |

The key insight: **avoid Zone 2 ("junk miles")**. Zone 2 is hard enough to accumulate fatigue but not hard enough to drive the strongest adaptations. Elite athletes naturally polarize, not pyramidalize.

## Why It Works

### Zone 1 Adaptations
- Mitochondrial biogenesis (more mitochondria)
- Fat oxidation capacity
- Capillary density increases
- Low fatigue cost → can accumulate high volume

### Zone 3 Adaptations
- VO2 max ceiling raised
- Lactate clearance improved
- Neuromuscular power at high intensities

### Zone 2 Problem
- High enough to cause significant sympathetic fatigue
- Not high enough to drive powerful adaptations
- Accumulates cortisol without proportional return
- Pushes Zone 1 rides up in intensity (the "grey zone" trap)

## Research Evidence (Seiler et al.)
- Descriptive studies of nationally/internationally competitive athletes converge on ~80/20 split
- 2024 systematic review (41 studies, 797 participants): polarized model outperforms pyramidal in key metrics
- Separate RCT: cyclists who shifted from threshold-heavy to polarized saw significant improvements in VO2 max and TT performance

## Practical Implementation (Cycling, 6 days/week)

### Week Structure
- **4 Zone 1 rides** (easy, conversational pace, 60–90 min each)
- **1–2 Zone 3 sessions** (intervals: 2×20 min threshold OR 5×3 min VO2 max)
- **0 Zone 2 sessions** (or minimal — 1 sweet spot session max during build phase)

### The "Talk Test" for Zone 1
Can hold a full conversation without breathing difficulty. Heart rate stays below ~75% max.

### Common Mistake
Most amateur cyclists do too much Zone 2 — they push easy rides too hard and interval days too easy, landing in a "medium everything" pattern with suboptimal adaptation.

## Seasonal Adjustment
- **Winter (base)**: 95% Zone 1, tiny amount of Zone 3
- **Build**: 80/20 classic split
- **Pre-competition**: 70/30 tilt toward Zone 3 for 3–4 weeks
- **Competition**: back to ~80/20 for maintenance
