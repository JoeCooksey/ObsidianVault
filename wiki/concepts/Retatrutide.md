# Retatrutide

Retatrutide (Eli Lilly; investigational code LY3437943) is a once-weekly injectable triple hormone receptor agonist — the most powerful weight-loss drug yet developed. It simultaneously activates three receptors: GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon. The glucagon component is the key differentiator: it raises resting metabolic rate and directly mobilizes stored fat, effects absent from both semaglutide and tirzepatide. As of 2026, retatrutide is in Phase 3 clinical trials and is **not yet FDA-approved**.

---

## Status (2026)

- **Phase 3 TRIUMPH program:** 8 trials in progress; 7 additional readouts expected in 2026
- **First successful Phase 3 result:** TRIUMPH-4 (obesity + knee osteoarthritis) — published 2025
- **NDA filing:** Expected Q4 2026 or early 2027
- **Estimated FDA approval:** Mid-to-late 2027
- **Currently available as:** Not legally available by prescription in the US

---

## Mechanism — The Triple Agonist Advantage

**GLP-1 receptor (same as semaglutide + tirzepatide):**
- Central appetite suppression (hypothalamus/brainstem)
- Slowed gastric emptying → prolonged satiety
- Glucose-dependent insulin secretion

**GIP receptor (same as tirzepatide):**
- Improved insulin sensitivity in peripheral tissues
- Shifts fat from visceral to subcutaneous depots
- Synergistic appetite suppression with GLP-1

**Glucagon receptor (unique to retatrutide — this is the key):**
- **Raises resting metabolic rate** — you burn more calories at rest
- **Promotes lipolysis** — directly breaks down stored fat into usable energy (fatty acids released from adipocytes)
- **Mobilizes liver fat** — directly targets hepatic steatosis; being studied for MASH/NAFLD treatment
- The glucagon signal is why retatrutide's weight loss (~26–28.7%) exceeds tirzepatide's (~20–22%): it's not just eating less, it's also burning the stored fat faster

## Dosing (Phase 3 — TRIUMPH Program)

All Phase 3 participants start at 2 mg/week and titrate every 4 weeks to target dose.

| Week | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | **2 mg/week** | Initiation — GI adaptation |
| 5–8 | **4 mg/week** | Escalation |
| 9–12 | **6 mg/week** | Mid-range; some patients may maintain here |
| 13–16 | **9 mg/week** | High-efficacy dose |
| 17+ | **12 mg/week** | Maximum studied dose; highest weight loss |

**Note:** The TRIUMPH program includes 4 mg and 9 mg as maintenance dose options, not just 12 mg. Maintenance dosing with 4 mg is expected to be studied specifically for long-term weight maintenance with a lower side-effect burden.

## Weight Loss Outcomes (Phase 2 + Phase 3 Data)

| Trial | Population | Duration | Weight Loss |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 2 (NEJM, 2023) | Obesity, no T2D | 48 weeks | **24.2%** (12 mg arm) |
| Phase 2 — T2D | Obesity + T2D | 36 weeks | **16.9%** (12 mg arm) |
| TRIUMPH-4 (Phase 3) | Obesity + knee OA | 68 weeks | **28.7%** (12 mg arm) |
| TRIUMPH-4 | Obesity + knee OA | 68 weeks | **26%** (9 mg arm) |

**28.7% mean body weight loss** at 68 weeks is the highest ever recorded for any pharmacological weight-loss agent in clinical trials. For a 250 lb person, that equals ~71.5 lbs of average weight loss.

---

## Additional Benefits (TRIUMPH-4 Data)

- **Osteoarthritis pain relief:** WOMAC pain score reduced by up to 75.8% (4.5 point reduction)
- **Physical function improvement:** Significant improvement in all measured function outcomes
- **Cardiovascular risk markers:** Significant reductions in non-HDL cholesterol and systolic blood pressure
- **Liver fat:** Direct mobilization of hepatic fat; separate Phase 2 trial for MASH showed positive results (Nature Medicine, 2024)

---

## Safety Signals

**Shared with semaglutide/tirzepatide:**
- Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation (most common — GI)
- Gallbladder disease
- Pancreatitis risk (black box)
- Thyroid C-cell tumor risk (black box — rodent data)

**Retatrutide-specific concern — Dysesthesia:**
- 8.8% of patients on 9 mg dose reported dysesthesia (abnormal skin sensations — tingling, burning, numbness)
- **20.9%** of patients on 12 mg dose reported dysesthesia
- Placebo rate: 0.7%
- This is a genuine safety signal under Phase 3 review. Whether it resolves with dose reduction or is dose-limiting will be a key regulatory question.

---

## Contraindications (Same Class)

- Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) or MEN2 syndrome
- Hypersensitivity
- Pregnancy
- Active pancreatitis

---

## Why Not Available Yet

The TRIUMPH Phase 3 program runs 8 trials across obesity, T2D, sleep apnea, osteoarthritis, and liver disease. All must complete before an NDA can be filed. Expected timeline:
- **7 additional Phase 3 readouts:** 2026
- **NDA filing:** Q4 2026 or early 2027
- **FDA review period:** ~10–12 months
- **Approval estimate:** Mid-to-late 2027
- **Commercial launch:** Shortly after approval

The dysesthesia safety signal will likely be scrutinized closely during FDA review and may influence the approved dose range.
