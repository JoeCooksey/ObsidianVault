# Peptide Tier List — Complete

> [!important] **Joe's context:** 19yo male, 5'9" / 156 lbs, cyclist 6×/week, lean bulk goal, EE student, existing supplement stack (creatine / D3+K2 / Mg glycinate / omega-3 / zinc). Possibly competitive cyclist → WADA rules apply. Tier placements reflect evidence × risk × Joe-specific relevance.

> [!warning] All peptides except FDA-approved ones (PT-141 / Vyleesi; Thymosin Alpha-1 / Zadaxin; Tesamorelin / Egrifta) require a prescription from a licensed physician through a compounding pharmacy in the US. "Research peptide" vendors are unregulated — no quality assurance, no legal protection. Never inject anything from an unverified source.

## Tier Definitions

| Tier | Meaning for Joe |
|------|----------------|
| **S** | High evidence + excellent safety + directly relevant to Joe's goals. Start here. |
| **A** | Strong evidence OR excellent safety + meaningful personal relevance. Worth considering with medical oversight. |

## S-Tier — Start Here

### GHK-Cu (Copper Peptide) — Topical
**What it does:** Stimulates collagen, elastin, and glycosaminoglycan synthesis in skin. Anti-inflammatory. Hair follicle support.

**Evidence:** Most evidence-backed cosmetic peptide. RCT: 22% skin firmness increase, 16% fine lines reduction over 12 weeks (0.05% serum). Outperformed Vitamin C cream AND retinoic acid for collagen production in one controlled trial. 7% hair count increase in 16-week Japanese RCT (weaker than minoxidil but additive).

**Route:** Topical serum or lotion. No injection. No systemic exposure.

**Risk:** Essentially zero. Topical copper is locally metabolized.

**Joe relevance (★★★★★):** He already has a skincare protocol (SPF50 + retinoid + Vitamin C). GHK-Cu slots in as the PM step between cleanser and retinoid. Directly relevant to the hair/skin goals from prior biohacking research. The safest peptide on this list.

**Protocol:** 1–2% topical GHK-Cu serum PM after cleansing; apply before retinoid OR on alternate nights if skin is sensitive. Products: NIOD Copper Amino Isolate Serum, Genoma GHK-Cu.

**Cost:** $30–80/month.

---

### Thymosin Alpha-1 (Tα-1) — Immune Support
**What it does:** Naturally produced by the thymus. Modulates innate + adaptive immunity (T-cell, NK cell, dendritic cell activation). Potent anti-inflammatory. Approved as Zadaxin in 35+ countries for hepatitis B/C, cancer immunotherapy support.

**Evidence:** Best human evidence base of any research peptide. 3,000+ patients studied across multiple diseases. Consistently safe and well-tolerated, including in immunocompromised and critically ill patients. One of the few peptides with Phase III trial data.

**Route:** SubQ injection 0.5–1.6 mg, 1–2×/week.

**Risk:** Extremely favorable. Rare injection site reactions. No known metabolic disruption.

**Joe relevance (★★★★☆):** Endurance athletes doing 6×/week cycling are chronically immunosuppressed (open window theory post-exercise). Getting sick disrupts training more than any other factor. Thymosin Alpha-1 is the most legitimate immune support peptide in existence. Use during heavy training blocks or when feeling rundown.

**Protocol:** 1.5 mg SubQ 2×/week during heavy training phases; reduce to 1×/week in base phase.

**Cost:** ~$80–120/month via compounding pharmacy.

## A-Tier — Worth Considering

### BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound)
**What it does:** 15-amino acid peptide derived from gastric juice proteins. Accelerates tendon, ligament, muscle, and gut healing. Potent anti-inflammatory via nitric oxide pathway. Angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation). Gastroprotective.

**Evidence:** 200+ studies in animals — extremely impressive across healing contexts. Human data is thin (as of early 2026: ~30 total subjects in 3 small pilot studies). Animal evidence: heals Achilles tendon and ACL in rats significantly faster; prevents and heals NSAID-induced gut damage; neuroprotective. Human: one 2021 retrospective (87.5% knee pain relief, n=16), one 2024 pilot (IBD, n=12), one 2025 safety study (n=2 IV injection, well-tolerated).

**Route:** SubQ injection 250–500 mcg/day; or oral capsule (lower systemic bioavailability but effective for gut).

**Risk:** Low in animal studies. No serious adverse events reported in small human trials. Concerns: (a) nearly all animal research comes from one Croatian group (Sikiric et al.) raising replication bias questions; (b) no long-term human safety data; (c) unregulated sources have contamination risk.

**Joe relevance (★★★★☆):** Cycling 6×/week → tendon stress (patellar, Achilles, IT band), overuse injuries, and gut issues under heavy load. BPC-157 is the single most studied peptide for the exact type of repair an endurance athlete needs. The oral form (capsule) is also the best entry point — no injection, gut-protective, legitimate use case. **Best use: upon developing overuse injury, not as permanent daily protocol.**

**Protocol:** 250 mcg SubQ 1×/day OR 400–500 mcg oral capsule 2×/day. Run for 4–8 weeks during injury recovery. Cycle off when healed.

**Cost:** $60–100/month via compounding pharmacy.

---

### Semax — Cognitive
**What it does:** Synthetic analog of ACTH (4-10) developed in Russia. Primary mechanism: increases BDNF and its receptor sensitivity. Also activates dopaminergic and serotonergic systems. Used clinically in Russia for stroke recovery, ADHD, and cognitive decline.

**Evidence:** Meaningful Russian clinical literature (BDNF upregulation, memory, attention confirmed in multiple studies). Functional connectomics study confirms CNS effects. Not replicated in large Western RCTs — the main evidence gap. Mechanism is sound and well-characterized.

**Route:** Nasal spray (best bioavailability); sublingual; SubQ injection.

**Risk:** Minimal reported side effects; mild nasal irritation. No significant adverse events in clinical use. No dependency. No withdrawal.

**Joe relevance (★★★★☆):** BDNF is brain fertilizer — it promotes new neural connections, long-term potentiation, and learning. An EE student doing deep self-study in circuits, Python, and math is literally trying to build new neural circuits. Semax enhances the biological substrate of that learning. Best timed on study days, not necessarily daily.

**Protocol:** 200–600 mcg nasal spray on study mornings (1–2 sprays). Cycle: 3 weeks on, 1 week off. Expected return to compounding status in 2026.

**Cost:** ~$50–80/month.

---

### Tesamorelin — Body Composition (GH Axis)
**What it does:** Modified GHRH analog. FDA-approved (Egrifta) for HIV-related lipodystrophy. Reduces visceral fat, improves body composition. Most clinically validated GH axis peptide.

**Evidence:** Multiple Phase III trials. Proven visceral fat reduction, improved triglycerides and IGF-1 in human subjects. Best evidence base of all GH-releasing peptides.

**Route:** SubQ injection, 1–2 mg/day.

**Risk:** A-tier safety among injectable GH peptides. Main risks: fluid retention, IGF-1 overshoot, hypersensitivity.

**Joe relevance (★★☆☆☆):** Joe doesn't have the medical indication (no HIV lipodystrophy). At 19, his natural GH is high and his body composition is healthy. This peptide shines at 30–50 when GH decline begins. **File for later (30s), not now.**

**Protocol:** Not recommended at 19. Reassess at 30+.

## B-Tier — Situationally Useful

### Ipamorelin — GH Pulse / Recovery
**What it does:** GHRP (Growth Hormone Releasing Peptide). Selectively stimulates GH release without significantly raising cortisol, prolactin, or ACTH. Produces a clean GH pulse. Often combined with CJC-1295 for synergy.

**Evidence:** Human data confirmed selective GH release (PubMed 9849822). No large body composition RCT in athletes. Combination protocols based largely on clinical anecdote.

**Route:** SubQ injection pre-sleep.

**Risk:** Unknown long-term effects on GH pulsatility. Possible IGF-1 elevation. Not WADA-banned (unlike MK-677).

**Joe relevance (★★★☆☆):** At 19 with peak natural GH, adding ipamorelin is redundant during normal training. The main value case: accelerating recovery from training blocks where natural GH response seems insufficient, or sleep quality optimization. Consider at 25+ if recovery becomes limiting.

**Protocol:** 100–200 mcg SubQ 30 min pre-sleep. Only meaningful if running with CJC-1295 (no-DAC). 8-week cycles with 4 weeks off.

---

### CJC-1295 (no-DAC) / Modified GRF 1-29 — GH Axis
**What it does:** GHRH analog (stimulates the hypothalamic-pituitary axis to release GH). Pairs with ipamorelin for synergistic GH pulses via dual mechanism (GHRH + GHRP pathways). The "no-DAC" version produces physiologically normal pulsatile GH release.

**Evidence:** Human RCT (PubMed 16352683): confirmed sustained GH + IGF-1 elevation, dose-dependent, well-tolerated. No direct athlete body comp trial.

**Avoid:** CJC-1295 WITH DAC — causes constant GH elevation (non-pulsatile), which is physiologically inferior. If stacking, always use "no-DAC" / Modified GRF 1-29.

**Route:** SubQ injection.

**Joe relevance:** Same as ipamorelin — overkill at 19. Useful if stacking GH peptides at 25+.

---

### Selank — Anxiety / Exam Stress
**What it does:** Synthetic analog of tuftsin. Modulates serotonin, dopamine, and GABA pathways. Anxiolytic without sedation. No dependency, no withdrawal. Developed in Russia alongside Semax.

**Evidence:** Russian clinical data on anxiety reduction and adaptive stress response. GABAergic gene expression changes confirmed (PMC4757669). Not replicated in Western RCTs. Side effects minimal.

**Route:** Nasal spray.

**Joe relevance (★★★☆☆):** The specific use case for Joe: exam periods, high-stakes sessions, or weeks where study stress is affecting sleep. Clean anxiolytic with no daytime cognitive impairment. Stack with Semax for the full cognitive + calm stack.

**Protocol:** 250–750 mcg nasal spray when needed (not daily). Pairs naturally with Semax.

---

### KPV (Lysine-Proline-Valine) — Gut / Inflammation
**What it does:** C-terminal tripeptide of alpha-MSH. Anti-inflammatory via NF-κB pathway inhibition. Reduces TNF-α, IL-6. Studied for IBD, ulcerative colitis, gut lining integrity. Does NOT cause skin darkening (unlike full alpha-MSH or MT-II).

**Evidence:** Animal + cell studies promising; limited published human data. Preclinical IBD evidence strong.

**Route:** Oral capsule or SubQ.

**Joe relevance (★★★☆☆):** Endurance athletes doing high-volume cycling often develop mild gut inflammation and increased intestinal permeability. KPV + BPC-157 oral is a well-reasoned gut stack. Use if GI issues emerge during heavy training.

**Protocol:** 500 mcg–2 mg oral, 1×/day during heavy training phases. Expected back to compounding status 2026.

---

### Sermorelin — GH (Lowest-Risk Option)
**What it does:** Synthetic GHRH analog (first 29 amino acids of native GHRH). FDA-approved history (pediatric GH deficiency). Shorter half-life than CJC-1295. Used as the "conservative" GH peptide option.

**Evidence:** Most clinical history of any GHRH analog. Longer track record in medical practice than CJC-1295.

**Joe relevance (★★☆☆☆):** Better choice than CJC-1295 if going the GH route — more medical history, more conservative. But still not indicated at 19.

---

### PT-141 (Bremelanotide) — Sexual Function
**What it does:** Melanocortin-3/4 receptor agonist. FDA-approved as Vyleesi for female hypoactive sexual desire. Works through CNS (brain-level libido signaling, not vascular like PDE5 inhibitors).

**Evidence:** Only FDA-approved peptide for sexual desire. Phase III data exists.

**Risk:** Nausea in 40% of users; flushing; temporary blood pressure increase.

**Joe relevance (★★☆☆☆):** At 19 with healthy hormones, this is not indicated. Only relevant if libido issues emerge from overtraining, hormonal disruption, or SSRIs. File for awareness only.

## Synergy Map — Which Peptides Pair Well

### Healing Stack (Best for Cycling Overuse Injuries)
```
BPC-157 (250 mcg SubQ 1×/day)
+
Thymosin Alpha-1 (1.5 mg SubQ 2×/week)
```
**Why:** BPC-157 handles local tissue repair (tendon, gut, muscle). Thymosin Alpha-1 modulates the immune/inflammatory response systemically. They work on different pathways and don't compete.
**Use when:** Experiencing persistent overuse injury OR during high-volume training blocks.

---

### Cognitive Stack (Best for EE Study Periods)
```
Semax (300 mcg nasal spray, morning of study day)
+
Selank (250–500 mcg nasal spray, afternoon if anxious or pre-exam)
```
**Why:** Semax is stimulatory and BDNF-upregulating (makes learning stick). Selank is anxiolytic and calming (prevents performance anxiety from degrading recall). Complementary CNS mechanisms with no known interaction.
**Use when:** Deep study blocks, exam weeks, new concept sprints.

---

### GH Axis Stack (30+ version; note to Joe)
```
CJC-1295 no-DAC (100–200 mcg SubQ)
+
Ipamorelin (100–200 mcg SubQ pre-sleep)
```
**Why:** CJC-1295 stimulates the hypothalamus (GHRH axis); ipamorelin stimulates the pituitary directly (GHRP axis). Two different inputs to the same output (GH pulse). Synergistic, not redundant. NOT for use at 19 — file for 30+.

---

### Skin + Hair Stack (Entry Level, No Injection)
```
GHK-Cu topical serum (PM skincare routine)
+
Collagen peptides 10g oral (morning with Vitamin C)
[Already in existing biohacking protocol]
```
**Why:** GHK-Cu drives local collagen synthesis in skin. Collagen peptides provide systemic hydroxyproline. Vitamin C is a cofactor for collagen crosslinking — already in Joe's AM routine. This stack is already partially in place; adding GHK-Cu serum completes it.

---

### Gut + Immunity Stack (Heavy Training Phases)
```
BPC-157 oral capsule (400–500 mcg 2×/day)
+
KPV oral (500 mcg–1 mg 1×/day)
```
**Why:** BPC-157 promotes gut mucosal healing and angiogenesis. KPV blocks NF-κB inflammatory cascade. Different mechanisms, same site of action (gut). May also reduce NSAID damage if Joe takes ibuprofen for cycling injuries.
