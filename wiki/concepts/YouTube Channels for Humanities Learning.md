# YouTube Channels for Humanities Learning

Ranked for self-directed learners in philosophy, history, economics, psychology, and interdisciplinary thinking. Complements [[YouTube Channels for STEM Learning]] for a complete intellectual media diet.

---

## S-Tier — Widest Intellectual Range

### Vsauce
- **Creator**: Michael Stevens
- **Subjects**: Science, philosophy, psychology, mathematics, and their intersections
- **Why S-tier**: The best curiosity-driven channel on YouTube. Asks questions no one else asks and pursues them to unexpected philosophical depths. Bridges STEM and humanities naturally.
- **Must-watch episodes**:
  - "The Zipf Mystery" — linguistics, statistics, and the hidden pattern in all language
  - "Is Anything Real?" — philosophy of perception and consciousness
  - "How Much of the Earth Can You See at Once?" — mathematics meets philosophy of perspective
  - "Juvenoia" — sociology and the psychology of generational panic
- **Note**: Original Vsauce channel; Vsauce2 and Vsauce3 are separate channels with lower ceiling

### Kurzgesagt – In a Nutshell
- **Creator**: Kurzgesagt studio (Munich)
- **Subjects**: Science, technology, existential risk, philosophy of existence, economics, biology
- **Why S-tier**: Best animated science-philosophy channel. High production, highly sourced, deliberately takes positions on controversial topics. Covers topics most channels avoid (death, meaning, nuclear war, simulation theory).
- **Must-watch episodes**:
  - "Optimistic Nihilism" — best intro to meaning-making as philosophy
  - "The Egg" — philosophy of consciousness and identity
  - "Why You Are Alone" — social isolation and evolutionary psychology
  - "The Most Efficient Way to Destroy the Universe" — physics and existential stakes

## A-Tier — Deep, Subject-Specific

### CrashCourse
- **Creator**: John Green (world history, literature), Hank Green (science, economics)
- **Subjects**: World history, US history, economics, psychology, philosophy, biology, chemistry
- **Format**: 10–15 minute structured lessons; curriculum-grade
- **Best for**: Survey knowledge across humanities; all playlists are complete courses
- **Must-watch playlists**: World History (Playlist 1 + 2), Philosophy, Economics, Psychology

### CGP Grey
- **Creator**: Anonymous (former teacher)
- **Subjects**: History, social systems, economics, political science, geography
- **Format**: Highly produced animations; irregular upload schedule; 5–20 minutes
- **Why A-tier**: Each video is densely researched and offers a non-obvious angle on its topic. More intellectually honest than most channels.
- **Must-watch videos**:
  - "Rules for Rulers" — game theory of political power
  - "Black Holes Explained" (from early catalog) — accessible cosmology
  - "The Simple Solution to Traffic" — systems thinking applied to transportation
  - "Humans Need Not Apply" — economics of automation; prescient from 2014

### TED-Ed
- **Subjects**: All disciplines; wide range of educators and animators
- **Format**: 4–7 minute animated lessons with discussion prompts
- **Best for**: Entry points to unfamiliar topics; excellent philosophy of science series

### Big Think
- **Subjects**: Science, philosophy, politics, psychology, technology
- **Format**: Short expert interviews (2–10 min) + longer lecture series
- **Notable contributors**: Richard Feynman archives, Steven Pinker, Michio Kaku, Daniel Dennett
- **Best for**: Getting expert opinions on complex questions quickly

### History Matters
- **Subjects**: History (world)
- **Format**: Animated 3–5 minute summaries
- **Best for**: Getting historical context fast; covers everything from ancient Rome to 20th century

### Academy of Ideas
- **Subjects**: Philosophy, psychology, history of ideas
- **Format**: Essay-style narrated videos with classical art visuals
- **Best for**: Nietzsche, Jung, Stoicism, existentialism; densest philosophy channel on YouTube

### The School of Life
- **Creator**: Alain de Botton (philosopher)
- **Subjects**: Philosophy applied to everyday life, psychology, meaning, relationships
- **Best for**: Philosophical ideas made emotionally relevant; excellent for Stoicism and Existentialism context

## A-Tier — Economics & Finance

| Channel | Best For |
|---------|----------|
| **Khan Academy Economics** | Macro/microeconomics foundations; curriculum-grade; AP-level to college |
| **Economics Explained** | Current economic events with macroeconomic context; accessible |
| **Ray Dalio / Principles** | "How the Economic Machine Works" (30 min) — best single economics video ever made; debt cycles |
| **Patrick Boyle** | Finance and hedge fund industry; technical but accessible; derivatives, funds, markets |

### "How the Economic Machine Works" — Ray Dalio
The single most important economics video on YouTube. 30 minutes. Dalio explains debt cycles, credit, and economic booms/busts using a simple model. Has been viewed 30M+ times. Required viewing before reading *The Intelligent Investor* or *The Psychology of Money*.
