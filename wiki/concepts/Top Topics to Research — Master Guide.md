## Why These 7

### 1. Power Electronics Deep Study
Joe's academic track *and* research track (Ranjram lab, FURI). Self-study above coursework pace = the single best signal to research PIs. Most EE students don't pick up the Erickson textbook until graduate school. Getting there in Year 1–2 is a compressible advantage.
→ See [[Power Electronics Deep Study Roadmap]]

### 2. AI/ML for Engineers
EE + ML is an extremely rare combination in the workforce. Power electronics + embedded ML = a career category of one. 3Blue1Brown + Andrej Karpathy + fast.ai = complete free education. The window where this feels exotic is closing — get there now.
→ See [[AI ML for Engineers Roadmap]]

### 3. Personal Finance & Investing
The compound interest clock started the day you got your first paycheck. At 10% annual return, $200/month starting at 19 = $1.87M at 65. Same investment starting at 30 = $634k. This is not a topic to defer to "when I have a real job." Setup takes 3 hours (Fidelity Roth IRA + VTI) and then runs automatically.
→ See [[Financial Literacy Roadmap]]

### 4. Negotiation
Every engineer negotiates their first job offer. Most leave $5,000–$20,000 on the table by accepting the first number. Salary negotiation compounds: a $10k raise at 22 becomes $150k+ over a 30-year career with raises, RSUs, and promotions indexed to base. Chris Voss's *Never Split the Difference* is a 6-hour read that pays for itself on the first negotiation.
→ See [[Negotiation Skills Roadmap]]

### 5. Stoicism & Philosophy
Not soft content — cognitive infrastructure. Stoicism is a decision-making framework, an emotional regulation system, and a resilience protocol. Install before the first academic failure, relationship rupture, or rejection letter. Ryan Holiday → Epictetus → Marcus Aurelius is the right reading order for an ambitious 19-year-old.
→ See [[Book List — Stoicism and Philosophy]] + [[Stoicism Self-Study Roadmap]]

### 6. Mathematics Deep Study
EE at the research level *is* applied mathematics. Linear algebra underlies signals, control theory, and ML. Complex analysis underlies AC circuit analysis and Fourier theory. ODEs underlie control systems and circuit transients. The MIT 18.06 linear algebra course (Prof. Gilbert Strang, free on OCW) is the best single math investment for an EE student.
→ See [[Calculus in Electrical Engineering]] + [[Differential Equations in Electrical Engineering]]

### 7. Sleep Science
Existing protocols in [[Sleep Habits Tier List]] are already research-grade. The remaining gap is understanding *why* — Walker's two-process model, glymphatic system, REM consolidation — so the habits stick. One book (*Why We Sleep*) closes this gap.
→ See [[Sleep Science Deep Guide]]

## The 90-Day Activation Plan

If starting from zero today (May 2026):

**Week 1** (immediate, 1–2 hrs total)
- Open Fidelity Roth IRA, buy VTI, set up $50/month auto-invest
- Download *Never Split the Difference* (audiobook or Kindle)
- Read the Enchiridion (Epictetus, 30 pages, free online)

**Month 1** (1 hr/day)
- Power Electronics: read Daniel Hart Ch. 1–3 (DC-DC converter fundamentals)
- AI/ML: start fast.ai Practical Deep Learning for Coders (free online)
- Finance: read *I Will Teach You to Be Rich* (Ramit Sethi)

**Month 2** (1 hr/day)
- Power Electronics: Erickson Ch. 1–5 + MIT OCW 6.622 lectures
- AI/ML: 3Blue1Brown "Neural Networks" series + fast.ai continued
- Negotiation: practice on a phone/internet bill renewal

**Month 3** (consolidate)
- Power Electronics: LTspice + STM32 PWM; start designing next converter
- AI/ML: Andrej Karpathy "Neural Networks: Zero to Hero" series
- Stoicism: begin *Meditations* (1 entry/day, Gregory Hays translation)
