# Exercise Tier List — Legs

Based on Jeff Nippard's evidence-based leg exercise rankings. Legs = three distinct areas requiring targeted training: **quads**, **hamstrings/glutes**, and **calves**.

---

## Leg Anatomy for Programming

| Muscle | Function | Primary Exercises |
|---|---|---|
| Quads | Knee extension | Squats, leg press, hack squat, leg extension |
| Hamstrings | Knee flexion + hip extension | RDL, leg curl, Nordic curl |
| Glutes | Hip extension + abduction | Hip thrust, RDL, lunges, squats |
| Calves (gastrocnemius) | Plantarflexion | Standing calf raise |
| Calves (soleus) | Plantarflexion | Seated calf raise |

---

## QUADS Tier List

### S-Tier

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| **Hack Squat (machine)** | Best quad isolation in the gym; high tension at stretched position; no spinal load |
| **Barbell Back Squat** | King of leg exercises; full quad + glute activation; strength foundation |
| **Pendulum Squat** | Similar to hack squat; excellent ROM; minimal spinal loading |
| **Smith Machine Squat** | Safe, high volume capacity; good for beginners |
| **Bulgarian Split Squat (BSS)** | Unilateral; superior quad stretch; exposes strength imbalances |

### A-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Barbell Front Squat | Higher quad emphasis than back squat; upper back demanding |
| Low-Bar Squat | More posterior chain; less pure quad |
| 45-Degree Leg Press | Volume workhorse; good for high reps without spinal load |
| Leg Extension | Best pure quad isolation; excellent as finisher or pre-exhaust |
| Reverse Nordic Curl | Advanced bodyweight quad exercise; maximizes rectus femoris stretch |

### B-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Lunge | Good unilateral option; balance-demanding |
| Goblet Squat | Beginner-friendly; limited loading ceiling |
| Sissy Squat | High quad stretch; knee stress for some |

### C-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Horizontal Leg Press | Limited ROM compared to 45-degree |
| Deadlift | Not a quad exercise primarily |
| Step Ups | Balance-limited; awkward progressive overload |
| Pistol Squat | Impressive but balance-limited; not superior to BSS |

### F-Tier — Avoid

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| Jump Squat | Not for hypertrophy; not for beginners |
| Bosu Ball Squat | Instability reduces load — kills hypertrophy |

## HAMSTRINGS Tier List

### S-Tier

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| **Romanian Deadlift (RDL)** | Hip hinge with maximum hamstring stretch; best overall hamstring builder |
| **Nordic Hamstring Curl** | Extremely high eccentric load; strongest evidence for hamstring hypertrophy |
| **Lying Leg Curl (machine)** | Best isolated hamstring curl; both hip and knee positions favorable |

### A-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Seated Leg Curl | Hip flexed position — better for biceps femoris long head |
| Good Morning | Hip hinge variation; erector-dominant but solid hamstring work |
| Stiff-Leg Deadlift | Similar to RDL but slight knee bend variation |

### B-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Conventional Deadlift | More posterior chain overall; not hamstring-specific |
| Glute-Ham Raise | Excellent but equipment availability limits use |

## GLUTES Tier List

### S-Tier

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| **Walking Lunges** | Dynamic hip extension + stretch; excellent glute activation |
| **45-Degree Back Extension** | Glute-focused hyperextension; high activation at full hip extension |
| **Machine Hip Abduction** | Best isolated glute med exercise; prevents hip drop and knee valgus |
| **Smith Machine Lunge (front foot elevated)** | Maximizes glute stretch and ROM |

### A-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Machine Hip Thrust | Better than barbell for comfort; excellent activation |
| Romanian Deadlift | Lower glute emphasis; #1 for lower glute development |
| Barbell Back Squat | Significant glute stimulus when squatting to depth |
| Bulgarian Split Squat | Unilateral stretch; strong glute + quad crossover |
| Kickbacks (cable or machine) | Best isolation for glute max peak contraction |
| Step Ups | Unilateral glute + quad |

### B-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Barbell Hip Thrust | Classic glute exercise; large setup hassle vs. machine version |
| Glute Bridge | Bodyweight; limited loading; regression |
| Cable Pull-Through | Hip hinge with constant cable tension |
| Curtsy Lunge | Strong glute med activation |
| Deadlifts | Glute involvement depends on technique |

### C-Tier

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Frog Pumps | Bodyweight; activation-level exercise only |
| Lateral Banded Walk | Warm-up use only |

### D-Tier — Avoid for Hypertrophy

| Exercise | Why |
|---|---|
| Donkey Kicks | Low resistance; activation drill at best |
| Fire Hydrants | Same — not a hypertrophy exercise |
| Kettlebell Swing | Power development, not glute hypertrophy |

## CALVES Tier List

| Exercise | Tier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Standing Calf Raise** | S | Gastrocnemius (knee straight) |
| **Seated Calf Raise** | S | Soleus (knee bent) — both muscles needed |
| Leg Press Calf Raise | A | Alternative to standing |
| Smith Machine Calf Raise | A | Easy progressive overload |
| Donkey Calf Raise | B | Higher hip hinge = more gastrocnemius stretch |

> Calves require HIGH reps (15–30) and full ROM (complete stretch at bottom). They are notoriously stubborn — train 4–6 days/week for best results.

---

## Programming Notes

- **Minimum leg session**: Squat variation (S-tier) + RDL or Nordic curl + leg extension/curl isolations
- Train quads and hamstrings with equal volume — imbalances cause knee injury
- Glutes respond well to both hip thrust/extension patterns AND squat depth — use both
- Calves: high frequency + high reps + full ROM is the only way forward
