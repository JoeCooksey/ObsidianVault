# Biohacking Tier List — Interventions, Products & Actions

Ranked by **evidence quality × effect size × cost-benefit ratio**. Tiers: S (transformative, high evidence) → A (strong, meaningful) → B (moderate, worth trying) → C (weak/mixed evidence) → D (hype, no evidence or unsafe).

---

## Category 1: Sleep Optimization

| Tier | Intervention | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **S** | Consistent wake time (7 days/week) | Single most powerful circadian anchor; free |
| **S** | 7–9 hours sleep per night | Decades of RCT evidence; underpins all other hacks |
| **S** | Dark + cool room (65–68°F / 18–20°C) | Core body temp drop is the sleep trigger |
| **A** | Morning sunlight within 30 min of waking | Resets cortisol peak timing; 5–10 min on clear day |
| **A** | No screens 60 min before bed (or blue-light glasses) | Blocks melatonin suppression |
| **A** | Oura Ring / WHOOP 4.0 | HRV + sleep stage tracking; $300–500 |
| **A** | Magnesium glycinate (300–400 mg) | Sleep onset + depth; see [[Sleep Optimization Supplements]] |
| **B** | Melatonin 0.1–0.5 mg (low-dose) | Chronobiotic, not sedative; use for travel/shift work |
| **B** | Mouth tape at night | Forces nasal breathing; cheap; mixed RCT evidence |
| **B** | 8Sleep Pod / sleep cooling mattress pad | Temp regulation during sleep; ~$2,000 |
| **B** | Red light bulbs in bedroom after sunset | Low-tech light environment fix |
| **C** | CBD for sleep | Weak and inconsistent evidence; expensive |
| **C** | Weighted blanket | Anxiety relief only; no sleep architecture benefit |
| **D** | High-dose melatonin (5–10 mg) | Pharmacological overdose; suppresses natural production |

## Category 2: Exercise & Movement

| Tier | Intervention | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **S** | Zone 2 cardio (150+ min/week) | VO2 max, mitochondrial biogenesis, metabolic health |
| **S** | Resistance training (2–4x/week) | Strongest longevity signal; muscle mass = metabolic reserve |
| **S** | VO2 Max intervals (1–2x/week) | 4-min efforts at ~106–120% FTP; see [[VO2 Max Interval Training]] |
| **A** | Daily walking (7,000–10,000 steps) | Underrated; linear dose-response with all-cause mortality |
| **A** | Flexibility/mobility work | Joint longevity; injury prevention |
| **B** | Sauna post-workout | Recovery + cardiovascular benefit (see heat therapy below) |
| **B** | Blood flow restriction (BFR) training | Hypertrophy with low load; useful for injury rehab |
| **C** | Vibration platforms (whole-body) | Weak evidence outside clinical bone density contexts |

## Category 3: Temperature Therapy

| Tier | Intervention | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **S** | Sauna (Finnish / infrared) 4x/week | ~40% reduction in cardiovascular mortality (Laukkanen et al.) |
| **A** | Cold water immersion (10–15°C, 10–15 min) | Reduces inflammation; increases norepinephrine 300% |
| **A** | Cold showers (daily, 2–3 min cold) | Free; stress resilience; mood boost via dopamine |
| **B** | Contrast therapy (hot-cold cycling) | Additive circulatory + recovery benefit |
| **B** | Infrared sauna (RLT wavelengths) | Lower temp, more portable; similar but softer effects |
| **C** | Cryotherapy chambers | $50–100/session; similar or inferior to ice baths; expensive |
| **D** | Whole-body cryotherapy for weight loss | No evidence for fat loss specifically |

## Category 4: Light Therapy

| Tier | Intervention | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **S** | Morning sunlight (10–30 min before 10am) | Free cortisol-melatonin axis calibration |
| **A** | Red light therapy panel (630–850 nm) | Mitochondrial ATP production; skin, recovery, inflammation |
| **A** | Joovv Solo / Mito Red Pro 1500X | $400–$700; best home panels; full-body coverage |
| **B** | SAD light box (10,000 lux) for morning | Circadian reset; useful in winter or low-sunlight climates |
| **B** | Heliotherapy / photobiomodulation helmets | LLLT for brain; nascent but interesting neurological data |
| **C** | Blue-light blocking glasses (daytime) | No evidence for daytime use; possible harm to focus |
| **C** | Infrared saunas as light therapy | RLT wavelengths penetrate differently; don't conflate |

## Category 5: Nutrition & Fasting

| Tier | Intervention | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **S** | Whole-food diet (Mediterranean/similar) | Most replicated diet-health dataset on earth |
| **S** | Protein adequacy (1.6–2.2 g/kg bodyweight) | Critical for muscle retention, satiety, mTOR cycling |
| **S** | Eliminating ultra-processed foods | Dose-response with all-cause mortality; higher than any supplement |
| **A** | Time-restricted eating (16:8 or 18:6) | Metabolic flexibility, autophagy; modest insulin sensitivity gains |
| **A** | Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM) | Real-time glucose feedback; Levels, Nutrisense, Dexcom G7 |
| **A** | Omega-3 fatty acids from fish/flax | Systemic inflammation reduction; see [[Supplement Tier List Complete]] |
| **B** | Ketogenic diet | Strong for epilepsy; metabolic improvement in diabetics; hard to sustain |
| **B** | 24-hour fasts (1–2x/month) | Autophagy ramp-up; insulin sensitization |
| **B** | Elimination diets for food sensitivities | High ROI if symptomatic; self-experiment |
| **C** | 3–7 day water fasts | Extreme; high catabolism risk; no benefit vs 24–48h fasts for healthy people |
| **C** | Carnivore diet | Short-term benefits real; long-term fiber absence problematic |
| **D** | Detox cleanses and juice fasts | Liver does the detoxing; no scientific basis |

## Category 6: Stress & Mental Optimization

| Tier | Intervention | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **S** | Meditation (10–20 min/day) | Structural brain changes at 8 weeks (MBSR RCTs); HRV ↑ |
| **S** | Social connection + community | Strongest longevity predictor after smoking cessation |
| **A** | Breathwork (box breathing, 4-7-8, cyclic sighing) | Immediate HRV + anxiety effect; free |
| **A** | Nature exposure / forest bathing | Cortisol and blood pressure reduction; RCT-confirmed |
| **A** | Journaling (gratitude or cognitive) | Mood, cognitive clarity; low time cost |
| **B** | Apollo Neuro wearable | Vagal nerve vibration; decent HRV data; $350 |
| **B** | Neurofeedback | Strong in ADHD; limited data in healthy adults |
| **C** | PEMF (pulsed electromagnetic field) devices | Nascent; some pain relief data; expensive |
| **C** | Floatation tanks (sensory deprivation) | Relaxation + creativity; not transformative for health |

## Category 8: Cognitive Enhancement

| Tier | Intervention / Product | Notes |
|------|----------------------|-------|
| **S** | Sleep (8h) | #1 cognitive intervention; see sleep category |
| **S** | Exercise (cardiovascular) | BDNF; hippocampal neurogenesis; memory benefit robust |
| **A** | Caffeine + L-Theanine stack | Best acute cognitive intervention; see [[Caffeine and L-Theanine Stack]] |
| **A** | Spaced repetition (Anki) | Not a biohack but the most evidence-backed learning tool |
| **A** | Cold exposure (pre-study) | Norepinephrine burst; focus and attention enhancement |
| **B** | Creatine monohydrate | Memory SMD 0.31; vegetarians get more benefit |
| **B** | Bacopa monnieri | Long-term memory; 4–12 weeks to see effect |
| **B** | Transcranial photobiomodulation | LLLT on skull; early pilot data for working memory |
| **C** | Racetams (piracetam, aniracetam) | Weak human evidence; popular in nootropic communities |
| **C** | Microdosing psychedelics | Anecdotal; recent trials show mostly placebo effect |
| **D** | "Brain training" apps (Lumosity) | No transfer to real-world cognition in RCTs |

## Category 9: Longevity & Anti-Aging Technologies

| Tier | Intervention / Product | Notes |
|------|----------------------|-------|
| **A** | Regular blood testing (every 6–12 months) | Biomarker tracking; Function Health, LabCorp |
| **A** | Rapamycin (off-label) | mTOR inhibition; strong animal longevity data; human evidence emerging |
| **A** | Metformin (off-label in healthy people) | AMPK activation; mixed new RCT data; consult physician |
| **B** | NMN / NR (NAD+ precursors) | NAD+ declines with age; supplementation raises NAD+ but longevity translation unproven |
| **B** | Biological age testing (TruAge, Levine clock) | Methylation-based epigenetic clocks; $300–$500 |
| **B** | Whole-body MRI scan (Prenuvo) | Cancer screening + cardiovascular; $2,500 |
| **B** | DEXA scan | Body composition gold standard; $50–150 |
| **C** | Senolytics (dasatinib + quercetin) | Clears senescent cells; human evidence very early |
| **C** | Hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT) | Reversal of telomere shortening claimed; expensive |
| **C** | Plasma young blood transfusion | No proven human benefit; Ambrosia shut down by FDA |
| **D** | GH (growth hormone) injections for anti-aging | Significant risks; limited longevity benefit in healthy adults |

## Category 10: Gut Health

| Tier | Intervention | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **S** | High-fiber diet (30+ plants/week) | Microbiome diversity; RCT-confirmed benefit |
| **A** | Fermented foods (kefir, kimchi, sauerkraut) | Stanford RCT: increases microbiome diversity better than fiber alone |
| **A** | Probiotics (Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium strains) | Strain-specific evidence; best for GI conditions |
| **B** | Prebiotics (inulin, FOS, GOS) | Feeds microbiome; combine with fiber diet |
| **B** | Digestive enzymes | Useful for diagnosed enzyme insufficiency or bloating |
| **C** | At-home microbiome testing (Viome, Zoe) | Highly variable accuracy; personalization benefit unclear |
| **D** | Colon cleanses / colonics | No evidence; disrupts microbiome |
