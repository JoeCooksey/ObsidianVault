# Two-Minute Rule

The principle that any new habit should be scaled down to take **less than two minutes** to start. The rule is not about doing the habit in two minutes — it is about making the *entry point* trivially easy.

## Core Idea

> "When you start a new habit, it should take less than two minutes to do."

The insight is that habits are entry points, not finish lines. The most important thing is to show up consistently — the quantity and quality can be scaled later.

**Examples of scaling:**
| Intended Habit | Two-Minute Version |
|---|---|
| Read every night | Pick up the book and read one page |
| Study for an exam | Open the textbook and read one paragraph |
| Exercise daily | Put on workout shoes and walk to the gym |
| Practice guitar | Pick up the guitar and play one chord |
| Write daily | Open the document and write one sentence |

## Decisive Moments

Related concept: **Decisive Moments** are the small choice-forks throughout the day that disproportionately shape the afternoon and evening.

> The moment you sit on the couch when you get home determines the next two hours.
> The moment you order at a restaurant determines your caloric intake for the meal.

The Two-Minute Rule makes the *good decisive moment* trivially easy to execute. The first two minutes of any action are the gateway.

## The Gateway Habit

Habits have a **gateway structure**: the start of the habit is not the habit itself, but rather the consistent ritual that makes the habit more likely to occur. Once you are in the motion, completing the full habit has low marginal resistance.

This connects to [[Limbic Friction]]: the hardest moment is the *transition into the behavior*, not the behavior itself. Reducing the transition cost to two minutes makes even high-friction tasks accessible on low-motivation days.

## Mastering the Art of Showing Up

Clear's framing: the goal at the beginning is not to perform the habit perfectly. It is to **cast votes for your identity** as someone who does this thing. Even a two-minute version of the habit counts as a vote.

This connects to [[Identity-Based vs Goal-Based Habits]]: each execution of the two-minute version is evidence that you are the kind of person who studies, exercises, reads, etc.

## Scaling Protocol

Once the two-minute habit is consistent (automatic), scale up gradually:
1. Phase 1: Master showing up (2 min / session)
2. Phase 2: Extend the time slightly (5–10 min)
3. Phase 3: Full habit (30–60 min or as appropriate)

Never skip Phase 1. The identity evidence accumulates first; the substance follows.
