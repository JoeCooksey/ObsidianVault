# Strength Training for Cyclists

Resistance training integrated into a cyclist's annual training plan, primarily during the off-season, to build muscular foundations that transfer to power and efficiency on the bike.

## Why Strength Matters for Cyclists
- Each pedal stroke is a single-leg force application — stronger legs = greater force per stroke
- Heavy strength training improves cycling economy (less O2 cost at same power output)
- Meta-analysis of 17 studies (European Journal of Applied Physiology): cyclists who added heavy lifting saw significant improvements in cycling efficiency **and** time trial performance
- Reduces injury risk (knee, hip, lower back) by strengthening stabilizers neglected during pure cycling

## Four-Phase Gym Progression (Off-Season Arc)

### Phase 1 — Cycling-Specific Adaptation (2–3 weeks)
- Purpose: prepare tendons, connective tissue, motor patterns for lifting load
- Low weight, high reps (15–20), single-leg emphasis
- Exercises: split squat, step-up, single-leg press, hip hinge, core activation
- Frequency: 2×/week

### Phase 2 — Hypertrophy (4–6 weeks)
- Purpose: increase muscle cross-sectional area for future strength/power
- Moderate weight, moderate reps (8–12), higher total volume
- Exercises: back squat, Romanian deadlift, leg press, hip thrust, lunges
- Rep schemes: 4 × 10, 3 × 12
- Frequency: 2–3×/week

### Phase 3 — Strength (4–6 weeks)
- Purpose: increase maximum force production
- Heavy weight, lower reps (3–6), longer rest periods (2–3 min)
- Exercises: back squat, deadlift, single-leg squat
- Rep schemes: 5 × 5, 4 × 4
- Frequency: 2×/week (fatigue management critical)
- **Pair with neuromuscular sprint work on bike**

### Phase 4 — Power (3–4 weeks, late off-season)
- Purpose: convert max strength to cycling-specific explosive power
- Moderate-heavy weight, explosive velocity (1–3 reps), plyometrics
- Exercises: trap bar deadlift (explosive), box jumps, bounding, leg press (fast concentric)
- **Directly coupled to sprint workouts on bike to transfer neuromuscular pattern**

## Seasonal Integration

| Season | Gym Phase | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Oct–Nov | Adaptation + Hypertrophy | 2–3×/week | Ride volume low |
| Nov–Dec | Hypertrophy → Strength | 2–3×/week | Ride volume building |
| Jan–Feb | Strength → Power | 2×/week | Start pairing with bike sprints |
| Mar–Apr | Power (tapering) | 1–2×/week | Ride intensity rising |
| May–Jun | Maintenance | 1×/week | Race season — minimal gym |
| Jun–Aug | None or 1×/week | Optional | Competition focus |

## Key Exercises for Cyclists
- **Back Squat / Goblet Squat** — quad/glute power base
- **Romanian Deadlift** — posterior chain (glutes, hamstrings) — neglected in cycling
- **Single-Leg Press / Bulgarian Split Squat** — cycling-specific unilateral loading
- **Hip Thrust / Glute Bridge** — glute activation for pedal stroke top
- **Copenhagen Plank** — adductor strength (knee tracking)
- **Core: Plank, Dead Bug, Pallof Press** — power transfer from core to pedals

## Timing Workouts Around Rides
- Strength session **after** a hard bike ride (not before) — or on separate easy days
- Allow 24 hours between heavy lifting and high-intensity bike sessions
- Hypertrophy/strength phases will cause temporary cycling performance dip — expected
