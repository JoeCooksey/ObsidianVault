# Toxic Positivity

The cultural pressure to suppress, dismiss, or override difficult emotions in favor of forced optimism. [[Susan David]] identifies this as a primary driver of poor mental health, burnout, and relationship damage.

## The Core Claim

Forcing positivity in the face of genuine difficulty does not make people more resilient — it makes them *less* resilient. The research supports the opposite of the cultural narrative:

> "When we force ourselves beyond our difficult emotional experiences — we may think we're just being strong, just getting on with it — but over time we are much more likely to experience lower levels of mental health, burnout, and real cost to our relationships."

## Common Forms

- "Look on the bright side"
- "At least you have X — other people have it worse"
- "Just think positive thoughts — negativity will manifest a bad reality"
- "You need to move on / get over it"
- "Be strong"
- Returning to school/work two days after a parent dies and "not dropping a single grade" (David's own experience)

All of these communicate the same message: *your difficult emotions are not welcome here.*

## Why It Backfires

Suppressed emotions do not disappear — they are stored and expressed elsewhere:

1. **Internal cost**: unprocessed pain drives compensatory behaviors (numbing, avoidance, perfectionism)
2. **Mental health cost**: sustained suppression is correlated with lower wellbeing and higher rates of burnout
3. **Relational cost**: people who suppress emotions become less authentic and harder to connect with
4. **Rigidity cost**: the habit of suppression prevents developing the emotional processing skills needed for resilience (see [[Emotional Rigidity]])

## The Broken Narrative

The popular framework — "negative emotions are bad, positive emotions are good" — leads people who feel "negative" emotions to suppress them and feel ashamed of them. This is both empirically wrong and harmful. See [[Emotions as Functional]].

> "There is something inhuman and profoundly wrong in the way we are navigating and seeing the essence of our humanity."

## What Replaces It

Not pessimism. Not wallowing. But **[[Gentle Acceptance]]** — acknowledging what you feel without judgment — followed by [[Emotion Granularity]] and [[Defusion Language]] to move through the emotion toward values-connected action.

The goal is not to feel good. It is to feel *accurately* — and then act wisely.
