# Daily Mobility Routine

A science-backed AM + PM protocol addressing the 5 key mobility bottlenecks: hip flexors, thoracic spine, hamstrings, ankle dorsiflexion, and shoulder/chest.

## Why Mobility Matters

Mobility = the ability to move joints through their full range of motion **under control**. It differs from flexibility (passive tissue length) — mobility requires strength through the range. Tight hip flexors, immobile thoracic spines, and restricted ankles are the most common upstream sources of lower back pain, knee pain, and shoulder dysfunction.

## The 5 Priority Mobility Targets

| Area | Why It Gets Tight | If Ignored |
|---|---|---|
| Hip flexors | Sitting (iliopsoas shortens at 90° hip flexion) | Anterior pelvic tilt, lower back pain |
| Thoracic spine | Forward head, keyboard posture | Neck/shoulder pain, poor breathing |
| Hamstrings | Sitting, cycling, running (eccentric only) | Posterior pelvic tilt, knee strain |
| Ankle dorsiflexion | Shoes, sedentary lifestyle | Limited squat depth, Achilles tendinitis |
| Shoulder/chest | Keyboard posture, bench press bias | Impingement, internal rotation loss |

## Morning Protocol (5–10 min — Dynamic Only)

**Rule:** Dynamic (movement-based) only in the AM. Never hold long static stretches cold — muscle temperature is lowest at waking, and static stretching before activation reduces power output by ~8% and sprint speed by 1.2%.

| Exercise | Sets × Duration | Targets |
|---|---|---|
| Cat-Cow | 2 × 10 reps (slow) | Spinal flexion/extension, lumbar decompression |
| 90/90 Hip Switch | 2 × 60 sec/side | Hip internal + external rotation |
| World's Greatest Stretch | 3 reps/side (flowing) | Hip flexor + thoracic rotation + hamstring |
| Ankle Wall Stretch | 2 × 30 sec/side | Ankle dorsiflexion |
| Deep Squat Hold | 2 × 30 sec (hold door or rack) | Hip, groin, ankle combined |

**Total time: ~7 minutes**

### World's Greatest Stretch — Step-by-Step
1. Start in plank position, body straight
2. Step right foot outside right hand (deep lunge)
3. Drop left elbow toward the floor or hover it — hold 2 sec (feel left hip flexor)
4. Rotate right arm up toward ceiling, eyes follow hand — hold 2 sec (feel thoracic open)
5. Return right hand to floor, step back to plank
6. Repeat on left side — that is 1 rep
7. Do 3 reps/side as a flow

*Covers hip flexors + thoracic rotation + hamstrings + ankle in ~90 seconds. Highest ROI stretch in existence.*

## Evening Protocol (5–12 min — Static Only)

**Rule:** Static holds (30–90 sec each) in the PM. Body temperature peaks 4–8 PM — tissues are most extensible. Slow static stretching activates the parasympathetic nervous system, lowering cortisol and heart rate — directly improving sleep onset.

**Timing:** 30–60 minutes before bed (not the last thing before sleeping).

| Exercise                 | Hold        | Targets                                   |
| ------------------------ | ----------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Child's Pose             | 60 sec      | Hip flexors, thoracic spine, shoulder     |
| Supine Twist             | 60 sec/side | Thoracic rotation, glutes, IT band        |
| Pigeon Pose / Figure-4   | 90 sec/side | Piriformis, glutes, hip external rotators |
| Doorway Chest Stretch    | 30 sec/side | Pectorals, anterior shoulder              |
| Supine Hamstring Stretch | 60 sec/leg  | Hamstrings, posterior chain               |
| Legs-Up-Wall             | 3–5 min     | Nervous system reset, lymphatic drainage  |

**Total time: ~12 minutes**

### Notes on Individual Stretches
- **Supine Twist**: Lie on back, pull one knee across body to the opposite side while arms extend in a T — keep both shoulders on the floor; rotate the head opposite to the knee for cervical decompression
- **Pigeon / Figure-4**: If pigeon is too intense, use figure-4 supine (cross one ankle over the opposite knee, pull both toward chest) — same piriformis target, lower intensity
- **Legs-Up-Wall**: Literal — slide hips to wall, extend legs straight up; activates vagus nerve, dumps cortisol, resets the nervous system before sleep; 3–5 min minimum

## Science Notes

| Finding | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Structural flexibility change requires 8 weeks minimum | 5–10 min/muscle/week for 8 consecutive weeks (multiple RCTs) |
| Evening body temp peaks → more extensible tissues | Sit-and-reach + spinal extension peak 4–8 PM (PLOS ONE 2013) |
| Morning dynamic stretching → larger neuromuscular gains | Jump height +10.6% AM vs. +6.0% PM (Tandfonline 2018) |
| PM static stretching improves sleep quality | 2016 systematic review; meditative movement → sleep quality |
| 5 min/day beats 20 min/week | Frequency and consistency are primary predictors of long-term gains |

## Joe-Specific Notes

- **Cycling emphasis**: hip flexors (chronic 90° position on bike) and ankle dorsiflexion are your highest-priority targets
- **Desk study emphasis**: thoracic spine and chest opening are critical given long laptop study sessions
- **Integration**: morning mobility can precede or follow morning sunlight (see [[Sleep Habits Tier List]]); evening routine pairs with wind-down journaling
- **Progression**: after 8 weeks of daily consistency, layer in loaded stretching (e.g., weighted hip flexor stretch, active pigeon) to build mobility strength, not just passive range
