# Food Health Tier List — Meats and Seafood

Ranking criteria: **pure healthiness** — protein quality (complete amino acids), omega-3 content, saturated fat level, mercury load (for fish), processing level, and disease association. Sources: American Heart Association, Harvard T.H. Chan, Ohio State Health, Cleveland Clinic, FDA seafood guidelines.

Weekly target: ≥2 servings of fatty fish (AHA recommendation); limit red meat to ≤2 servings/week; avoid processed meats.

---

## S Tier — Optimal Protein Sources

| Meat / Seafood | Per 3 oz Serving | Why |
|----------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Wild Salmon** | 25g protein, 1.8g EPA+DHA omega-3 | King of protein foods; highest omega-3 of large fish; vitamin D (rare in food), B12, selenium; CVD prevention evidence is unambiguous; low mercury |
| **Sardines** | 21g protein, 2g omega-3 | Highest omega-3 per serving AND lowest mercury of any oily fish; calcium (you eat the bones), vitamin D, B12; cheap and shelf-stable |
| **Anchovies** | 24g protein, 1.7g omega-3 | Same omega-3 profile as sardines; very low mercury; high calcium; intense umami makes them useful in small quantities |

---

## A Tier — Excellent Protein, Eat Often

| Meat / Seafood | Per 3 oz Serving | Why |
|----------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Atlantic Mackerel** | 22g protein, 1.5g omega-3 | More omega-3 than salmon per gram; B12, selenium; **Note: King Mackerel is high mercury — only Atlantic/Pacific** |
| **Herring** | 20g protein, 1.7g omega-3 | More omega-3 than tuna; B12, selenium; lower mercury than most oily fish; smoked herring is higher sodium |
| **Rainbow Trout** | 23g protein, 1.0g omega-3 | Low mercury; good omega-3; B12, phosphorus; farmed trout is safe and omega-3 rich |
| **Chicken Breast** | 26g protein, 2g fat | Leanest common meat; only 2g total fat, <1g saturated fat; B vitamins (niacin, B6); selenium; versatile |
| **Turkey Breast** | 25g protein, 2g fat | Near-identical to chicken breast; slightly more B12; dark turkey meat has more protein than dark chicken meat with similar saturated fat |

---

## B Tier — Good Protein, Some Caveats

| Meat / Seafood | Per 3 oz Serving | Why |
|----------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Canned Light Tuna** | 22g protein, 0.26g omega-3 | Low mercury (skipjack); good protein; decent omega-3; budget-friendly; limit to 2–3 servings/week for mercury caution |
| **Shrimp** | 18g protein, 0.3g fat | Very lean; low mercury; iodine, selenium, B12; formerly avoided for cholesterol — evidence shows dietary cholesterol has minor impact |
| **Bison / Game Meat** | 22g protein, 2g fat | 25% more protein than beef; significantly less saturated fat; wild game also lower in inflammatory omega-6; grass-only diet |
| **Grass-Fed Beef (lean cuts)** | 21g protein, 5g fat | Higher omega-3 than conventional beef; conjugated linoleic acid (CLA) — anti-inflammatory; iron, zinc, B12; 2–3× more omega-3 than grain-fed |
| **Pork Tenderloin** | 22g protein, 3g fat | Leanest cut of pork; often leaner than chicken thigh; B vitamins (particularly thiamin); underrated lean meat |
| **Oysters** | 8g protein per 3oz | Zinc (highest food source — one oyster covers DV); selenium, vitamin D, B12, omega-3; EPA/DHA; liver health; modest serving covers many deficiencies |

---

## C Tier — Adequate Protein, Notable Downsides

| Meat / Seafood | Per 3 oz Serving | Why |
|----------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Conventional Beef (lean cuts)** | 21g protein, 7–9g fat | Quality protein, iron (heme iron — most bioavailable form), zinc, B12; but higher saturated fat; limit to 1–2/week; grass-fed preferred |
| **Lamb** | 21g protein, 8g fat | Iron, zinc, B12, selenium; higher saturated fat than beef per calorie; conjugated linoleic acid (CLA) present; limit frequency |
| **Whole Chicken (with skin)** | 21g protein, 10g fat | Skin adds significant saturated fat; skin-off brings it to A tier; dark meat more flavorful but more fat |
| **Albacore Tuna (canned white)** | 20g protein, 0.7g omega-3 | Higher omega-3 than light tuna but also 3× the mercury; FDA recommends max 1 serving/week for adults |
| **Tilapia** | 21g protein, 1g fat | Very lean; but extremely low omega-3 — some studies show more omega-6 than omega-3, potentially pro-inflammatory at high intake; fine occasionally |
| **Pork Chops / Ribs** | 22g protein, 9–15g fat | Fattier cuts of pork; protein is good but fat load diminishes health value; not a concern occasionally |
| **Liver (beef/chicken)** | 26g protein, 5g fat | Most nutrient-dense organ meat — B12 (3400% DV!), iron, folate, vitamin A (risk of over-supplementation); vitamin A toxicity if eaten >1/week; C tier due to this risk |

## D Tier — Processed or Poor Nutritional Profile

| Meat / Seafood | Why |
|----------------|-----|
| **Deli Meats (ham, salami, bologna)** | High sodium (600–1200mg/serving); nitrites → nitrosamines; WHO Group 1 carcinogens (processed meat); minimal nutritional upside vs. whole cuts |
| **Bacon** | High saturated fat; nitrosamines from nitrite preservatives; 400–600mg sodium/2 strips; cancer-associated (colorectal) |
| **Sausages / Bratwurst** | High saturated fat, high sodium, often contain fillers; processed meat classification |
| **Imitation Crab (Surimi)** | Ultra-processed fish paste; high sodium, low protein per calorie vs. real seafood; added starch, flavoring |

---

## F Tier — Active Harm

| Meat / Seafood | Why |
|----------------|-----|
| **Hot Dogs / Vienna Sausages** | WHO Group 1 carcinogen category (processed meat); nitrosamines; highest sodium per calorie of any meat product; virtually no quality protein per dollar |
| **Fast Food Burgers / Fried Chicken** | Trans fats from industrial frying; ultra-processed patties; high sodium (1000–2000mg+); high calorie density with low nutrient return |
| **King Mackerel / Swordfish / Shark** | Mercury levels high enough that FDA advises all adults to avoid; accumulation can cause neurological damage; not worth any nutritional upside |

---

## Key Principles for Meats

1. **Fatty fish dominates** — salmon, sardines, mackerel, herring carry omega-3 that no other protein food matches
2. **Omega-3 content is the primary differentiator** in protein foods; it's what most people are deficient in
3. **Poultry > beef > pork** for cardiovascular safety when choosing land animals
4. **Processed = automatic D/F tier** regardless of base meat; the processing creates carcinogenic compounds
5. **Mercury accumulates up the food chain** — bigger, older predatory fish (swordfish, king mackerel, shark) accumulate the most
6. **Grass-fed vs grain-fed matters** — omega-3 content in beef is directly tied to what the cow eats; grass-fed has 2–3× more
