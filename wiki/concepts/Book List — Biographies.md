# Book List — Biographies

> Biographies are the cheapest MBA + life coaching available. The best ones let you spend 10 hours inside the mind of the person you most want to understand.

## S-Tier — Transformative

- **Leonardo da Vinci** — Walter Isaacson — the ultimate polymath; curiosity as methodology; observation over assumption; shows that expertise is built, not born; ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Steve Jobs** — Walter Isaacson — obsessive design thinking, reality distortion field, vertical integration as philosophy; the costs and benefits of extreme vision
- **Elon Musk** — Ashlee Vance (2015) or Walter Isaacson (2023) — first-principles reasoning; physics-constrained ambition; extreme risk tolerance; Vance is more objective, Isaacson has more recent access

## A-Tier — High Value

- **Benjamin Franklin: An American Life** — Walter Isaacson — America's original entrepreneur; polymath autodidact; pragmatic virtue over idealism; self-invention as ongoing project
- **Alexander Hamilton** — Ron Chernow — immigrant striver who built America's financial system; ambition + genius + fatal flaw; essential American founding context
- **Napoleon: A Life** — Andrew Roberts — military genius + administrative brilliance + catastrophic hubris; most comprehensive single-volume Napoleon; 900 pages but worth it
- **Titan: The Life of John D. Rockefeller** — Ron Chernow — methodical accumulation → Standard Oil monopoly → unprecedented philanthropy; the complete capitalist arc
- **Einstein: His Life and Universe** — Walter Isaacson — thought experiments over calculation; imagination as the true scientific tool; patents office and all
- **The Wright Brothers** — David McCullough — two bicycle mechanics who changed the world through disciplined experimentation and no funding; underdog science

## B-Tier — Solid

- **Team of Rivals** — Doris Kearns Goodwin — Lincoln's genius for managing people who hated him; emotional intelligence under extreme pressure; reads fast for its length
- **The Power Broker** — Robert Caro — Robert Moses, urban power, how one unelected man shaped New York for 50 years; the definitive study of institutional power (1,300 pages — a serious investment)
- **Andrew Carnegie** — David Nasaw — rags to richest man in America to giving it all away; the original tech-billionaire-turned-philanthropist arc
- **Churchill: Walking with Destiny** — Andrew Roberts — comprehensive Churchill; leadership under existential pressure; rhetoric and resolve

## Reading Notes

- Isaacson biographies are the most readable; start with *da Vinci* or *Jobs*
- Chernow biographies are denser but more authoritative; *Hamilton* is the best entry
- Caro's *Power Broker* and his LBJ series are the pinnacle of American political biography — commit or skip
- Biographies of scientists (Feynman, Einstein, Darwin) also belong here — cross-reference [[Book List — Science]]
