# Book List — Self-Help, Discipline, and Productivity

> Curated tier list for building systems, mental toughness, and sustained output. Atomic Habits already ingested — this list extends it.

## S-Tier — Install These Operating Systems

### Atomic Habits — James Clear (2018) ← ALREADY INGESTED ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Four Laws of Behavior Change; Identity-based habits; 1% compounding
- See [[Atomic Habits]] for full summary

### Can't Hurt Me — David Goggins (2018)
- David Goggins: 300-lb pest control worker → Navy SEAL → ultramarathon record holder → Army Ranger → pull-up world record
- **The 40% Rule**: when your mind says stop, you're only at 40% of your actual capacity
- **Accountability Mirror**: brutal daily self-honesty; write your insecurities and failures on sticky notes
- **Callusing the Mind**: deliberately do things that suck to make hard things normal
- **Cookie Jar**: mental repository of past hard things you've done — access when quitting feels justified
- Joe context: high overlap with training 6 days/week — mental toughness tools apply directly to cycling workouts and study sprints

### Deep Work — Cal Newport (2016)
- Core thesis: the ability to focus without distraction on a cognitively demanding task is becoming rare and simultaneously more valuable
- **Deep Work vs. Shallow Work**: deep = cognitively demanding, distraction-free, pushes skill ceiling; shallow = logistical, replicable, low value
- **4 Philosophies of Deep Work**: Monastic (cut everything), Bimodal (deep/shallow seasons), Rhythmic (daily fixed block), Journalistic (squeeze whenever possible)
- **Depth Budget**: track deep work hours per day; 4 hours is often the ceiling for cognitively demanding work
- Joe context: EE self-study, LTspice, Python, circuit analysis — all require deep work; shallow work (scrolling, email) competes for the same blocks

## A-Tier — Read Within 12 Months

### The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People — Stephen Covey (1989)
- Framework: be → do → have (not have → do → be); character ethics over personality ethics
- Habit 1: Be Proactive — you choose your response; stimulus → response gap
- Habit 2: Begin with the End in Mind — personal mission statement; clarity on values before action
- Habit 3: Put First Things First — time matrix (Important/Urgent quadrants); Q2 is where growth lives
- Habit 7: Sharpen the Saw — renewal in four dimensions (physical, social, mental, spiritual)
- Habit 4–6: Think Win-Win → Seek First to Understand → Synergize — interdependence; relational intelligence
- Dense but evergreen; every year you're older, a different habit becomes the most relevant one

### Essentialism: The Disciplined Pursuit of Less — Greg McKeown (2014)
- "Not everything matters equally." Essentialism is the ruthless elimination of everything that is not essential
- **The Essentialist's way**: less but better; invest in fewer things that return dramatically more
- **The Non-Essentialist trap**: yes to everything → spread too thin → marginal in many areas → excellent in none
- **Trade-off is a feature**: you can do anything but not everything; saying yes to one thing is always saying no to another
- Essential question: "Is this the most important thing I could be doing right now?"
- Joe context: year 1 ASU — high temptation to spread across clubs, projects, side-hustles; Essentialism is the antidote

### Man's Search for Meaning — Viktor Frankl (1946)
- Holocaust survival narrative + logotherapy (meaning-based psychotherapy)
- Core insight: even in the most dehumanizing circumstances, humans retain the freedom to choose their attitude
- **Will to Meaning > Will to Pleasure or Power**: Frankl's direct challenge to Freud and Adler
- **Tragic Optimism**: find meaning in suffering, guilt, and death rather than despite it
- One of the most important books ever written; 140 pages; re-reads infinitely

### Make Your Bed — Admiral William McRaven (2017)
- Based on UT Austin commencement speech (80M views on YouTube)
- 10 lessons from Navy SEAL BUD/S training applied to life
- Key lesson: start every day by completing a task (making your bed) — sets the tone for the day; small wins compound
- Lesson 2: find someone to paddle with — no one succeeds alone
- Lesson 6: slide down the obstacle head first — take calculated risks when the reward is worth it
- Short (128 pages), powerful, rereads well

### 12 Rules for Life — Jordan Peterson (2018)
- Rule 1: Stand up straight with your shoulders back — lobster dominance hierarchies; posture as self-respect signal
- Rule 4: Compare yourself to who you were yesterday, not to who someone else is today
- Rule 6: Set your house in perfect order before you criticize the world
- Rule 11: Do not bother children when they are skateboarding — let people take risks; growth requires danger
- Dense; rewards slow reading; Rule 1, 4, 6, and 10 are highest priority

## Core Mental Models

- **40% Rule** (Goggins): you're only at 40% when you feel done — the remaining 60% is mental
- **Deep Work Hours Are Finite**: 4 hours/day is a hard ceiling for most; protect them like gold
- **Essentialism Trade-off**: every yes is a no to something else; choose deliberately
- **Meaning > Comfort** (Frankl): pursuing meaning produces more resilience than pursuing pleasure
- **Q2 Quadrant** (Covey): Important but Not Urgent is where all growth, relationships, and prevention live — most people never get there because Urgent consumes them
