# Book List — Economics

> Economics is the study of how people make decisions under scarcity. Understanding incentive structures is one of the most portable thinking tools available.

## S-Tier

- **Economics in One Lesson** — Henry Hazlitt — Bastiat's broken window systematized; the seen and the unseen effects of policy; 150 pages; reads in a sitting; ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Freakonomics** — Levitt & Dubner — incentives explain everything; economic thinking applied to unexpected domains (sumo wrestling, crack dealers, baby names); best gateway to economic reasoning

## A-Tier

- **The Worldly Philosophers** — Robert Heilbroner — history of economic thought from Smith to Keynes to Veblen; essential intellectual context for understanding where modern economics came from
- **Basic Economics** — Thomas Sowell — the most comprehensive economics primer for non-economists; no equations; 700 pages of first-principles reasoning; reads like extended Hazlitt
- **Poor Economics** — Banerjee & Duflo (Nobel 2019) — what actually reduces poverty; randomized controlled trials in development economics; evidence-based thinking at its best
- **The Undercover Economist** — Tim Harford — everyday economics explained accessibly; why Starbucks charges what it charges; asymmetric information; price discrimination

## B-Tier

- **The Wealth of Nations** — Adam Smith — the foundational text (dense); read Book I (division of labor) and Book IV (free trade argument) minimum; essential context for everything downstream
- **Capital in the Twenty-First Century** — Piketty — r > g; wealth inequality over centuries; read the introduction and data chapters; controversial but data-rich
