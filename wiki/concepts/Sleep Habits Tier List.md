# Sleep Habits Tier List

All habits, products, and actions that impact sleep — ranked S–F by evidence strength and effect size. Covers both **sleep improvers** and **sleep destroyers** in a single reference.

> [!key-insight] The hierarchy
> Circadian anchoring > sleep environment > behavioral habits > supplements. Products and pills amplify a good foundation; they cannot replace it.

---

## S-Tier — High Impact, Strong Evidence

| Habit / Action | Type | Key Mechanism | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Consistent wake time (7 days/week)** | Action | Anchors circadian clock; adenosine pressure | Single most important lever. Weekends included. |
| **Morning sunlight exposure (5–20 min)** | Action | +50% cortisol awakening response → sets 14–16 hr sleep timer | Overcast = 20–30 min; sunny = 5–10 min. No sunglasses. |
| **Cool bedroom (60–67°F / 15–20°C)** | Environment | Core body temp must drop ~1°C to initiate sleep | Coldest room in the house is ideal. |
| **Complete darkness** | Environment | Melatonin production requires darkness | Blackout curtains or sleep mask. Cover all LED indicator lights. |
| **CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia)** | Intervention | Breaks insomnia maintenance cycles at the cognitive/behavioral root | Gold standard for chronic insomnia; beats sleep meds long-term; 70–80% response rate. |

## A-Tier — Strong Evidence, High ROI

| Habit / Action | Type | Key Mechanism | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **No caffeine within 8–10 hrs of bed** | Action | Caffeine half-life 4–6 hrs; 400 mg 6 hrs before bed = −1 hr sleep | Cutoff = ~1–2 PM for most people |
| **No alcohol within 3 hrs of bed** | Action | Alcohol fragments REM, reduces sleep efficiency by ~1–2% | Even 1–2 drinks measurably reduce REM; lowers sleep efficiency |
| **No nicotine (especially evenings)** | Action | Most sleep-disruptive substance tested; +6 min WASO; −43 min in insomniacs | Evening nicotine = worst substance for sleep continuity |
| **Consistent wind-down routine (30–60 min)** | Action | Reduces arousal via conditioned stimulus; lowers cortisol | Dim lights, calm activity, no high-stimulation media |
| **Regular exercise (not within 2–3 hrs of bed)** | Action | Increases slow-wave sleep; reduces sleep onset; lowers anxiety | Morning or afternoon optimal; evening vigorous exercise = A-tier risk |
| **No screens within 60 min of bed** | Action | Blue light suppresses melatonin up to 23%; delays sleep onset | Use night mode or blue-light glasses if unavoidable |

## B-Tier — Good Evidence, Meaningful Effect

| Habit / Product | Type | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Magnesium glycinate (200–400 mg)** | Supplement | RCTs show improved sleep efficiency, reduced awakenings, lower cortisol | Take 30–60 min before bed. Oxide form poorly absorbed. See [[Sleep Optimization Supplements]] |
| **Melatonin (0.1–0.5 mg)** | Supplement | 400+ studies; −7–12 min sleep onset; best for phase-shifting | Dose is far lower than pharmacy packaging. 5–10 mg = overdose. |
| **White/brown noise machine** | Product | Masks acoustic disruptions that cause micro-awakenings | More effective than silence in noisy environments |
| **No large meals 2–3 hrs before bed** | Action | Digestion elevates core temp and can cause GERD, blocking sleep onset | Light snack OK; large high-fat or spicy meal = disruption |
| **Weighted blanket (15–25 lb)** | Product | Deep touch pressure → cortisol↓, serotonin↑, melatonin↑ | Flinders University review: significant improvement for insomnia |
| **Dim/red light in evenings** | Environment | Red/amber wavelengths minimally suppress melatonin vs. blue/white light | Smart bulbs set to warm color at sunset. Avoid overhead lights. |
| **Fruits, vegetables, and carbohydrates at dinner** | Action | 2025 NSF study: associated with lower sleep fragmentation index | Mediterranean-style evening meal pattern |

## C-Tier — Moderate or Mixed Evidence

| Habit / Product | Type | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L-theanine (200–400 mg)** | Supplement | Systematic review of 13 RCTs; modest improvement in sleep quality and satisfaction | Best combined with magnesium; doesn't cause sedation |
| **Blue light blocking glasses** | Product | Reduces melatonin suppression from screens; weaker than cutting screens entirely | Use amber-lens glasses, not clear "gaming" glasses |
| **Tart cherry juice / concentrate** | Supplement | Multiple small RCTs; contains melatonin; improves duration + efficiency | 240 mL twice daily in studies; relatively low melatonin content |
| **Lavender aromatherapy** | Product | Pilot RCTs show improved perceived sleep quality; weak objective data | Best as ritual signal, not a standalone fix |
| **Valerian root (300–600 mg)** | Supplement | Mixed meta-analyses; some benefit in insomnia; high placebo component | Well-tolerated; more evidence than most herbals |
| **Power nap (10–20 min before 3 PM)** | Action | Improves alertness without disrupting nighttime sleep | Keep to <20 min and before 3 PM; otherwise disrupts sleep pressure |
| **Hot shower/bath 60–90 min before bed** | Action | Induces skin vasodilation → core temp drops faster after shower | Counter-intuitive: the drop after warming accelerates sleep onset |

## D-Tier — Weak or Indirect Evidence

| Habit / Product | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Ashwagandha KSM-66** | Supplement | Reduces stress → may improve sleep secondarily; not a direct sleep agent; small industry-funded trials |
| **CBD for sleep** | Supplement | Weak direct sleep evidence; may reduce anxiety that disrupts sleep; inconsistent formulations |
| **Chamomile tea** | Supplement | Mild anxiolytic effect; mostly placebo/ritual value; negligible RCT data |
| **Cold shower immediately before bed** | Action | Lowers core temp short-term but stimulating; no strong sleep trial data |
| **Diphenhydramine (Benadryl/ZzzQuil)** | Product | Causes sedation but degrades sleep quality; anticholinergic; cognitive impairment with regular use. Do not use. |
| **Journaling before bed** | Action | May reduce pre-sleep cognitive arousal; evidence is mostly observational |
| **Oura/WHOOP sleep tracking** | Product | Awareness tool only; tracking does not improve sleep directly; may cause "orthosomnia" anxiety |
| **High-dose melatonin (5–10 mg)** | Supplement | Pharmacological overdose; morning grogginess; may suppress natural production long-term |

## F-Tier — Active Sleep Destroyers

Habits and products that reliably wreck sleep. Avoid entirely or reduce aggressively.

| Destroyer | Quantified Effect | Category |
|---|---|---|
| **Irregular sleep schedule (social jetlag)** | Delays melatonin, disrupts circadian timing; compounds every day missed | Schedule |
| **Bright overhead lights after dark** | Suppresses melatonin; signals daytime to the brain | Light |
| **Screens/blue light within 1 hr of bed** | Melatonin suppression up to 23%; delays sleep onset significantly | Light |
| **Caffeine within 6 hrs of bed** | −60+ min sleep time even at 400 mg dose (landmark 2013 study) | Substance |
| **Evening alcohol (any amount)** | Fragments sleep architecture; destroys REM; rebounds as insomnia in second half of night | Substance |
| **Smoking / nicotine (evening)** | Highest WASO increase of all substances; −43 min sleep in insomniacs | Substance |
| **Late-night large meals** | Core temp elevation, GERD risk; impairs sleep onset | Diet |
| **Hot bedroom (>70°F)** | Body cannot drop core temp for sleep initiation | Environment |
| **Naps >30 min or after 4 PM** | Reduces adenosine pressure; delays sleep onset at night | Schedule |
| **Chronic stress unmanaged** | Evening cortisol spike suppresses melatonin; activates fight-or-flight | Psychology |
| **Overtraining without recovery** | Elevates cortisol and sympathetic tone; wrecks REM and deep sleep | Exercise |

## Quick-Reference Stack (Evidence-Optimized Protocol)

```
MORNING
├── Wake at the same time every day (no exceptions)
├── Sunlight 5–20 min within 30 min of waking (outside, no sunglasses)
└── Delay caffeine 90–120 min after waking (let cortisol peak naturally)

AFTERNOON
├── Caffeine cutoff at 1–2 PM
├── Exercise window: ideally before 5 PM
└── Power nap if needed: 10–20 min, before 3 PM

EVENING (2–3 hrs before bed)
├── Dim lights → warm/red light only
├── Stop screens or switch to blue-light glasses
├── No large meals, no alcohol, no nicotine
└── Magnesium glycinate 200–400 mg

WIND-DOWN (30–60 min before bed)
├── Low-stimulation activity (reading, stretching, journaling)
├── Bedroom: cool (65°F), dark (blackout curtains or mask), quiet or white noise
└── Melatonin 0.5 mg if needed (circadian shifting or irregular schedule)

IN BED
└── Same bedtime ± 30 min every night
```

## Scoring Rubric

| Tier | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **S** | Multiple RCTs or meta-analyses; large effect size (>30 min sleep improvement or >5% efficiency); mechanistically understood |
| **A** | Multiple studies; clear mechanism; consistent effect; high practical impact |
| **B** | RCT evidence with meaningful effect; some heterogeneity in results |
| **C** | Mixed evidence; smaller effects; works better as complement than standalone |
| **D** | Mostly observational or very small trials; indirect mechanism; low effect size |
| **F** | Actively harmful; measurably disrupts sleep architecture or circadian timing |
