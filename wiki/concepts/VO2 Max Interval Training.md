# VO2 Max Interval Training

High-intensity interval sessions specifically designed to push time spent at or above 90% of VO2 max, the primary driver of aerobic ceiling adaptation.

## What VO2 Max Represents
VO2 max is the maximum volume of oxygen the body can consume during maximal exercise. It is the single strongest predictor of endurance performance capacity. Higher VO2 max = bigger engine.

## Interval Protocols

### Classic VO2 Max Intervals
| Parameter | Range |
|---|---|
| Duration per interval | 2–5 minutes |
| Intensity | 106–120% of FTP |
| Recovery ratio | 1:1 (equal work:rest) |
| Sessions per week | 2 (maximum, 36–48 hrs apart) |
| Total time-in-zone per session | 15–20 minutes |
| Block length | 2–3 weeks |

**Example Session**: 6 × 3 min @ 115% FTP, 3 min easy spin recovery between. Total: 18 min in zone.

### Short Interval Variant (research-supported)
- Intervals: 30 sec ON / 15 sec OFF ("30/15s")
- Intensity: all-out effort
- Finding: short intervals accrue *more* time above 90% VO2 max than longer intervals at same target power — greater VO2 max stimulus per session

### Longer Threshold Intervals
- 2 × 20 min @ FTP (Functional Threshold Power)
- Primarily trains FTP, not VO2 max directly
- Useful in build phase before VO2 max blocks

## When to Train VO2 Max

### Seasonal Timing
- **Not in base phase** (Oct–Dec): body not ready, recovery cost too high
- **Late build phase** (Mar–Apr): foundation built, introduce first VO2 max blocks
- **Pre-competition** (Apr–May): primary focus, 2–3 block cycles
- **In-season** (Jun–Aug): maintenance only — 1 session/week, no full blocks

### Block Structure
A VO2 max block = 2–3 weeks of 2×/week VO2 max sessions, followed by 1 recovery week. After recovery, VO2 max is tested or reassessed.

If doing a 16-week build toward peak:
- Weeks 1–10: sweet spot base (threshold work)
- Weeks 11–16: VO2 max interval phase

## Key Research Findings
- 2024 study: time spent above 90% VO2 max during intervals is the primary driver of VO2 max gains — not total session duration or power
- Short intervals (2–3 min) outperform long intervals (10–15 min) for VO2 max stimulus because they allow athletes to sustain true high intensity throughout
- Block periodization of VO2 max work produces greater gains than year-round low-dose distribution

## Cautions
- Requires 36–48 hours recovery between sessions
- More than 2×/week leads to overreaching without added benefit
- Must be built on aerobic base — high VO2 max work on poor base is inefficient and injury-prone
- Stop block if resting HR is elevated >5 bpm from baseline
