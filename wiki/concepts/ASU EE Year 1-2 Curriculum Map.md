# ASU EE Year 1–2 Curriculum Map

The Electrical Engineering BSE at ASU front-loads math and physics in Year 1. No EE-specific courses appear until Term 3A (Year 2). This creates a window for self-directed EE skill-building in Year 1.

## Course Sequence

| Term | Courses | EE Relevance |
|------|---------|--------------|
| 1A | MAT 265 (Calc I), FSE 100 (Intro Engineering) | Foundations; team project in FSE 100 |
| 1B | MAT 266 (Calc II), ENG 101/105 | Technical writing, calculus depth |
| 2A | PHY 121/122 (Mechanics + Lab), CHM 114/116 | First lab course; mechanics foundation |
| 2B | MAT 267 (Calc III) | Multivariable and vector calc |
| 3A | PHY 131/132 (E&M + Lab), EEE 120 (Digital Design) | **First EE course** — digital logic, Boolean algebra |
| 3B | MAT 275 (Diff Equations), CSE 100/110 (Programming) | Diff eq critical for Circuits + Signals; first coding |
| 4A | EEE 202 (Circuits I) | **Pivot course** — uses LTspice + MATLAB |
| 4B | EEE 241 (Electromagnetics), MAT 343 (Linear Algebra) | EM theory; linear algebra for signals/controls |

## Key Insight
The gap between Term 1A and the first real EE course (EEE 120, Term 3A) is **four semesters**. Students who use this window to self-study LTspice, Python, breadboard circuits, and Git arrive at EEE 202 with a decisive advantage.

## FURI Opportunity
ASU's Fulton Undergraduate Research Initiative (FURI) is open to sophomores and above — a fresh goal to target after Year 1 for research lab experience.
