# Book List — Writing and Communication

> Writing is thinking made legible. The ability to write clearly is one of the highest-leverage skills in engineering, business, and life — and one of the least taught.

## S-Tier

- **On Writing Well** — William Zinsser — the definitive guide to clear nonfiction; every unnecessary word is stripped; writing as thinking; read and apply immediately; ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **The Elements of Style** — Strunk & White — 100 pages covering 80% of what you need; read once a year; the permanent reference

## A-Tier

- **Bird by Bird** — Anne Lamott — the creative process; "shitty first drafts" as the required starting point; writing as practice, not performance
- **Several Short Sentences About Writing** — Verlyn Klinkenborg — sentence-level mastery; teaches you to stop before you think you're done; deeply practical
- **Writing to Learn** — William Zinsser — writing across disciplines (science, math, history); how writing clarifies what you don't understand

## B-Tier

- **The Sense of Style** — Steven Pinker — cognitive science of clear writing; addresses the curse of knowledge directly; best modern update to Strunk & White
- **Word by Word** — Kory Stamper — how words enter the dictionary; history of language; for the word-obsessed

## Application for EE Students

- Technical writing (lab reports, FURI proposals, research papers) improves faster from *On Writing Well* than from any writing course
- The skill of "write the conclusion first, then the evidence" maps directly to engineering proposals and memos
- Clear writing → clearer thinking → cleaner code and cleaner circuit design
