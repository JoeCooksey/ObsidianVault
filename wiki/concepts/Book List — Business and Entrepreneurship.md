# Book List — Business and Entrepreneurship

> Business and strategy books for someone who may build companies or lead technical teams. Prioritized by insight density and timelessness over trend-chasing.

## S-Tier — The Canon

### Zero to One — Peter Thiel (2014)
- Most contrarian business book ever written; derived from Thiel's Stanford CS183 startup course
- **Core thesis**: competition is for losers; build a monopoly by creating something new (0→1), not copying (1→n)
- **The Contrarian Question**: "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?" — the startup version is "What valuable company is nobody building?"
- **Secrets**: there are still secrets in the world (things that are true but few people know); find a secret, build a company around it
- **Power Law**: venture portfolios (and careers) follow power laws — a small number of bets return more than all others combined; concentrate, don't diversify
- Joe context: most relevant once he has built real technical skills; reread at 22 when you're thinking about what to build

### Good to Great — Jim Collins (2001)
- 5-year research project comparing companies that made the jump from good to great vs. those that didn't
- **Level 5 Leadership**: the highest level is a paradoxical combination of personal humility + fierce professional will (not charisma)
- **Hedgehog Concept**: intersection of (1) what you're deeply passionate about, (2) what you can be best in the world at, (3) what drives your economic engine
- **Flywheel**: no single defining moment — sustained pushing in a consistent direction; the breakthrough is a result of consistent effort, not a sudden push
- **First Who, Then What**: get the right people on the bus before deciding where to go

### Shoe Dog — Phil Knight (2016)
- Nike origin memoir from Phil Knight; raw, honest, funny, painful
- **Best business autobiography ever written** — most business memoirs sanitize the story; Knight doesn't
- Themes: obsession over comfort, the near-death experience of every growing company, the importance of people who believe in you, luck ± preparation
- Written at 78, looking back; deeply humble about how much was luck; deeply clear about what wasn't

### The Lean Startup — Eric Ries (2011)
- Methodology for building companies under conditions of extreme uncertainty
- **Build-Measure-Learn loop**: the minimum unit of progress is a validated learning, not a feature shipped
- **MVP** (Minimum Viable Product): the smallest experiment that generates the most learning; not the smallest product
- **Pivot vs. Persevere**: systematic framework for deciding whether your hypothesis about the market is wrong or just your implementation
- **Vanity Metrics vs. Actionable Metrics**: downloads and page views vs. retention, conversion, referral rates

## A-Tier

### The Hard Thing About Hard Things — Ben Horowitz (2014)
- No theoretical frameworks; raw CEO lessons from someone who actually almost went bankrupt multiple times
- **"There's no recipe for leading a company through a crisis; there's only the lonely, difficult job of deciding."**
- Topics: laying off people, firing executives, wartime vs. peacetime CEOs, when to follow advice and when to ignore it
- Most honest book on what leadership in a real company actually feels like
- Read after Zero to One and Good to Great — this is the ground-level version

### The E-Myth Revisited — Michael Gerber (1995)
- Why 80% of small businesses fail: the founder is a skilled technician who doesn't know how to build a system
- **E-Myth** (Entrepreneurial Myth): assuming technical competence at a craft = ability to run a business that sells that craft
- The Three Roles: Entrepreneur (vision), Manager (order), Technician (execution) — most founders are 80% Technician
- **Work ON the business, not just IN it**: build systems that run without you; the business should be the product
- Joe context: highly relevant if he freelances or starts a side business in EE/tech

### Deep Work — Cal Newport (2016)
- (Also in productivity list) — in a business context: the rarest and most valuable professional skill is the ability to do cognitively demanding work without distraction
- Applied to business: organizations reward shallow-looking busyness; the people who can do deep work become disproportionately valuable

## Core Mental Models

- **Monopoly Thinking** (Thiel): competition destroys value; the goal is to build something so good you're the only option
- **Power Law** (Thiel/VC): in most domains, a small number of things (employees, products, investments) produce most of the value; find those and concentrate
- **Flywheel** (Collins): consistent, sustained effort in a consistent direction creates momentum that looks like sudden success from the outside
- **Level 5 Leadership** (Collins): humility + fierce will, not charisma; the loudest CEO in the room is rarely the most effective
- **Build-Measure-Learn** (Ries): every action is a hypothesis; test it with the smallest viable experiment; learn, iterate

## Entrepreneur's Reading Order
1. *Zero to One* — what to build and why
2. *The Lean Startup* — how to test whether you're right
3. *Good to Great* — how to build it well once validated
4. *The Hard Thing About Hard Things* — what it actually feels like
5. *Shoe Dog* — the emotional truth of building something real
