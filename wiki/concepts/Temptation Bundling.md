# Temptation Bundling

A strategy for making necessary-but-unattractive habits more appealing by pairing them with something immediately enjoyable. Operates on the Craving stage of the [[Habit Loop]].

## Formula

> **Pair an action you WANT to do with an action you NEED to do.**

Originated from a study by behavioral economist Katy Milkman (Wharton), who allowed people to listen to audiobooks only while at the gym. Gym attendance increased significantly.

## Examples

| WANT (Attractive) | NEED (Necessary) |
|---|---|
| Favorite podcast / playlist | Study session or exercise |
| Coffee | Morning journaling |
| Netflix episode | Cardio on the stationary bike |
| Catching up with a friend | Walk or outdoor run |
| Comfortable music | Doing taxes or admin work |

## Why It Works: The Dopamine Mechanism

Dopamine does not spike only at reward delivery — it spikes in **anticipation** of the reward (Wolfram Schultz, 1990s reward prediction error research). This anticipatory dopamine is the *craving* phase of the [[Habit Loop]].

By pairing a tempting activity with a necessary one:
1. The anticipatory dopamine from the WANT activity bleeds into the NEED activity
2. Over time, the brain associates the NEED activity with the dopamine signal
3. The NEED activity becomes less aversive (or even pleasant)

This is Law 2 of the [[Four Laws of Behavior Change]]: **Make It Attractive**.

## Design Rules

1. **The WANT must be genuinely enjoyable** — mild preference is not enough; it should be something you actively look forward to
2. **The WANT should only happen during the NEED** — exclusivity is key; if you can listen to the podcast at any time, the bundle loses power
3. **The pairing must make sense logistically** — don't pair activities that require full cognitive attention on both sides
4. **Start immediately** — begin the WANT activity at the same moment as the NEED activity (not as a reward after)

## Connection to Motivation Ritual

A related concept from Clear: the **Motivation Ritual**. Before a hard or difficult habit, do something you genuinely enjoy for a few minutes. This primes the dopamine system and transfers some anticipatory excitement to the upcoming task.

Sequence: [Enjoyable ritual] → [Hard habit]

Example: 5 minutes of favorite music → study session start.

The bundle is the concurrent version; the ritual is the sequential version.
