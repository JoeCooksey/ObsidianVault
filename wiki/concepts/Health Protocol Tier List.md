# Health Protocol Tier List — Evidence-Based Prescriptions

Ranked by **evidence quality × effect size × cost-benefit**. Unlike the broad [[Biohacking Tier List]], this page gives you the *specific prescription* — temperature, duration, frequency, timing — not just the category. Based on peer-reviewed literature through April 2026.

**Tiers:**
- **S** — Transformative effect; robust cohort/RCT evidence; everyone should do this
- **A** — Strong benefit; meaningful effect size; worth the investment
- **B** — Promising; limited or mixed RCTs; do it if you enjoy it
- **C** — Weak or inconsistent evidence; likely not worth the cost/time
- **D** — Hype, no credible evidence, or net harmful

---

## S-Tier Protocols

### 1. Strength Training
**Prescription:** 2–4 sessions/week × 45–60 min; compound lifts (squat, hinge, push, pull, carry); progressive overload.
**Minimum dose:** Even 1 set/exercise × 1x/week reduces all-cause mortality 15%.
**Optimal dose:** ~60 min/week total → maximum 27% mortality risk reduction (dose-response curve flattens above this).
**Why it works:** Muscle mass = metabolic reserve; insulin sensitivity; mTOR cycling; structural bone and connective tissue maintenance.
**Evidence:** Multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses. Resistance training → 15% all-cause mortality ↓, 19% CVD mortality ↓, 14% cancer mortality ↓ vs. no training.
**Cost:** Free (bodyweight) to gym membership.

---

### 2. Zone 2 Cardio
**Prescription:** 150–200+ min/week; pace where you can hold a conversation but not sing; ~60–70% max HR; nasal breathing works well as a proxy.
**Best modalities:** Cycling, rowing, brisk walking, swimming.
**Why it works:** Mitochondrial biogenesis; increases mitochondrial density and oxidative capacity; improves VO2 max infrastructure (stroke volume, capillary density).
**Note:** Each 1 mL/kg/min increase in VO2 max ≈ 45 additional days of life expectancy (46-year follow-up study).
**Evidence:** Strong epidemiological and mechanistic evidence. Zone 2 is the aerobic base; combine with VO2 max intervals for full longevity signal.
**Cost:** Free.

---

### 3. VO₂ Max Intervals
**Prescription:** 1–2x/week; Norwegian 4×4 method: 4 intervals × 4 min at ~90–95% max HR with 3–4 min active recovery between; or 6–10 × 1 min all-out with 1 min rest.
**When to add:** Layer on top of Zone 2 base; do not replace Zone 2 entirely.
**Why it works:** VO2 max is the single strongest predictor of longevity and all-cause mortality (higher than any biomarker); intervals drive the ceiling up faster than Zone 2 alone.
**Evidence:** 2025 narrative review: higher intensities more efficient per time-unit for mitochondrial capacity; each 1-MET increase in fitness ≈ 13% lower all-cause mortality.
**Cost:** Free.

---

### 4. Finnish Sauna
**Prescription:** 4–7x/week; 80–100°C (176–212°F); 20 min/session; dry air (10–20% humidity); cool down between rounds if doing multiple.
**Minimum dose:** 2x/week still shows benefit; benefit is linear with frequency.
**Why it works:** Passive cardiovascular conditioning; HR rises to 100–150 bpm (mimics moderate exercise); improved endothelial function, reduced arterial stiffness, autonomic nervous system modulation, lower systemic blood pressure.
**Evidence:** Laukkanen et al. (n=2,315, 20-year follow-up): 4–7x/week → 40% ↓ all-cause mortality vs. 1x/week; 63% ↓ sudden cardiac death. Dose-response confirmed. Second Finnish study (2024) confirms sauna + low systolic BP = synergistic mortality benefit.
**Contraindications:** Unstable angina, recent MI, severe aortic stenosis.
**Cost:** $2–10/session at a gym; $3–20k for home installation.

---

### 5. Sleep 7–9h with Fixed Wake Time
**Prescription:** Same wake time every day (including weekends); 65–68°F (18–20°C) room; pitch dark; no alcohol within 3h of bed; no food within 2–3h.
**Single most powerful variable:** Consistent wake time anchors the cortisol peak and melatonin onset; consistency matters more than total hours.
**Why it works:** Memory consolidation, cellular repair (glymphatic clearance), hormone regulation (GH, testosterone, cortisol), immune function.
**Evidence:** Decades of epidemiological data. <6h/night → 2× all-cause mortality risk at 25-year follow-up. Sleep is the foundation every other protocol builds on.
**Cost:** Free.

---

### 6. Daily Walking (7,000–10,000 Steps)
**Prescription:** 7,000–10,000 steps/day; brisk pace preferred (>100 steps/min); can be accumulated throughout the day.
**Why it works:** NEAT (non-exercise activity thermogenesis); linear dose-response relationship with all-cause mortality; counters sedentary physiology independent of formal exercise.
**Evidence:** Multiple large prospective cohorts; 7,000 steps/day = ~50% lower mortality vs. <4,000; diminishing returns above 10,000. Underrated alongside formal exercise.
**Cost:** Free.

## A-Tier Protocols

### 7. Cold Water Immersion (CWI)
**Prescription:** 10–15°C (50–59°F); 10–15 min; 3–5x/week for recovery; or as needed post-exercise.
**Minimum dose for norepinephrine release:** Even 2–3 min at ≤15°C reliably increases norepinephrine 200–300%.
**Timing:** Do NOT do CWI within 4–6 hours immediately post-strength training — blunts hypertrophy signal (mTOR inhibition). Fine post-endurance.
**Why it works:** Inflammation reduction; norepinephrine and dopamine release (mood, focus, alertness); exercise recovery (reduces DOMS); cold-shock protein induction.
**Evidence:** 2025 PLOS One systematic review confirms: reduces muscle damage after exercise, improves quality of life (cold showers), reduces stress (effect delayed ~12h). Mood/immunity boost inconsistent.
**Cost:** Cold plunge tub $200–$5,000; cold shower is free.

---

### 8. Time-Restricted Eating (TRE) — 16:8
**Prescription:** Eat all calories within an 8-hour window; fast the other 16h; align eating window to daylight (e.g., 9am–5pm or 12pm–8pm).
**Why it works:** Lower insulin secretion; metabolic switch from glucose to ketones at ~12–16h fast; improved insulin sensitivity, gut microbiome diversity, autophagy ramp-up.
**Evidence:** 2025 Oxford meta-analysis (16/8 TRE): significant improvements in fasting glucose, HOMA-IR, insulin, HDL-C. Important caveat: no superior weight loss vs. calorie restriction; most benefit in sedentary/physically inactive populations. Active athletes may not see glucose benefit.
**Caution:** If physically active, ensure protein targets are still met within the eating window.
**Cost:** Free.

---

### 9. Morning Sunlight
**Prescription:** 10–30 min direct outdoor sunlight within 60 min of waking; no sunglasses; overcast = 4× the dose (10 min clear → 20–30 min overcast).
**Why it works:** Triggers cortisol pulse for alertness; calibrates the SCN (suprachiasmatic nucleus) circadian clock; sets timing of melatonin onset 12–14h later; vitamin D synthesis (bonus).
**Evidence:** Robust circadian biology literature. Huberman protocol popularized it; foundational neuroscience is well-established. Disrupted light exposure → metabolic and mood dysregulation.
**Cost:** Free.

---

### 10. Meditation (MBSR-style)
**Prescription:** 10–20 min/day; any method (focused attention, body scan, open monitoring); consistency > technique; apps like Waking Up or Headspace are fine scaffolding.
**Why it works:** Structural gray matter increases in prefrontal cortex and hippocampus at 8 weeks of MBSR; HRV ↑; stress hormone modulation; reduces amygdala reactivity.
**Evidence:** Multiple RCTs of MBSR (8-week mindfulness-based stress reduction): anxiety ↓, depression ↓, HRV ↑, cortisol ↓. Brain structure changes replicated across labs.
**Cost:** Free (or $10/mo app).

---

### 11. Red Light Therapy (PBM / RLT)
**Prescription:** 630–850 nm wavelengths; 10–20 min/day, 10–20 cm distance from panel; target affected tissue; consistent daily use for 4–8 weeks before evaluating.
**Best evidence applications:** Androgenic alopecia (hair loss), skin collagen and elasticity, wound healing, cancer-therapy-related oral mucositis, peripheral neuropathy, musculoskeletal pain.
**Why it works:** Cytochrome c oxidase (complex IV) in mitochondria absorbs red/NIR photons → ↑ ATP production, ↑ nitric oxide release, ↓ oxidative stress, ↑ local blood flow.
**Evidence:** 2025 consensus review by 20+ specialists confirms safe and effective for specific conditions. Skin: collagen density ↑ in controlled trials. Hair: LLLT ≥ 25 mW/cm² devices show RCT-level evidence. Weaker evidence for systemic "energy" effects.
**Products:** Joovv Solo, Mito Red Pro 1500X ($400–700); or low-cost panels (~$150).
**Cost:** $150–$700 device (one-time).

---

### 12. Resistance Training (Heavy Days, 1–3 Rep Max Focus)
**Prescription:** 1–2x/week heavy strength work (85–95% 1RM; 1–5 reps); focuses neural recruitment and tendon/bone adaptation separate from hypertrophy work.
**Why it works:** Grip strength and maximal strength are independent longevity predictors (Ruiz et al., 2008); tendon/bone stiffness prevents fractures and sarcopenia with aging.
**Cost:** Gym membership or home rack.

## B-Tier Protocols

### 13. Cold Showers (Daily)
**Prescription:** 2–3 min cold at end of shower; contrast (hot→cold) works too; weekly minimum 5x.
**Why it works:** Catecholamine release (norepinephrine, dopamine); mood and alertness boost; habituates cold tolerance.
**Evidence:** Observational; one RCT showed cold showers after a warm shower reduced sick days 29%; quality-of-life improvement reported. Not as strong as CWI immersion.
**Cost:** Free.

---

### 14. Infrared Sauna
**Prescription:** 45–60°C (113–140°F); 20–45 min; 3–5x/week.
**Why it works:** Lower ambient temp allows longer session; infrared penetrates tissue directly; similar cardiovascular loading to Finnish sauna but less intense.
**Evidence:** Weaker cohort evidence than Finnish sauna; most studies are Finnish; infrared lacks the large prospective mortality data. Mechanistically plausible. Good option when Finnish is inaccessible.
**Cost:** $1,000–$5,000 for home unit.

---

### 15. Contrast Therapy (Hot-Cold Cycling)
**Prescription:** 3–4 rounds: 5 min sauna/hot (38–42°C) → 1–2 min cold plunge (10–15°C); finish cold for recovery, finish hot for relaxation.
**Why it works:** Vascular "pumping" — alternate vasodilation and vasoconstriction; additive circulatory benefit; may enhance lymphatic clearance.
**Evidence:** Limited RCTs; athlete recovery studies show benefit over cold or heat alone; mechanism is sound but longevity data absent.
**Cost:** Requires access to both sauna and cold plunge.

---

### 16. Breathwork Protocols
**Prescription (options):**
- Box breathing: 4s in / 4s hold / 4s out / 4s hold — for acute stress regulation
- 4-7-8: 4s in / 7s hold / 8s out — pre-sleep parasympathetic activation
- Cyclic sighing: double inhale through nose + long exhale — fastest real-time anxiety reduction (Stanford 2023 RCT)
- Wim Hof: 30 deep cycles + breath hold; raises norepinephrine; not for swimming or driving
**Why it works:** Directly controls HRV and ANS balance; CO₂ tolerance training; vagal tone.
**Evidence:** Cyclic sighing outperformed meditation for acute anxiety in 2023 Stanford RCT. Wim Hof method raises NE, lowers cytokines (Kox et al., 2014 PNAS). Breathwork for HRV/stress: A-tier; for longevity: insufficient data.
**Cost:** Free.

---

### 17. 24-Hour Fasts (1–2×/Month)
**Prescription:** Skip dinner one evening, eat next evening — 24h fast; water, black coffee, and electrolytes allowed.
**Why it works:** Autophagy ramp-up; insulin sensitization reset; AMPK activation; ketone production.
**Evidence:** Strong mechanistic evidence from animal models; human data on autophagy induction is growing but less definitive. Benefits beyond TRE are real but modest for otherwise healthy people.
**Cost:** Free.

---

### 18. Nature Exposure / Forest Bathing
**Prescription:** 20–30 min in green/natural spaces 3–5x/week; remove from urban noise and screens; walking is a bonus, not required.
**Why it works:** Reduced cortisol (salivary), ↓ blood pressure, ↓ HR; phytoncides (tree volatile compounds) may enhance NK cell activity.
**Evidence:** Multiple RCTs show cortisol and blood pressure reductions; Japanese shinrin-yoku studies replicated. Effect size modest but consistent.
**Cost:** Free.

---

## C-Tier Protocols

### 19. Cryotherapy Chambers
**Prescription:** −110°C to −160°C; 2–4 min; marketed for recovery and fat loss.
**Why it falls short:** No large-scale RCT data; most meta-analyses show it is comparable to (not superior to) cold water immersion at a fraction of the cost ($50–100/session vs. free cold shower). Skin exposure is brief; core temperature does not drop as much as CWI. Fat loss claims: no credible evidence.
**Verdict:** Pay for CWI or cold showers instead.

---

### 20. Whole-Body Vibration Platforms
**Prescription:** 25–50 Hz, 10–15 min; promoted for bone density, muscle activation.
**Why it falls short:** Evidence limited to clinical contexts (osteoporosis, sarcopenia in elderly); no meaningful benefit over resistance training in healthy adults; mechanism sound but effect size tiny.
**Verdict:** Do squats instead.

---

### 21. Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT)
**Prescription:** 1.5–2.0 ATA; 60–90 min sessions; 20–40 session protocols.
**Why it falls short:** One Israeli RCT claimed telomere lengthening and senescent cell reduction; not replicated; $200–500/session; no mortality evidence. Legitimate clinical uses (wound healing, CO poisoning) — not a general health protocol.
**Verdict:** Evidence doesn't justify recreational use cost.

---

### 22. Extended Water Fasting (3–7 days)
**Why it falls short:** Catabolism of lean muscle mass; limited incremental benefit over 24–48h fasts for otherwise healthy people; micronutrient depletion; refeeding syndrome risk. High-risk vs. reward.
**Verdict:** Stick to TRE + occasional 24h fasts.

---

### 23. Carnivore / Zero-Carb Diet Long-Term
**Why it falls short:** Short-term: inflammatory markers improve, autoimmune anecdotes compelling. Long-term: zero dietary fiber → microbiome diversity collapses; fiber is independently linked to longevity. No long-term RCT data.
**Verdict:** No fiber = problematic long-term; better to optimize protein within a whole-food diet.

---

## D-Tier Protocols

### 24. Detox Cleanses / Juice Fasts
**Why it's D-tier:** The liver and kidneys are the detox organs. "Detoxification" through juice fasting has zero mechanistic or clinical basis. Often causes glycemic spikes from fruit juice.

---

### 25. Colon Cleanses / Colonics
**Why it's D-tier:** Disrupts microbiome; no evidence of health benefit; risk of perforation and electrolyte imbalance.

---

### 26. Young Plasma / Blood Transfusions
**Why it's D-tier:** Company "Ambrosia" shut down by FDA; no proven human longevity benefit; significant risks of blood-borne pathogens and immune reactions.

---

### 27. GH (Growth Hormone) Injections for Anti-Aging
**Why it's D-tier:** IGF-1 elevation increases cancer risk with chronic use; significant adverse effects (edema, joint pain, insulin resistance); limited longevity evidence in healthy adults. Clinically appropriate for diagnosed GHD only.

---

### 28. Ozone Therapy / Hydrogen Water
**Ozone therapy:** Injection/insufflation is unsafe; oral/topical exposure is not bioavailable for systemic effects.
**Hydrogen water:** A few small trials for oxidative stress; effect sizes trivial; no clingevity outcome data. Expensive.

## Protocol Stack Recommendation (Joe — 19M, trains 6×/week)

**Non-negotiable S-tier daily stack:**
1. Fixed wake time + 7–9h sleep
2. Morning sunlight 10–20 min
3. 7,000+ steps (often covered by training)
4. Zone 2 cardio built into cycling base
5. Strength training 2–3×/week (overlaps cycling)

**Add when possible:**
- Sauna 3–4×/week post-workout (gym or home)
- Cold water immersion post-endurance (not post-lifting)
- TRE 16:8 — align eating window with training

**Layer in (if enjoying it):**
- 10 min meditation daily
- Cyclic sighing breathwork pre-study/exam
- Red light therapy panel for hair/recovery
