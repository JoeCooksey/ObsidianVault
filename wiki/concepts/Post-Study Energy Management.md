# Post-Study Energy Management

The cognitive resource available for focused work is finite — roughly 4 hours of true deep work per day for most people (Cal Newport). After class exhausts this budget, forcing more effortful technical work produces low-quality output and accelerates burnout.

## The Core Model

**Energy × Focus × Time = Actual Learning Output**

After a full day of class:
- Energy ≈ 20–30% baseline
- Even 2 hours of low-energy technical practice < 30 focused minutes next morning

The most productive thing to do with a depleted evening is *restore energy for tomorrow*, not grind through low-quality reps tonight.

## Recovery Activity Tier List (Post-Class)

| Tier | Activity | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| S | Zone 2 cardio / cycling | BDNF release, metabolic clearance, different cognitive circuit |
| S | Walk outside (no phone) | Attention Restoration Theory; directed attention restores in nature |
| S | Real social time | Activates reward circuits distinct from work; Harvard 80-yr study |
| A | Reading (enjoyable books) | Low cognitive load; knowledge compounding; spaced repetition |
| A | Cook a real meal | Sensory, concrete, goal-complete — different modality than study |
| A | Journal 10 min | Consolidates learning; reduces rumination; clears working memory |
| B | 3Blue1Brown / Veritasium | Educational but low-effort; feeds curiosity without draining |
| B | Light organization | Sets up tomorrow's success at very low cost |
| C | One episode of a good show | Passive but fine in moderation; don't autoplay |
| C | Chess.com 15 min | Light tactical engagement |
| F | Social media scrolling | Feels passive, actually degrades attention (P300 research) |
| F | YouTube rabbit holes | Algorithm-driven; no restoration; degrades next-day deep work |

## The Guilt Reframe

Guilt about not doing LTSpice or programming after class is based on the false model that hours-in = progress-out. The correct model accounts for energy multiplier. Reading *Ego Is the Enemy* at 20% energy > forcing LTSpice at 20% energy. Both build Joe — one just fits the available resource.

## The 10-Minute Diagnostic

Not every low-motivation episode is true depletion. Test with a 10-minute forced start:
- If still dragging after 10 min → genuine fatigue; stop and recover
- If absorbed → [[Limbic Friction]] was the issue; keep going

## Schedule Architecture Fix

Technical outside-study works best when placed *before* class cognitive drain:
- Morning block (post-ride warm-up) → LTSpice or Python
- Short + bounded (45–60 min, never open-ended)
- Not contingent on "if I have energy after class"

## Shutdown Ritual (Newport)

A verbal or written shutdown signal ("Shutdown Complete") after the last class task gives the brain explicit permission to stop work-related rumination. Without it, low-grade work anxiety continues through recovery time, preventing genuine restoration.
