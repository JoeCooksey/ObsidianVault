# Relationship Action Plan (RAP)

A planning framework from [[Keith Ferrazzi]]'s *Never Eat Alone* that converts vague networking intentions into a concrete, goal-aligned contact strategy.

---

## The Framework

The RAP bridges your life goals and your contact list. The process:

### Step 1 — Write Your Goals
Set goals at three time horizons:
- **3-year goal** — where do you want to be? (job, position, skill, life situation)
- **1-year goal** — what must be true by then to be on track for the 3-year goal?
- **90-day goal** — what is the first concrete step that moves you toward the 1-year goal?

Goals should be specific and outcome-oriented, not activity-oriented.

### Step 2 — Map the People
For each goal at each time horizon, ask: **Who do I need to know?**

This can be:
- Specific named individuals (ideal)
- Categories of people ("a SiC engineer at Wolfspeed")
- Connectors who know the people in the category

Build a list. These become your priority contacts.

### Step 3 — Give First
Before making any ask, find one way to provide value to each priority contact:
- Share a relevant article or insight
- Make an introduction they would find valuable
- Offer help with a problem they've mentioned publicly

The ask — if it ever comes — arrives in a context of established trust.

### Step 4 — Build It Before You Need It
The 90-day horizon ensures you are always building relationships *before* the need becomes urgent. Relationships built in desperation are weak; relationships built during calm periods are durable.

---

## Why It Works

Most people's networking is reactive — they reach out when they need something. The RAP makes it proactive:
- You know *who* to reach out to (goal-derived list, not random)
- You know *what* to offer (something tied to their interests, discovered via homework)
- You have *time* to give first because you started before you needed anything
