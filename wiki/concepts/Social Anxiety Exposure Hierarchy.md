# Social Anxiety Exposure Hierarchy

A ranked list of anxiety-provoking social situations used in CBT to guide graduated exposure. Work bottom-up; don't advance until current step drops to ≤25 SUDS.

## SUDS Scale
Subjective Units of Distress Scale: 0 (none) → 100 (worst imaginable anxiety).

## Template Hierarchy (customize to your triggers)

| SUDS | Situation |
|------|-----------|
| 5–10 | Eye contact + nod at a stranger passing |
| 15 | Say "hi" or smile at a cashier or barista |
| 20 | Comment on weather / brief pleasantry |
| 30 | Ask a stranger for directions or a small favor |
| 35 | Start small talk with a coworker you rarely speak to |
| 45 | Hold a 2-min conversation with an acquaintance |
| 55 | Join a group conversation already in progress |
| 60 | Introduce yourself to someone you don't know at a party |
| 65 | Share a personal opinion that someone might disagree with |
| 70 | Ask someone to hang out / make a specific plan |
| 75 | Approach someone attractive or clearly high-status |
| 80 | Attend a networking event / professional gathering alone |
| 85 | Give a short speech or toast in front of 5–10 people |
| 90 | Lead a group meeting or facilitate a discussion |
| 95 | Public speaking to a large audience |

## Protocol
1. Pick the lowest item ≥25 SUDS
2. Enter the situation; notice anxiety peaks and then drops (extinction curve)
3. Stay in the situation until SUDS drops ≥50% from peak
4. Repeat same step until SUDS at entry ≤25
5. Advance to next rung

## Why It Works
Anxiety is a false alarm that extinguishes with repeated non-catastrophic exposure. The brain learns: "this situation is not actually dangerous." This is Pavlovian extinction — the conditioned threat response weakens without the predicted bad outcome.
