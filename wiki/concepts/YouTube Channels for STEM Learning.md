# YouTube Channels for STEM Learning

Ranked by **depth × accuracy × accessibility** for self-directed learners. Focus: mathematics, physics, engineering, computer science, AI/ML, biology, and neuroscience.

---

## S-Tier — Maximum Intellectual ROI

### 3Blue1Brown
- **Creator**: Grant Sanderson (former Khan Academy)
- **Subjects**: Pure and applied mathematics, machine learning
- **Why S-tier**: Animated visual intuition for concepts that textbooks teach mechanically. No peer for building deep understanding. Custom animation library (Manim) makes abstract math visual.
- **Must-watch series**:
  - *Essence of Calculus* (11 videos) — visual derivation of all core calculus concepts
  - *Essence of Linear Algebra* (16 videos) — eigenvectors, transformations, determinants as visuals
  - *Neural Networks* (4 core videos) — gradient descent, backpropagation from first principles
  - *Differential Equations* (5 videos) — ODE intuition directly relevant to RLC circuits
- **For Joe**: Essence of Linear Algebra directly supports MAT 267 and EEE 350 Signals; Neural Networks is the gateway to Phase 4 AI track

### Veritasium
- **Creator**: Derek Muller (PhD physics education)
- **Subjects**: Physics, engineering, science, mathematics
- **Why S-tier**: Accurate, experiment-driven, and accessible. Most reliable mainstream science channel. 20M+ subscribers. Covers cutting-edge topics within months of publication.
- **Must-watch videos**:
  - "Why Gravity is NOT a Force" — general relativity made intuitive
  - "How Electricity Actually Works" — field-based model of electrical energy flow; relevant to EE
  - "The Discovery That Transformed Pi" — mathematics history

### PBS Space Time
- **Creator**: Matt O'Dowd (astrophysicist)
- **Subjects**: Astrophysics, cosmology, quantum field theory, general relativity
- **Why S-tier**: Graduate-level depth without dumbing down. Covers QFT, black hole thermodynamics, dark matter, and the nature of time at a level no other YouTube channel reaches. Comments are full of physics PhDs.
- **Must-watch**: Quantum Mechanics series, General Relativity series, "Does Time Flow Forward?" playlist

### Andrej Karpathy
- **Creator**: Andrej Karpathy (former Tesla AI Director, OpenAI co-founder)
- **Subjects**: Deep learning, LLMs, neural networks
- **Why S-tier**: "Neural Networks: Zero to Hero" playlist builds GPT from scratch in Python — used in Stanford and MIT courses. No-BS, engineer-to-engineer style.
- **Must-watch series**: *Neural Networks: Zero to Hero* (10 videos) — micrograd → makemore → nanoGPT

## A-Tier — Deep, Reliable, Curriculum-Grade

### MinutePhysics
- **Subjects**: Physics
- **Format**: Stick-figure animations, 3–6 minutes
- **Best for**: Quick conceptual fixes before class; complex phenomena reduced to essentials

### Numberphile
- **Subjects**: Mathematics
- **Format**: Conversation-style with expert mathematicians
- **Best for**: Mathematical culture and appreciation; motivates why math topics matter

### SmarterEveryDay
- **Creator**: Destin Sandlin (aerospace engineer)
- **Subjects**: Engineering, applied science, physics
- **Best for**: Mechanical and physical intuition; slow-motion footage explains phenomena words can't

### Two Minute Papers
- **Creator**: Károly Zsolnai-Fehér (computer graphics researcher)
- **Subjects**: AI and ML research
- **Format**: 3–8 minute breakdowns of new papers; weekly cadence
- **Best for**: Staying current with AI research without reading full papers

### StatQuest with Josh Starmer
- **Subjects**: Statistics, machine learning mathematics
- **Best for**: The mathematical foundations of ML without the abstraction overload; "BAAAAAM" teaching style

### Huberman Lab
- **Creator**: Andrew Huberman (Stanford Neuroscience Professor)
- **Subjects**: Neuroscience, biology, performance optimization
- **Format**: Long-form (~2 hours); dense notes available
- **Best for**: Neuroscience of habits, sleep, focus, hormones; backed by primary research citations
- **Already in wiki**: [[Andrew Huberman]]

### Khan Academy
- **Subjects**: Math (all levels), physics, chemistry, economics, CS, biology
- **Best for**: Filling gaps in foundational knowledge; structured curriculum with practice problems
- **Note**: Not for advanced topics — best as a foundation repair tool

### Mark Rober
- **Creator**: Mark Rober (former NASA engineer)
- **Subjects**: Applied science, engineering, inventions
- **Best for**: Science applied to spectacularly engineered real-world projects; high motivation factor
