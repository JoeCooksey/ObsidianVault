# Social Ladder Climbing

The strategic navigation of social hierarchies to gain access, status, respect, and influence. When done with genuine value creation, it doesn't look like climbing at all.

## The Three Inputs

**Value** — Develop interesting skills, opinions, and stories. Be genuinely worth knowing.
**Visibility** — Show up consistently to the right contexts (recurring events, communities, shared spaces).
**Consistency** — Social capital compounds. One-off meetings don't build relationships; the follow-up does.

## Route to Higher-Status People
High-status individuals are rarely cold-approachable. The path:
1. Build genuine relationships with people 1–2 levels up
2. Find "super connectors" — people with dense, high-quality networks — and invest there first
3. Get introduced (warm) rather than cold-approach (cold → low conversion, low impression)
4. One super connector > 20 weak connections (see [[Super Connectors]])

## What Actually Works

**Be genuinely interesting**: Have opinions. Tell good stories. Know something deeply.
**Be useful before you need anything**: Give first — information, intros, opportunities (see [[Generosity-First Networking]])
**Follow up obsessively**: The first meeting is just the beginning; the follow-up is the relationship (see [[Follow-Up Formula]])
**Show up repeatedly**: Frequency > intensity; casual recurring contact > one impressive meeting

## What Doesn't Work
- Pure status-seeking (treating people as rungs, not people) — high-status people sense this immediately
- Showing up once and waiting to be noticed
- Impressing without being useful
- Name-dropping instead of relationship-building

## The Paradox
Research shows: the more individuals aggressively climb the hierarchy, the stronger and less mobile the hierarchy becomes for everyone. Good social climbing doesn't look like climbing — it looks like genuine curiosity, generosity, and showing up.
