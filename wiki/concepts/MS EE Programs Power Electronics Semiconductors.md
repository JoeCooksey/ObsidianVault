# MS EE Programs — Power Electronics & Semiconductors

Quick-reference breakdown of US graduate programs for the physical side of EE. For the full synthesis see [[Research - Top MS EE Programs Physical Side]].

## Tier 1 — Dedicated Physical-Side Programs

### NC State — MS in Wide Bandgap Semiconductors (MS-WBGS)
- **Launch**: Fall 2026
- **Joint**: ECE + Materials Science Engineering
- **Focus**: SiC/GaN device physics, fabrication, characterization, power systems (EV drivetrains, DCFC, RF)
- **Centers**: FREEDM Systems Center + PowerAmerica Institute (49 industry members)
- **Industry pipeline**: WBG job portal; Wolfspeed, Onsemi, STMicro, Navitas, GaN Systems
- **Format**: Hybrid — online coursework + on-campus summer practicum
- **Verdict**: Best-in-class for WBG semiconductors; only dedicated program of its kind

### Virginia Tech — MS ECE via CPES
- **Focus**: Power electronics systems, converter design, magnetics, thermal packaging
- **Center**: Center for Power Electronics Systems (CPES) — NSF ERC, ~100 researchers, $6–7M/year
- **Degrees**: MS (research-based) or MEng (project-based)
- **Curriculum**: Power electronics packaging, systems integration, advanced topologies
- **Verdict**: #1 for pure power electronics — converters, inverters, magnetics, digital control

### UT Austin — MS in Semiconductor Science and Engineering
- **Launch**: Fall 2025
- **Focus**: 4 tracks — semiconductor manufacturing, circuits & systems, heterogeneous integration, semiconductor devices
- **Funding**: Most students receive industry RA: stipend + tuition + health insurance
- **Local ecosystem**: Samsung Fab, TI, NXP, Applied Materials all in Austin
- **GRE**: Not required; GPA ≥ 3.0
- **Verdict**: Best new program; strongest for device fabrication and manufacturing career track

## Tier 2 — Top EE Programs with Physical-Side Depth

### Purdue — MS in Microelectronics & Semiconductors
- **Format**: Online available (professional-track)
- **Focus areas**: Devices & manufacturing / Circuit design / System design
- **School**: Top-10 engineering; Birck Nanotechnology Center
- **Verdict**: Best option if working full-time; high prestige; not as research-intensive as Tier 1

### MIT — MS/SM EECS
- **Ranking**: #1 globally (tied)
- **Labs**: Microsystems Technology Laboratories (MTL), Research Lab of Electronics (RLE)
- **Admission**: Extremely competitive; research experience almost required
- **Verdict**: Opens every door but doesn't specialize in power electronics; best for academic/research track

### Stanford — MS EE (terminal)
- **Ranking**: #1 globally (tied)
- **Format**: Terminal MS, 1.5–2 years, no thesis required
- **Research**: Eric Pop Lab (devices), E3S Center (energy)
- **Verdict**: Top prestige; great semiconductor device research; not power-electronics focused

### UC Berkeley — MS or MEng EECS
- **Ranking**: #1 globally (tied)
- **MS**: Research-track; primarily intended for PhD students
- **MEng**: Industry-track professional master's; 1 year; capstone project
- **Labs**: Berkeley Nanosciences and Nanotechnology Institute (BNNI), BSAC (sensors/actuators)
- **Verdict**: MEng = excellent industry-pipeline; MS = competitive and PhD-adjacent
