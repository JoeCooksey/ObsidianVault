# Joe's Custom Seasonal Training Plan

**Joe's four-season training framework:**
- **Spring (March–May)**: ENDURANCE — build aerobic engine, VO2 max
- **Summer (June–August)**: STRENGTH — maximize strength output, CNS power
- **Fall (September–November)**: SPEED — anaerobic capacity, neuromuscular power, sprint ability
- **Winter (December–February)**: MUSCLE — hypertrophy, body composition, muscle mass

This is Joe's specific framework. It differs from the standard periodization model in [[Seasonal Fitness Periodization]] — the standard model puts strength in winter and VO2 max in spring. Joe's model swaps strength to summer and shifts hypertrophy to winter. Both are valid; Joe's structure aligns strength with summer motivation and outdoor access, and hypertrophy with the cold/indoor environment of winter.

**Joe profile**: 19-year-old, cyclist background (strong quads, strong aerobic base), weak posterior chain and upper body, college student at ASU.

---

## Interference Effect Rules (applies across all seasons)

1. Lift first if both lifting and cardio are in the same session
2. Separate hard cardio and hard lifting by 6+ hours minimum if on same day
3. Use cycling (not running) during heavy lift phases — cycling doesn't create the eccentric damage that running does
4. 2 Zone 2 sessions/week = the year-round minimum to prevent aerobic detraining
5. No VO2 max intervals during strength or power peaks (they drain CNS recovery)

## WINTER — MUSCLE (December–February)

### Goal
Maximum hypertrophy. Build muscle mass across upper body (priority), posterior chain (priority), and legs (maintenance for Joe).

### Why Winter
Cold weather → indoor focus naturally → ideal for 5–6 days/week gym. Caloric surplus is easier (comfort foods, reduced outdoor activity). Long dark nights = more sleep. Best macrocycle for body composition building.

### Nutrition
- **Calories**: +200–300 kcal/day lean bulk
- **Protein**: 1.6–2.2 g/kg bodyweight (≈0.75–1.0 g/lb)
- **Timing**: 3–5 protein meals; post-workout meal within 2 hrs
- **Rate of gain**: target 0.25–0.5% bodyweight/week

### Lifting Split — Upper/Lower (4 days/week)
This is the S-tier split for hypertrophy: 2× frequency per muscle group, manageable fatigue.

**Upper A (Monday) — Chest + Back dominant**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Bench Press | 4×8–10 | Control eccentric 3 sec |
| Chest-Supported Row | 4×8–10 | Squeeze at top — eliminate lower back from equation |
| Incline DB Press | 3×10–12 | Upper chest emphasis |
| Lat Pulldown (neutral grip) | 3×10–12 | Full stretch at top |
| Cable Lateral Raise | 3×12–15 | Priority for visual width — light weight, controlled |
| Bayesian Cable Curl | 3×12 | Cable behind body = stretch-loaded; highest hypertrophy signal |
| Overhead Cable Extension | 3×12 | Long head stretch position |

**Lower A (Tuesday) — Quads + Hamstrings**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Hack Squat or Leg Press | 4×8–10 | S-tier quad exercise (better ROM than barbell squat for hypertrophy) |
| Romanian Deadlift (RDL) | 4×8–10 | Priority: posterior chain is Joe's weakness; feel the hamstring stretch |
| Bulgarian Split Squat | 3×8–10 ea | Hip flexor stretch + quad unilateral balance |
| Lying Leg Curl | 3×10–12 | Hamstrings at stretch = maximum stimulus |
| Hip Thrust | 3×10–12 | Glute priority; full extension at top, squeeze 1 sec |
| Calf Raise | 4×15–20 | Slow lowering, full stretch at bottom |

**Upper B (Thursday) — Shoulders + Arms dominant**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Overhead Press (OHP) | 4×8–10 | Priority: upper body weakness for Joe |
| Barbell or DB Row | 4×8–10 | Heavy; pull to hip, not chest |
| Cable Fly or Pec Deck | 3×12–15 | Stretch-loaded chest isolation |
| Seated Cable Row | 3×10–12 | Mid-back, rhomboids, rear delt |
| Face Pulls | 3×15 | ALWAYS in program: rear delt + external rotator health |
| Hammer Curl | 3×12 | Brachialis = arm thickness |
| Dips or Skull Crusher | 3×10–12 | Triceps priority (60% of arm size) |

**Lower B (Friday) — Posterior Chain dominant**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Conventional Deadlift | 4×6–8 | Joe's priority: strengthen posterior chain from top to bottom |
| Hip Thrust (heavier) | 4×8–10 | Load progression goal: 1.5× bodyweight |
| Good Morning | 3×10–12 | Hamstring + lower back simultaneously |
| Nordic Curl | 3×6–8 | Highest hamstring hypertrophy + ACL protection; hardest bodyweight exercise |
| Leg Extension | 3×12–15 | Quad isolation, stretch-loaded at full extension |
| Reverse Hyperextension | 3×12–15 | Lower back + glute tie-in; use machine or improvise |

### Cardio — Maintenance Only
- **2× Zone 2 per week** — 45 min each (Wednesday + Saturday or Sunday)
- **Modality**: cycling (stationary or outdoor) — preserves Joe's cycling base without interfering with lifting
- **No intervals** — VO2 max work drains CNS recovery needed for lifting
- **Rule**: cardio on separate days from lifting, or 6+ hrs apart

### Weekly Template

| Day | Session | Focus |
|-----|---------|-------|
| Monday | **Upper A** | Chest / back / arms |
| Tuesday | **Lower A** | Quads / hamstrings / glutes |
| Wednesday | **Zone 2 cardio** | 45 min cycling, conversational |
| Thursday | **Upper B** | Shoulders / arms / back |
| Friday | **Lower B** | Posterior chain priority |
| Saturday | **Zone 2 cardio** | 45 min cycling |
| Sunday | **Full rest + mobility** | AM dynamic 7 min, PM static 12 min |

### Key Metrics to Track
- Weekly bodyweight (same day/time/conditions)
- Target: +0.25–0.5 lb/week (lean bulk)
- Monthly photo comparison
- Lift progression: add rep or add weight each week

## SPRING — ENDURANCE (March–May)

### Goal
Maximize cardiovascular fitness: Zone 2 aerobic base, lactate threshold, VO2 max. Build the biggest aerobic engine of the year while maintaining winter muscle.

### Why Spring
Weather improves → outdoor riding and running become available. Mental reset after winter gym grind. Spring social atmosphere encourages active outdoor movement. Pre-summer timing: enter summer lean and aerobically fit.

### Nutrition
- **Calories**: slight deficit (−200–300 kcal/day) or maintenance
- Gradual cut: 8–10 weeks from end of February to late April
- **Protein**: 2.2–2.6 g/kg (elevated to preserve winter muscle while in deficit)
- **Carb timing**: carb-load before long Zone 2 sessions (45–60 min rides/runs)

### Three-Phase Spring Progression

**Phase 1 — Base Re-entry (Weeks 1–4, March)**
- Pure Zone 2 building, 3–4 sessions/week, 45–60 min
- Let aerobic system re-adapt after the winter lifting-dominant phase
- No threshold or intervals yet

**Phase 2 — Threshold Introduction (Weeks 5–8, April)**
- 2–3 Zone 2 sessions maintained
- Add 1 Zone 4 threshold session (4 × 5 min at Zone 4, 2 min easy recovery)
- LT1 rises = can sustain higher paces without accumulating lactate

**Phase 3 — VO2 Max Blocks (Weeks 9–12, May)**
- 2 Zone 2 sessions (slightly reduced duration)
- 2 VO2 max interval sessions (Norwegian 4×4 or 5 × 3 min)
- Peak spring fitness here — highest VO2 max of the year

### Lifting — Maintenance (2×/week Full Body)
Do NOT stop lifting — maintain winter muscle gains with just enough stimulus.

**Full Body A (Monday)**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Squat | 3×6–8 (heavier — maintenance) |
| Pull-Up (weighted if able) | 3×6–8 |
| Bench Press | 3×6–8 |
| RDL | 3×6–8 |
| OHP | 2×8–10 |

**Full Body B (Thursday)**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|----------|-------------|
| Deadlift | 3×4–5 (heavy — prevent strength loss) |
| Incline Row or Cable Row | 3×8 |
| Hip Thrust | 3×8–10 |
| Lat Pulldown | 3×8 |
| Face Pulls | 2×15 |

*Note: drop volume significantly vs. winter — 2 sets less per exercise. Increase relative intensity (higher % of 1RM) to maintain neuromuscular signal with lower fatigue cost.*

### Weekly Template (Phase 3 — Peak Spring)

| Day | Session | Notes |
|-----|---------|-------|
| Monday | **Full Body A** (maintenance lift) | Lift first; done in 45 min |
| Tuesday | **Zone 2 cardio** | 60 min — base maintenance |
| Wednesday | **VO2 Max Intervals** | Norwegian 4×4 or 5 × 3 min |
| Thursday | **Full Body B** (maintenance lift) | Posterior chain emphasis |
| Friday | **Zone 2 cardio** | 60 min |
| Saturday | **Long Zone 2** | 75–90 min easy outdoor ride/run |
| Sunday | **Full rest + mobility** | Static PM protocol |

### Cardio Protocols — Spring Progression

**Zone 2 (Weeks 1–12):**
> 45–90 min at 60–70% max HR; conversational pace; cycling preferred

**Zone 4 Threshold (Weeks 5–12):**
> 4 × 5 min at Zone 4 (80–90% max HR) / 2 min easy recovery
> Feel: hard, labored breathing, sustainable but not comfortable for >10 min

**VO2 Max — Norwegian 4×4 (Weeks 9–12):**
> 4 × 4 min at 90–95% max HR / 3 min active recovery
> Feel: near-maximal effort; can speak only 2–3 words

**VO2 Max — 5 × 3 min variant:**
> 5 × 3 min at max sustainable effort / 3 min recovery
> Slightly more total reps; 3-min intervals accumulate more time above 90% VO2 max

## SUMMER — STRENGTH (June–August)

### Goal
Maximize raw strength. Move the most weight possible across the main compound lifts (squat, deadlift, bench, OHP, row). Build CNS power and 1RM strength.

### Why Summer
High motivation (summer energy, daylight, aesthetics drive). Lighter schedule for many students. Best time for consistency — no winter school crush. The physique built in winter now has to perform. Strength season follows endurance season naturally: aerobic base is high, body is lean from spring cut, ready to push CNS limits.

### Key Rule: Minimize Cardio Interference
During a strength peak, cardio must be dialed back to avoid competing for CNS recovery. Running especially interferes — eccentric muscle damage from running blunts strength adaptation. Switch entirely to cycling for cardio during summer.

### Nutrition
- **Calories**: slight surplus or maintenance (+100–200 kcal)
- Full caloric surplus is less critical for strength gains than for hypertrophy
- **Protein**: 1.8–2.2 g/kg maintained
- **Creatine**: 3–5 g/day (if not already on it — significant strength boost at low cost)

### Lifting Split — Upper/Lower Strength (4–5 days/week)

**Lower A (Monday) — Quad dominant, heavy**

| Back Squat | 5×3–5 | 82–87% 1RM |
| Romanian Deadlift | 4×4–5 | Heavy, controlled |
| Hack Squat (2nd quad exercise) | 3×5–6 | 78–83% 1RM |
| Lying Leg Curl | 3×6–8 | Controlled; don't sacrifice form |

**Upper A (Tuesday) — Push dominant, heavy**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Intensity |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Bench Press | 5×3–5 | 82–87% 1RM — priority lift |
| Weighted Pull-Up | 4×4–5 | Supinated grip; heavy |
| Overhead Press | 3×5–6 | 78–83% 1RM |
| Cable Row | 3×6–8 | Heavy, full ROM |
| Face Pulls | 2×12 | Shoulder health maintenance |

**Zone 2 Cardio (Wednesday) — Recovery + Maintenance**
- 45 min cycling, Zone 2 (60–70% max HR)
- Flush legs, active recovery between Mon/Tue and Thu/Fri

**Lower B (Thursday) — Posterior chain dominant, heavy**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Intensity |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Conventional Deadlift | 5×3–5 | 83–88% 1RM — #1 priority for Joe |
| Hip Thrust (heavy) | 4×5–6 | Load toward 1.5× BW |
| Bulgarian Split Squat | 3×5–6 ea | Unilateral strength + hip flexor |
| Nordic Curl | 3×5–6 | Highest hamstring strength carry-over |

**Upper B (Friday) — Pull dominant, heavy**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Intensity |

## FALL — SPEED (September–November)

### Goal
Develop speed, anaerobic capacity, neuromuscular power, and explosive strength. This phase develops fast-twitch fiber recruitment, maximal sprint output, and explosive force — qualities that make all future training more effective.

### What "Speed" Means for General Fitness
Speed = the ability to produce maximum force in minimum time. Trains:
- **Sprint speed** (linear and lateral acceleration)
- **Explosive power** (vertical jump, broad jump, power cleans)
- **Anaerobic capacity** (how long you can sustain high intensity before dropping off)
- **Fast-twitch fiber development** (Type IIa and IIx fiber recruitment and growth)

### Why Fall
Cooldown from summer heat → ideal for outdoor sprint work (comfortable temperatures). Post-strength season: CNS and connective tissues are strong and ready for explosive loading. Speed work serves as the transition from strength → winter hypertrophy by converting CNS patterns to higher-velocity outputs.

### Nutrition
- Transition to maintenance or slight surplus
- **Calories**: +100–200 kcal/day
- **Protein**: 1.8–2.2 g/kg
- Begin preparing for winter lean bulk

### Sprint Protocols — Three Levels

**Sprint Level 1 — Power Sprints (max speed, full recovery)**
> 8–10 × 30 sec all-out sprint / 2–3 min full recovery  
> Run at absolute maximum speed on flat surface or slight hill  
> Focus: stride length + stride rate (true max speed)  
> Sessions/week: 2  
> Recovery: 36–48 hrs between sessions

**Sprint Level 2 — Repeated Sprint Ability (RSA)**
> 10 × 10 sec all-out / 90 sec easy jog recovery  
> More metabolic than Level 1; builds anaerobic capacity  
> Sessions/week: 1 (paired with Level 1 in alternating weeks)

**Sprint Level 3 — Hill Sprints (power + speed endurance)**
> 6–8 × 20 sec uphill all-out / walk back down (~90 sec recovery)  
> Hill adds resistance → forces higher power output per stride  
> Fewer injury risk than flat sprints at max speed  
> Best starting point if new to sprinting

### Power Lifting (fall adaptation of lifting)
Shift from heavy slow lifts (summer) to explosive power lifts — same muscles, but velocity emphasis. Key principle: **try to move the bar as fast as possible on the concentric phase**, even with 75–80% loads.

**Power Lower (Monday)**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Box Jump | 4×5 | Full effort, land soft; hip hinge reset between reps |
| Power Clean or Trap Bar Jump | 4×3 | Explosiveness > load; start with bar or light plates |
| Back Squat — Explosive | 4×3–4 @ 75–80% | FAST concentric; 3 sec eccentric |
| Hip Thrust — Explosive | 3×5 | Drive hard off floor; not slow controlled |
| Sprint Protocol Level 1 | 8 × 30 sec | After lifting, or same day AM/PM |

**Power Upper (Wednesday)**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Med Ball Chest Throw or Explosive Push-Up | 4×5 | Plyometric press movement |
| Push Press | 4×3–4 | Leg drive initiates; arms finish overhead |
| Pull-Up — Explosive | 4×4 | Start dead hang; explode to chin over bar |
| Barbell Row — Explosive | 3×5 | Pull fast; controlled lowering |
| Face Pulls | 2×15 | Shoulder health maintenance |

**Power Full Body (Friday)**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Squat Jump (weighted or BW) | 4×5 | Jump on concentric |
| Romanian DL — Explosive | 3×5 | Fast hip extension |
| Hip Thrust Explosive | 3×5 | |
| Sprint Protocol Level 2 | 10 × 10 sec | Post-session or separate AM session |

### Weekly Template

| Day | Session | Notes |
|-----|---------|-------|
| Monday | **Power Lower + Sprints** | Sprint first if AM/PM split; power lift after |
| Tuesday | **Zone 1 active recovery** | 30 min easy cycling or walk; flush legs |
| Wednesday | **Power Upper** | Explosive pressing + pulling |
| Thursday | **Hill Sprints** | 6–8 × 20 sec; or zone 2 (recovery week) |
| Friday | **Power Full Body + Sprints** | Squat jump, RDL, hip thrust explosive |
| Saturday | **Zone 2 cardio** | 45 min cycling; maintain aerobic base |
| Sunday | **Full rest + mobility** | AM + PM mobility protocol |

### Fall 3-Phase Progression

| Weeks | Focus | Lifting Load | Sprint Volume |
|-------|-------|-------------|---------------|
| 1–4 | Intro to explosive work | 65–75% 1RM, fast | Build sprint volume gradually |
| 5–8 | Speed-strength peak | 75–83% 1RM, explosive | Peak sprint sessions 3×/week |
| 9–12 | Convert to power-endurance | HIIT + power circuit finishers | Shift to RSA (repeated sprint ability) |

## Season Transition Protocols

### Winter → Spring (Late February)
1. Drop 1 lifting day per week (from 4 to 3)
2. Add 1 cardio session per week over 2 weeks
3. Reduce lifting intensity by ~15% (lower % 1RM)
4. Increase rep ranges (6–8 → 8–12)
5. 1-week transition of unstructured movement only (walks, casual cycling) as full reset

### Spring → Summer (Late May)
1. Taper cardio over 2 weeks (4 sessions → 2 sessions/week)
2. Increase lifting intensity gradually week by week
3. Shift rep ranges from 8–12 → 6–8 → 4–6 over the first 3 weeks of summer
4. Switch cardio to cycling only (not running — preserves legs for heavy squats/deadlifts)

### Summer → Fall (Late August)
1. Reduce % 1RM on all lifts by 10–15%
2. Introduce box jumps and explosive movements as warm-up for 2 weeks before full power split
3. Add first sprint session (Level 3 hill sprints — safest entry)
4. Maintain 2 Zone 2 sessions/week throughout

### Fall → Winter (Late November)
1. Eliminate sprint sessions over 2 weeks (replace with HIIT cardio, then Zone 2 only)
2. Shift lifting velocity back to slow/controlled (3-sec eccentric returns)
3. Increase rep ranges into 8–12 hypertrophy range
4. Begin lean bulk caloric surplus (+200 kcal, then +300 kcal)
5. 1-week full deload before starting winter program

## Joe-Specific Adjustments

Given **strong quads / weak posterior chain and upper body** (cyclist background):
- Every season: add an extra set of posterior chain work (RDL, hip thrust, Nordic curl) versus the programs above
- Every season: prioritize OHP and pulling volume (rows, pull-ups, face pulls) over pressing
- **Winter**: dedicate Lower B entirely to posterior chain — do not substitute a quad exercise
- **Spring**: both full-body maintenance days must include RDL + pull-up
- **Summer**: Lower B (deadlift + hip thrust priority) is the most important day of the week
- **Fall**: power clean and hip thrust explosive develop glute/hamstring power to close the cyclist imbalance
