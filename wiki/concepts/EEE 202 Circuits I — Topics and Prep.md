# EEE 202 Circuits I — Topics and Prep

**Instructor**: Dragica Vasileska | **Lab Instructor**: Myhajlenko
**Textbook**: Irwin, *Basic Engineering Circuit Analysis*, 10th ed. (Wiley)
**Software**: PSpice, MATLAB

## Topic Map (in order)

| # | Unit | Core Skills |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Fundamentals** | Ohm's Law, KVL, KCL, single-loop, single-node-pair circuits |
| 2 | **Circuit Analysis** | Series-parallel resistors, dependent sources, nodal analysis, mesh analysis |
| 3 | **Network Theorems** | Superposition, source transformation, Thevenin, Norton equivalents |
| 4 | **Energy Storage** | Capacitors (I = C dV/dt), inductors (V = L dI/dt), transient step response |
| 5 | **First/Second-Order ODEs** | RC, RL natural/forced/complete response; RLC damping cases |
| 6 | **AC Analysis** | Phasors (Euler's formula), impedance Z = R + jX, AC steady-state |
| 7 | **Frequency Domain** | Transfer functions H(jω), Bode plots, passive filters (LP/HP/BP) |
| 8 | **Laplace Transforms** | S-domain circuit models, initial conditions, partial fractions, inverse Laplace |

## Must-Know Math Before Day 1

- Algebra: complex number arithmetic (magnitude, phase, conjugate)
- Euler's formula: e^(jωt) = cos(ωt) + j sin(ωt)
- Derivatives and integrals (Calculus I/II level)
- Factoring and partial fractions (for Laplace inverse)

## Summer Prep Priority

1. **Units 1–3 first** — these are the first 4-5 weeks of class; master them before arrival
2. **Complex numbers** — the phasors in Unit 6 are pure complex arithmetic; drill now
3. **Laplace tables** — memorize the 8 most common pairs before Unit 8
