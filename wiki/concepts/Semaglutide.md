# Semaglutide

Semaglutide is the first-generation blockbuster GLP-1 receptor agonist developed by Novo Nordisk. It is a GLP-1 analog with ~94% structural homology to human GLP-1, engineered with a fatty acid chain that extends its half-life to ~7 days, enabling once-weekly dosing. It is the same molecule sold under three brand names depending on indication and route.

---

## Brand Names and Indications

| Brand | Form | Indication | Max Dose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ozempic** | SubQ injection | Type 2 Diabetes | 2 mg/week |
| **Wegovy** | SubQ injection | Chronic weight management | 2.4 mg/week |
| **Rybelsus** | Oral tablet | Type 2 Diabetes | 14 mg/day |
| **Oral Wegovy** | Oral tablet | Chronic weight management | 25 mg/day |

> FDA approved oral semaglutide for weight management in December 2025 (the first GLP-1 pill for obesity).

---

## Mechanism

Semaglutide acts exclusively on the **GLP-1 receptor**:

1. **Central appetite suppression** — crosses the blood-brain barrier; activates GLP-1 receptors in the hypothalamus and brainstem, reducing hunger signals and food noise (intrusive food thoughts)
2. **Gastric emptying delay** — slows how fast food leaves the stomach, prolonging fullness
3. **Glucose-dependent insulin secretion** — stimulates insulin only in the presence of elevated glucose; low hypoglycemia risk in non-diabetics
4. **Glucagon suppression** — reduces post-meal glucagon, limiting liver glucose output

Half-life ~7 days → reaches steady state after 4–5 weeks of weekly dosing.

## Dosing Table (Wegovy — Weight Management)

| Week | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | **0.25 mg/week** | Initiation dose — not therapeutic for weight loss; GI adaptation only |
| 5–8 | **0.5 mg/week** | Begin to feel appetite suppression |
| 9–12 | **1 mg/week** | Meaningful weight loss begins for most patients |
| 13–16 | **1.7 mg/week** | Significant appetite reduction |
| 17+ | **2.4 mg/week** | Target maintenance dose; most weight loss occurs here |

**Note:** If 2.4 mg is not tolerated, 1.7 mg is an acceptable long-term maintenance dose. Never skip titration steps — GI side effects are significantly worse.

## Oral Semaglutide Dosing (Oral Wegovy — Weight Management)

| Phase | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1–4 | 3 mg/day | Initiation |
| Weeks 5–8 | 7 mg/day | Escalation |
| Weeks 9–12 | 14 mg/day | Mid-range |
| Week 13+ | **25 mg/day** | Target maintenance |

Take on an empty stomach with ≤4 oz water; wait 30 minutes before eating. Food dramatically reduces oral bioavailability.

## Key Clinical Notes

- **SELECT trial (2024):** Major milestone — 20% reduction in cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke, CV death) in patients with pre-existing cardiovascular disease. Semaglutide is now FDA-approved specifically for cardiovascular risk reduction.
- **Half-life:** 7 days. Missing one dose will not wipe out efficacy but don't make it a habit. Resume the following week's normal schedule.
- **Injection site:** Abdomen, thigh, or upper arm — rotate sites each week.
- **Storage:** Refrigerate (2–8°C) before first use; can stay at room temp (max 30°C) for up to 56 days after first use.

---

## Side Effects

**Very common (>10%):** Nausea (most common; peaks during dose escalation), constipation, diarrhea, vomiting

**Common (1–10%):** Fatigue, headache, dizziness, abdominal pain, injection site reactions, belching/flatulence, GERD

**Serious (rare):** Acute pancreatitis, gallbladder disease (gallstones), worsening diabetic retinopathy (in T2D patients), kidney injury (usually secondary to dehydration from vomiting)

**Black box warning:** Thyroid C-cell tumors (rodent data; human relevance unestablished; 2026 meta-analysis of 101,732 patients found no significant association)

---

## Contraindications

- Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN2)
- Hypersensitivity to semaglutide
- Pregnancy (discontinue 2 months before planned conception)
- Active or history of pancreatitis (relative contraindication)

---

## How It Differs from Tirzepatide

Semaglutide acts only on GLP-1 receptors. Tirzepatide adds GIP receptor agonism, which provides synergistic insulin sensitization and superior fat redistribution. In SURMOUNT-5 (direct head-to-head), tirzepatide produced 47% more weight loss than semaglutide at matched doses and timepoints. Semaglutide's advantage: it has the larger long-term evidence base and the SELECT cardiovascular outcomes trial data.
