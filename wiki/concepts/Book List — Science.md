# Book List — Science

> Goal: build actual scientific intuition, not just facts. The best science books teach you how scientists think, not just what they concluded.

## S-Tier — Foundational

- **The Feynman Lectures on Physics** — Richard Feynman — the gold standard physics curriculum; all three volumes; freely available online at feynmanlectures.caltech.edu; read even if you're taking physics courses — they are superior; ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **The Selfish Gene** — Richard Dawkins — evolution through the gene's perspective; "meme" coined here; reshapes how you think about competition, cooperation, and design; foundational evolutionary biology
- **A Short History of Nearly Everything** — Bill Bryson — how science discovered what it knows, told as narrative; exceptional breadth from cosmology to geology to biology; best single-volume overview of all of science

## A-Tier — High Value

- **Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!** — Richard Feynman — technically memoir, actually a masterclass in how a physicist thinks; curiosity, play, and intellectual honesty as methodology
- **The Gene** — Siddhartha Mukherjee — Mendel to CRISPR; history + science of genetics; most readable genetics book in print; won the Pulitzer
- **QED: The Strange Theory of Light and Matter** — Feynman — quantum electrodynamics explained without equations; arrow diagrams only; genuinely mind-bending; accessible to anyone
- **The Emperor of All Maladies** — Siddhartha Mukherjee — biography of cancer; medicine and the hunt for a cure as detective story; Pulitzer Prize-winning narrative science
- **Astrophysics for People in a Hurry** — Neil deGrasse Tyson — compressed cosmology; best one-sitting overview of the universe and our place in it
- **The Double Helix** — James Watson — how the DNA structure was actually found; the sociology, competition, and credit disputes of real science; important character study

## B-Tier — Solid

- **The Structure of Scientific Revolutions** — Thomas Kuhn — paradigm shifts; how science actually changes its mind; meta-science; "paradigm" as a concept starts here
- **The Elegant Universe** — Brian Greene — string theory and the quest for a unified theory; accessible but rigorous; visually imaginative
- **Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid** — Douglas Hofstadter — self-reference, consciousness, formal systems; math + music + art + logic; one of the most mind-expanding books written; non-trivial time investment (800+ pages)
- **The Age of Wonder** — Richard Holmes — Romantic-era scientists; the birth of modern science as adventure and imagination

## For EE Students

- Feynman Lectures Vol. II is Maxwell's equations in the best pedagogical form ever written — read alongside EEE 202
- *QED* explains what electrons actually do, not the simplified model you get in circuit theory
- *The Selfish Gene* applies directly to evolutionary algorithms and emergent complexity — cross-reference [[Heterogeneous Computing]]
