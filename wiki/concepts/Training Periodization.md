# Training Periodization

The systematic structuring of training into phases (cycles) to peak performance at the right time while avoiding overtraining and injury.

## Core Framework

### Three-Level Cycle Structure
| Level | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Macrocycle** | Full season / year | Annual performance arc |
| **Mesocycle** | 4–6 weeks | Phase-specific adaptation block |
| **Microcycle** | 1 week | Daily load arrangement |

Standard mesocycle rhythm: **3 hard weeks + 1 recovery week** (reduce volume ~40%, maintain some intensity).

### Four Macrocycle Phases (Matveyev, 1960s — still dominant)
1. **Preparatory** — General base + sport-specific strength
2. **Competition** — Peak intensity, race-readiness sharpening
3. **Transition** — Active recovery, deload
4. **(Repeat)**

Modern cycling expands the preparatory phase into sub-phases (see below).

## Cycling-Specific Annual Structure

### Phase 1 — General Preparation / Base (Oct–Dec)
- High volume, low intensity (Zone 1–2)
- Gym strength training 2–3×/week (hypertrophy + strength phases)
- Aerobic engine building
- **Goal**: develop fat oxidation, build muscular foundation

### Phase 2 — Specific Preparation / Build (Jan–Mar)
- Introduce sweet spot and threshold work
- Reduce gym to 1–2×/week (power phase, sport-specific)
- Increase ride volume and structure
- **Goal**: raise FTP, convert strength gains to cycling power

### Phase 3 — Pre-Competition / Intensity (Apr–May)
- VO2 max interval blocks (2–3 week focused blocks)
- High-intensity intervals: 2–5 min at 106–120% FTP
- Volume pulled back slightly to absorb intensity
- **Goal**: raise power ceiling, sharpen race fitness

### Phase 4 — Competition / Peak (Jun–Aug)
- Maintain fitness with reduced volume
- Race-specific efforts, openers, sharpening
- Strength training minimal or suspended
- **Goal**: perform, not build

### Phase 5 — Transition (Sep–Oct)
- Unstructured riding or cross-training
- Full mental/physical break from structured training
- 2–4 weeks fully off, then easy riding
- **Goal**: recover and reset motivation

## Key Principles
- **Progressive overload**: each mesocycle increases demand before recovery week
- **Specificity**: adaptations are specific to the stimulus applied
- **Reversibility**: fitness decays without stimulus — don't skip phases
- **Individuality**: adjust volume/intensity to personal recovery capacity

## Common Models
- **Traditional (Matveyev)**: high volume early, add intensity later
- **Block Periodization**: concentrate one quality per 2–4 week block (see [[Block Periodization]])
- **Polarized (Seiler)**: 80% low / 20% high, avoid the middle zone (see [[Polarized Training]])
