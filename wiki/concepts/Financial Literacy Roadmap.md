# Financial Literacy Roadmap

> Phase-by-phase guide from financial zero to financially independent. Sequence matters — skipping phases creates expensive holes.

## The Five Phases

### Phase 0 — Financial Foundation
**Goal:** Stop bleeding money and build minimal safety net.

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| Budget | Track all income and expenses for 30 days | Can't optimize what you don't measure |
| Bank | Open fee-free checking + high-yield savings (Fidelity, Schwab, Ally ≥ 4% APY) | Avoid monthly fees; earn interest while you save |
| Eliminate high-interest debt | Pay off credit cards, payday loans | 20%+ interest rate is guaranteed wealth destruction |
| Starter emergency fund | Save $1,000 first | Prevents going back into debt on first crisis |

**Completion signal:** You know exactly where every dollar goes; no high-interest debt; $1k saved.

---

### Phase 1 — First Financial Wins
**Goal:** Capture free money and open investing infrastructure.

| Step | Action | Priority |
|------|--------|---------|
| Employer match | Contribute enough to 401(k) to get 100% employer match | Instant 50–100% return beats every other use of money |
| Open Roth IRA | Fidelity or Schwab, buy VTI or FXAIX | Most tax-advantaged vehicle for young people |
| Build credit | One secured card, pay in full monthly | Credit score unlocks housing, cars, employment checks |

**Key number:** 2026 Roth IRA limit = $7,500/year (requires earned income of at least that amount).

---

### Phase 2 — Build the Moat
**Goal:** Emergency fund complete; savings automated; financial behavior installed.

- Grow emergency fund: 3 months of expenses minimum (6 months for self-employed or single income)
- Automate: set recurring Roth IRA contribution on payday — remove willpower from equation
- Read: *Psychology of Money* (Housel) + *I Will Teach You to Be Rich* (Sethi)
- Understand taxes: W-2 vs. 1099 self-employment tax; standard deduction ($15,000 single in 2026); which bracket you're in

---

### Phase 3 — Wealth Accumulation
**Goal:** Consistently invest 15–20% of gross income into diversified low-cost index funds.

**Core portfolio options (pick one):**
- **Simplest**: VTWAX (Vanguard Total World Stock Index) — one fund, whole market
- **Classic 2-fund**: VTI (70%) + VXUS (30%) — US + international
- **Fidelity equivalent**: FZROX (US) + FZILX (international) — 0% expense ratio

**The rules:**
1. Dollar-cost average every paycheck regardless of market conditions
2. Never sell in a downturn — missing the 10 best trading days cuts returns by 60%
3. Minimize costs: expense ratio < 0.10% as a hard ceiling

---

### Phase 4 — Tax Optimization
**Goal:** Maximize tax-advantaged space before investing in taxable accounts.

| Account | 2026 Limit | Tax Benefit |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Roth IRA | $7,500/year | Tax-free growth + withdrawals |
| 401(k)/403(b) | $24,500/year | Pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth 401k) |
| HSA | $4,300 single / $8,550 family | Triple tax advantage (deduct, grow, withdraw tax-free for healthcare) |
| **Total shielded** | **~$36,300/year** | |

**Asset location rule:** Put bonds and REITs in tax-advantaged accounts; equities in taxable.

---

### Phase 5 — Financial Independence
**Goal:** Build a portfolio large enough to sustain spending without employment.

- **The FI number**: Annual expenses × 25 (assumes 4% safe withdrawal rate)
  - $40k/year expenses → $1,000,000 needed
  - $60k/year expenses → $1,500,000 needed
  - $80k/year expenses → $2,000,000 needed
- **Coast FIRE**: The earlier milestone — when your portfolio, left alone, reaches FI at retirement age without further contributions
- **Sequence-of-returns risk**: Shift toward bonds as you approach withdrawal phase; 80/20 stocks-bonds at 10 years out; 60/40 at 5 years out

## The 50/30/20 Budget Rule

| Bucket | % of Take-Home | Examples |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| Needs | 50% | Rent, utilities, groceries, insurance, minimum debt payments |
| Wants | 30% | Dining out, entertainment, subscriptions, clothing upgrades |
| Savings/Investing | 20% | Emergency fund, Roth IRA, taxable brokerage, debt payoff |

**As income grows, maintain 20% floor — ideally target 30–40% savings rate for early FI.**

## Key Behaviors (Behavioral Finance)

- **Never sell in a panic** — missing the 10 best S&P days over 20 years cuts annualized return from 6% to 2.4%
- **Automate everything** — automatic transfers remove willpower from the equation
- **Ignore financial media** — news optimizes for engagement, not your returns; check portfolio quarterly
- **Net worth tracking** — monthly snapshot (assets − liabilities); the number that actually matters
- **Reasonable > Optimal** — a 7% return strategy you maintain for 30 years beats a 12% strategy you abandon
