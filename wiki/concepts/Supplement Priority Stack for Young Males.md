# Supplement Priority Stack for Young Males

Practical implementation guide. Ordered by evidence strength, safety margin, and cost-effectiveness. Build the stack in tiers — don't start everything at once.

---

## Tier 1 — Non-Negotiable Foundation
*Fix these before anything else. Low cost, high evidence, addresses common deficiencies.*

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Monthly Cost |
|-----------|------|--------|-------------|
| **Vitamin D3** | 2,000–4,000 IU | Morning with food | ~$5 |
| **Vitamin K2** (MK-7) | 100 mcg | With D3 | ~$5 |
| **Magnesium Glycinate** | 300–400 mg elemental | Evening / pre-sleep | ~$10 |
| **Omega-3** (Fish or Algal Oil) | 1,000–2,000 mg EPA+DHA | With meals | ~$15 |

**Total Tier 1: ~$35/month**

Get a basic blood panel first if possible: vitamin D (25-OH), ferritin, zinc. Targeted correction beats blind supplementation.

---

## Tier 2 — Well-Evidenced Performance
*Add after Tier 1 is consistent for 2–4 weeks.*

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Monthly Cost |
|-----------|------|--------|-------------|
| **Creatine Monohydrate** | 3–5 g | Any time (consistent) | ~$5–10 |
| **Caffeine + L-Theanine** | 100–200 mg caffeine + 200–400 mg L-theanine | Pre-study session | ~$10 |

**Notes:**
- Creatine benefits physical performance (high confidence) and cognitive performance under load (medium confidence)
- Caffeine+L-theanine is situational — use for focus sessions, not daily all-day dosing
- Cycle caffeine: avoid tolerance buildup. At minimum take 1–2 caffeine-free days per week.

**Total Tier 2: ~$15–20/month**

---

## Tier 3 — Longer-Term / Situational
*Add one at a time. Give 8–12 weeks to assess. These have real but modest evidence.*

| Supplement | Dose | Best Use Case | Monthly Cost |
|-----------|------|---------------|-------------|
| **Bacopa Monnieri** | 300–600 mg (45% bacosides) | Semester-long retention | ~$15 |
| **Rhodiola Rosea** (SHR-5) | 300–680 mg | Exam/deadline stress periods | ~$15 |
| **L-Theanine** alone | 200–400 mg | Pre-sleep anxiety, calm focus | ~$8 |

**Note:** Don't stack all of Tier 3 simultaneously — you won't know what's working.

---

## Tier 4 — Low Evidence / Not Worth It Yet
*Skip these. Insufficient human evidence or cost-to-benefit not justified.*

| Supplement | Reason to Skip |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Lion's Mane** | Promising animal/preliminary data; too early for healthy young adults |
| **NMN / NR** (NAD+) | Longevity research; no clear benefit in healthy 19-year-olds |
| **Noopept, Racetams** | Unregulated in US; insufficient safety data; prescription in some countries |
| **Ginkgo biloba** | Evidence mixed; drug interactions with anticoagulants; no clear benefit in healthy young adults |
| **High-dose melatonin (5–10 mg)** | Pharmacological overdose; use 0.1–0.5 mg only |

## What Actually Moves the Needle Most

In order of evidence and impact for a 19-year-old EE student:

1. **Sleep** (7.5–9 hours, consistent schedule) — not a supplement but the highest-leverage intervention
2. **Exercise** (3–5× per week, even just 30 min) — acute cognitive benefit, sleep improvement, stress reduction
3. **Vitamin D + Magnesium** (most likely deficient)
4. **Creatine** (safe, cheap, dual physical+cognitive benefit)
5. **Caffeine + L-Theanine** (best acute study tool)
6. **Omega-3** (long-term brain membrane health)
7. **Bacopa** (if committing to a semester-long experiment)

> [!key-insight] The basics are the gains
> Most supplement marketing targets the 5% gains on top of a solid baseline. A 19-year-old sleeping 6 hours, eating poorly, and not exercising will get more from fixing those three things than from any nootropic stack.

## Safety Note

> [!gap] This is compiled from peer-reviewed research, not medical advice. Before starting any supplement, particularly if on medications, consult a physician. Get blood work if possible — knowing your actual levels prevents wasted money and over-supplementation.
