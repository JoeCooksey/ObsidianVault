# Workout Splits Tier List

Evidence-based ranking of all major workout splits for muscle building. Key finding: no split has an inherent advantage — consistency and adequate volume matter more than split choice. That said, some splits are more efficient for different experience levels.

---

## Tier List

### S-Tier — Optimal for Most Goals

| Split | Days/Week | Frequency/Muscle | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PPL (Push Pull Legs) 6-day** | 6 | 2× | Intermediate–advanced; maximum volume |
| **Upper/Lower 4-day** | 4 | 2× | All levels; best balance of volume + recovery |

**PPL 6-day**: Each muscle trained 2× per week with high total weekly volume. Push = chest/shoulders/triceps; Pull = back/biceps; Legs = quads/hamstrings/glutes. Best overall hypertrophy split for dedicated trainees.

**Upper/Lower 4-day**: 2 upper sessions + 2 lower sessions per week. Clean 2× frequency. Easier to structure progressive overload. Excellent for intermediate lifters. Studies show equivalent results to 6-day splits.

---

### A-Tier — Very Effective

| Split | Days/Week | Frequency/Muscle | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Full Body 3-day** | 3 | 3× | Beginners; general fitness |
| **Full Body 5-day** | 5 | 5× | Advanced; prioritizing frequency over volume |
| **Upper/Lower 3-day** | 3 | 1.5× | Busy schedules; intermediate |

**Full Body 3-day**: Every session trains all major groups. Superior strength gains (+50% over same volume), excellent for beginners whose nervous system adaptation drives early progress. Lower absolute volume per session but high frequency.

---

### B-Tier — Good, With Caveats

| Split | Days/Week | Frequency/Muscle | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PPL 3-day** | 3 | 1× | Decent volume, low frequency — a limitation |
| **Arnold Split (6-day)** | 6 | 2× | Pairs chest/back and shoulders/arms |

---

### C-Tier — Suboptimal

| Split | Days/Week | Frequency/Muscle | Problem |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bro Split** | 5 | 1× | 1× frequency per muscle — research shows 2× is better |
| **2-day Full Body** | 2 | 2× | Too low volume and frequency for most goals |

Bro splits (Chest Mon, Back Tue, Shoulders Wed, Arms Thu, Legs Fri) dominated the bodybuilding era but leave muscles undertrained — only hit once per week, leaving 6 days of recovery with no re-stimulation.

---

### D-Tier

| Split | Problem |
|---|---|
| **1-day per week (once/week full body)** | Insufficient stimulus — barely maintenance |
| **Random/no split** | No progressive overload structure |

---

## Which Split to Choose — Decision Table

| Situation | Recommended Split |
|---|---|
| Beginner (0–1 year) | Full Body 3-day |
| Intermediate (1–3 years) | Upper/Lower 4-day or PPL 6-day |
| Advanced (3+ years) | PPL 6-day |
| Limited time (3 days) | Full Body 3-day or PPL 3-day |
| Lagging muscle group | Add frequency via whatever split you're on |
| In season athlete | Upper/Lower 3–4-day |

## Sample 4-Day Upper/Lower Split

**Monday (Upper A)**
- Bench Press 4×6–8
- Cable Row 4×8–10
- Incline DB Press 3×10–12
- Lat Pulldown 3×10–12
- Lateral Raise 3×12–15
- Bicep Curl 3×12–15

**Tuesday (Lower A)**
- Barbell Squat 4×6–8
- Romanian Deadlift 3×8–10
- Leg Press 3×10–12
- Leg Curl 3×12–15
- Calf Raise 4×12–15

**Thursday (Upper B)**
- Overhead Press 4×6–8
- Weighted Pull-Up 4×6–8
- Dumbbell Incline Press 3×10–12
- Cable Row 3×10–12
- Pec Deck 3×12–15
- Tricep Pushdown 3×12–15

**Friday (Lower B)**
- Deadlift 3×5
- Bulgarian Split Squat 3×8–10
- Hack Squat 3×10–12
- Leg Extension 3×12–15
- Nordic Curl or Leg Curl 3×8–10

---

## Sample PPL 6-Day Split

**Push A (Mon/Thu)**: Bench press, OHP, incline DB press, lateral raise, tricep pushdown, overhead extension
**Pull A (Tue/Fri)**: Deadlift/rack pull, lat pulldown, cable row, face pulls, bicep curl
**Legs A (Wed/Sat)**: Squat, leg press, RDL, leg curl, leg extension, calf raise
Rest: Sunday
