## Finding a Mentor — Priority Steps

1. **Read 1–2 papers** from their group (Google Scholar) first
2. **Office hours** (if in a course they teach) → visit 2–3 times, build rapport, then ask
3. **Cold email** if no course contact:
   - 7–12 sentences: who you are → what specifically you read → what you offer → ask for 15-min meeting
   - Attach resume + unofficial transcript
   - One professor at a time; wait 7 business days before following up
4. **The ask**: "Would you be willing to mentor me through FURI?"

## What Happens During the Semester

| Phase | Activity |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1–4 | Literature review + environment setup |
| Weeks 3–6 | Experimental or simulation design |
| Weeks 5–12 | Data collection / runs / iterations |
| Weeks 10–14 | Analysis + written summary + abstract |
| Weeks 13–15 | Poster design and print |
| Week 16 | **Fulton Forge Student Research Expo** (public poster presentation) |

Also required: FURI workshops, mid-semester and final progress summaries.

---

## Fulton Forge Poster Specs

- **Size**: 23" × 35" (horizontal or vertical)
- **Required sections**: Title + Authors | Background | Methods | Results | Conclusions | References
- **Rule**: less text, more charts/graphs/diagrams
- **Data**: use tables or figures — never paragraphs for results
- **Conclusions**: 2–4 sentences max
- **Print**: print.asu.edu/print-lab/student-showcases/fulton-forge-showcase
