# FFMI — Fat-Free Mass Index & Natural Muscle Potential

FFMI is the gold-standard metric for measuring muscularity normalized for height. More useful than BMI or scale weight for tracking physique development.

---

## Formula

```
FFMI = Lean Body Mass (kg) / Height (m)²
```

Where:
- Lean Body Mass = Total Weight × (1 − Body Fat %)
- Use metric units (kg and m)

---

## FFMI Scale for Males

| FFMI | Description |
|---|---|
| 16–18 | Untrained / below average |
| 18–20 | Average physique; some training history |
| 20–21 | "Looks like you lift" — noticeable muscularity |
| 21–23 | Above average; dedicated lifter; impressive at shirt-off |
| 23–25 | Highly muscular; natural bodybuilder level; competitive potential |
| 25+ | Near absolute natural limit (exceptional genetics + 10+ years) |

**Natural FFMI limit:** ~25 for elite genetic responders; most natural lifters plateau at 22–23 after many years of dedicated training.

---

## FFMI Reference at 5'9" (1.7526 m, height² = 3.0716)

| Physique Goal | FFMI | Lean Mass | Total Weight @ 12% BF | Total Weight @ 15% BF |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Decent / "looks fit" | 20 | 133 lbs (60.4 kg) | 151 lbs | 157 lbs |
| Great natural physique | 22 | 149 lbs (67.6 kg) | 169 lbs | 175 lbs |
| Near natural ceiling | 25 | 169 lbs (76.8 kg) | 192 lbs | 199 lbs |

**At 156 lbs, 5'9":**
- At 10% BF → FFMI ≈ 20.7
- At 15% BF → FFMI ≈ 19.6
- At 20% BF → FFMI ≈ 18.4

---

## Why FFMI Over BMI
BMI cannot distinguish fat from muscle. A highly muscular natural lifter at 175 lbs / 5'9" has BMI = 25.8 (technically "overweight") but FFMI ~22 (great physique). FFMI removes this ambiguity.

---

## Steroid Detection Threshold
The original Kouri et al. (1995) paper found that drug-free lifters rarely exceeded FFMI 25 even with optimal genetics and decades of training. FFMI >26–27 is considered a strong statistical flag for PED use.
