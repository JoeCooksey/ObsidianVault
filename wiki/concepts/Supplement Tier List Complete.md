# Supplement Tier List — Complete

Tiers: **S** (transformative, highest evidence + safety) | **A** (strong evidence, meaningful effect) | **B** (moderate evidence, situational value) | **C** (weak/mixed, use cautiously) | **D** (unsupported or risky)

> [!note] This is educational only. Consult a physician before starting any supplement protocol. Dosages listed are common research-supported ranges — not prescriptions.

---

## 1. Vitamins

| Tier | Supplement | Dose | Notes |
|------|-----------|------|-------|
| **S** | Vitamin D3 | 2,000–5,000 IU/day | 36% of 18–29yo deficient; immune, bone, mood, testosterone |
| **S** | Vitamin K2 (MK-7) | 90–200 mcg/day | Pair with D3; directs calcium to bone not arteries |
| **A** | Vitamin B12 | 500–1,000 mcg | Vegans/vegetarians critically need this; methylcobalamin preferred |
| **A** | Folate (Methylfolate) | 400–800 mcg | Active form (not folic acid) for MTHFR variants |
| **A** | Vitamin C | 500–1,000 mg/day | Collagen synthesis, immune; high dose not harmful |
| **B** | Vitamin B1 (Thiamine) | 100 mg | Carb metabolism; fatigue in high-exercise athletes |
| **B** | Vitamin B6 (P5P) | 10–25 mg | Neurotransmitter synthesis; P5P = active form |
| **B** | Niacin (B3) / Nicotinamide Riboside | 500 mg–1g | NAD+ precursor; flush vs. no-flush debate |
| **B** | Riboflavin (B2) | 400 mg | Migraine prevention; cofactor in energy metabolism |
| **B** | Vitamin A (Retinol) | 2,500–5,000 IU | Vision, immune; toxicity risk at high doses |
| **B** | Vitamin E (mixed tocopherols) | 200–400 IU | Antioxidant; synthetic alpha-tocopherol alone may be harmful |
| **C** | High-dose B-complex | — | Most people get adequate B from diet; waste if replete |
| **C** | Vitamin K1 | 100 mcg | Coagulation support only; K2 more useful |
| **D** | Mega-dose Vitamin C (10g+/day) | — | No proven additional benefit vs 1g; kidney stone risk |
