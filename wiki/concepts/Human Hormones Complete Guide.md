# Human Hormones Complete Guide

The human body produces 50+ hormones. This page covers the ~14 most impactful to understand and optimize, with evidence-based protocols for each.

## Quick-Reference Tier List (Optimization Leverage)

| Tier | Hormone(s) | Why It Matters |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| **S** | Sleep (GH + Melatonin + Testosterone) | All three reset during sleep; one behavior optimizes three hormones |
| **S** | Cortisol | Chronic elevation sabotages every other hormone on this list |
| **S** | Insulin | Central metabolic regulator; insulin resistance cascades into every disease |
| **A** | Testosterone | Strength, energy, confidence, libido, mood |
| **A** | Thyroid (T3/T4) | Sets your entire metabolic rate; deficiency mimics depression and fatigue |
| **A** | Dopamine | Motivation, focus, reward — the "want" system |
| **A** | Serotonin | Mood stability, impulse control, social confidence |
| **B** | Estrogen / Progesterone | Critical for women; both genders need balance |
| **B** | Growth Hormone | Recovery, muscle, fat burning — mostly automated by sleep |
| **B** | Oxytocin | Trust, bonding, stress buffering |
| **C** | Leptin / Ghrelin | Hunger regulation; hard to optimize directly |
| **C** | Adrenaline / Norepinephrine | Fight-or-flight; optimize by reducing triggers, not chasing boosts |
| **C** | DHEA | Adrenal precursor hormone; upstream of testosterone/estrogen |

## 1. Cortisol — The Stress Hormone

**Produced by:** Adrenal cortex  
**Primary function:** Stress response, blood sugar regulation, inflammation control, circadian timing  
**Healthy pattern:** High in the morning (cortisol awakening response → alertness), low at night

### Why it matters
Chronic high cortisol → muscle breakdown, visceral fat gain, suppressed immune function, impaired memory (hippocampal shrinkage), and directly blocks T4→T3 thyroid conversion. It is the master saboteur of every other hormone.

### How to optimize
**S-Tier (biggest levers):**
- Consistent wake time + morning sunlight → anchors the cortisol awakening response and prevents dysregulation
- 7–9 hours of sleep → night is when cortisol bottoms out and recovery hormones dominate
- Eliminate chronic stressors (financial, relational, overtraining) → cortisol is *adaptive* but not designed to be chronically elevated

**A-Tier:**
- Resistance training 3–4x/week → trains the body to produce and clear cortisol efficiently
- HRV biofeedback / breath work → direct parasympathetic activation; lowers nocturnal cortisol spikes
- Limit caffeine after noon → caffeine is a cortisol agonist; late-day use extends the stress hormone into recovery windows

**B-Tier:**
- Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) 300–600 mg KSM-66 → meta-analyses show 15–30% cortisol reduction
- Phosphatidylserine 400–800 mg → blunts cortisol response to exercise; one of the better-studied supplements
- Rhodiola rosea → adaptogen; reduces fatigue-driven cortisol

**Destroyers:** Chronic sleep debt, overtraining without recovery, alcohol (spikes cortisol 15–30% night after drinking), high sugar diet (blood sugar crashes → cortisol spike), social isolation

## 2. Insulin — The Metabolic Master

**Produced by:** Pancreatic beta cells  
**Primary function:** Blood sugar regulation; shuttles glucose into cells; anabolic signal for muscle and fat  
**Goal:** Maximize insulin *sensitivity* (cells respond well at low doses); avoid insulin *resistance*

### Why it matters
Insulin resistance is the upstream cause of Type 2 diabetes, PCOS, NAFLD, metabolic syndrome, and cardiovascular disease. High chronic insulin also suppresses fat burning and promotes fat storage.

### How to optimize insulin sensitivity
**S-Tier:**
- Resistance training → most powerful single intervention; muscle is the body's glucose sink. Meta-analysis: combined resistance+cardio outperforms either alone
- Reduce added sugar and refined carbohydrates → eliminates the repeated glucose spikes that exhaust beta cells over time
- Maintain a healthy body weight / reduce visceral fat → visceral fat directly causes insulin resistance via inflammatory cytokines

**A-Tier:**
- Intermittent fasting (16:8 or 18:6) → a 2025 meta-analysis found significant reductions in fasting glucose, HbA1c, and HOMA-IR; fasting lowers insulin by eliminating the eating window
- High-fiber diet (30–40 g/day) → soluble fiber slows glucose absorption; supports gut microbiome which indirectly affects insulin signaling
- Cold exposure (cold showers, cold plunge) → activates GLUT4 transporters independently of insulin

**B-Tier:**
- Walk 10–15 min after meals → post-meal walks reduce glucose spikes by 20–30%
- Apple cider vinegar (1–2 tbsp before high-carb meals) → acetic acid slows gastric emptying and starch digestion; modest but real effect
- Berberine 500 mg 2–3x/day → considered as effective as metformin in some RCTs for insulin sensitization

**Destroyers:** Sedentary lifestyle, high sugar/refined carb intake, chronic sleep deprivation (one night of 4h sleep → 25% decrease in insulin sensitivity), visceral obesity

## 3. Testosterone — Drive and Anabolism

**Produced by:** Testes (men), ovaries + adrenals (women)  
**Primary function:** Muscle growth, bone density, libido, energy, confidence, red blood cell production  
**Normal male range:** 300–1000 ng/dL; optimal 600–900 ng/dL

### See also
[[Testosterone Habits Tier List]] — full tier list of habits ranked by testosterone impact

### How to optimize (summary)
**S-Tier:** Heavy compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, bench) → most potent natural testosterone stimulus | Sleep 7–9 hours → ~80% of daily testosterone is produced during sleep | Reduce body fat to 10–20% → adipose tissue converts testosterone to estrogen via aromatase | Vitamin D 2000–5000 IU/day (if deficient) → Vitamin D acts as a steroid hormone; deficiency = low T

**A-Tier:** Zinc 25–45 mg/day (if deficient) | Limit alcohol → suppresses Leydig cell testosterone production dose-dependently | Manage cortisol → cortisol and testosterone have an inverse relationship | Adequate dietary fat (≥30% of calories from fat including saturated) → testosterone is synthesized from cholesterol

**B-Tier:** Ashwagandha KSM-66 → multiple RCTs show 15–20% free testosterone increase | Fenugreek → modest evidence | Tongkat ali (Eurycoma longifolia) → promising RCTs, mixed quality

**Destroyers:** Chronic overtraining without recovery, obesity, chronic alcohol, sleep deprivation, endocrine disruptors (BPA, phthalates), chronic stress

## 4. Estrogen — The Complexity Hormone

**Produced by:** Ovaries (primary, women), adrenals, adipose tissue  
**Types:** Estradiol (E2) = most potent; Estrone (E1) = dominant in menopause; Estriol (E3) = pregnancy  
**Function:** Bone density, cardiovascular protection, cognitive function, mood regulation, reproductive cycle

### Why it matters
Both too low *and* too high estrogen cause problems. "Estrogen dominance" (high estrogen relative to progesterone) is common in modern environments due to xenoestrogens.

### How to optimize
**For healthy estrogen metabolism:**
- Eat cruciferous vegetables daily (broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts, kale) → contain DIM (diindolylmethane) and I3C, which shift estrogen metabolism toward the safer 2-hydroxy pathway
- High fiber diet → fiber binds estrogen in the gut and promotes excretion; reduces enterohepatic recirculation
- Maintain healthy body fat → excessive adipose tissue converts androgens to estrogen via aromatase (both men and women)
- Avoid xenoestrogens: BPA in plastics, phthalates in fragrances, parabens in personal care products, pesticides on non-organic produce

**Supplements with evidence:**
- DIM (diindolylmethane) 100–200 mg/day → promotes favorable estrogen metabolism
- Calcium D-glucarate 500–1000 mg → inhibits beta-glucuronidase enzyme that allows reabsorption of estrogen in the gut

**For low estrogen (peri/menopause):**
- Phytoestrogens (flaxseed, soy, legumes) → weak estrogen-like activity; may reduce hot flashes moderately
- Consult physician for bioidentical estrogen HRT — evidence strongly supports benefits for bone, cardiovascular, and cognition when started within 10 years of menopause

## 5. Progesterone — The Calming Counterbalance

**Produced by:** Corpus luteum (women, post-ovulation), adrenals (both sexes)  
**Function:** Balances estrogen, supports uterine lining, promotes sleep, calming (GABA agonist)

### How to optimize
- Manage stress → cortisol and progesterone compete for the same pregnenolone precursor ("pregnenolone steal"); chronic stress robs progesterone production
- Zinc and B6 → support luteal phase progesterone synthesis
- Vitex agnus-castus (Chasteberry) 20–40 mg standardized extract → multiple RCTs show improved luteal phase length and progesterone levels by reducing excess prolactin (D2 agonism); takes 3–6 months
- Avoid xenoestrogens (same list as estrogen section) → xenoestrogens displace progesterone from receptors
- For men: reduce aromatization (see estrogen section); low progesterone in men associated with anxiety and poor sleep

## 6. Thyroid Hormones (T3/T4) — The Metabolic Rate Setter

**Produced by:** Thyroid gland  
**Types:** T4 (thyroxine, storage form) → converted to T3 (triiodothyronine, active form)  
**TSH:** Pituitary signal; high TSH = thyroid underperforming  
**Function:** Sets metabolic rate, body temperature, heart rate, energy, cognition, mood

### Why hypothyroidism is underdiagnosed
Subclinical hypothyroidism mimics depression, fatigue, brain fog, cold intolerance, weight gain, and hair loss. Standard TSH-only testing misses T3 conversion problems.

### How to optimize
**Essential micronutrients (without these, T3/T4 cannot be synthesized):**
- **Iodine** → required building block of T3/T4; sources: seaweed (nori), seafood, dairy, iodized salt. Do not megadose — excess iodine can suppress thyroid (Wolff-Chaikoff effect)
- **Selenium** 200 mcg/day → essential for T4→T3 conversion enzyme (deiodinase); 1–2 Brazil nuts = ~200 mcg; deficiency in up to 50% of thyroid patients
- **Zinc** 25–40 mg/day → supports hormone synthesis; deficient in ~40% of thyroid patients
- **Tyrosine** → amino acid backbone of T3/T4; found in eggs, dairy, chicken, fish

**Lifestyle:**
- Manage cortisol → cortisol directly blocks T4→T3 conversion; this is why stressed people feel hypothyroid despite normal labs
- Sleep → TSH peaks overnight; sleep deprivation blunts this pulse
- Gut health → ~20% of T4→T3 conversion occurs in the gut; dysbiosis impairs conversion

**Avoid:**
- Raw cruciferous vegetables in large amounts (goitrogens) if iodine-deficient — cooked is fine
- Fluoride in high amounts → competes with iodine at thyroid uptake
- Chronic undereating → prolonged caloric restriction reduces T3 (metabolic adaptation)

## 7. Human Growth Hormone (HGH) — Recovery and Body Composition

**Produced by:** Anterior pituitary  
**Function:** Muscle and bone growth, fat metabolism, cellular repair, immune support  
**Peak:** Childhood/puberty; declines ~15% per decade after age 30

### Why optimize
HGH is the primary overnight recovery signal. It mobilizes fat for fuel while preserving muscle — the ideal body composition signal.

### How to optimize
**S-Tier:**
- **Deep sleep** → 75% of daily HGH is released in pulses during slow-wave (deep) sleep. Every hour of missed or disrupted sleep is a direct HGH loss
- **Intermittent fasting** → 2021 study: 37.5-hour fast increased basal GH concentration by ~10x. Mechanism: low insulin disinhibits GH release. 16:8 fasting produces meaningful (2–4x) acute spikes

**A-Tier:**
- High-intensity exercise (HIIT, heavy compound lifts) → single best exercise stimulus for acute GH release; exceeds steady-state cardio by 3–5x
- Reduce body fat (especially visceral) → visceral fat is associated with GH resistance and blunted pulsatile release
- Reduce sugar and refined carbs before bed → insulin spike before sleep blunts the overnight GH pulse

**B-Tier:**
- Sauna (Finnish-style, 170°F, 20 min, 2–3 sessions) → 2–3x GH increase; mechanism unclear but robust in studies
- L-arginine 3–6 g before bed or exercise → inhibits somatostatin (GH suppressor); evidence moderate
- Melatonin 0.5–1 mg → may enhance GH pulse timing; don't take >1 mg

## 8. Dopamine — The Motivation Molecule

**Produced by:** Substantia nigra and ventral tegmental area (brain)  
**Function:** Motivation, anticipation/reward, focus, movement, learning, working memory  
**Key insight:** Dopamine is about *wanting* and *pursuing*, not pleasure itself

### Why it matters
Low dopamine = procrastination, loss of motivation, inability to start tasks, anhedonia, ADHD-like symptoms. High, stable dopamine = driven, focused, learning rapidly.

### How to optimize
**S-Tier:**
- **Morning sunlight** → direct dopamine synthesis stimulation in the retina and raphe nuclei; 5–10 min outdoors within 30 min of waking
- **Exercise** → the most potent natural dopamine elevator; combination of movement + accomplishment; both dopamine and serotonin increase post-exercise
- **Goal completion** → dopamine system is driven by novelty and milestone achievement; break large goals into specific milestones → dopamine hit on each completion

**A-Tier:**
- **Cold exposure** (cold shower 30–60 sec) → study: 250% increase in dopamine that lasts for hours (not just a spike)
- **Protein with tyrosine** (chicken, eggs, dairy, avocados, pumpkin seeds, bananas) → tyrosine is the direct dopamine precursor
- **Deep focus work** (flow states) → dopamine and norepinephrine both elevated during flow; protect focus windows

**B-Tier:**
- Avoid excessive dopamine "junk food" (social media, video games, porn) → these superstimulate dopamine receptors → downregulation → baseline motivation drops
- Mucuna pruriens (L-DOPA) 100–300 mg → direct dopamine precursor; effective but use sparingly to avoid desensitization
- Caffeine → indirectly supports dopamine by blocking adenosine (which suppresses dopamine); best used strategically

**Destroyers:** Phone scrolling first thing in morning (spikes dopamine before earning it → rest of day feels flat), sugar highs, excessive alcohol, sleep deprivation, rumination/chronic stress

## 9. Serotonin — The Contentment Molecule

**Produced by:** Raphe nuclei (brain); gut enterochromaffin cells (95% of body's serotonin)  
**Function:** Mood stability, impulse control, digestion, social behavior, sleep (serotonin → melatonin)  
**Key insight:** Serotonin is about *having* and *satisfaction*, not pursuit

### How to optimize
**S-Tier:**
- **Sunlight** → direct serotonin synthesis; serotonin levels correlate with hours of bright light exposure; seasonal affective disorder is largely a serotonin deficiency
- **Exercise** → increases both synthesis and release; running especially elevated serotonin in animal models
- **Gut health** → 95% of serotonin is in the gut; a healthy microbiome (fermented foods, fiber, prebiotics) directly supports serotonin production

**A-Tier:**
- **Tryptophan-rich foods** → serotonin precursor; turkey, salmon, eggs, tofu, cheese, oats; best absorbed with a small carbohydrate serving (insulin drives competing amino acids into muscle, leaving tryptophan available to cross the blood-brain barrier)
- **Social connection** → physical touch and positive social interactions elevate serotonin; isolation reliably lowers it
- **Gratitude practice** → activates prefrontal-serotonin pathways; 3 items of gratitude daily shows measurable mood improvements in 2–4 weeks

**B-Tier:**
- Tryptophan supplement 500–1000 mg or 5-HTP 50–100 mg (evening) → direct serotonin precursors; don't combine with SSRIs (serotonin syndrome risk)
- Mindfulness meditation → increases serotonin receptor density over time

**Destroyers:** Chronic stress, gut dysbiosis (leaky gut), ultra-processed diet, social isolation, lack of sunlight, chronic sleep deprivation, alcohol (short-term boost, long-term depletion)

## 10. Melatonin — The Darkness Hormone

**Produced by:** Pineal gland  
**Function:** Circadian rhythm master signal; initiates sleep; potent antioxidant; immune modulator  
**Key insight:** Melatonin is not a sedative — it is a *darkness* signal that tells the body "it's night"

### How to optimize
- **Block artificial light after sunset** → melatonin is suppressed by light (especially blue 480 nm); even dim phone screen in dark room = 23% melatonin suppression
- **Morning sunlight** → paradoxically, bright morning light *entrains* the circadian rhythm so melatonin releases at the right time at night
- **Keep bedroom dark** (blackout curtains or sleep mask) → allows full melatonin expression
- **Consistent sleep schedule** → melatonin release is time-stamped by circadian clock; irregular schedule = blunted or mistimed release

**Supplementation:**
- Most people take 5–10 mg (pharmacy doses) — this is likely 10–20x too much
- Optimal dose: **0.1–0.5 mg** taken 30–60 min before bed for sleep onset; higher doses don't improve sleep and may cause next-day grogginess
- For jet lag / shift work: 0.5–3 mg timed to destination timezone

## 11. Oxytocin — The Bonding Molecule

**Produced by:** Hypothalamus, released by posterior pituitary  
**Function:** Bonding, trust, social recognition, uterine contractions (childbirth), breast milk letdown, stress buffer  

### How to optimize
- **Physical touch** → most potent natural oxytocin trigger; hugs, massage, physical intimacy. A 20-second hug elevates oxytocin measurably
- **Eye contact** → sustained, warm eye contact (with people you trust) elevates oxytocin; dog-owner mutual gaze shown to do the same
- **Acts of generosity / giving** → prosocial behavior activates the oxytocin system; volunteering, gift-giving, random acts of kindness
- **Cold plunge with others** → shared hardship elevates oxytocin + dopamine + adrenaline
- **Meditation and prayer** → increases feelings of connectedness which correlates with oxytocin tone
- Note: oxytocin has a "dark side" — it also strengthens in-group/out-group biases (tribal thinking); it bonds *and* otherizes

## 12. Leptin and Ghrelin — The Hunger Hormones

**Leptin:** Produced by fat cells → signals satiety to hypothalamus → reduces appetite  
**Ghrelin:** Produced by stomach → hunger signal; peaks before meals, drops after eating  
**Key problem:** Leptin resistance — obese individuals produce more leptin but the brain stops hearing it

### How to optimize
- **Sleep** → single meal of sleep deprivation: ghrelin +24%, leptin −18%; sleep-deprived people eat ~300 more calories/day
- **Don't eat within 2–3 hours of bed** → keeps the eating/fasting cycle aligned with circadian rhythms
- **Eat adequate protein** (0.7–1g/lb body weight) → protein is the most satiating macronutrient per calorie; suppresses ghrelin more effectively than fat or carbs
- **Fiber** → slows digestion and sustains leptin/satiety signals
- **Reduce ultra-processed food** → hyperpalatable foods impair the brain's response to leptin signaling
- **Exercise** → improves leptin sensitivity over time

## 13. Adrenaline / Epinephrine & Norepinephrine — Fight or Flight

**Produced by:** Adrenal medulla (adrenaline); locus coeruleus / adrenal medulla (norepinephrine)  
**Function:** Acute stress response — increase heart rate, blood pressure, mobilize glucose and fat, sharpen focus  
**Key insight:** These are acutely useful and not something to chronically "optimize up" — the goal is robust on/off control

### How to optimize (control, not maximize)
- **Cold exposure** → safest way to practice the catecholamine stress response; regular cold training improves autonomic control (turn on *and* off the stress response quickly)
- **Exercise** → regular exercise trains adrenal efficiency; the same stressor releases less adrenaline in trained individuals
- **Breath work** → box breathing, 4-7-8 breathing → activates vagal brake and rapidly lowers norepinephrine after a stress spike
- **Reduce chronic background stressors** → the goal is large amplitude (strong response when needed) with fast recovery; chronic stress creates low-amplitude, never-recovering baseline

## 14. DHEA — The Precursor Hormone

**Produced by:** Adrenal glands  
**Function:** Precursor to testosterone and estrogen; peaks at age 25, declines ~2%/year  
**Note:** Often marketed as a "fountain of youth" supplement; evidence for healthy adults is weak

### What actually supports DHEA
- Everything that lowers cortisol (since cortisol and DHEA are inversely related; adrenal stress production shifts toward cortisol at DHEA's expense)
- Resistance training
- Adequate sleep
- **Supplementation:** DHEA 25–50 mg/day is prescription in some countries; evidence strongest for post-menopausal women with adrenal insufficiency; weaker for healthy adults. Consult physician before supplementing.
