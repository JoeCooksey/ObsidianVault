# Food Health Tier List — Legumes

Ranking criteria: **pure healthiness** — protein content, fiber (especially prebiotic fiber), glycemic index, micronutrient density, digestibility, and disease prevention evidence. Sources: Healthline, Harvard School of Public Health, MD Anderson, USDA MyPlate nutritional data.

General note: all beans and legumes are health-positive foods. This ranking is relative within the category — even D-tier legumes are better than most C-tier foods in the overall list.

---

## S Tier — The Best Legumes

| Legume | Per 1 Cup Cooked | Why |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| **Lentils (red, green, black)** | 18g protein, 15.6g fiber, 358mcg folate | Highest fiber of any common legume; fastest to cook (no soaking); easiest to digest; one of best folate sources; iron, manganese, molybdenum; GI ~30 (very low); all colors are excellent |
| **Black Beans** | 15g protein, 15g fiber | High antioxidant content (ORAC scores comparable to berries due to anthocyanins in the black skin); iron, magnesium, folate; shown to reduce LDL and blood sugar spikes |

---

## A Tier — Excellent Legumes

| Legume | Per 1 Cup Cooked | Why |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| **Soybeans / Edamame** | 28g protein (complete — all 9 amino acids) | Highest protein of any legume; 63% DV protein per cup; isoflavones (phytoestrogens — evidence shows protective for breast/prostate cancer at moderate intake); iron, calcium |
| **Chickpeas (Garbanzo)** | 15g protein, 12.5g fiber | Manganese, folate; GI ~28 (very low); shown to reduce LDL by ~5% in trials; versatile; aquafaba (liquid) has egg-white properties |
| **Kidney Beans** | 15g protein, 13.1g fiber | Iron (high — 5.2mg/cup), potassium, folate; antioxidant-rich red pigment; **must cook thoroughly** — raw kidney beans contain phytohaemagglutinin (toxic) |
| **Navy Beans** | 15g protein, 19.1g fiber | Highest fiber of any common bean (19g/cup); potassium (1185mg); cardiovascular protection; B vitamins |

---

## B Tier — Strong All-Rounders

| Legume | Per 1 Cup Cooked | Why |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| **Pinto Beans** | 15g protein, 15.4g fiber | Magnesium, phosphorus, folate; antioxidant polyphenols; very common in Western diet; nearly identical to black beans nutritionally |
| **White Beans (Cannellini)** | 17g protein, 11g fiber | Highest potassium of beans (1004mg/cup — equivalent to a banana × 2.5); protein, iron; versatile in Italian cuisine |
| **Split Peas** | 16g protein, 16.3g fiber | High fiber; protein; B vitamins; faster cooking than most beans; GI ~22 (one of lowest legumes) |
| **Mung Beans** | 14g protein, 15.4g fiber | Magnesium, potassium, B vitamins; sprouted mung beans have higher nutrient bioavailability; easily digestible |
| **Adzuki Beans** | 17g protein, 16.8g fiber | High in folate; manganese; antioxidants; common in East Asian diets associated with longevity |

---

## C Tier — Good But Less Impressive

| Legume | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Lima Beans** | Protein, fiber, potassium; but lower antioxidant content; some people find them difficult to digest; less nutritionally dense than top-tier beans |
| **Canned Beans (high sodium)** | Nutritional profile is equivalent to dried cooked; high sodium (400–600mg/serving) reduces net health value; **solution: rinse canned beans under water — removes 40% of sodium** |
| **Peanuts** | Technically a legume; high protein (7g/2 tbsp), folate, niacin; but also high caloric density and high omega-6; ranked with nuts for practical purposes — see [[Food Health Tier List — Nuts and Seeds]] |

---

## D Tier — Legume Products to Limit

| Product | Why |
|---------|-----|
| **Refried Beans (canned/fast food)** | Same bean base but often made with lard, high sodium, additives; fat content negates some benefits |
| **Bean Dips with oils/preservatives** | Ultra-processed versions strip out much of the benefit; often high sodium, low fiber |
| **Soy Protein Isolate (processed)** | Heavily processed soy; phytates and lectins; less beneficial than whole soybeans or edamame |

---

## Key Principles for Legumes

1. **Lentils are the practical champion** — highest fiber, fastest cooking, easiest digestion, one of the lowest GI foods in existence
2. **All beans beat ultra-processed foods** — even the least impressive legume (lima beans) beats most packaged snack foods on every health metric
3. **Soak dried beans 8+ hours** — reduces oligosaccharides that cause gas; also reduces phytic acid (anti-nutrient that blocks mineral absorption)
4. **Rinsing canned beans** removes 40% of sodium — makes canned beans nearly equivalent to home-cooked
5. **Legumes are the shared ingredient of every long-lived population** (Blue Zones): Loma Linda: soybeans; Sardinia: chickpeas/fava; Nicoya: black beans; Okinawa: tofu; Ikaria: lentils
6. **Glycemic index of legumes is remarkably low** (20–45) due to fiber + resistant starch — they cause slower, lower blood sugar rises than almost any carbohydrate source
