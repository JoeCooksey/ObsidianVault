# PC & Mobile Games — Cognitive Benefit Tier List

**Framework:** Games scored across 7 cognitive dimensions — working memory, executive function, cognitive flexibility, spatial reasoning, perceptual/processing speed, logical reasoning, pattern recognition — weighted by quality of scientific evidence and real-world transfer.

**Platform key:** [PC] = desktop/Steam only | [M] = mobile only | [PC+M] = both

---

## S-Tier — Maximum Cognitive ROI

> Strong peer-reviewed evidence + broad cognitive impact + real-world transfer

| Game | Platform | Primary Cognitive Benefit | Evidence |
|------|----------|--------------------------|----------|
| **Chess / Chess.com** | PC+M | Working memory, forward planning, executive function, pattern recognition | Decades of research; IQ correlation meta-analyses |
| **StarCraft II** | PC | Cognitive flexibility, multitasking, rapid decision-making | PLOS One RCT; faster/more accurate cognitive flexibility tests vs. controls |
| **Portal 2** | PC | Spatial reasoning, physics-based problem-solving, persistence | Carnegie Mellon/FSU study: beat Lumosity on spatial skill + problem-solving |
| **Factorio** | PC | Systems thinking, logistics optimization, constraint satisfaction | Community + expert consensus; no direct RCT but involves the most complex multi-system reasoning of any game |
| **The Talos Principle** | PC+M | Formal deductive logic, rule-construction, philosophical reasoning | Puzzle design forces logical proof-building; expert puzzle designer consensus |

## A-Tier — High Cognitive Benefit

> Good evidence + targets multiple cognitive systems + meaningful real-world application

| Game | Platform | Primary Cognitive Benefit | Notes |
|------|----------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Civilization VI / V** | PC+M | Long-horizon planning, resource allocation, probabilistic reasoning | Turn-based = allows deliberate thinking; complex enough to avoid plateau |
| **Baba Is You** | PC+M | Abstract rule-manipulation, meta-cognition, reframing | Forces you to question axioms of puzzle logic; deepest "second-order thinking" of any puzzle game |
| **The Witness** | PC | Inductive reasoning, pattern discovery, observational acuity | 600+ puzzles; hidden meta-rules emergent from environment; pure rule-inference |
| **Into the Breach** | PC | Tactical consequence-mapping, constraint satisfaction, full-info decision-making | Perfect-information turn-based chess with consequence chains |
| **Kerbal Space Program** | PC | Physics intuition, iterative hypothesis testing, engineering reasoning | Players develop genuine orbital mechanics understanding through trial-and-error |
| **BrainHQ** | PC+M | Processing speed, working memory, attention | 100+ peer-reviewed studies; FDA-qualified cognitive device; strongest evidence base of any app |
| **Elevate** | M | Verbal fluency, math processing, language speed | Clinical study: 69% greater cognitive improvement vs. control over 4 weeks |

## B-Tier — Solid Cognitive Benefits

> Decent evidence or strong theoretical basis + enjoyable enough to sustain long sessions

| Game | Platform | Primary Cognitive Benefit | Notes |
|------|----------|--------------------------|-------|
| **CS2 / Valorant / Apex Legends** | PC | Perceptual speed, visual attention, reaction time, spatial awareness | 2023 APA meta-analysis: action game players outperform non-gamers in perception + attention; 2025 study: more gray matter in frontoparietal attention network |
| **XCOM 2** | PC | Tactical risk assessment, resource management under scarcity, planning | Turn-based tactics; meaningful failure teaches consequence prediction |
| **Tetris Effect** | PC+M | Spatial manipulation under time pressure, pattern recognition | Research: Tetris training → increased cortical thickness + more efficient neural use |
| **Mini Metro / Mini Motorways** | PC+M | Graph-based optimization, real-time systems thinking, spatial planning | Elegantly designed around logistics constraint satisfaction |
| **Hearthstone** | PC+M | Probability assessment, deck construction, opponent modeling | Requires tracking card counts and anticipating states 2–3 moves ahead |
| **Sudoku apps** | M | Deductive logic, constraint propagation, elimination reasoning | Extensively studied; direct logical deduction with no memory tricks |
| **Monument Valley 1 + 2** | M | Spatial reasoning, perspective manipulation, non-Euclidean geometry | Optical illusion puzzles train spatial thinking in novel reference frames |
| **2048** | M | Combinatorial planning, spatial pattern recognition | Simple but requires look-ahead planning to avoid terminal state |
| **Lumosity** | PC+M | Working memory, processing speed, attention | 100M+ users; 2015 study showed improvement in short-term + working memory; but FTC settlement for exaggerated claims; weaker than BrainHQ |

## C-Tier — Moderate Benefit, Primarily Entertainment

> Some cognitive engagement but low deliberate challenge, or benefits plateau quickly

| Game | Platform | Primary Cognitive Benefit | Notes |
|------|----------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Baldur's Gate 3** | PC | Decision-tree planning, working memory (lore), narrative reasoning | Engaging but most decisions don't require rigorous logical analysis |
| **Among Us** | PC+M | Social deductive reasoning, verbal argumentation, probability assessment | Deduction is the mechanism but games are too short for deep practice |
| **Pokémon** | PC+M | Type-system strategy, planning, rule-system mastery | Strategic depth under-utilized by most players; deeper with competitive play |
| **Clash Royale** | M | Real-time micro-strategy, card probability, reaction speed | Genuine strategy depth but heavily monetized to reduce skill expression |
| **NYT Games / Wordle** | PC+M | Verbal fluency, word retrieval, constraint-based reasoning | Short daily sessions keep usage healthy; vocabulary and pattern use |
| **Minecraft** | PC+M | Spatial creativity, resource planning, construction reasoning | Low cognitive floor — can be done mindlessly; benefit depends on engagement mode |

| **Subway Surfers / Temple Run** | M | Reflex only; no strategic depth; automatic after first hour |
| **Stardew Valley** | PC+M | Good for stress relief and mood but not cognitively challenging; low executive function demand |

## F-Tier — Neutral to Actively Harmful

> Exploits cognitive vulnerabilities OR zero cognitive demand with addictive design

| Game | Platform | Why F-Tier |
|------|----------|------------|
| **Gacha games (AFK Arena, Raid: Shadow Legends, Genshin Impact gacha mechanics)** | M | 2025 arXiv study: impairs inhibitory control; reinforces probability blindness; designed to exploit cognitive biases, not exercise them; gambling-adjacent behavioral loops |
| **Idle/clicker games** | M | Zero cognitive demand; pure variable-ratio reward schedule (slot machine psychology) |
| **Slot-machine mobile games** | M | Probability blindness reinforcement; exploitative design targeting reward-seeking circuits |

## Joe-Specific Recommendations

Given Joe is a 19yo EE student who wants to improve thinking — but gaming is already rated C-tier in the [[Free Time Tier List]] at "1–2 hrs/week" cap:

**Best use of limited game time (if playing):**
1. **Chess.com** — 15–20 min/day during breaks; proven, measurable, transferable to engineering planning
2. **Portal 2** (one-time) — 8–10 hour campaign; exceptional spatial reasoning and problem-solving workout
3. **Factorio** (careful) — systems thinking directly analogous to circuit/firmware design; but highly addictive time sink; cap at weekends only
4. **Into the Breach** — short sessions (15 min/game); tactics analogous to debugging/constraint satisfaction

**Avoid entirely:**
- Any gacha mobile game (active cognitive harm)
- Idle/clicker games (zero benefit + habit formation risk)

**Key insight:** For an EE student, the cognitive skills that matter most (systems thinking, logical debugging, spatial reasoning for PCB layout) are better trained by LTspice circuits and Python projects than by any game — but if gaming, choose games that train the same skills.
