# Muscle Building Nutrition Guide

Evidence-based nutritional protocol for maximizing muscle hypertrophy. Sources: PMC/PubMed clinical research, ISSN guidelines, Schoenfeld et al.

---

## Caloric Targets

| Goal | Calories vs. TDEE | Rate of Weight Change |
|---|---|---|
| Lean bulk | +200–300 kcal/day | ~0.25–0.5% bodyweight/week |
| Standard bulk | +400–500 kcal/day | ~0.5–1% bodyweight/week |
| Cut (preserve muscle) | −300–500 kcal/day | ~0.5–1% bodyweight/week |
| Recomp | TDEE ±0 | Slow; works best in beginners |

> Rate matters more than total. Gaining faster than 0.5% bodyweight/week increases fat gain with minimal extra muscle.

---

## Protein — The Most Important Macro

| Variable | Target |
|---|---|
| **Daily protein** | **1.6–2.2 g/kg bodyweight** (0.7–1.0 g/lb) |
| Upper limit | ~2.2 g/kg — beyond this, no additional benefit |
| Minimum (cutting) | 2.2–2.4 g/kg (muscle preservation) |
| Per meal | 0.4–0.55 g/kg (to maximize MPS per meal) |
| Frequency | 3–5 meals per day |

**At 5'9" / 165 lbs (75 kg)**: ~120–165 g protein per day. Target 150 g/day as practical middle.

### Best Protein Sources

| Source | Protein/100g | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chicken breast | 31g | Lean, cheap, versatile |
| Greek yogurt | 10g | Easy snack; casein-heavy |
| Eggs | 13g | Complete amino acid profile |
| Whey protein | 25g (per scoop) | Fast-digesting; ideal post-workout |
| Salmon/tuna | 25–29g | Omega-3 bonus |
| Lean beef | 26g | High in creatine + zinc |
| Cottage cheese | 11g | Casein; ideal before bed |
| Lentils | 9g | Plant-based; lower leucine |

## Macronutrient Distribution

| Macro | Percentage | Per 3,000 kcal example |
|---|---|---|
| **Protein** | 25–30% | 188–225 g |
| **Carbohydrates** | 50–55% | 375–413 g |
| **Fats** | 20–25% | 67–83 g |

- **Carbs are not optional** for hard training — they fuel glycolysis and replenish muscle glycogen
- **Fats**: ≥0.5 g/kg/day minimum for hormonal function (especially testosterone synthesis)
- Never drop fat below 20% of calories — testosterone and estrogen synthesis depend on dietary fat

---

## Meal Timing

| Variable | Evidence-Based Practice |
|---|---|
| **Meals per day** | 3–5 (maximize MPS frequency) |
| **Pre-workout meal** | 1–2 hrs before; protein + carbs |
| **Post-workout window** | Within 1–2 hours; protein + carbs |
| **Before bed** | Casein protein (cottage cheese, Greek yogurt) — slow digestion overnight |
| **Morning protein** | Don't skip — first meal restarts MPS after overnight fast |

> The post-workout anabolic window is real but not as narrow as once thought. Total daily protein matters more than timing — but timing still helps optimize gains.

## Supplements — Evidence-Based Tier List

| Supplement | Tier | Dose | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Creatine monohydrate** | S | 3–5 g/day | Post-workout preferred | Most evidence-backed supplement; increases muscle ATP, strength, size |
| **Whey protein** | S | 20–40 g per serving | Post-workout / whenever needed | Not magic — just dietary protein |
| **Caffeine** | A | 3–6 mg/kg body weight | 30–60 min pre-workout | Performance + strength boost; see [[Caffeine Complete Guide]] |
| **Beta-alanine** | B | 3.2–6.4 g/day | Any time | Tingles; buffers lactate; benefits endurance more than hypertrophy |
| **Citrulline malate** | B | 6–8 g | Pre-workout | Reduces muscle soreness; mild performance benefit |
| **Vitamin D** | B | 2,000–5,000 IU/day | With fat-containing meal | 42% of Americans deficient; affects testosterone and immunity |
| **Omega-3 (fish oil)** | B | 2–3 g EPA+DHA/day | With meal | Anti-inflammatory; may reduce DOMS |
| **ZMA / Zinc** | C | 10–30 mg zinc/day | Before bed | Only beneficial if deficient |
| **Pre-workout blends** | C | Varies | Pre-workout | Usually just caffeine + stimulants — buy components separately |
| **BCAAs** | D | N/A | N/A | Redundant if protein intake is adequate — waste of money |
| **Testosterone boosters** | F | N/A | N/A | No clinical evidence; snake oil |

### Creatine Protocol
- **Loading**: 20 g/day for 5–7 days (optional — same saturation in 3–4 weeks without loading)
- **Maintenance**: 3–5 g/day
- **Timing**: Post-workout is marginally superior; any time works
- **Form**: Creatine monohydrate — don't pay for "kre-alkalyn" or "HCl" — same efficacy, higher price

## Hydration

| Variable | Target |
|---|---|
| Daily water | 3–4 liters for active individuals |
| Intra-workout | 500–750 ml/hr of exercise |
| Post-workout (rehydration) | 150% of sweat loss |

Dehydration of 2% bodyweight reduces strength by ~6% and endurance significantly. Don't train dry.

## Practical Daily Meal Template (3,000 kcal, ~165 g protein)

| Meal | Example | ~Protein |
|---|---|---|
| **Breakfast** | 3 eggs + 2 egg whites + oats + banana | 30g |
| **Lunch** | 200g chicken breast + rice + vegetables | 45g |
| **Snack** | Greek yogurt + mixed nuts | 20g |
| **Post-workout** | Whey shake + apple | 30g |
| **Dinner** | 200g salmon + sweet potato + greens | 40g |
| **Before bed** | Cottage cheese or Greek yogurt | 15g |

**Total**: ~180 g protein / ~3,100 kcal

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Not eating enough | Calculate TDEE; add 200–300 kcal |
| Insufficient protein | Track for 2 weeks until it's automatic |
| Skipping carbs | Kills training performance and glycogen |
| Too large a surplus | Extra fat gain without extra muscle; stay within 300–500 kcal |
| Neglecting sleep | GH is released primarily during deep sleep; muscle is built at night |
| BCAAs/supplements over food | Whole food protein is superior; supplements are a gap-filler |
