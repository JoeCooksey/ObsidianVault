# Books

Synthesis of ideas from books across all genres. The goal is not summaries — it is extracting and connecting the ideas.

## Sub-areas
- Non-fiction / Ideas
- Technical books
- Philosophy
- Narrative non-fiction
- Fiction (themes and ideas)

## Key Concepts
- Desire to feel important — the deepest human craving (Carnegie); underpins all influence and leadership
- Appreciation vs. flattery — sincere appreciation is earned and specific; flattery is detectable and cheap
- Operant reinforcement — praise every improvement immediately; the signal must reach the person in the moment
- Identity lever — treat people as who they could be and they rise to it; give a reputation to live up to

## Key Entities
- [[Dale Carnegie]] — *How to Win Friends and Influence People* (1936); human relations training pioneer

## Cross-Domain Connections
- **Psychology / Emotions**: Carnegie's empathy techniques require the defusion and perspective-taking that [[Emotional Agility]] describes
- **Habits**: "Give someone a fine reputation to live up to" is identity-based habit formation — [[Identity-Based vs Goal-Based Habits]]
- **Neuroscience**: Carnegie's praise-every-improvement is operant reinforcement — maps to [[Neuroplasticity and Habit Formation]]
