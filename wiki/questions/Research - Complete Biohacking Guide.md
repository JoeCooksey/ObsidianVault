# Research: Complete Biohacking Guide

## Overview

Biohacking is the systematic application of lifestyle interventions, nutrition, technology, and supplementation to optimize human performance, health, and longevity. The field spans free/low-cost practices (sleep, sunlight, cold exposure) up to cutting-edge technologies (continuous glucose monitoring, epigenetic clocks, peptide therapeutics). Evidence quality varies enormously — from decades of RCT data (exercise, sleep, Mediterranean diet) to emerging single-lab findings (senolytics, NAD+ precursors).

**The core finding of all longevity research: the highest-ROI interventions are free.** Sleep, exercise, and diet quality outperform every supplement and device combined.

## Key Findings

1. **Sleep is the master variable.** 6h vs. 8h sleep causes 24% more next-day calorie intake (RCT), impairs cognitive performance equivalently to being legally drunk, and is associated with 2× all-cause mortality risk. No supplement, device, or protocol compensates for chronic sleep deprivation. The single best sleep intervention is a consistent wake time every day of the week — free, immediate, and profound.

2. **Exercise is the most robust longevity intervention known.** VO2 max is the strongest predictor of all-cause mortality — being in the top fitness quintile reduces mortality by ~5× vs. the bottom quintile (more powerful than smoking cessation alone). Zone 2 cardio + resistance training + VO2 max intervals constitutes the full protocol. Grip strength is a surprisingly robust proxy: every 5 kg decline in grip strength = 17% increase in all-cause mortality (Lancet, n=140,000).

3. **Diet quality outperforms any supplement.** Ultra-processed food elimination is more impactful than any supplement ever studied. The Mediterranean pattern is the most replicated dietary dataset. Protein adequacy (1.6–2.2 g/kg/day) is underappreciated for muscle and metabolic health. Eating 30+ different plants per week maximizes microbiome diversity more than any probiotic.

4. **The foundation supplement stack for a young male is short:** Vitamin D3+K2, Magnesium Glycinate, Omega-3 (EPA/DHA), Zinc, Creatine. These address common deficiencies (36% of young adults are vitamin D deficient) and provide unambiguous evidence-backed performance benefits. Every exotic supplement should wait until these are in place.

5. **ApoB is the most important cardiovascular biomarker.** Standard LDL-C can be misleading (lean mass hyper-responders have high LDL-C but normal particle count). ApoB directly measures the number of atherogenic particles. Optimal <70 mg/dL. hs-CRP below 0.8 mg/L indicates low systemic inflammation — this is the longevity target, not just "below 3."

6. **Fasting insulin and HOMA-IR are the earliest metabolic alarm signals.** HbA1c only becomes abnormal years after insulin resistance begins. Fasting insulin <5 μIU/mL indicates excellent insulin sensitivity. This is the most actionable test most people never order — fix this first before any supplementation or biohacking.

7. **Temperature hormesis (sauna + cold) has strong mechanistic and epidemiological data.** Finnish sauna 4×/week associated with ~40% reduced cardiovascular mortality (Laukkanen et al., n=2,315, 20-year follow-up). Cold exposure acutely increases norepinephrine ~300% and chronically builds stress resilience via HSP and BAT activation. Contrast therapy (hot → cold → hot) is additive.

8. **Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) is the highest-information biohack.** Two weeks of CGM reveals how your specific body responds to foods, sleep, stress, and exercise. Many people discover dramatic glucose spikes from "healthy" foods (white rice, certain fruits). Keeping glucose 70–90 mg/dL with low variability correlates with energy, mood, and metabolic health. Levels, Nutrisense, or Dexcom G7 are the leading consumer options.

## Contradictions & Nuances

- **More is not better for most interventions.** Low-dose melatonin (0.1–0.5 mg) is effective; 10 mg suppresses natural production. Vitamin D is beneficial to correct deficiency; mega-dosing above 10,000 IU causes toxicity. Cold exposure for 10–15 min at 10–15°C is beneficial; extreme prolonged cold is harmful.

- **Individual variation is real.** CGM data shows that two people eating identical meals can have wildly different glucose responses (Weizmann Institute personalized nutrition study). Genetic variants (ApoE4, MTHFR) meaningfully change optimal supplement protocols.

- **Supplement industry marketing dramatically outpaces evidence.** Only 16% of cognitive supplement labels in a 2023 audit had per-ingredient safety warnings. Most proprietary blends are under-dosed. Always identify the specific compound, dose, and study population before purchasing.

- **Longevity pharma (rapamycin, metformin) is promising but premature for broad use.** Rapamycin extends lifespan in every animal model tested (up to 25% in mice). PEARL trial and other human studies are ongoing. The risk/benefit tradeoff for healthy young people is not established.

## Open Questions

1. At what age should longevity-specific interventions (NMN, senolytics, rapamycin) begin, given that most evidence comes from older or diseased populations?

2. Is microbiome composition a cause or consequence of health outcomes? Personalized nutrition via microbiome testing (Viome, Zoe) claims personalization benefit — but accuracy and actionability remain under scrutiny.

3. What is the optimal HRV target for a 19-year-old male training 6 days/week, and how much of HRV improvement from cold exposure, meditation, and sleep compounds vs. plateaus?

4. Can epigenetic age clocks accurately measure rate of aging reversal from lifestyle interventions within 6–12 month windows, or are the error bars too wide for individual-level feedback?

5. How does the stack interaction between NMN + resveratrol + berberine + rapamycin affect the mTOR/AMPK/SIRT1 axis simultaneously? Theoretical synergy vs. potential antagonism at the pathway level is uncharacterized.
