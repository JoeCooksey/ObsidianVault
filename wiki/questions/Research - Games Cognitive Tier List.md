# Research: PC & Mobile Games Ranked by Cognitive Benefit

**Question:** Which games — PC and mobile — are most beneficial for improving how you think?

**Answer:** Tier list of 40+ games ranked S–F by cognitive ROI, based on peer-reviewed research, meta-analyses, and expert consensus. See [[Video Games Cognitive Benefits]] for the full ranked list.

---

## 8 Key Findings

1. **Portal 2 beat Lumosity in a controlled study.** Carnegie Mellon / Florida State researchers found Portal 2 players outperformed Lumosity (dedicated brain training) on problem-solving, spatial skill, and persistence. Entertainment games can outperform purpose-built brain trainers when they demand deeper engagement.

2. **StarCraft II is the best-studied single game for cognitive flexibility.** A PLOS One RCT found that women assigned to play StarCraft II completed cognitive flexibility tests faster and more accurately than controls who played The Sims. RTS games train the brain's ability to rapidly switch between information streams — a measurable general-intelligence component.

3. **Action/FPS games reliably improve perceptual speed and visual attention.** A 2023 APA meta-analysis confirmed that action game players show consistent advantages in perception, top-down attention, and spatial cognition. A 2025 ScienceDirect study found FPS players have greater gray matter volume in the frontoparietal attention control network.

4. **BrainHQ is the most scientifically validated brain training platform.** Unlike Lumosity (which settled FTC fraud claims for false efficacy advertising), BrainHQ is backed by 100+ peer-reviewed studies and is the only brain training tool FDA-qualified as a cognitive device. For pure cognitive training without game entertainment, BrainHQ > all other apps.

5. **Gacha games actively impair inhibitory control.** A 2025 arXiv study confirmed that prolonged gacha game engagement impairs inhibitory control — the brain's ability to suppress impulsive responses — and reinforces cognitive biases around probability. Gacha mechanics are designed to exploit cognitive vulnerabilities, not exercise them.

6. **The key cognitive dimensions games can train:** working memory (holding + manipulating info), executive function (planning, decision-making, impulse control), cognitive flexibility (task-switching, strategy adaptation), spatial reasoning, perceptual/processing speed, logical/deductive reasoning, and pattern recognition.

7. **Transfer to real life is game-type dependent.** Strategy and puzzle games show the strongest transfer to real-world problem-solving and planning. Action games primarily improve low-level perceptual skills. "Brain training" apps improve their own metrics but show weaker real-world transfer than complex game environments.

8. **Variety matters more than any single game.** Research consistently shows that cognitive benefits plateau when a single game becomes automatic. Rotating across game types (strategy, puzzle, action) exercises different cognitive systems and avoids the "plateau effect" that limits dedicated brain training apps.
