# Research — How to Find an EE Mentor at ASU

**Question:** How can I find a mentor for electrical engineering at ASU?

## 8 Key Findings

1. **Every ASU engineering freshman gets a peer mentor automatically** — you are assigned one of 25 peer mentors (upper-classmen) on day one; they hold office hours, run events, and are the easiest first mentor to activate. Visit students.engineering.asu.edu/peer-mentors/ to see yours and schedule time.

2. **FURI is the highest-leverage formal faculty mentorship pathway** — the Fulton Undergraduate Research Initiative pairs you with a faculty mentor for one funded semester ($1,500 stipend + $400 supplies); apply starting sophomore year; in Fall 2025 alone, 117 students participated. The direct path: attend faculty office hours now → build rapport → ask to be a FURI applicant → submit application in Year 2.

3. **The two highest-priority power electronics faculty to approach at ASU** are Prof. Mike Ranjram (MAPEL lab, mike.ranjram@asu.edu) — WBG devices + miniaturized power converters, has supervised FURI undergrads — and Prof. Raja Ayyanar (rayyanar@asu.edu) — PV/wind/EV/WBG. Both are in the Power Grid Engineering and Power Electronics cluster (faculty.engineering.asu.edu/directory/ecee/power-grid-engineering/). For semiconductors specifically, Prof. Yuji Zhao researches GaN and Ga₂O₃ ultra-wide bandgap.

4. **Cold email works but requires homework first** — the formula: read 1–2 papers → write 7–12 sentences (who you are, what specifically you liked in their work, what you can contribute, ask for a 15-min meeting or office hours visit) → send one email at a time → wait 7 business days before following up. Do not mass-email. Personalization is the entire point.

5. **IEEE ASU Student Branch is the S-tier social entry point to the professional mentorship pipeline** — 600+ members; hosts tech-talks, industry seminars, alumni panels, resume workshops; attending and joining a project sub-team means you meet the exact network that can introduce you to engineers, professors, and internship contacts without any cold outreach. Go to one event in week 1.

6. **The IEEE Collabratec Mentoring Program gives access to global industry mentors** — any IEEE member can activate Mentee status on Collabratec, fill in technical interests, and self-select from a directory of available professional mentors. Cost = IEEE student membership (~$32/year). Also: IEEE Power Electronics Society (PELS) runs 6-month mentoring pairings and mentorship roundtables at the APEC conference.

7. **Barrett Honors Alumni Mentorship Program** (October–March, contact barrettmentors@asu.edu) pairs current Barrett students with EE alumni in a structured 3-tier commitment program. Only available if you are in Barrett or qualify to join. The directory already has EE alumni from ASU ('22) and CMU ready to mentor.

8. **The Engineering Futures Scholarship Program** bundles financial aid + assigned mentor + structured meetings (3×/semester with mentor, 1×/semester with faculty) for eligible first-year EE students; applications open each fall. Not universally available (financial need + Pell Grant required), but if you qualify, it is a free structured mentorship pipeline through graduation.

## Tier List — Mentorship Channels for EE at ASU

### S-Tier (activate immediately)
- **ASU Peer Mentor** — automatic assignment; easiest first connection; office hours available now
- **IEEE ASU sub-team** — best organic path to professional and faculty networks; 600+ members; find project sub-team → relationship forms naturally over weeks

### A-Tier (pursue this semester / Year 1)
- **Prof. Ranjram (MAPEL) office hours** — power electronics WBG lab, FURI-friendly, matches Joe's track perfectly
- **Prof. Ayyanar office hours** — PV/EV/WBG, wider topical match, one of the most senior power EE faculty
- **IEEE Collabratec Mentor Program** — self-select global industry mentor; ~$32/year IEEE student membership required; high signal-to-noise

### B-Tier (year 2 and beyond)
- **FURI faculty research** — the gold standard faculty mentorship; apply sophomore year (Year 2); costs a semester of commitment but pays $1,500 + research credit + strongest mentorship relationship possible
- **Barrett Honors Alumni Mentorship** — structured, October–March; only if enrolled in Barrett

### C-Tier (supplemental)
- **Engineering Futures Scholarship** — if financially eligible, apply each fall; structured mentoring included
- **IEEE PES / PELS Mentorship** — conference-level roundtables and 6-month pairings; best used after you have some technical footing
- **LinkedIn cold outreach to ASU ECEE alumni** — functional but lowest signal; use only after exhausting warm channels

## Action Stack for Joe (Year 1)

1. **This week**: Go to one IEEE ASU event → introduce yourself → ask what project sub-teams are active
2. **Week 1–2**: Contact your assigned peer mentor; schedule first office hours meeting
3. **Month 1**: Activate IEEE student membership ($32/yr) → set up Collabratec Mentee profile → browse available power electronics mentors
4. **Month 2–3**: Visit Prof. Ranjram or Prof. Ayyanar office hours with a prepared question based on one of their papers; express long-term interest in power electronics research
5. **Year 2 (Spring 2027)**: Apply for FURI with a faculty mentor already identified; target MAPEL or Ayyanar's lab
6. **Ongoing**: IEEE sub-team = your warm network that replaces cold outreach for everything else
