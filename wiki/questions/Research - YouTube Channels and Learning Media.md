# Research — Best YouTube Channels and Learning Media

**Question:** What are the best YouTube channels and videos to learn high-level subjects? Also, what are the best documentaries and movies for learning?

**Answer:** Curated tier lists across four media types — YouTube channels for STEM, YouTube channels for Humanities/AI, documentaries, and intellectual films. See [[YouTube Channels for STEM Learning]], [[YouTube Channels for Humanities Learning]], [[Educational Documentaries]], and [[Intellectual Films]] for full ranked lists.

---

## 8 Key Findings

1. **3Blue1Brown is the uncontested #1 channel for mathematical intuition.** Grant Sanderson's animated series (Essence of Calculus, Essence of Linear Algebra, Neural Networks) have no peer for building visual mathematical understanding. His neural networks series is the single best free introduction to how ML works. For an EE student working through Calc 1–3 and differential equations, this channel is highest-leverage by a wide margin.

2. **PBS Space Time is the highest-ceiling physics channel on YouTube.** Hosted by astrophysicist Matt O'Dowd, it covers quantum field theory, general relativity, black holes, and cosmology at near-graduate level without dumbing down. Veritasium (Derek Muller) is the best for accessible physics — experiments, interviews, and viral impact — but PBS Space Time is where you go when you want to go deep.

3. **Vsauce and Kurzgesagt are the best for interdisciplinary intellectual range.** Vsauce (Michael Stevens) weaves science, math, psychology, and philosophy through curiosity-driven questions — his videos are the closest thing to a philosophy seminar on YouTube. Kurzgesagt makes animated deep-dives into existential science and technology topics with exceptional visual production. Both cover more ground per channel than any specialist.

4. **Andrej Karpathy's channel is the elite entry point for AI/ML.** His "Neural Networks: Zero to Hero" series builds GPT from scratch — it is used in university courses worldwide. For anyone serious about understanding AI from the ground up, this channel is the direct path from concept to practitioner. Two Minute Papers (Károly Zsolnai-Fehér) is the best for tracking AI research breakthroughs.

5. **Cosmos (Carl Sagan, 1980) is the greatest science documentary ever made.** Rated 9.3/10 on IMDb. 13 episodes covering the origin of life, cosmology, history of science, and humanity's place in the universe. Sagan's narration and philosophy make it uniquely moving. Still unmatched after 45+ years. The Neil deGrasse Tyson remake (2014) extends it visually and is also excellent.

6. **Oppenheimer (2023) and The Imitation Game (2014) are the S-tier intellectual films.** Oppenheimer is the most intellectually dense mainstream film ever made — it covers quantum mechanics, political philosophy, ethics of science, and Cold War history simultaneously. The Imitation Game dramatizes Turing's mathematics and the birth of computing. Both deliver more ideas per hour than almost any alternative.

7. **The Social Dilemma (2020, Netflix) is the most immediately relevant documentary for anyone in the digital age.** It covers behavioral manipulation by tech platforms, attention economics, algorithmic amplification, and the design mechanics behind social media addiction. Anyone building a second brain or trying to reduce phone use should watch this — it provides the mechanistic model for why the habits are so hard to break.

8. **The best self-education documentary arc** (7 films, ~30 hours total): Cosmos (Sagan) → Particle Fever → The Elegant Universe → The Story of Math → Inside Bill's Brain → The Social Dilemma → Becoming Warren Buffett. This stack covers physics, mathematics, particle physics, tech business strategy, and value investing at a depth unavailable through any other 30-hour format.

## Joe-Specific Recommendations

Given the EE + math focus, these are highest ROI in order:

1. **3Blue1Brown** — start with "Essence of Linear Algebra" (18 videos, ~3 hours); directly supports MAT 267 and future ML coursework
2. **Andrej Karpathy "Neural Networks: Zero to Hero"** — activate if/when the AI track opens (Phase 4 of income plan)
3. **PBS Space Time** — watch the QFT and Electromagnetism series alongside PHY 121/122
4. **Cosmos (Carl Sagan)** — watch during Year 1; essential cultural and intellectual foundation for any scientist
5. **Oppenheimer** — watch the film, then read the Kai Bird biography for deeper context on the ethical dimension
6. **Particle Fever** — watch before touching any quantum physics coursework; makes the abstract stakes real
