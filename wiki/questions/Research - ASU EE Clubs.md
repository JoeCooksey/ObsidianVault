# Research: ASU EE Clubs for Electrical Engineers

## Overview
ASU's Fulton Schools of Engineering hosts 58+ student organizations across academic, competitive, honors, and professional categories. For electrical engineering students, the landscape splits into **project-based clubs** (hands-on builds, portfolio, real engineering deliverables) and **networking/professional clubs** (industry contacts, honor societies, career development). The best clubs are unambiguous: IEEE main branch for networking, Solar Devils and SDSL for project work.

## Key Findings

### Project-Based Clubs

- **Solar Devils** is the best EE-specific project club at ASU — the electrical sub-team does battery pack design, BMS, and solar panel integration (MPPT, DC-DC conversion) directly transferable to WBG/EV career track; competes in American Solar Challenge and World Solar Challenge; won first place at Change the World 2024 (Source: [students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/solar-devils](https://students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/solar-devils/)) — confidence: high

- **Sun Devil Satellite Laboratory (SDSL)** offers Altium PCB design experience + industry partnerships with Northrop Grumman, Lockheed Martin, Orbital ATK; weather balloon sub-team launches every semester — fastest path to Year 1 deliverables; CubeSat Coconut expected launch April 2026 (Source: [students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/sdsl](https://students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/sdsl/)) — confidence: high

- **Sun Devil Rocketry** conducted its first successful liquid engine test (2-second, 300 lb thrust) on April 3, 2025 — avionics sub-team is active; formerly Daedalus Astronautics; three research teams + two intro teams + outreach (Source: sundevilrocketry.github.io) — confidence: high

- **ASU Drone Club** teaches design/build/program/fly across FPV racing drones and payload drones; embedded systems, firmware, hardware design skills — confidence: medium

- **Combat Ready Robotics (CRR)** hosts workshops + Sun Devil Smackdown competition (3 lb combat robots); shorter cycle than SDSL/Solar; good rapid prototyping exposure (Source: [students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/crr](https://students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/crr/)) — confidence: high

### Networking & Professional Clubs

- **IEEE ASU Student Branch** has 600+ members and 20 officers; organizes "Dinner With Engineers" speed-networking with Boeing, Honeywell, Intel, Texas Instruments; free general membership (worldwide IEEE ~$32/year student); Outstanding Large Student Branch 2018 (Source: [students.engineering.asu.edu/ieee](https://students.engineering.asu.edu/ieee/about/)) — confidence: high

- **IEEE PES Chapter** is IEEE's Power and Energy Society sub-chapter at ASU — directly aligned with WBG/SiC/GaN power electronics career track; active Instagram (@ieee_pes_asustudentchapter) and LinkedIn; less visible than main IEEE branch (Source: Instagram/LinkedIn) — confidence: medium

- **Eta Kappa Nu (HKN)** sends member resumes directly to industry partners (rare); "Lunch With Professors" provides structured faculty access; FURI research pathway; invitation-based for top EE/CE students; won Key Chapter recognition 2020–21 (Source: [students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/eta-kappa-nu](https://students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/eta-kappa-nu/)) — confidence: high

- **Tau Beta Pi** requires top 1/8 junior or top 1/5 senior GPA; offers 32 Fellowships of $10,000 for graduate study + 250 Scholarships of $2,000; Graduate School & Corporate Recruiting Fairs; pan-engineering (all disciplines) (Source: [students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/tau-beta-pi](https://students.engineering.asu.edu/clubs/tau-beta-pi/)) — confidence: high
