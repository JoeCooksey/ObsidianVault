# Research — Bulking vs Cutting & Ideal Body Composition

**Question:** Should a 5'9" male at 156 lbs bulk or cut? What are ideal body weight and muscle mass targets?

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Joe's Current Stats
- **Height:** 5'9" (175.26 cm / 1.7526 m)
- **Weight:** 156 lbs (70.76 kg)
- **BMI:** 23.04 — healthy range (18.5–24.9); no health urgency to change weight direction
- **FFMI estimate** (depends on body fat %):
  - At 10% BF → FFMI ≈ 20.7 ("looks athletic")
  - At 15% BF → FFMI ≈ 19.6 (decent, room to grow)
  - At 18% BF → FFMI ≈ 18.9 (average; recomp territory)
  - At 20% BF → FFMI ≈ 18.4 (soft, cut recommended first)

### 2. The Core Decision — Bulk vs Cut vs Recomp
The single most important variable is **body fat percentage (BF%)**, not scale weight.

| Your BF% | Recommendation | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **<10%** | Lean bulk (+300 cal/day) | Already lean; maximize muscle gain window |
| **10–15%** | Lean bulk or recomp | Either works; choose based on motivation |
| **15–20%** | Recomp (if <1 yr training) or cut first | P-ratio degrades above 20%; recomp most efficient for relatively new lifters |
| **>20%** | Cut to 12–15% first, then bulk | High BF impairs insulin sensitivity and muscle gain efficiency |

**Visual self-test:**
- Can you see your abs at all? → Likely <15% BF → lean bulk
- Can you see a faint outline of abs but covered by fat? → ~15–20% → recomp/cut
- No visible ab definition, noticeable belly? → >20% → cut first

At 5'9"/156 lbs with BMI 23, the most probable scenario for an active person is **12–18% BF** — the lean bulk or recomp zone.

### 3. Body Recomposition — The Best Option for Beginners/Intermediates
If training experience is <2 years: **body recomposition is likely optimal.**
- Eat at or near maintenance calories (TDEE ± 100–200 cal)
- Protein: 1.6–2.2 g/kg = **113–156 g/day** (aim for ~140–160 g)
- Progressive overload in the gym is the primary driver
- Scale weight may barely move while body composition dramatically improves
- Track: waist measurement, progress photos (every 4 weeks), strength numbers

New lifters experience "newbie gains" — can simultaneously lose fat and gain muscle with minimal caloric manipulation.

### 4. Lean Bulk Protocol (If Choosing to Bulk)
- **Caloric surplus:** +200–300 cal/day above TDEE
- **Weight gain rate target:**
  - Beginners (Year 1–2): 1–1.5 lbs/month (0.25–0.375 lb/week)
  - Intermediates (Year 2–4): 0.5–1 lb/month
- **Protein:** 140–160 g/day minimum
- **Anything above +500 cal/day = mathematically guaranteed excess fat storage** at 3:1 or worse fat-to-muscle ratio

### 5. Cut Protocol (If BF% >18–20%)
- **Caloric deficit:** 300–500 cal/day below TDEE
- **Target weight loss rate:** 0.5–1% of bodyweight/week
- **Floor:** Cut to ~12–15% BF, then transition to lean bulk
- **Protein stays HIGH during cut:** 1.8–2.2 g/kg = **128–156 g/day** to preserve muscle
- **Maintain strength training during cut** — this is non-negotiable for muscle preservation

### 6. Joe's TDEE Estimate (at 156 lbs, 6-day/week training)
Using Mifflin-St Jeor BMR (assuming age 19–20):
- BMR ≈ 1,708 cal/day
- Very active (cycling 6×/week): TDEE ≈ **2,800–3,000 cal/day**

| Goal | Daily Calories |
|---|---|
| Cut | ~2,300–2,500 cal |
| Recomp | ~2,700–2,900 cal |
| Lean Bulk | ~3,000–3,300 cal |

### 7. Ideal Body Weight Targets at 5'9"
These are *physique milestone* targets, not requirements. Body weight alone is meaningless; lean mass (FFMI) is the real metric.

| Physique Level | FFMI | Lean Mass | Total Weight @ 12% BF | Total Weight @ 15% BF |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Current (est. 15% BF) | ~19.6 | ~133 lbs | — | 156 lbs |
| "Looks like you lift" | 20–21 | ~135–142 lbs | ~153–161 lbs | ~159–167 lbs |
| Great natural physique | 22 | ~149 lbs | ~169 lbs | ~175 lbs |
| Near natural limit | 25 | ~169 lbs | ~192 lbs | ~199 lbs |

**Realistic natural physique goal (2–5 years of training):** FFMI 21–22 at 12–15% BF = **165–175 lbs.** This is an excellent, impressive, and fully achievable natural physique.

### 8. Muscle Gain Timeline (Natural)
| Training Level | Monthly Muscle Gain | Annual Muscle Gain |
|---|---|---|
| Beginner (Year 1) | 1.5–2 lbs | 15–20 lbs |
| Early intermediate (Year 2) | 1–1.5 lbs | 10–15 lbs |
| Intermediate (Year 3) | 0.5–1 lb | 5–10 lbs |
| Advanced (Year 4+) | 0.25–0.5 lb | 2–5 lbs |

**Ceiling reality check:** Going from FFMI ~19.6 → 22 requires ~15 lbs of pure lean mass. At beginner-intermediate rates, this is a 1.5–3 year project (with consistent training + nutrition).

## Decision Tree for Joe

```
Am I currently training consistently? 
├── NO → Start training first; eat at maintenance + high protein
└── YES → 
    What is my BF%?
    ├── <15% → LEAN BULK (+200–300 cal; gain 1–1.5 lbs/month)
    ├── 15–20% → RECOMP (maintenance ± 100 cal; protein 140–160g/day)

```

**Most likely scenario at 156 lbs / 5'9":** BF is probably 13–18%. If already training, **lean bulk or recomp** is the call. If not training yet, start training at maintenance — newbie gains will do more than any dietary manipulation alone.
