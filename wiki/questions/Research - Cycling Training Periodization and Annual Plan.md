# Research: Cycling Training Periodization & Annual Plan

**Query**: What does research say about cycling training periodization — specifically prioritizing strength in winter and VO2 max in summer? Provide a 6-day/week training plan (rest Sunday).

---

## 8 Key Research Findings

1. **Seasonal periodization is well-validated.** Traditional cycling periodization (base/strength in winter → build in spring → VO2 max blocks pre-summer → peak in summer) is backed by decades of sport science and elite athlete data. The phases are distinct for a reason — each builds on the last.

2. **Winter base is necessary but not sufficient.** CTS research warns that pure Zone 1 volume in winter without strength work or occasional intensity will not produce peak performance alone. You need the gym component.

3. **Strength training transfers to cycling performance.** A meta-analysis of 17 studies shows heavy resistance training significantly improves cycling economy and TT performance. The mechanism: higher max force per pedal stroke → lower relative effort at racing pace.

4. **VO2 max blocks, not year-round dosing.** Block periodization of VO2 max intervals (concentrated 2–3 week blocks) produces greater gains than spreading VO2 max work thin across the year. 2 sessions/week per block, 2–3 blocks total.

5. **The polarized model (Seiler) outperforms threshold-heavy training.** Research on elite athletes converges on ~80% easy / ~20% hard. Zone 2 "comfortably hard" training accumulates fatigue without proportional adaptation. Avoid the grey zone.

6. **Short intervals beat long for VO2 max stimulus.** 2–4 min intervals at 106–120% FTP produce more time above 90% VO2 max than longer efforts, driving stronger aerobic ceiling adaptations. 30/15 microburst protocols also effective.

7. **Strength phases follow a 4-step arc.** Adaptation → Hypertrophy → Strength → Power. Rushing this arc (skipping hypertrophy) reduces power transfer gains. Each phase is 3–6 weeks; total off-season gym arc = ~16 weeks.

8. **FTP base precedes VO2 max blocks.** 16 weeks of sweet spot base training before 6 weeks of VO2 max blocks is a common evidence-supported periodization sequence. Do not attempt sustained VO2 max blocks without an aerobic base.

## 5 Open Questions

1. How much does training age (beginner vs. well-trained) affect the optimal winter-to-summer transition timeline?
2. Is there a VO2 max ceiling where further interval blocks stop producing gains? (Plateau threshold)
3. How does a student schedule with variable sleep and study stress modulate recovery between phases?
4. Do strength gains fully reverse during the competition phase if gym drops to 1×/week?
5. What is the optimal timing between the last heavy strength session and first VO2 max block?

## 6-Day/Week Training Plan (Rest: Sunday)

**Profile**: 19M, 6 days/week cyclist, no specific race calendar referenced — plan structured around annual cycle.

### Training Zones Reference
| Zone | Name | % FTP | % Max HR | Feel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Z1 | Recovery | <55% | <68% | Very easy, conversation |
| Z2 | Endurance | 56–75% | 69–83% | Easy, talking |
| Z3 | Tempo | 76–90% | 84–94% | Moderately hard |
| Z4 | Threshold (FTP) | 91–105% | 95–99% | Hard, limited speech |
| Z5 | VO2 Max | 106–120% | >100% | Very hard, 2–5 min max |

---

### PHASE 1 — BASE + STRENGTH (Oct – Dec, ~12 weeks)
*Goal: aerobic engine, muscular foundation. 80% Z1–Z2 riding + gym 2–3×/week.*

**Week structure (repeat 3× then 1 recovery week)**

| Day | Session | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | Gym (Hypertrophy) | 60 min | Squat, RDL, hip thrust, core |
| Tue | Z1–Z2 Endurance Ride | 60–75 min | Conversational pace |
| Wed | Gym (Hypertrophy) | 60 min | Split squat, leg press, Copenhagen plank |
| Thu | Z1–Z2 Endurance Ride | 75–90 min | Slightly longer, stay easy |
| Fri | Gym (Strength) or Z1 Ride | 60 min | Alt: 3rd gym day (adaptation phase only) |
| Sat | Long Z1–Z2 Ride | 90–120 min | Longest ride of the week |
| Sun | **Rest** | — | Full off day |

*Recovery Week (week 4)*: drop all gym sessions, rides to Z1 only, cut volume 40%.

---

### PHASE 2 — BUILD + POWER (Jan – Mar, ~10 weeks)
*Goal: raise FTP, convert strength to cycling power. Sweet spot + one threshold session/week. Gym 2×/week (Strength→Power phases).*

| Day | Session | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | Gym (Strength/Power) | 45–60 min | Heavier, explosive — squat, deadlift, box jumps |
| Tue | Z3 Tempo / Sweet Spot | 60–75 min | 2 × 20 min @ 85–90% FTP with 5 min rest |
| Wed | Z1 Easy Ride | 60 min | Active recovery after Tuesday |
| Thu | Gym (Strength/Power) | 45–60 min | Sprint bike workout after (10–15 min neuromuscular sprints) |
| Fri | Z4 Threshold Intervals | 60–75 min | 3 × 12 min @ FTP, 4 min recovery |
| Sat | Long Z1–Z2 Ride | 90–120 min | Aerobic base maintenance |
| Sun | **Rest** | — | — |

*3 hard weeks + 1 recovery week. On recovery week: no gym, all rides Zone 1, reduce to 4 sessions.*

---

### PHASE 3 — VO2 MAX BLOCKS (Apr – May, ~8 weeks = 2 × 3-week blocks)
*Goal: raise aerobic ceiling. Two VO2 max sessions/week in the first 3 weeks, recover, repeat. Drop gym to 1×/week.*

**Block A: Weeks 1–3**

| Day | Session | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | Z1 Easy / Light Gym | 60 min | Maintain strength (1×/week, moderate weight) |
| Tue | VO2 Max Intervals | 60–70 min | 6 × 3 min @ 115% FTP / 3 min rest. 15–18 min in zone. |
| Wed | Z1 Easy Ride | 60 min | Recovery — keep HR < 70% max |
| Thu | VO2 Max Intervals | 60–70 min | 5 × 4 min @ 110% FTP / 4 min rest |
| Fri | Z1–Z2 Endurance | 60–75 min | Aerobic base, do not push |
| Sat | Long Z1–Z2 Ride | 90–120 min | Conversational, mental break from intensity |
| Sun | **Rest** | — | — |

**Week 4: Recovery (between blocks)**

| Day | Session | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Mon–Sun | Z1 easy only, 4 sessions | 45–60 min each |

**Block B: Weeks 5–7** — same structure as Block A, progress intervals:
- 8 × 2 min @ 120% FTP (short interval variant) / 2 min rest
- OR 5 × 5 min @ 108% FTP / 5 min rest

**Week 8: Recovery** — ease into competition phase.

---

### PHASE 4 — COMPETITION / MAINTENANCE (Jun – Aug)
*Goal: maintain fitness, perform. 1–2 hard sessions/week, mostly Z1–Z2. Gym 0–1×/week.*

| Day | Session | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | Z1 Easy | 45–60 min | Legs fresh for hard day |
| Tue | Race-Specific Effort | 60–75 min | Criterium-style efforts / punchy sprints / TT efforts |
| Wed | Z1 Easy | 60 min | Recovery |
| Thu | Threshold Maintenance | 60 min | 2 × 15 min @ FTP — just maintenance |
| Fri | Z1 / Openers | 45 min | Short easy ride, 3 × 30 sec jumps at end |
| Sat | Long Z1–Z2 OR Event | 90–120 min | Race or group ride |
| Sun | **Rest** | — | — |

---

### PHASE 5 — TRANSITION (Sep – Oct, ~4 weeks)
*Goal: mental and physical reset. No structure required.*
- Week 1–2: completely off the bike (walk, swim, casual sport — nothing structured)
- Week 3–4: easy unstructured riding 3×/week, 30–45 min
- No gym
- Then begin new base phase (Phase 1) in October

## Implementation Notes

**FTP Test**: Establish your FTP at the start of Phase 2 and again before Phase 3. Standard protocol: 20-minute all-out effort × 0.95 = FTP.

**Heart Rate as Backup**: If no power meter, use HR. Zone 2 = conversational pace, cannot easily sing but can speak. VO2 max = 2–3 min hard efforts where speaking is impossible.

**Gym-to-Bike Order**: Always ride first on days with both sessions, or separate by 4+ hours. Exception: during hypertrophy phase, order matters less since gym volume is the focus.

**Student Calendar Adjustment**: EE semester exams fall in Dec and Apr — consider dropping gym sessions (not bike sessions) during exam weeks. Aerobic base is forgiving. Strength phases can pause 1–2 weeks without significant regression.

**Signs to Pull Back**: resting HR elevated >5 bpm, persistent muscle soreness >3 days, mood decline, declining interval performance over 2 consecutive sessions.
