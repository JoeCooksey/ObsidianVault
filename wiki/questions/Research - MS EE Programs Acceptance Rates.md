# MS EE Programs — Acceptance Rates

**Research question:** What are the graduate admissions acceptance rates for the top MS EE programs identified in the physical side (power electronics & semiconductors) research?

> **Important caveat:** Universities rarely publish program-specific graduate acceptance rates. The figures below are compiled from official admissions statistics, third-party aggregators (GradCafe, Yocket), and institutional reports. Treat ranges as estimates unless marked as official.

## Key Findings

1. **No university in this list officially publishes their MS ECE acceptance rate.** Figures come from graduate enrollment reports, third-party aggregators, and student-reported outcomes on GradCafe/Reddit. This is typical for graduate programs — unlike undergraduate admissions, graduate acceptance rates are rarely disclosed.

2. **MIT does not offer a standalone terminal MS to external applicants.** All external admits enter the PhD program; the SM (master's) is earned en route to the PhD. Acceptance rate ~6–9% is for the PhD track.

3. **Stanford's ~15% is relatively accessible compared to its overall graduate selectivity (~5.2% overall).** EE is one of Stanford's more accessible graduate departments because it is large and professionally oriented. The admitted pool is still extremely competitive.

4. **UC Berkeley has two tracks with different selectivity.** The MEng (industry-oriented, 1 year) admits ~20–25% and averages a 3.7 GPA. The research MS/PhD track is significantly more selective.

5. **Purdue is the most accessible high-prestige option** at ~37%, with an average admitted GPA of 3.6. The online availability of the Microelectronics & Semiconductors track further increases access.

6. **Virginia Tech and NC State are the most achievable targets** for strong undergrad students. VT recommends 3.3+ GPA; NC State requires 3.0 minimum with a competitive bar of 3.25+.

7. **NC State MS-WBGS (Fall 2026 launch) has no published acceptance rate yet.** As an inaugural cohort, the admit rate is unknowable in advance. First cohorts of new specialized programs often have higher acceptance rates than established ones — there is no backlog of alumni competition and the program needs to fill seats. This may be an early-mover advantage.

8. **UT Austin's new SSE program (Fall 2025) has no historical acceptance rate data.** The program explicitly offers industry-funded fellowships to most admitted students — this implies it is not volume-maximizing and may be moderately selective (~15–25%).

## What Moves the Needle Most (Any School)

The acceptance rate is only half the picture — the denominator (applicant quality) matters more. All the programs above have self-selected applicant pools of strong engineering students. Differentiators:

| Factor | Impact | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| GPA ≥ 3.5+ | High | Baseline; 3.0 gets you considered at most; 3.5+ is competitive |
| Research / lab experience | Very High | FURI, capstone project, internship lab work; name-drop in SOP |
| Named faculty in SOP | Very High | "I want to work with Prof. X on Y because Z" — generic SOPs get rejected |
| LTSpice / SPICE / simulation | High | Specifically valued at VT CPES and NC State |
| WBG-specific skills (SiC/GaN) | High | Differentiator for NC State, UT Austin SSE, VT CPES |
| GRE | Low-None | Mostly optional or waived across all 10 programs now |
| Undergrad school prestige | Moderate | ASU Fulton is T-25 engineering; not disqualifying, helps to have strong GPA |
| Letters of recommendation | High | 3 required; engineering faculty >> other letter writers |
