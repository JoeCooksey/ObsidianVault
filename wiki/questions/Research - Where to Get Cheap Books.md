# Where to Get Cheap Books

## Summary

Books can be obtained for free or at steep discounts through a layered strategy: start with free digital options (library apps, BookBub, Project Gutenberg), then move to used book sites for physical copies. The full tier list below ranks every major method from free to paid.

---

## Tier List

### S-Tier — Free

| Method | How It Works | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Public Library + Libby App** | Free borrowing, physical + digital (ebooks/audiobooks) via Libby/OverDrive with a library card | Any book, especially new releases |
| **BookBub Daily Deals** | Free service; curates 0–$3 ebook deals + free ebooks daily across 40+ genres | Ebooks, discovering new authors |
| **Project Gutenberg** | 70,000+ free public domain ebooks | Classics (anything pre-1928) |
| **Book Swapping** | BookMooch / PaperBackSwap — list books you own, earn credits, request others | Physical books you already own |
| **Little Free Library** | Take a book, leave a book; neighborhood boxes | Casual reads, serendipity |

**Free > $0 threshold**: library card takes 5 minutes to get and unlocks most books ever published.

---

### A-Tier — Under $5

| Site | Model | Strengths | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ThriftBooks** | Used book marketplace, 13M+ titles | Free shipping over $10; ReadingReward loyalty program; very clean interface | Best single destination for cheap used physical books |
| **Better World Books** | Used books + social mission | Often runs 6-for-$15 sales; free worldwide shipping; funds literacy nonprofits | Good for bulk buying |
| **AbeBooks** | Amazon subsidiary, individual sellers | Books from $1; huge selection including rare/out-of-print | Watch for high shipping on cheap listings |
| **Book Outlet** | Publisher overstock | 50–90% off cover price on new surplus books | Selection rotates; great for browsing |
| **Biblio** | Independent seller marketplace | Rare, first editions, out-of-print; privacy-focused alternative to AbeBooks | Best for collector copies |
| **Alibris** | Used book marketplace | Books from $1 from individual sellers | Similar to AbeBooks; sometimes cheaper |

---

### B-Tier — Low-Cost Paid

| Method | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Kindle Unlimited** | $9.99/month | Unlimited reading from 4M+ titles; best value if reading 3+ books/month |
| **Half Price Books** | Physical stores (30+ states) + online | "Super Buy" section: books under $2, $5, $10 | Good for in-person browsing |
| **Thrift Stores** (Goodwill, Salvation Army, etc.) | Often $0.50–$2 | Highly variable selection; best in college towns and suburbs |
| **Garage / Estate Sales** | $0.25–$1 | Incredible finds possible; requires legwork |
| **Library Book Sales** | $0.25–$3 | Annual or quarterly; often sell entire shelves at $1/bag day |

---

### C-Tier — Moderate Cost

| Method | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Amazon Used Marketplace** | Used copies often $1–5 + shipping; AbeBooks usually beats it |
| **eBay** | Auctions can yield $1 books; bulk lots available |
| **Bookshop.org** | Supports indie bookstores; full price but ethically sourced |
| **Powell's Books** | Largest US indie bookstore (Portland); online + physical; used selection |

---

### D-Tier — Avoid for Cheap Books

| Method | Why |
|---|---|
| Barnes & Noble retail | Full MSRP; only worthwhile with B&N Premium membership ($39.99/yr) |
| Target / Walmart | No meaningful discount on most titles |
| Airport / hotel shops | 20–40% above MSRP |

## Price Comparison Tools

Use these **before** buying anywhere:

- **BookFinder.com** — compares 100,000+ sellers for new, used, rare books simultaneously
- **BookScouter.com** — especially strong for textbooks; shows buyback prices too
- **AbeBooks search** — proxy for global used book market pricing

**Rule**: For any book >$10, run BookFinder first. Savings of 50–80% are routine.

---

## Digital-First Strategy (Joe's Context)

Given the book list from prior autoresearch (finance, stoicism, psychology, business), here's the optimal sourcing path:

1. **Check Libby first** — most popular books (Atomic Habits, Psychology of Money, etc.) have library copies; wait list is usually <2 weeks
2. **Sign up for BookBub** — free; set preferences to your genres; check daily or let email alerts come in
3. **For books you want to own**: ThriftBooks for $3–6 copies; run BookFinder first
4. **Classics (Meditations, Crime & Punishment, etc.)**: Free on Project Gutenberg; also $1–3 on ThriftBooks

**Cost estimate**: Joe's 9-book Phase 1–2 reading list costs ~$0 via library, or ~$25–35 total via ThriftBooks used copies.

---

## Key Findings

- **Libby (library app) is the highest-leverage free option** — free ebooks and audiobooks from your public library card; most people don't use it
- **ThriftBooks is the best single used-book site** — large inventory, rewards program, reliable condition grading
- **BookBub is completely free and surfaces $0–$1 ebook deals daily** — no reason not to subscribe
- **BookFinder.com eliminates overpaying** — price comparison across 100k+ sellers
- **Project Gutenberg**: all the great classics (Stoicism, Dostoyevsky, Dickens) are completely free
- **Library book sales** are the hidden gem for physical books — $1/bag day is real
- **Better World Books** bundles cheapness with a social mission (literacy nonprofits)
