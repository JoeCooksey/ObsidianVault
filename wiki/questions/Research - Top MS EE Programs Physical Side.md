# Top MS EE Programs — Physical Side (Power Electronics & Semiconductors)

**Research question:** What are the top graduate schools for a master's degree in electrical engineering focused on the physical side — power electronics, semiconductor devices, and wide bandgap (WBG) materials?

## Key Findings

1. **Virginia Tech CPES is the #1 dedicated power electronics research institution in the US.** The Center for Power Electronics Systems (CPES) runs $6–7M in annual research, has ~100 researchers, and produces more power electronics PhDs than anywhere else. If your goal is converters, inverters, and magnetics — VT is the destination.

2. **NC State has the only dedicated MS in Wide Bandgap Semiconductors in the country (Fall 2026 launch).** The MS-WBGS is jointly offered by ECE and Materials Science; curriculum covers SiC/GaN device physics, fabrication, characterization, and power electronics system applications. It is plugged into PowerAmerica (49 industry members) and the FREEDM Center — direct pipeline to WBG employers.

3. **UT Austin launched an MS in Semiconductor Science and Engineering in Fall 2025.** Four tracks: manufacturing, circuits & systems, heterogeneous integration, semiconductor devices. Most students receive industry-funded fellowships covering tuition + stipend. This is the fastest-rising dedicated semiconductor MS program.

4. **MIT, Stanford, and UC Berkeley are tied #1 globally but are not physical-side specialists.** They offer the broadest EE graduate education and have excellent semiconductor device research groups, but admission is extremely competitive and the programs are not tailored to power electronics or WBG specialization — you'd need to self-select into the right research group.

5. **Purdue's MS in Microelectronics & Semiconductors is the most accessible high-prestige semiconductor MS.** Available online, within a top-10 engineering school, with focus areas in devices & manufacturing, circuit design, and system design. Strong semiconductor ecosystem with the Birck Nanotechnology Center.

6. **Georgia Tech, UIUC, and University of Michigan round out the top tier for broad power/devices graduate study.** Georgia Tech is strong in power systems and smart grids. UIUC has a well-regarded power and energy systems concentration. Michigan has a strong solid-state devices and microelectronics track with the SSEL lab.

7. **Admission to top programs increasingly favors demonstrated lab/project experience over GPA alone.** Programs like UT Austin's SSE explicitly state that industry or research experience in semiconductors strengthens the application. For VT CPES, prior LTspice/hardware experience and a clear power electronics research interest are differentiators.

8. **For Joe specifically: NC State MS-WBGS (or VT for power electronics) is the highest-leverage path given existing WBG/EV career interest.** NC State's program connects directly to SiC/GaN employers that map exactly to the 18-month skill plan already in the wiki. The PowerAmerica job/internship portal is an immediate career asset.

## NC State MS-WBGS — Closest Match to Joe's Career Track

**Program highlights:**
- Jointly offered by ECE + Materials Science Engineering
- Hybrid delivery: online coursework + intensive on-campus summer practicum
- Curriculum: semiconductor physics → SiC/GaN device structures → fabrication → characterization → power electronics system application (converters, inverters, EV drivetrains)
- Access to cleanrooms, nanofab labs, power electronics testbeds
- PowerAmerica portal: job and internship postings from 49 WBG member companies (Wolfspeed, Onsemi, STMicro, Navitas, GaN Systems, etc.)
- FREEDM Center: future renewable energy grid research
- Launch: **Fall 2026** (applications likely open Fall 2025 for this cohort)

**Why this maps to Joe's plan:**
The existing skill plan (device physics → converter design → WBG specialization → digital control) is essentially what NC State MS-WBGS formalizes as a degree. The PowerAmerica industry portal is the Livermore-equivalent for WBG — direct pipeline to the exact employers (Wolfspeed, Onsemi, STMicro) identified in the EE Physical Side research.

## Virginia Tech CPES — Best for Pure Power Electronics

**Program highlights:**
- CPES is an NSF Engineering Research Center with $6–7M annual research budget
- ~100 professors, graduate students, visiting scholars, and staff
- Curriculum: power electronics packaging, systems design and integration, advanced power topologies, thermal management
- MS (research-based) and MEng (project-based)
- Industry partnerships and technology transfer focus
- Blacksburg, VA campus (lower cost of living than tech hubs)

## UT Austin SSE — Best New Program for Semiconductor Manufacturing/Devices

**Program highlights:**
- New Fall 2025 launch — very current curriculum (not legacy)
- 4 tracks: semiconductor manufacturing, circuits & systems, heterogeneous integration, semiconductor devices
- Most admitted students receive: stipend + tuition + health insurance via industry-funded RA
- 30 credit hours; includes master's report
- Austin, TX: Samsung Fab, NXP, Texas Instruments, Applied Materials all local
- GRE not required; minimum 3.0 GPA

## Admission Signals (Physical Side Programs)

| Factor | What Counts |
|---|---|
| GPA | 3.0 minimum almost universal; competitive admits ~3.5+ |
| GRE | Increasingly optional or waived at most programs |
| Research/lab experience | High weight at VT CPES, MIT, Stanford, Berkeley |
| LTspice/SPICE simulation | Valued at VT CPES and NC State |
| Statement of Purpose | Must name specific faculty and why their research fits |
| Letters of recommendation | 3 required; engineering faculty > others |
| Undergrad school | Top engineering schools help but not disqualifying |

## Joe's Recommended Path

Given Joe's profile (19yo ASU EE freshman, WBG/EV physical side interest, Livermore CA base):

1. **Build the 18-month skill plan in parallel with ASU coursework** (see [[EE Physical Side — Actionable Skill Plan]])
2. **Target ASU FURI** (Fulton Undergraduate Research Initiative) in Year 2 — get a research mentor and publication or poster by junior year
3. **Apply to NC State MS-WBGS Fall 2026 cohort** during senior year — this is the most direct pipeline to WBG employers
4. **Backup applications**: VT CPES (for power electronics emphasis), UT Austin SSE (semiconductor manufacturing/devices), Purdue online MS (if employment-concurrent)
5. **LLNL connection**: Sandia-NC State collaborations exist; a WBG MS from NC State could directly feed Sandia's power electronics group in Livermore
