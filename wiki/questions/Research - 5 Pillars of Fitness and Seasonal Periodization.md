# Research — 5 Pillars of Fitness and Seasonal Periodization

## Core Question
What are the 5 pillars of fitness, and how should training be periodized to improve each pillar across the seasons of the year?

---

## Finding 1 — Two Major "5 Pillars" Frameworks Exist

The **ACSM (American College of Sports Medicine)** defines the canonical 5 components:
1. Cardiovascular Endurance
2. Muscular Strength
3. Muscular Endurance
4. Flexibility / Mobility
5. Body Composition

**Peter Attia's longevity framework** uses 4 pillars (Stability, Strength, Zone 2, VO2 Max) with Stability as the foundational base rather than a standalone goal. The ACSM framework is broader and better suited for general fitness; Attia's is optimized for long-term health span. Both are valid; they address different user goals.

---

## Finding 2 — Cardiovascular Endurance Should Be Built Seasonally in Two Layers

Layer 1 is aerobic **base** (Zone 1–2), which should dominate the **Fall/Winter preparatory period** — high volume, low intensity, building mitochondrial density and fat oxidation. Layer 2 is **VO2 max work** (Zone 4–5 intervals), which should be inserted in concentrated 2–3 week blocks in **Spring/pre-competition**, not scattered year-round.

The minimum effective dose for Zone 2 per Attia: **3 hours/week** (4 × 45-min sessions). VO2 max minimum: 1 × 30-min session/week (4–6 × 4-min hard intervals).

---

## Finding 3 — Strength Training Follows a Classic 4-Phase Arc Across the Year

The NSCA/NASM standard strength periodization phases:
1. **Anatomical Adaptation / Muscular Endurance** — high reps (15–25), low load (50–60% 1RM), fall entry
2. **Hypertrophy** — moderate reps (8–12), moderate load (65–75% 1RM), winter
3. **Strength** — low reps (3–6), high load (80–90% 1RM), late winter/early spring
4. **Power** — very low reps (1–3), high load (85–95% 1RM) + explosive work, spring/pre-competition

Each phase is typically a 4–6 week mesocycle with a 3:1 work-to-deload ratio. During in-season (summer), strength training is reduced to 1–2×/week maintenance.

---

## Finding 4 — Muscular Endurance Is the Bridge Between Cardio and Strength

Muscular endurance (20–25 rep range, 50–70% 1RM, 3–6 sets) is trained most effectively during the **early preparatory phase (fall)**. It builds the tissue tolerance that protects joints during later heavy loading and also improves lactate threshold for cardio events. Neglecting this phase and jumping straight to hypertrophy or strength is a top periodization mistake.

---

## Finding 5 — Flexibility and Mobility Are Year-Round, Not Seasonal — But Emphasis Shifts

Flexibility (tissue elongation, passive) requires months of consistent work. Mobility (neuromuscular, joint range under load) responds faster — within weeks. The most effective programming:
- **Daily**: 10–20 min dynamic mobility before training; static stretching post-workout
- **Winter emphasis**: Dedicated mobility sessions 2–3×/week when training volume is lower
- **In-season**: Maintain with 10-min daily work; don't neglect — injury risk rises without it

The "dynamic first, static later" principle should apply in every session regardless of season.

---

## Finding 6 — Body Composition Follows a Seasonal Bulk/Cut Rhythm

The optimal annual body composition cycle aligns with seasons:
- **Fall/Winter (Oct–Feb)**: Lean bulk — mild caloric surplus (+200–300 kcal/day), strength phase, build muscle
- **Spring (Mar–May)**: Cut — mild caloric deficit (−300–500 kcal/day), maintain training, lose fat
- **Summer (Jun–Aug)**: Maintain or slight cut — visible physique, performance-focused nutrition

Key constraint: **never cut during a strength phase** (you lose muscle). **Never bulk in-season** (excess mass hurts performance). Bulk and cut phases should be 12 weeks minimum each for meaningful effect.

---

## Finding 7 — The Full Seasonal Annual Plan (Northern Hemisphere)

| Season | Period | Primary Focus | Secondary |
|--------|--------|---------------|-----------|
| **Fall** (Sep–Nov) | General Prep | Aerobic base, muscular endurance, mobility | Body recomp |
| **Winter** (Dec–Feb) | Specific Prep | Hypertrophy → Strength, Zone 2 volume | Lean bulk nutrition |
| **Spring** (Mar–May) | Build / Pre-Competition | Power, VO2 max blocks, cut | Convert gym gains to sport |
| **Summer** (Jun–Aug) | Competition / Peak | Maintain fitness, performance, show physique | Active recovery |

Microcycle rhythm across all seasons: **3 hard weeks + 1 deload week** (reduce volume ~40%, keep some intensity).

---

## Finding 8 — Stability Is the Missing Pillar (Attia's Contribution)

The ACSM 5 components omit **stability/foundational movement quality** — the ability to load joints safely, balance, and dissipate force. Attia names this the most underrated pillar. Stability training (single-leg work, rotational control, hip/spine stability) should be woven into **every phase**, especially during early preparatory and transition periods. Without it, strength and power gains are built on a shaky foundation that leads to injury.
