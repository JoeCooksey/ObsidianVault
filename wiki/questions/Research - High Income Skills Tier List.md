# Research — High Income Skills Tier List

## Query
What are the highest-income skills (general and EE-specific) in 2026, and what is an actionable plan to build the top ones?

## Key Findings

1. **AI/ML Engineering is the single highest-paying learnable skill in 2026** — $155k–$250k base; LLM specialists earn 25–40% premium pushing total comp to $200k–$350k; AI industry projected to hit $500B. This is the clearest breakout skill of the decade.

2. **Analog/Mixed-Signal IC Design is the highest-paid EE specialization** — median $222k at top companies, 90th percentile $349k. Hardest to enter (10–15 year mastery curve), but Apple/Qualcomm/NVIDIA/ADI/TI compete fiercely for talent. Scarcity drives price.

3. **FPGA Engineering is the most underrated high-paying EE skill** — avg $175k, top earners $251k+. Defense, aerospace, HPC, and crypto all need FPGA. Sandia National Lab (5 min from Joe in Livermore) runs extensive FPGA programs. Verilog/VHDL skills are the gateway.

4. **Power Electronics (WBG specialist) sits at $112k–$230k with the steepest demand growth** — SiC traction inverter engineers and GaN OBC engineers are the rarest hires at Wolfspeed, Onsemi, STMicro, Navitas, and EPC. EV transition drives demand for 10–20 years. This is Joe's primary career track and the highest-leverage starting point.

5. **Embedded Firmware + RTOS is the widest on-ramp to $150k+** — $114k–$244k range; C programming + RTOS + communication protocols (CAN/SPI/I2C) is the #1 hiring filter across automotive, aerospace, and defense. Fastest path from "EE student" to six figures.

6. **General high-income skills (non-EE) follow a clear hierarchy** — AI/ML engineering > Cloud Architecture > Cybersecurity > Data Science > Specialized Software Engineering. All pay $120k+ at entry level; all are learnable as a second skill stack alongside EE.

7. **Freelance ceiling vs. employment ceiling differ dramatically** — Elite AI/ML engineers freelancing on Upwork/Toptal pull $120–$250/hr ($200k–$450k/yr). Copywriting and digital marketing peak at $50–$75/hr for most. Technical skills have compounding leverage; writing/marketing skills hit ceilings faster.

8. **Skill stacking (EE + Python/AI) creates a compensation gap** — Power electronics engineers who can also write Python automation scripts and apply ML to circuit optimization are commanding 20–30% salary premiums at companies like Tesla, Apple, and Qualcomm. Joe's EE track + Python/AI second skill = the highest-leverage combination available to an EE student in 2026.
