# Research: Food Health Tier Lists

## Question
Build comprehensive tier lists of foods ranked purely by healthiness — overall and by category (meats, vegetables, fruits, legumes, nuts/seeds, grains, dairy).

## Key Sources
- CDC Powerhouse Fruits & Vegetables Nutrient Density Scoring (PMC4049200)
- Tufts Food Compass — comprehensive nutrient profiling linked to chronic disease outcomes
- American Heart Association — Picking Healthy Proteins; omega-3 recommendations
- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health — Dairy, Nuts, Grains, Ultra-processed foods
- PMC Umbrella Review — Nuts/seeds and cardiovascular outcomes (PMC9776667)
- FDA Seafood Advice — Mercury and omega-3 guidelines
- WHO Processed Meat Classification — Group 1 carcinogen (hot dogs, processed deli meat)
- NIH ODS — Omega-3 fatty acids factsheet

## Summary of Key Findings

### The Unambiguous S-Tier Foods (eat most)
1. **Fatty fish** (wild salmon, sardines, mackerel, herring) — omega-3 EPA/DHA; strongest single nutrient for CVD, brain, inflammation
2. **Dark leafy greens** (watercress, spinach, chard, kale) — CDC top scorers; most vitamins/minerals per calorie of any food
3. **Berries** (blueberries, blackberries, raspberries) — highest antioxidant density, lowest GI, highest fiber of fruits
4. **Lentils** — best legume; highest fiber, lowest GI, easiest to digest
5. **Walnuts + Chia + Flax** — ALA omega-3; 28g nuts/day cuts CVD 21% and all-cause mortality 22% (meta-analysis)

### Clear F-Tier Foods (avoid)
1. **Processed meats** (hot dogs, sausages, deli meats) — WHO Group 1 carcinogen; nitrosamines
2. **Sugary drinks** (soda, juice, energy drinks) — linked to 32 chronic conditions; zero nutrition
3. **Ultra-processed snacks** (chips, cookies, fast food) — 60% of American daily calories; mechanistically harmful
4. **High-mercury fish** (king mackerel, swordfish, shark) — neurological damage risk

### Universal Principles Across All Categories
- **Whole > processed** at every level without exception
- **Color intensity** correlates with phytochemical/antioxidant content (darker = better)
- **Omega-3 content** is the primary differentiator in protein foods
- **Fermented > fresh > pasteurized** in dairy for probiotic benefit
- **Low GI + high fiber** = metabolic protection; the formula holds across all food types
- **Ultra-processing = harm** regardless of macronutrient content

### The Holy Trinity (every dietary framework agrees)
Dark leafy greens + fatty fish + berries — if you ate ONLY these three categories, you'd cover most known nutritional needs and deficiency risks
