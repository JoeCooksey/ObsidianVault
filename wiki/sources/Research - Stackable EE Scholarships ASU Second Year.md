# Research — Stackable EE Scholarships: ASU Second Year

8-finding synthesis from autoresearch on scholarships stackable with NAU + FAFSA for a second-year EE student at ASU.

---

## Finding 1: DoD SMART is the highest-value undergraduate scholarship available to an EE student — by a large margin

The DoD SMART Scholarship-for-Service provides full tuition + a $30,000–$46,000 annual stipend + summer internships at DoD facilities + a civilian job offer upon graduation. No other undergraduate scholarship comes close in total dollar value. The tradeoff is a post-graduation service commitment to a DoD facility equal to the length of scholarship funding. Application opens August 1 and closes the first Friday in December each year. If the user is open to national security or defense engineering work, this is the one to pour effort into.

**Source**: [smartscholarship.org](https://www.smartscholarship.org/smart/en)

---

## Finding 2: Fulton Schools Scholarship Universe is OPEN TO CONTINUING STUDENTS — not just incoming freshmen

A common misconception is that Fulton scholarships are only for new admits. The ASU Engineering Student Hub explicitly states: scholarships are available to "both new (incoming freshman and transfer students) and continuing engineering students." The portal opens November 1 and closes February 1. With $1.7M+ in the pool and selection criteria that include talent, personal story, and leadership (not just GPA), this is the highest-ROI annual action for a returning EE student.

**Source**: [students.engineering.asu.edu/scholarships-funding/undergraduate/faq](https://students.engineering.asu.edu/scholarships-funding/undergraduate/faq/)

---

## Finding 3: Goldwater requires institutional nomination through ASU ONSA — start this pipeline in Fall 2026

The Barry Goldwater Scholarship (up to $7,500/yr) requires the university to nominate candidates; you cannot apply directly. ASU's Office of National Scholarships Advisement (ONSA) handles this. As a sophomore, the user is in the ideal eligibility window. The critical prerequisite is demonstrable research experience — FURI is the direct feeder. FURI outreach this summer → FURI participation Fall 2026 → ONSA meeting Fall 2026 → Goldwater nomination January 2027 is the correct sequence.

**Source**: [goldwaterscholarship.gov/eligibility](https://goldwaterscholarship.gov/eligibility/)

---

## Finding 4: AFCEA explicitly targets sophomores and juniors — the timing is perfect

The AFCEA STEM Majors Scholarship ($2,500–$5,000) requires sophomore or junior enrollment at the time of application. Going into second year is the entry point for this eligibility window. Requirements are 3.0 GPA, U.S. citizenship, and full-time enrollment at an accredited 4-year school — all likely met. It's a professional society scholarship with a moderate applicant pool and a straightforward application.

**Source**: [afcea.org/stem-majors-scholarships](https://www.afcea.org/stem-majors-scholarships)

---

## Finding 5: IEEE AESS Engineering Scholarship is $10,000 with a lower GPA bar (2.8) and small applicant pool

The IEEE AESS awards only 2 scholarships per year at $10,000 each — making it a low-competition, high-value target. The GPA minimum is 2.8 (vs. 3.0 for PES and AFCEA), the focus is EE and Systems Engineering, and evaluation is based on essay quality and financial need. Requires AESS Student Membership (cheap) and 3 letters of recommendation. The small number of awards means a well-written essay has a meaningful probability of winning.

**Source**: [ieee-aess.org/awards/education-awards/engineering-scholarship](https://ieee-aess.org/awards/education-awards/engineering-scholarship)

---

## Finding 6: Bold.org profile creation is a one-time action that auto-matches to dozens of scholarships

The Bold.org platform offers the $25,000 "Be Bold" No-Essay Scholarship (no GPA requirement, no major requirement, evaluated on profile and personal story) plus hundreds of EE-specific scholarships with varying requirements. Creating a profile takes ~20 minutes and auto-matches to all eligible awards. This is the lowest effort-per-dollar action in the scholarship stack and should be completed first.

**Source**: [bold.org/scholarships/the-be-bold-no-essay-scholarship](https://bold.org/scholarships/the-be-bold-no-essay-scholarship/)

---

## Finding 7: NAU Scholarship renewal is automatic but conditional — 3.0 GPA and 30 credits per year are non-negotiable

The existing NAU Scholarship renews for up to 8 semesters (4 years) but requires: (1) cumulative 3.0 GPA and (2) full-time enrollment completing 30 credits annually. Losing NAU costs $5,500–$17,500/yr — more than most of the external scholarships being chased. Protecting the GPA floor is the highest-dollar defensive action in the financial stack. Everything else is upside; this is downside protection.

**Source**: [financialaid.asu.edu](https://financialaid.asu.edu)

---

## Finding 8: ASU ONSA is the unlocking mechanism for the highest-prestige, highest-dollar programs

The Office of National Scholarships Advisement at ASU (onsa.asu.edu) provides advising for Goldwater, DoD SMART, Fulbright, NSF GRFP, and other nationally competitive programs. For programs requiring institutional nomination (Goldwater), ONSA is the required path — students who apply without institutional advising are disqualified. ONSA also provides application review and strategy coaching for non-nomination programs. Scheduling a meeting in Fall 2026 semester is the highest-leverage single calendar action for the prestigious scholarship pipeline.

**Source**: [onsa.asu.edu/scholarships](https://onsa.asu.edu/scholarships)
