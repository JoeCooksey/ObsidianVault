# EEE 202 Course Page — ASU

ASU faculty course page for EEE 202 Circuits I. Documents the standard topic sequence and textbook used across instructors.

## Key Claims

- **Textbook**: Irwin, *Basic Engineering Circuit Analysis*, 10th Ed. (Wiley) — high confidence, consistent across syllabi
- **8-unit topic sequence**: Fundamentals → Circuit Analysis → Network Theorems → Energy Storage → ODEs → AC Analysis → Frequency Domain → Laplace — high confidence
- **Software**: PSpice and MATLAB used throughout — high confidence
- **Lab section** (EEE 202 Lab, Myhajlenko) runs concurrently with lecture; hands-on application of lecture topics
- Course objectives include: analyze complex DC/AC circuits analytically and with PSpice; apply Laplace transforms correctly

## What It Contributes

Confirms the canonical topic sequence and software stack for EEE 202. The Tsakalis page has been stable for multiple years and reflects the standard course structure regardless of instructor.
