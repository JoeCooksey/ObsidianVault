# MIT 18.06 Linear Algebra OCW — Gilbert Strang

The gold standard free linear algebra course. 34 video lectures by Gilbert Strang, full problem sets with solutions, and ZoomNotes PDF. Used globally for self-study.

## Key Claims

- Covers: systems of equations, matrix operations, LU factorization, vector spaces, eigenvalues, SVD, positive definite matrices — high confidence
- **18.06SC variant** includes recitation problem sets with worked solutions — best for self-study — high confidence
- ZoomNotes PDF (available on OCW) provides concise lecture notes
- Strang's emphasis: column space / null space / row space / left null space (4 fundamental subspaces) — the conceptual core
- Eigenvalues as the heart of matrix theory: "A basis of eigenvectors takes you to the heart of a matrix in a way that elimination cannot" — Strang
- License: CC BY-NC-SA (free to use, adapt, share non-commercially)

## Recommended Sequence for MAT 343 Prep

| Lectures | Topic | MAT 343 Unit |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| 1–10 | Systems, elimination, LU, transpose | Units 1–3 |
| 11–17 | Vector spaces, null/column space, independence | Units 5–6 |
| 18–23 | Orthogonality, projection, least squares, Gram-Schmidt | Unit 8 |
| 21–24 | Eigenvalues, eigenvectors, diagonalization | Unit 7 |
| 29–33 | SVD, positive definite matrices | Unit 9 |

**Selective study**: Lectures 1–17 + 21–24 = minimum viable prep for MAT 343.

## What It Contributes

Best freely available linear algebra course; directly maps to MAT 343 at ASU. More rigorous than Khan Academy but less mechanical — focuses on understanding why, not just how. The 18.06SC variant is structured for self-study and includes graded problem sets.
