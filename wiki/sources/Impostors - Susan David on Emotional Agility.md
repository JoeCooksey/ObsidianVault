# Impostors: A Harvard Psychologist Teaches Us How to Increase Our Emotional Intelligence

**Source**: Impostors podcast (Morning Brew)  
**Host**: Alex Lieberman  
**Guest**: [[Susan David]], psychologist, author of *Emotional Agility*, co-founder of Harvard's Institute of Coaching  
**Topic**: Emotional agility — practical tools for navigating difficult emotions

---

## Context

Susan David's framework grew from two sources: growing up in apartheid South Africa under a culture of emotional denial, and losing her father to cancer at age 15. A teacher who handed her a journal and said "write like no one is reading" became the catalyst — showing that turning *toward* emotions, not away from them, is the path to resilience.

---

## Core Framework

**[[Emotional Agility]]**: The ability to be with thoughts, emotions, and stories in ways that are compassionate, curious, and involve courage to take values-connected steps.

**[[Emotional Rigidity]]**: Fusing with thoughts, emotions, and stories so they drive action away from values rather than toward them.

---

## Practical Tools

### 1. Gentle Acceptance
End the struggle with whether you're "allowed" to feel what you feel. Stop the Type 2 emotion loop ("I'm anxious about my anxiety"). Simply acknowledge: *I feel this. What I'm going through is hard.* See [[Gentle Acceptance]].

### 2. Emotion Granularity
Replace broad brushstroke labels ("stressed") with precise identification. "Stressed" gives the brain nothing to work with. "Disappointed," "unsupported," or "I know this is the wrong job" each activate specific action pathways. See [[Emotion Granularity]].

### 3. Defusion Language
Replace fusion language ("I am sad") with observer language ("I notice I'm feeling sad"). The "I am" construction collapses the distance between self and emotion — leaving no space for wisdom, values, or choice. Victor Frankl: *between stimulus and response there is a space; in that space is our power to choose.* You are the sky, not the cloud. See [[Defusion Language]].

## Key Supporting Ideas

- **[[Emotions as Functional]]**: Darwin's insight — emotions are adaptive signals, not weaknesses. Boredom signals you value learning. Anger signals something important is at stake.
- **[[Toxic Positivity]]**: The cultural pressure to suppress difficult emotions makes people *less* resilient over time — not more. Suppression leads to burnout and relationship damage.
- **Secure base** (Bowlby): Self-compassion functions like a child's secure caregiver — the internal knowledge "I've got my own back" enables exploration, risk-taking, and recovery from failure.
- **Identity fusion**: Over-identifying with a role ("I AM an entrepreneur/doctor/athlete") makes self-worth entirely dependent on performance in that role. Values-grounded identity is more resilient.
