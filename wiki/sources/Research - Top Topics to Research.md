# Research — Top Topics to Research

> Autoresearch synthesis: what are the most valuable topics for Joe to research, and what are the full roadmaps? 9 web searches run. 7 pages created.

---

## Finding 1: The EE+AI Combo Is Underutilized and Extremely Rare
- EE students who learn ML occupy a category that almost no hiring manager sees: they understand physical systems (power converters, motors, sensors) AND can model them with learned functions
- Ranjram's ML-CEMS is active proof that this intersection is where frontier power electronics research is going
- The free education pipeline (fast.ai → Karpathy → CS231n → Sutton & Barto RL) now matches anything in a paid graduate program
- **No other investment per hour studied has a higher expected ROI for Joe in 2026**

## Finding 2: Power Electronics Self-Study Should Go Hart → Erickson, Not Erickson First
- *Fundamentals of Power Electronics* (Erickson & Maksimovic) is the definitive graduate-level text, but its entry bar is high
- *Power Electronics* by Daniel Hart is the correct undergrad-friendly first pass — covers all topologies, accessible language
- MIT OCW 6.622 (Prof. David Perreault) is the best free complement; full lectures, problem sets, solutions
- University of Colorado's Coursera Power Electronics Specialization (taught by Erickson's group) is the structured online path
- Phil's Lab on YouTube is the best practical channel for PCB + power electronics combined

## Finding 3: Personal Finance Is Already Well-Documented in the Wiki
- [[Financial Literacy Roadmap]] already contains a complete 5-phase roadmap with specific fund tickers, tax-advantaged account limits, and compound interest tables
- The missing pieces were book recommendations and YouTube channels
- Best books: *I Will Teach You to Be Rich* (Sethi, best for 20s), *The Simple Path to Wealth* (JL Collins, Bogleheads indexing philosophy), *The Psychology of Money* (Housel, behavioral layer)
- Best YouTube: Ben Felix (evidence-based, Canadian), The Plain Bagel, Andrei Jikh (beginner-friendly)

## Finding 4: Stoicism Already Has a Book List; Sequence Was Missing
- [[Book List — Stoicism and Philosophy]] was comprehensive but unsequenced
- Correct reading order for a 19yo: Daily Stoic (vocabulary) → Ego Is the Enemy (most relevant for ambition) → Enchiridion (primary text) → Meditations → Letters from a Stoic
- Einzelgänger on YouTube is the best Stoicism video channel (philosophical visual essays)
- Practice without books: the 5-minute morning journal (premeditatio + virtue intention) + 5-minute evening review is the minimum viable practice

## Finding 5: Sleep Science Was Protocol-Rich but Mechanism-Poor
- [[Sleep Habits Tier List]] already contains a complete evidence-based protocol (S through F tier, full protocol stack)
- What was missing: the neuroscience behind the protocols (two-process model, glymphatic system, stage architecture, memory consolidation mechanisms)
- Matthew Walker's *Why We Sleep* (2017) remains the best single source despite some contested statistics
- Huberman + Walker 6-part series on Huberman Lab is the best free audio equivalent
- Maiken Nedergaard's glymphatic system discovery (University of Copenhagen) is the most recent foundational finding — explains why sleep clearing of amyloid-beta connects to Alzheimer's risk

## Finding 6: Negotiation Is Completely Absent from the Wiki
- No negotiation page existed; this is a significant gap given Joe's career trajectory
- Chris Voss's *Never Split the Difference* is the consensus starting point (tactical psychology, FBI-derived)
- Getting to Yes (Fisher & Ury) provides the structural complement (BATNA, principled negotiation)
- Salary negotiation on first job is the single highest-ROI application: $10k raise at 22 compounds to $250k+ in cumulative career earnings
- Black Swan Group YouTube + Chris Voss MasterClass are the best course complements

## Finding 7: Mathematics Deep Study Is Partially Covered
- [[Calculus in Electrical Engineering]] and [[Differential Equations in Electrical Engineering]] exist
- MIT OCW 18.06 Linear Algebra (Prof. Gilbert Strang) is the universally recommended first course — free, complete, legendary
- Linear algebra is the exact mathematical foundation for both EE (state-space, signals) and ML (neural network layers, SVD, PCA)
- Gilbert Strang's *Introduction to Linear Algebra* (6th ed.) is the companion textbook

## Finding 8: A Master Index Was Missing
- The wiki had roadmaps spread across domains but no single "what should I research and why" meta-page
- [[Top Topics to Research — Master Guide]] created as a ranked table with ROI, urgency, and cross-topic synergies
- Most important cross-synergy: Power Electronics × AI/ML → digital learned control of power converters = active research frontier, and Ranjram's lab is already there
