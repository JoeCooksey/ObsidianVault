## Finance & Investing
→ See [[Book List — Finance and Investing]]

**S-tier** (must-read, foundational):
- *The Intelligent Investor* — Benjamin Graham — value investing bible; Warren Buffett's #1 recommendation
- *The Psychology of Money* — Morgan Housel — behavioral finance masterwork; why behavior > intelligence in investing
- *The Essays of Warren Buffett* — compiled letters on business and investment philosophy

**A-tier** (read within 12 months):
- *Just Keep Buying* — Nick Maggiulli — data-driven debunking of personal finance myths
- *I Will Teach You to Be Rich* — Ramit Sethi — practical systems for automating wealth in your 20s
- *Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order* — Ray Dalio — macro cycles, empires, geopolitics & markets
- *The Little Book of Common Sense Investing* — John Bogle — index fund thesis; the mathematical argument for passive investing
- *One Up On Wall Street* — Peter Lynch — how individual investors can beat professionals

**B-tier** (solid, read when ready):
- *Rich Dad Poor Dad* — Robert Kiyosaki — mindset shift on assets vs. liabilities; foundational framing (not technical)
- *A Random Walk Down Wall Street* — Burton Malkiel — efficient market hypothesis; historical data
- *The Millionaire Next Door* — Stanley & Danko — empirical study of actual wealth accumulation habits

## Self-Help, Discipline & Productivity
→ See [[Book List — Self-Help, Discipline, and Productivity]]

**S-tier**:
- *Atomic Habits* — James Clear — already ingested; ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Joe; Four Laws of Behavior Change
- *Can't Hurt Me* — David Goggins — mental toughness via extreme adversity; 40% rule (you're only at 40% when you think you're done)
- *Deep Work* — Cal Newport — deliberate focused work in distracted age; the only path to rare & valuable skill

**A-tier**:
- *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People* — Stephen Covey — be → do → have model; proactivity, prioritization (P/PC balance); timeless
- *Essentialism: The Disciplined Pursuit of Less* — Greg McKeown — systematic elimination of the non-essential; "less but better"
- *Man's Search for Meaning* — Viktor Frankl — logotherapy from Holocaust survival; meaning > pleasure as core human driver
- *Make Your Bed* — Admiral McRaven — discipline via small wins; 10 life lessons from Navy SEAL training
- *12 Rules for Life* — Jordan Peterson — order vs. chaos; stand up straight; take responsibility; rigorous self-authorship

**B-tier**:
- *Getting Things Done (GTD)* — David Allen — external system for capturing + processing tasks; classic productivity infrastructure
- *The Power of Habit* — Charles Duhigg — habit loop (cue-routine-reward) precursor to Clear; organizational habits too
- *Eat That Frog!* — Brian Tracy — tackle hardest task first; 21 time-management principles

## Stoicism & Philosophy
→ See [[Book List — Stoicism and Philosophy]]

**S-tier** (primary texts first):
- *Meditations* — Marcus Aurelius — private journal of a Roman emperor; dichotomy of control; most re-readable Stoic text
- *Ego Is the Enemy* — Ryan Holiday — ego as the obstacle to every great ambition; essential for ambitious young men
- *The Obstacle Is the Way* — Ryan Holiday — Stoic framework applied to modern challenges; amor fati in action

**A-tier**:
- *Letters from a Stoic (Epistulae Morales)* — Seneca — 124 letters; practical Stoic wisdom on time, friendship, death; highly readable
- *The Daily Stoic* — Ryan Holiday & Steve Hanselman — 366-day entry format; best Stoic on-ramp for beginners
- *Discourses* — Epictetus — dichotomy of control at full depth; "some things are in our power, some are not"

**B-tier**:
- *Status Anxiety* — Alain de Botton — philosophical study of why we crave status and how to escape its grip
- *The Courage to Be Disliked* — Ichiro Kishimi (Adlerian) — liberation from others' expectations via Adlerian psychology; reads as Socratic dialogue

## Psychology, Persuasion & Negotiation
→ See [[Book List — Psychology and Persuasion]]

**S-tier**:
- *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* — Robert Cialdini — 6 principles (reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity); foundational
- *Never Split the Difference* — Chris Voss — FBI hostage negotiation techniques applied to everyday life; tactical empathy + mirroring
- *How to Win Friends and Influence People* — Dale Carnegie — already in index; timeless human relations principles

**A-tier**:
- *Thinking, Fast and Slow* — Daniel Kahneman — System 1 vs System 2; cognitive biases; Nobel Prize framework
- *Pre-Suasion* — Robert Cialdini — the moment before the ask matters as much as the ask itself
- *Getting to Yes* — Fisher & Ury — principled negotiation; BATNA; win-win over positional bargaining

**B-tier**:
- *The 48 Laws of Power* — Robert Greene — realist's guide to power dynamics; read alongside Stoic texts as a counter
- *Crucial Conversations* — Patterson, Grenny et al. — high-stakes communication when emotions run hot
- *Influence Is Your Superpower* — Zoe Chance (Yale) — modern update to Cialdini; practical and data-backed

## Business & Entrepreneurship
→ See [[Book List — Business and Entrepreneurship]]

**S-tier**:
- *Zero to One* — Peter Thiel — monopoly thinking; secrets; going from 0→1 vs 1→n; the contrarian question
- *Good to Great* — Jim Collins — 5-year study of what separates great companies; flywheel + hedgehog concept
- *Shoe Dog* — Phil Knight — Nike origin story; raw, honest memoir of building a brand; best business autobiography
- *The Lean Startup* — Eric Ries — build-measure-learn loop; validated learning; minimum viable product

**A-tier**:
- *The Hard Thing About Hard Things* — Ben Horowitz — no-BS startup and leadership advice; what business books don't tell you
- *The E-Myth Revisited* — Michael Gerber — why most small businesses fail (working IN vs ON the business); systems mindset
- *Deep Work* — Cal Newport — (also in self-help) — most valuable professional skill is undistracted focused work

**B-tier**:
- *Blue Ocean Strategy* — Kim & Mauborgne — create uncontested market space; strategy canvas
- *The Innovator's Dilemma* — Clayton Christensen — how incumbents get disrupted by sustaining vs. disruptive innovation
- *48 Laws of Power* — Robert Greene — (also in persuasion) — strategic thinking in organizational environments

## Fiction — Classics & Modern Must-Reads
→ See [[Book List — Fiction Must-Reads]]

**S-tier Classics** (highest literary ROI):
- *Crime and Punishment* — Dostoevsky — psychology of guilt, pride, and redemption; Raskolnikov as cautionary archetype of unchecked intellect
- *The Count of Monte Cristo* — Alexandre Dumas — patience, revenge, transformation, wealth; most page-turning classic ever written
- *1984* — George Orwell — totalitarianism, language, power, surveillance; more relevant every year
- *East of Eden* — John Steinbeck — good vs. evil; timshel ("thou mayest"); father-son legacy; the great American novel
- *The Brothers Karamazov* — Dostoevsky — faith, doubt, free will, morality at the deepest level

**S-tier Modern**:
- *The Road* — Cormac McCarthy — fatherhood, survival, hope in total collapse; minimalist prose at maximum emotional power
- *Dune* — Frank Herbert — leadership, ecology, religion, power; most influential sci-fi novel ever; read before watching
- *All Quiet on the Western Front* — Erich Maria Remarque — war's horror from the perspective of a 19-year-old soldier; counterweight to glorification

**A-tier** (excellent, accessible):
- *The Great Gatsby* — F. Scott Fitzgerald — ambition, illusion, class, the American Dream's dark side; 200 pages
- *The Alchemist* — Paulo Coelho — following your Personal Legend; simple, fable-style, resonates deeply at 19–25
- *To Kill a Mockingbird* — Harper Lee — moral courage in the face of injustice; Atticus Finch as character ideal
- *Ender's Game* — Orson Scott Card — strategy, leadership, isolation, moral cost of winning; gripping for technical minds
- *The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy* — Douglas Adams — absurdist philosophy + genuine wit; counterbalance to intensity

**B-tier** (great but niche):
- *One Hundred Years of Solitude* — Gabriel García Márquez — magical realism; multigenerational saga; dense but rewarding
- *Brave New World* — Aldous Huxley — soma, consumerism, manufactured happiness; companion to 1984; feels more like 2026
- *The War of Art* — Steven Pressfield — technically a self-help book framed as creative fiction; Resistance as the enemy of creation

## Joe's Recommended Reading Order
*Given Joe's profile: 19yo EE student, ambitious, high cognitive bandwidth, wants skills + meaning*

### Phase 1 — Foundation (months 1–3)
1. *The Psychology of Money* — immediately applicable; reshapes money mindset
2. *Ego Is the Enemy* — essential before big ambitions create blind spots
3. *Deep Work* — install the operating system for skill acquisition
4. *Can't Hurt Me* — push past perceived limits; recalibrate what hard means

### Phase 2 — Systems (months 4–6)
5. *The Intelligent Investor* (chapters 1, 8, 20 minimum) — foundational investment framework
6. *Never Split the Difference* — every negotiation from salary to relationships
7. *Zero to One* — thinking about how to create, not just optimize
8. *The Count of Monte Cristo* — read for pure joy; patience + transformation archetype

### Phase 3 — Depth (months 7–12)
9. *Meditations* — Marcus Aurelius — daily re-reading material
10. *Thinking, Fast and Slow* — understand your own cognitive biases
11. *East of Eden* — the question of who you choose to become
12. *Man's Search for Meaning* — meaning > comfort; foundational life philosophy
