# Research — Peptide Tier List (Personalized for Joe)

**Query:** Tier list of every peptide by effectiveness vs. risk, personalized for Joe, with synergies.

**Joe's Profile for Personalization:**
- 19-year-old male, 5'9" / 156 lbs, BMI ~23 (healthy)
- Cyclist 6×/week (zone 2 + intensity); lean bulk / recomp goal
- EE student at ASU — cognitive performance matters
- Existing stack: creatine, D3+K2, magnesium glycinate, omega-3, zinc
- Skincare protocol already in place (SPF50, retinoid, Vitamin C serum)
- Possibly competitive cyclist → WADA considerations apply

## Key Findings

### 1. Human Evidence Is Thin Across the Board
Despite huge biohacking buzz, most peptides have near-zero published human RCTs. As of March 2026, BPC-157 — one of the most-studied "research peptides" — has fewer than 30 total human subjects in published studies. This is not a reason to avoid all peptides, but it IS a reason to tier by risk profile, not just theoretical benefit. The rule: **evidence quality × safety window × personal relevance**.

### 2. Regulatory Shift in Feb 2026
On February 27, 2026, HHS Secretary RFK Jr. announced ~14 of 19 previously restricted peptides are expected to return to legal compounding status (licensed pharmacies with valid prescriptions). This includes BPC-157, Thymosin Alpha-1, CJC-1295, AOD-9604, Selank, Semax, KPV, and Epithalon. This represents a meaningful shift — access via telehealth/compounding pharmacy is becoming more viable.

### 3. GH-Axis Peptides Are Overkill at 19
Ipamorelin, CJC-1295, sermorelin, and related GH secretagogues are primarily valuable for adults 30+ who have declining growth hormone. At 19, GH is at lifetime peak. Using GH peptides risks disrupting a healthy GH pulsatile rhythm, insulin sensitivity, and IGF-1 feedback loops. The risk/reward is unfavorable unless there's a specific medical reason.

### 4. The Healing Peptides (BPC-157 / TB-500) Have the Best Athlete Rationale
For a cyclist doing 6 sessions/week, overuse injuries (tendons, joints, gut inflammation) are the primary health risk. BPC-157 has the strongest mechanism and the most animal evidence for tissue repair. It's the one peptide worth understanding at Joe's current level. TB-500 is the systemic complement but is WADA-banned.

### 5. Cognitive Peptides (Semax, Selank) Are Uniquely Relevant for a Student
Semax upregulates BDNF (brain fertilizer) — most relevant for the kind of focused learning Joe is doing in EE self-study. Selank is a clean anxiolytic for exam stress — no sedation, no dependency. This pairing is arguably Joe's highest-priority peptide stack if he ever uses peptides.

### 6. GHK-Cu Is the Safest High-Value Peptide — and Joe Already Has a Context for It
GHK-Cu has RCT-confirmed collagen, skin elasticity, and hair benefits (topical). It fits directly into Joe's existing skincare protocol. It's the only peptide with a clean safety profile, meaningful human evidence, and zero injection required. This should be Joe's entry-point peptide.

### 7. WADA Banned = Hard No for Joe
If Joe cycles competitively at any level, TB-500, AOD-9604, MK-677, and all GH secretagogues are prohibited under WADA's S2 (Peptide Hormones and Related Substances) and S4 (Hormone Modulators) categories. This moves them from "C-tier evidence" to "D-tier for Joe." Never take banned substances for recreational performance.

### 8. Synergy Framework: Two Complementary Axes
Every useful peptide stack targets either (a) the **GH axis** (CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin; sermorelin) or (b) the **tissue repair / inflammation axis** (BPC-157 + KPV). Mixing both axes does NOT compound side effects, making BPC-157 + Semax a well-reasoned recovery + cognition stack. The "Wolverine Stack" (BPC-157 + TB-500) is the most discussed healing combo — BPC-157 handles local repair, TB-500 handles systemic cell migration and anti-inflammation. TB-500 is WADA-banned, so substitute with Thymosin Alpha-1 (not banned, better immune evidence).

3. Are compounding pharmacy sources of BPC-157 reliably at pharmaceutical grade, and how do you verify?
4. Is there a safe, testable biomarker (like IGF-1 blood test) that could confirm a peptide is actually working?
