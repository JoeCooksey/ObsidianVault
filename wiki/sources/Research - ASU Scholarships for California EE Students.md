# Research: ASU Scholarships for California EE Students

**Topic**: Best scholarships for a California student attending ASU for electrical engineering
**Date**: 2026-05-03
**Method**: Autonomous web research (6 searches)

---

## 8 Key Findings

**1. WUE is dead for Fall 2026 — the Commitment Scholarship replaces it.**
The Western Undergraduate Exchange (WUE) — which gave Western-state students 150% of in-state tuition (saving ~$15,000/year) — is being phased out starting Fall 2026. ASU's replacement is the Commitment Scholarship ($5,500–$7,500/yr), which covers fewer dollars but is available to all out-of-state U.S. citizens, not just Western states. Net effect: California students starting Fall 2026 lose ~$7,500–$9,500/yr vs. WUE era.

**2. NAU Scholarship + Commitment Scholarship can stack to $25,000/year — no application needed.**
Both are automatically awarded at admission. The New American University (NAU) Scholarship gives $5,500–$17,500/year based purely on high school GPA in core subjects (SAT/ACT optional but additive if submitted by May 1). Stacking these two gives $11,000–$25,000/year before any separate application is filed.

**3. Cal Grants are completely off the table for ASU.**
California's primary state grant programs (Cal Grant A, B, C) are restricted to California institutions only — not portable to out-of-state schools. This is a hard constraint. ASU students from California lose access to all California state aid programs.

**4. The Fulton Schools award $1.7M+ to undergrads annually — and it's not just for A students.**
The Ira A. Fulton Schools scholarship portal (now on Scholarship Universe) is the highest-leverage supplemental source for ASU EE students. Application opens November 1, deadline February 1. Eligibility includes academic merit AND non-traditional criteria like talent, personal story, and creativity — a student who applies thoughtfully has a genuine shot even without a 4.0.

**5. IEEE PES Scholarship Plus is the top external scholarship for EE students.**
Up to $10,000/year, min 3.0 GPA, April 30 deadline, renewable. Power and energy focus aligns exactly with the WBG/power electronics track at ASU. This is the single best external scholarship for an ASU EE student — especially one targeting power electronics research (FURI with Ranjram or Ayyanar).

**6. Federal Pell Grant is the only California-sourced aid that is portable to ASU.**
Up to ~$7,395/year, income-based via FAFSA. Filing FAFSA by March 2 (California priority deadline) is mandatory to access this. It does not reduce based on the student's major or school ranking — it only depends on family income (EFC).

**7. ACEC California and most California-based engineering scholarships require in-state enrollment.**
ACEC California ($1k–$10k) requires enrollment in a California engineering program. Most California-specific scholarship programs have similar restrictions. The only paths out of California that travel with a student are federal aid (Pell/loans) and national/professional organization scholarships (IEEE, SWE, NSPE).

**8. Optimal strategy: automate the base, then layer with targeted applications.**
The auto-awarded ASU scholarships (NAU + Commitment) handle the base layer without any work. FAFSA handles federal eligibility. The real ROI multiplier is the IEEE PES Scholarship Plus (up to $10k) + Fulton Schools portal (up to $5k+). A student who applies to both gets ~$15,000–$25,000 above the auto-awarded floor for perhaps 10–15 hours of application work — the best dollars-per-hour of any action available.
